Smart Cities Mission at 10: how technology is reshaping urban India

Key facts

  • Smart Cities Mission completes 10 years in 2025
  • Covers 100 cities across India
  • Original budget: Rs 98,000 crore
  • Key tech: command centres, smart traffic, digital citizen services

What happened

The Times of India reports on the 10-year milestone of the Smart Cities Mission, launched in 2015, assessing how technology deployment across 100 chosen cities has changed urban management. The report covers integrated command centres, smart water and waste management systems, and digital public services as key achievements of the decade-long initiative.

The context

The Smart Cities Mission was launched by PM Modi in June 2015 with a budget of Rs 98,000 crore, targeting 100 cities for technology-driven urban redevelopment. Cities like Pune, Ahmedabad, and Surat have been cited as standout performers. The mission has faced criticism for uneven implementation and a focus on greenfield projects over inclusive upgrades.

Why it matters

The success or failure of smart city infrastructure directly affects the daily commute, water supply, waste disposal, and public safety of hundreds of millions of urban Indians, making it one of the most consequential government technology programmes in the country.

What's next

Expect the government to announce a Smart Cities Mission 2.0 framework, likely with a renewed focus on AI-driven urban services and greater private-sector participation.
