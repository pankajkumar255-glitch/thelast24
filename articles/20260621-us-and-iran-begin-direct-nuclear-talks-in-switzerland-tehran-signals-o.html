US and Iran Begin Direct Nuclear Talks in Switzerland, Tehran Signals Openness to Deal

Key facts

  • US and Iran holding direct talks in Switzerland
  • Iran calls it a chance to "turn over a new leaf"
  • Trump threatened to "hit harder" if talks fail
  • Iran warned US to "be careful" amid the exchanges
  • Lebanon conflict also raised by Iran in the talks

The United States and Iran sat down for direct negotiations in Switzerland on Saturday, launching what both sides cautiously framed as a potential turning point in years of fractured diplomacy over Tehran's nuclear programme. Iran's delegation described the talks as a chance to "turn over a new leaf," signalling at least a nominal willingness to engage seriously after a prolonged period of maximum-pressure sanctions and rising regional tensions.

The backdrop to the talks, however, remained charged. President Donald Trump, even as negotiations opened, issued a stark warning that the US would "hit harder" if Iran failed to reach an agreement — the kind of coercive diplomacy that has defined his approach to Tehran since his first term. Iranian officials were quick to respond, publicly advising the US to "be careful," reflecting the deep mistrust that continues to shadow both delegations at the table.

Iran also indicated that the Lebanon conflict featured prominently on its agenda during the Switzerland discussions, suggesting that any eventual framework would need to address not just nuclear thresholds but the broader web of regional proxy conflicts in which Tehran is deeply embedded. That complicates the path to any swift agreement, as the US and Israel remain focused narrowly on halting Iran's uranium enrichment.

The Switzerland talks represent the most substantive direct US-Iran engagement in years. Whether they produce a durable framework or collapse under the weight of mutual suspicion — as previous rounds have — will have consequences stretching well beyond the two countries, touching oil markets, Middle East stability, and the security calculations of nations from Israel to India.
