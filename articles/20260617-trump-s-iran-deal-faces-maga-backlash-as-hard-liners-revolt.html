Trump's Iran Deal Faces MAGA Backlash as Hard-Liners Revolt

What happened

The Times of India reports that Donald Trump's Iran deal is facing significant pushback from within the MAGA movement, with conservative hard-liners opposing any agreement with Tehran. The revolt highlights a deep internal divide within the Republican camp over Middle East policy.

The context

Trump built much of his foreign policy brand on maximum-pressure tactics against Iran, including withdrawing from the 2015 nuclear deal signed under Obama. Reaching any form of agreement with Tehran now represents a sharp reversal that many of his core supporters find difficult to accept.

Why it matters

A collapse of the US-Iran agreement could reignite regional tensions, push oil prices higher, and disrupt energy markets that India depends on heavily for its import bill. Conversely, a stable deal could ease energy costs and reduce geopolitical risk.

What's next

Expect Trump to face mounting congressional and grassroots pressure from the right as details of the Iran MoU become clearer.
