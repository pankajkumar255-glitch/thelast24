Apple's MacBooks and iPads get up to 70% costlier in India after latest price hike

Key facts

  • MacBooks and iPads up to 70% costlier in India
  • India identified as the hardest-hit market from Apple's price hike
  • Both MacBook and iPad product lines affected

Apple's latest round of price increases has hit India harder than any other market, with MacBooks and iPads becoming up to 70% more expensive, according to a report by The Economic Times. The sharp hike places India among the most expensive countries to buy Apple's computing devices, a development that is likely to dampen demand for the brand's mid-to-high tier products.

The price revision covers Apple's popular iPad and MacBook lines, which are widely used by students, professionals, and creative workers across India. A jump of up to 70% on select models represents one of the steepest single-revision increases Apple has imposed on any major market, and analysts have pointed to a combination of import duties, currency fluctuations, and global supply chain adjustments as contributing factors.

For Indian consumers, the timing is significant. Apple had been gradually expanding its retail and manufacturing footprint in India, positioning the country as both a production hub and a growing consumer market. A price hike of this magnitude could slow that consumer momentum, particularly in the mid-range segment where Apple had been making inroads against Android competitors.

The development also raises questions about Apple's India strategy. While the company has invested in local iPhone assembly to reduce costs, iPads and MacBooks are still largely imported, leaving them more exposed to duty and forex pressures. Consumers and resellers alike will be watching to see whether Apple adjusts its pricing further or introduces more affordable configurations to soften the blow.
