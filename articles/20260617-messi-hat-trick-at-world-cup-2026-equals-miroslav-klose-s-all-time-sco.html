Messi hat-trick at World Cup 2026 equals Miroslav Klose's all-time scoring record

Key facts

  • Messi hat-trick came in World Cup 2026 on June 17
  • Equals Miroslav Klose's all-time World Cup scoring record
  • Klose's record of 16 goals stood since Germany's 2014 triumph
  • Messi won the World Cup with Argentina in 2022

What happened

ESPN India reports that Lionel Messi scored a hat-trick during a World Cup 2026 match on June 17, equalling Miroslav Klose's record for the most goals scored in FIFA World Cup history. The feat came in live match action, with ESPN providing continuous updates as the milestone unfolded.

The context

Miroslav Klose set the all-time World Cup scoring record with 16 goals across four tournaments for Germany, surpassing Brazil's Ronaldo at the 2014 World Cup. Messi, widely regarded as one of the greatest footballers ever, won the FIFA World Cup with Argentina in 2022 and has continued playing into the 2026 edition.

Why it matters

Messi matching Klose's record is a defining sporting milestone of this World Cup and will dominate global sports conversation; for Indian fans, it is a rare live brush with football history at a tournament their own country is not part of.

What's next

Expect Messi to be one more goal away from breaking the record outright, making every subsequent Argentina match a landmark watch.
