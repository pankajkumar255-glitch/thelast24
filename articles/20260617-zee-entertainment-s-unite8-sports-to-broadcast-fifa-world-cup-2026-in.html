Zee Entertainment's Unite8 Sports to broadcast FIFA World Cup 2026 in India

What happened

The Times of India reports that Zee Entertainment's Unite8 Sports has officially secured broadcasting rights to air the FIFA World Cup in India. The announcement confirms which Indian platform will carry the world's biggest football event in 2026.

The context

FIFA World Cup 2026 will be the first edition with 48 teams, hosted jointly by the USA, Canada, and Mexico, making it the largest World Cup in history. Broadcasting rights for marquee global events in India are fiercely contested among platforms including JioStar and Sony. Zee's Unite8 Sports is the company's dedicated sports broadcasting entity, looking to establish itself as a major player in the segment.

Why it matters

Indian football fans now know where to tune in for the FIFA World Cup 2026, affecting subscription and viewership decisions for millions of households. For Zee Entertainment, landing such a high-profile property is a critical boost to Unite8 Sports' credibility and subscriber base.

What's next

Expect Zee to announce specific channel and streaming details for the World Cup broadcast, along with potential subscription offers to attract viewers ahead of the tournament.
