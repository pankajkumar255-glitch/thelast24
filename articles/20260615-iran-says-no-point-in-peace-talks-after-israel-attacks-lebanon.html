Iran Says 'No Point' In Peace Talks After Israel Attacks Lebanon

What happened

NDTV reports that Iran has slammed the US following Israeli attacks on Lebanon, with Iranian officials stating there is 'no point' in peace talks. The statement appears to directly contradict US President Trump's claim that an Iran peace deal is 'now complete.' The remarks escalate diplomatic uncertainty in the region even as Trump declared the Strait of Hormuz open.

The context

Iran and the US have had no formal diplomatic relations since 1980, and negotiations over Iran's nuclear programme and regional influence have long been fraught. Iran backs Hezbollah in Lebanon and views Israeli military operations there as an extension of what it calls a US-backed assault. Iran's rejection of talks — or at minimum its public fury — is a critical data point in assessing whether Trump's 'deal' claim holds substance.

Why it matters

If Iran genuinely withdraws from or rejects peace talks, regional tensions could re-escalate, threatening oil supply routes and pushing crude prices higher — a direct hit to Indian consumers through costlier petrol, diesel, and everyday goods.

What's next

Expect further clarification from both Washington and Tehran in the next 24-48 hours on whether any formal agreement actually exists.
