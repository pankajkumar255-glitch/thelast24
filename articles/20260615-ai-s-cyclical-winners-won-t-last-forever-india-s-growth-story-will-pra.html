AI's Cyclical Winners Won't Last Forever. India's Growth Story Will: Prashant Jain

What happened

Moneycontrol reports an exclusive interview in which veteran fund manager Prashant Jain states that AI's cyclical market winners will not last forever, while arguing that India's structural growth story has greater longevity. Jain distinguishes between momentum-driven global AI plays and India's domestic consumption and investment-led growth.

The context

Prashant Jain managed the flagship HDFC Equity Fund for over two decades and is one of India's most closely followed equity investors. His views on market cycles and India's long-term growth potential are widely referenced by institutional and retail investors alike. The comment comes amid a global surge in AI-related stocks that has drawn significant capital away from emerging markets including India.

Why it matters

For millions of Indian mutual fund investors, this is a signal to reassess whether chasing global AI-themed funds is more prudent than staying in India-focused equity funds that track the structural growth story. It also reinforces confidence in domestic consumption and infrastructure-linked investments.

What's next

Expect continued debate among fund managers and analysts on the sustainability of AI valuations versus India's domestic equity premium, particularly as Q1 FY27 earnings season approaches.
