India-UK Trade Deal Takes Centre Stage as PM Modi Meets Starmer at G7

What happened

NDTV reports that PM Modi met UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer at the G7 Summit, with the India-UK trade deal described as taking centre stage in their discussions. The meeting signals fresh political momentum behind negotiations that have stretched over multiple rounds. No final agreement was announced but the bilateral engagement at the G7 level underlines the deal's priority status.

The context

India and the UK began formal FTA negotiations in January 2022. Talks have covered tariffs on whisky, cars, and textiles, as well as visa arrangements for Indian professionals. The change in UK government from the Conservatives to Labour under Starmer had briefly introduced uncertainty, but both sides have since reaffirmed commitment to the deal.

Why it matters

A finalised India-UK FTA would create new export opportunities for Indian textiles, pharmaceuticals, and IT services, while potentially making British goods like Scotch whisky and luxury cars cheaper in India. It would also ease mobility for Indian students and professionals heading to the UK.

What's next

Expect an announcement on a target timeline or a specific round of concluding talks to follow the G7 sideline meeting between Modi and Starmer.
