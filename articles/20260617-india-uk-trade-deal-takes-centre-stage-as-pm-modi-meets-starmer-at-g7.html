India-UK Trade Deal Takes Centre Stage as PM Modi Meets Starmer at G7

What happened

NDTV reports that the India-UK Free Trade Agreement took centre stage when PM Modi met UK PM Keir Starmer at the G7 Summit. The meeting signals fresh momentum in negotiations that have been ongoing since 2022. No final agreement was announced, but the bilateral engagement at the G7 level indicates political will on both sides to close the deal.

The context

India and the UK launched FTA negotiations in January 2022 but talks have repeatedly hit snags over market access, immigration, and tariff structures. The G7 Summit provides a high-profile diplomatic setting that often accelerates bilateral commitments. India has been actively expanding its trade treaty network, having recently concluded deals with the UAE and signed a limited pact with Australia.

Why it matters

A completed India-UK FTA would open preferential access for Indian exporters — particularly in textiles, pharmaceuticals, and IT services — to one of Europe's largest economies, creating jobs and boosting merchandise exports. British goods, including cars and whisky, could become cheaper in India, affecting domestic manufacturers and consumers alike.

What's next

Expect both governments to signal a timeline for concluding the deal in the coming weeks, with trade ministry officials likely to hold follow-up technical rounds after the G7 meeting.
