Vaibhav Sooryavanshi falls for 21 against Sri Lanka A, raising consistency concerns again

What happened

The Times of India reports that Vaibhav Sooryavanshi was dismissed for 21 against Sri Lanka A in a recent match, flagging this as another instance of the young batter getting a start but failing to go on to a significant score. The report frames it as part of a concerning pattern.

The context

Vaibhav Sooryavanshi became a household name after his record-breaking IPL century for Rajasthan Royals at an exceptionally young age, earning comparisons with greats of the game. He has since been part of India A and India U19 programmes, where the pressure to perform consistently is high. Converting starts into big scores is the key benchmark used by selectors to assess readiness for international cricket.

Why it matters

Sooryavanshi's development is one of Indian cricket's most closely watched stories, and repeated low scores at A-team level could delay his path to a senior India call-up. It also raises questions about the management of young prodigies under intense public expectation.

What's next

Expect selectors and coaches to closely monitor Sooryavanshi's next innings for signs that he can address this specific weakness before the India U19 or A-team's upcoming fixtures.
