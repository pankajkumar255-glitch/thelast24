Supreme Court asks Centre and states to respond on Aadhaar-as-citizenship-proof plea

What happened

The Hindu and The Indian Express both report that the Supreme Court has issued notices to the Centre and states seeking their response on a plea challenging the alleged misuse of Aadhaar cards as proof of Indian citizenship. The court's decision to seek a government response indicates it has found the petition worthy of examination.

The context

Aadhaar, administered by the Unique Identification Authority of India, was designed exclusively as a proof of identity and residence, not citizenship. However, concerns have grown that authorities use it as a de facto citizenship document, particularly in the context of the National Register of Citizens and related exercises. The distinction is legally significant — holding an Aadhaar card confers no citizenship rights.

Why it matters

A misuse finding could trigger a judicial directive that changes how governments conduct identity verification, affecting hundreds of millions of Aadhaar holders and potentially reshaping citizenship-linked welfare and legal processes across India.

What's next

Expect the Centre and state governments to file their affidavits within the court's stipulated deadline, after which the matter is likely to be listed for a substantive hearing.
