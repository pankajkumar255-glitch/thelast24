India A penalised 10 runs during batting against Sri Lanka A in Dambulla — Vipraj Nigam at the centre

What happened

Hindustan Times reports that India A were handed a 10-run penalty while batting against Sri Lanka A in Dambulla, with Vipraj Nigam named as the reason for the sanction. The report explains the specific incident that led match officials to impose this rarely-seen punishment.

The context

Run penalties in cricket are governed by the Laws of Cricket, typically applied for deliberate time-wasting, ball-tampering, or other serious infractions. India A tours are crucial proving grounds for players on the fringes of the senior national team, and any controversy involving emerging players draws significant scrutiny from selectors and fans alike.

Why it matters

The penalty directly cost India A runs in the match, potentially affecting the series result and individual players' records under assessment by national selectors. It also raises questions about discipline and awareness of playing regulations among India's next generation of cricketers.

What's next

Expect the BCCI and team management to review the incident and likely issue a clarification or corrective directive to the India A squad before the next match in the series.
