HDFC Bank Names Rajiv Kumar as Non-Executive Chairman, Puneet Sharma as New CFO

Key facts

  • Rajiv Kumar, ex-financial services secretary, named Non-Executive Chairman
  • Puneet Sharma, outgoing Axis Bank CFO, likely to join as HDFC Bank CFO
  • Marks significant leadership churn at India's largest private sector bank

HDFC Bank announced a pair of major leadership appointments, naming former financial services secretary Rajiv Kumar as its new Non-Executive Chairman and moving to hire Puneet Sharma, the recently departed CFO of Axis Bank, as its own Chief Financial Officer.

Rajiv Kumar's appointment as part-time Chairman brings a seasoned government hand to HDFC Bank's boardroom. Kumar previously served as financial services secretary in the Union government, overseeing banking and insurance policy, and his appointment underscores the bank's intent to strengthen its regulatory and institutional relationships at the highest level.

The CFO transition is equally notable. Axis Bank confirmed that Puneet Sharma would be exiting as its finance chief, and Moneycontrol reported that HDFC Bank is in line to bring him on board — a rare instance of a top finance executive moving directly between two of India's biggest private banks. The development has been described as part of a broader churn in the finance function at large private lenders.

Together, the appointments represent a significant reset in HDFC Bank's senior leadership, at a time when the bank continues to integrate HDFC Ltd following their landmark merger. Investors and analysts will be watching how the new leadership steers capital strategy, regulatory engagement, and the bank's next growth phase.
