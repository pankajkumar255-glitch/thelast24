Rohit Sharma becomes oldest Indian man to play an ODI, scripts history in Dharamsala

What happened

India Today reports that Rohit Sharma scripted history during the first ODI against Afghanistan in Dharamsala by becoming the oldest man to play an ODI for India. The Times of India adds that he also became only the second Indian opener to achieve a significant batting landmark in the format. Both publications covered the milestone as part of their match reporting from Dharamsala. The records came during a match India won comfortably in rain-affected conditions.

The context

Rohit Sharma has been one of India's most decorated ODI cricketers, holding the record for the most double centuries in the format and being a three-time IPL-winning captain. He took over as India's ODI captain and has led the side in multiple ICC tournaments including the 2023 ODI World Cup final. At 37, he continues to be a first-choice opener, though questions about succession planning have grown in Indian cricket circles. His longevity rivals legends like Sachin Tendulkar and MS Dhoni.

Why it matters

Rohit's continued presence at the top of the order provides India with experience and stability ahead of upcoming ICC events. His record-breaking appearance is a cultural moment for Indian cricket fans who have followed his career across formats for nearly two decades. It also raises natural conversations about transition and how long India's senior guard will continue at the highest level.

What's next

Expect Rohit's form and fitness in the Afghanistan series to be closely scrutinised by selectors as they plan for the ICC Champions Trophy and beyond. Questions about the long-term ODI captaincy succession are likely to intensify as his records continue to reflect his advancing age in international cricket.
