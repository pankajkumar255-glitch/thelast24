India Coach Breaks Silence on Vaibhav Sooryavanshi Controversy, Says Sri Lankan Players Should Also Be Educated

What happened

The Times of India reports that India's coach has broken his silence on the controversy involving Vaibhav Sooryavanshi, saying that Sri Lankan players should also be educated about conduct on the field. The statement implies that the incident involved provocation or behaviour from the Sri Lankan side as well. The coach's remarks come as the controversy had been building in the lead-up to the on-field spat reported later in the evening.

The context

Vaibhav Sooryavanshi is one of Indian cricket's most talked-about young talents, having made a high-profile entry into top-level cricket as a teenager. Controversies involving young players and on-field conduct have previously led to ICC disciplinary reviews and board interventions. India-Sri Lanka cricket relations have generally been cordial, making a coach's public call for education of Sri Lankan players a notably firm statement.

Why it matters

The coach's intervention raises the stakes of what might otherwise be treated as a minor on-field incident, potentially drawing both cricket boards and the ICC into a formal review of player conduct.

What's next

Expect both the BCCI and Sri Lanka Cricket to respond formally, and watch for any ICC match referee report or disciplinary charges.
