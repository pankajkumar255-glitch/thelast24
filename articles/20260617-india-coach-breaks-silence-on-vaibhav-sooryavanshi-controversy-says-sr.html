India coach breaks silence on Vaibhav Sooryavanshi controversy, says Sri Lankan players should be educated too

What happened

The Times of India reports that India's cricket coach has broken his silence on the Vaibhav Sooryavanshi on-field controversy, stating that Sri Lankan players should also be educated about on-field conduct. The Hindu had earlier reported that Sooryavanshi lost his cool in an on-field spat during a match. The coach's statement implies the incident involved provocation from the opposition side as well.

The context

Vaibhav Sooryavanshi is one of Indian cricket's most celebrated young talents, having burst onto the scene with outstanding performances at a very young age. On-field spats in international cricket involving young players draw intense scrutiny because of the standards expected of India's cricketers. The incident involving Sri Lankan players adds a bilateral diplomatic dimension to what is otherwise a disciplinary matter.

Why it matters

The coach's public remarks signal India's official stance going into future engagements with Sri Lanka and set a precedent for how young players are mentored and protected. For fans, it raises questions about how prodigies are managed under the pressure of high-profile international cricket.

What's next

Expect the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) or match officials to issue a formal statement or take action regarding the conduct of players involved in the spat.
