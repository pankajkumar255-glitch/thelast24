Universities of Bristol, York, and New South Wales Get Approval to Set Up Campuses in India

What happened

PIB reports that Letters of Approval were handed over in the presence of Union Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan to the University of Bristol, the University of York, and the University of New South Wales, authorising them to set up campuses in India. The ceremony marks formal regulatory clearance for these three institutions to establish a physical academic presence in the country. The University of Bristol and the University of York are prominent U.K. institutions, while the University of New South Wales is one of Australia's leading research universities. This is part of India's broader effort under the National Education Policy 2020 to internationalise its higher education landscape.

The context

India's National Education Policy 2020 explicitly encouraged the entry of top foreign universities into the country to raise the quality of higher education and reduce the outflow of students and foreign exchange. The UGC introduced a framework for foreign higher educational institutions to set up campuses in India in 2023. Deakin University and the University of Wollongong from Australia were among the first to receive approval, with campuses being established in GIFT City, Gujarat. India sends hundreds of thousands of students abroad each year, representing a significant drain on foreign exchange that the government aims to partially reverse.

Why it matters

For Indian families, access to internationally accredited degrees from reputed universities in India could dramatically reduce the financial burden of overseas education, which can cost Rs 30–60 lakh or more. It also expands the quality of academic choices available domestically and could improve employability for graduates. The presence of foreign universities is expected to raise research output and bring new pedagogical approaches to Indian campuses. It also signals that India is successfully positioning itself as an attractive destination for global academic institutions.

What's next

Expect the three universities to announce specific campus locations, programme details, and intake timelines in the coming months. Watch for more foreign universities to apply for approval as India's regulatory framework for foreign campuses becomes more established.
