Amazon pours $13 billion more into India; Indian IT goes on $4.5 billion M&A spree

Key facts

  • Amazon investing additional $13 billion in India
  • Indian IT sector on $4.5 billion M&A spree in H1
  • Focus areas include AI, cloud, and digital services

Amazon has committed an additional $13 billion to its India operations, one of the largest single-country investment pledges the company has made, reflecting the strategic weight New Delhi now carries in Amazon's global playbook. The investment is expected to flow into cloud infrastructure, data centres, logistics networks, and the burgeoning artificial intelligence ecosystem that Amazon Web Services is racing to dominate.

In parallel, Indian IT companies have gone on a $4.5 billion M&A spree in the first half of the year, snapping up firms with niche capabilities in AI, cybersecurity, and enterprise digital transformation. The acquisition wave suggests that India's technology majors are no longer content to grow purely through headcount and organic capability-building — they are buying speed, talent, and intellectual property.

The convergence of inbound foreign investment and outbound M&A activity paints a picture of an Indian technology sector operating with considerably more confidence and capital than it did even two or three years ago. Amazon's bet, in particular, validates India not just as a cost-efficient services hub but as a genuine consumer and cloud market in its own right.

Together, the two developments represent billions of dollars flowing through India's tech economy in a single news cycle — a scale that would have seemed implausible a decade ago and that is reshaping the country's position in the global technology order.
