Supreme Court quantifies homemakers' labour, setting a precedent for compensation claims

What happened

The Hindu examines what prompted the Supreme Court to formally quantify the labour of homemakers, noting the court has now set out a method for calculating compensation for unpaid domestic work in legal cases. The report explores the reasoning behind the court's decision to assign economic value to homemaking.

The context

Indian courts, particularly in motor accident compensation cases under the Motor Vehicles Act, have historically struggled to assign income figures to homemakers since they receive no salary. Various High Courts have used ad hoc figures, leading to inconsistency. The Supreme Court's intervention seeks to create a uniform and dignified standard of valuation.

Why it matters

The ruling directly benefits families of homemakers in accident compensation cases, ensuring they receive fairer payouts from insurers and liable parties rather than token amounts. It also formally acknowledges in law what economists have long argued: that unpaid domestic work has substantial economic value that society must recognise.

What's next

Expect lower courts and motor accident claims tribunals across India to begin applying the Supreme Court's formula in pending and new compensation cases.
