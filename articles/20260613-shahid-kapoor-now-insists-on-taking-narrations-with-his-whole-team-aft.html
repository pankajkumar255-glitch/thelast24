Shahid Kapoor Now Insists on Taking Narrations with His Whole Team After Deva and O Romeo Failed

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Shahid Kapoor is now insisting on taking script narrations alongside his team rather than hearing them alone, following the commercial failures of his recent films Deva and O Romeo. The change in approach is reportedly a direct response to the underperformance of both projects. This shift suggests Kapoor is actively seeking a more collaborative decision-making process to avoid further missteps. The report highlights how the actor is recalibrating his career strategy in response to audience feedback.

The context

Shahid Kapoor has had a mixed box-office track record in recent years, with highs like Kabir Singh and Jersey and lows including his recent releases. Deva, an action thriller, and O Romeo both failed to make the expected commercial impact. The Hindi film industry has been facing a broader challenge with big-ticket releases underperforming, putting pressure on A-list stars to be more selective. Collaborative script evaluation is not unprecedented in Bollywood but is becoming more common as production budgets and marketing costs rise.

Why it matters

For audiences and the industry alike, a star of Kapoor's stature changing his decision-making process signals the increased accountability actors face after repeated box-office failures. It also points to the growing influence of collective creative judgment over individual star instinct in Bollywood. This could set a trend for how top actors manage their project pipelines.

What's next

Expect Kapoor's next project announcement to be closely watched as a test of this new approach. Industry observers will be looking to see whether his upcoming choices reflect a stronger script-first strategy.
