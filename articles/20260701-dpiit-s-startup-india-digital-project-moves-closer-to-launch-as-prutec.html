DPIIT's Startup India digital project moves closer to launch as PruTech qualifies

Key facts

  • PruTech qualifies for DPIIT's Startup India digital modernisation project
  • Project aims to upgrade the government's primary startup support platform
  • Startup India portal handles DPIIT recognition for thousands of startups
  • Reported by Moneycontrol

The government's flagship Startup India programme is moving toward a significant digital upgrade, with technology firm PruTech qualifying for the DPIIT's digital project that will modernise the platform, Moneycontrol reported. The development marks a concrete step forward in what has been an eagerly anticipated overhaul of the state's primary startup-support infrastructure.

The Startup India portal currently handles registrations, DPIIT recognition certificates, compliance filings, and connections to government schemes for tens of thousands of startups. A more capable, modern digital backend could accelerate recognition timelines, improve access to scheme benefits, and reduce the bureaucratic friction that founders frequently cite as a pain point.

PruTech's qualification brings the project to the implementation threshold. While the full scope and timeline of the digital overhaul have not been disclosed in available reports, the fact that a vendor has now been qualified suggests the initiative is moving from planning to execution. DPIIT has been pushing to make Startup India a more dynamic, data-driven platform rather than a largely static registry.

The upgrade arrives as India's startup ecosystem continues to scale — with thousands of new ventures seeking recognition each year — making a robust, responsive digital infrastructure increasingly essential. For founders, particularly those outside metro cities, a more functional government portal can be the difference between accessing entitled benefits promptly or losing months to administrative delays.
