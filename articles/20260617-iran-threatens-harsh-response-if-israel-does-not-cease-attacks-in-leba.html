Iran threatens 'harsh response' if Israel does not cease attacks in Lebanon

Key facts

  • Iran threatened a 'harsh response' over Israeli attacks in Lebanon
  • Israel has been striking Hezbollah targets in Lebanese territory
  • Iran is Hezbollah's primary state backer
  • Trump also criticised Israeli Lebanon attacks at G7 the same day

What happened

The Guardian's Middle East crisis live blog reports that Iran has threatened a 'harsh response' if Israel fails to stop its attacks in Lebanon. The warning marks a direct escalation from Tehran, coming as Israel continues military operations against Hezbollah targets in Lebanese territory.

The context

Iran is the primary state backer of Hezbollah, the Lebanese militant group that has been in sustained conflict with Israel since the Gaza war reignited in October 2023. Iran and Israel have previously exchanged direct strikes in 2024, and any renewed Iran-Israel confrontation carries serious risks of a wider regional war. The US has historically served as a restraining force, but Trump's G7 comments signal reduced American cover for Israeli actions.

Why it matters

A direct Iran-Israel clash in Lebanon would threaten regional stability and could send oil prices sharply higher, increasing fuel and goods costs for ordinary Indians while also endangering the nearly nine million Indians living and working across the Gulf.

What's next

Expect close monitoring of Israeli military activity in Lebanon and any Iranian or Hezbollah retaliatory moves in the coming days.
