Why India Is Spending ₹91,000 Crore on Great Nicobar

What happened

The Hindu reports on the rationale and scale of India's ₹91,000 crore development programme for Great Nicobar Island, examining both the strategic and economic case for the investment. The project, being developed by the Andaman and Nicobar Islands Integrated Development Corporation, has drawn attention for its environmental impact on a biodiversity-rich island as well as its geopolitical significance.

The context

Great Nicobar Island lies close to the Malacca Strait, through which an estimated 80 percent of global oil trade and a third of all world trade passes. India currently lacks a world-class transshipment hub, leaving significant port revenue to Colombo, Singapore, and Port Klang. The project is also seen through a security lens as China expands its naval presence and port investments across the Indian Ocean.

Why it matters

If the project succeeds, it could generate thousands of jobs, capture significant transshipment revenue for India, and give the Indian Navy a strategic forward base. However, the massive public investment and environmental clearances on a fragile island ecosystem have prompted scrutiny from conservationists and parliamentarians.

What's next

Expect ongoing debates on environmental clearances and displacement of indigenous communities to intensify as construction timelines are firmed up and contractors are mobilised.
