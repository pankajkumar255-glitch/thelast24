FIFA World Cup 2026: Scotland and Brazil Gear Up for Opening Matches

What happened

ESPN reports that the FIFA World Cup 2026 is progressing on June 13, with live updates covering Scotland and Brazil as they prepare for their opening fixtures. The report tracks match-day developments as the expanded 48-team tournament gets underway.

The context

The 2026 World Cup is the first to feature 48 teams, up from 32, expanding opportunities for smaller footballing nations. The US, Canada, and Mexico are co-hosting across multiple cities. Brazil enters as perennial favourites while Scotland's qualification ended a lengthy absence from the tournament.

Why it matters

The World Cup drives massive global viewership and advertising revenue; for Indian fans and broadcasters, it is one of the biggest sporting events of the year and a key driver of streaming subscriptions and sports media growth in India.

What's next

Expect group-stage results to quickly reshape the knockout bracket picture, with Brazil's opening performance drawing the most global attention.
