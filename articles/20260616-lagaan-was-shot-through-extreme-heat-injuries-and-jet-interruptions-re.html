Lagaan was shot through extreme heat, injuries and jet interruptions, recalls actor Yashpal Sharma

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Yashpal Sharma, who played Lakha in Lagaan, has spoken about the challenging shooting conditions on the film, describing extreme weather, injuries sustained during production, and interruptions from jets flying over the outdoor shoot locations.

The context

Lagaan, released in 2001 and directed by Ashutosh Gowariker, was a landmark Hindi film that blended a colonial-era cricket story with high-stakes drama, earning an Oscar nomination and becoming one of the highest-grossing Bollywood films of its time. The film was shot extensively on outdoor locations in Bhuj, Gujarat, making it particularly vulnerable to weather and logistical challenges. Its legacy as a cinematic achievement has only grown in the years since its release.

Why it matters

Nostalgia for Lagaan runs deep among Indian audiences, and first-hand accounts from cast members keep the film culturally alive and relevant for newer generations. Such stories also highlight the physical and logistical demands placed on film crews in the pre-digital era of Indian cinema.

What's next

Expect Yashpal Sharma's interview to spark renewed interest in Lagaan's production history, possibly encouraging retrospective screenings or documentary interest around the film's 25th anniversary approaching in 2026.
