India's dependence on services exports is rising as trade composition shifts

Key facts

  • Services exports rising as share of India's total trade earnings
  • IT, BPO and financial services lead the services export surge
  • U.S. and Europe are dominant buyers of India's services exports
  • Goods exporters face separate headwinds from global tariff pressures

India's trade composition is shifting in a structurally significant way, with the country's reliance on services exports growing steadily as a share of total export earnings. The trend, highlighted in a Business Standard analysis, underscores both the scale of India's competitive advantage in IT, business process outsourcing and financial services, and the relative difficulty its goods exporters have had in breaking into global markets at scale.

Services exports — dominated by software and IT-enabled services, which account for the bulk of the category — have proved resilient even as merchandise exports have faced headwinds from global demand slowdowns, supply chain disruptions, and now escalating tariff pressures from major economies. India's services trade surplus has become a crucial cushion for the current account, partly offsetting persistent deficits on the goods side.

However, the growing concentration in services also introduces vulnerabilities. A significant portion of India's services export revenue is tied to demand from the United States and Europe — markets that are themselves navigating economic uncertainty, automation pressures, and, in the U.S. case, political pressure to keep jobs onshore. Any softening in outsourcing demand or tightening of work visa regimes could dent earnings meaningfully.

The analysis comes at a moment when policymakers are trying to use the ongoing FTA wave to boost merchandise exports — in textiles, electronics, and engineering goods — and reduce the lopsided dependence on services. Balancing the two will be critical to ensuring India's export growth story remains broad-based and resilient.
