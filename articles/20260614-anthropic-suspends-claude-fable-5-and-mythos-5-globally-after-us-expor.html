Anthropic Suspends Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Globally After US Export Control Directive

What happened

BBC, NDTV, Indiatimes, Moneycontrol, The Indian Express, and Telegraph India all report that Anthropic has suspended global access to its Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models following a US export control directive citing security concerns. The suspension took effect within days of the models' public launch, cutting off users outside the US from what were Anthropic's most capable offerings. Telegraph India notes the block is global in scope, while Moneycontrol and The Indian Express detail that the US government's directive specifically bars foreigners from accessing these models.

The context

Anthropic, one of the leading AI safety-focused companies and a close competitor to OpenAI, had just released Fable 5 and Mythos 5 as its frontier models. US export control laws, traditionally applied to hardware and weapons, are increasingly being extended to advanced software and AI systems as Washington seeks to prevent cutting-edge technology from reaching adversaries. This is among the most high-profile instances of AI models being pulled back from global users on national security grounds.

Why it matters

Indian businesses, researchers, and developers who had begun integrating or evaluating these models now face an abrupt loss of access, potentially disrupting workflows and AI-driven projects. The episode signals that reliance on US-based AI platforms carries a geopolitical risk that Indian users and companies cannot ignore.

What's next

Expect the Indian tech and policy community to intensify calls for domestically developed AI alternatives, and watch for Anthropic to clarify whether and when non-US access might be restored.
