What crashed Air India Flight AI-171? A long year of questions, no answer

What happened

India Today reports that it has been a long year since the crash of Air India Flight AI-171, and no definitive answer has emerged about what caused the aircraft to go down. The report frames the anniversary as a moment to assess the investigation's progress — and to note its lack of conclusive findings. The absence of answers is significant both for the families of those who died or were affected and for the wider public's confidence in Indian aviation safety. The investigation presumably involves India's Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau and potentially international aviation bodies given Air India's international operations.

The context

Air India, now owned by the Tata Group after being privatised in 2022, has been undergoing a major transformation including fleet expansion and operational overhaul. Aviation accidents in India are investigated by the Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau under the Ministry of Civil Aviation, and complex investigations can take years, as seen with high-profile crashes globally. The AI-171 crash would have been one of the most significant aviation incidents in India in recent memory given the attention it has received. International aviation safety norms require thorough investigation to prevent recurrence, making the lack of a clear answer after a year a matter of public interest.

What's next

Expect continued pressure from victims' families and aviation safety advocates for a timeline on the investigation's findings. Any official update from the Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau or the Ministry of Civil Aviation will be closely watched by the public and the aviation industry.
