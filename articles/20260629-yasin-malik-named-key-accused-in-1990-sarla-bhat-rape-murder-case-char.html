Yasin Malik Named Key Accused in 1990 Sarla Bhat Rape-Murder Case, Chargesheet Filed After 36 Years

Key facts

  • SIA files 737-page chargesheet in 1990 Sarla Bhat rape-murder case
  • Yasin Malik named main accused; four JKLF operatives also named
  • Sarla Bhat was a Kashmiri Pandit nurse killed in 1990
  • Case reaches court 36 years after the killing
  • Chargesheet includes witness accounts and Bitta Karate interview

Thirty-six years after the rape and murder of Sarla Bhat, a Kashmiri Pandit nurse whose killing became a landmark case of Kashmir's terror era, the State Investigation Agency has filed a 737-page chargesheet naming JKLF chief Yasin Malik as the main accused. Four other JKLF operatives are also named in the document, which arrives as Malik is already serving a life sentence in a separate terror-funding case.

Sarla Bhat was raped and killed in 1990, branded an 'informant' by militants in what investigators say was a targeted act of terror against the Kashmiri Pandit community during the violence that drove hundreds of thousands from the Valley. The SIA chargesheet draws on witness testimony and includes details from an interview with Bitta Karate, a name long associated with killings from that period.

The case had remained unresolved for decades, making it one of the most painful symbols of unpunished violence from the Kashmir insurgency. The decision to file a formal chargesheet now signals the government's intent to pursue accountability for crimes against Kashmiri Pandits, many of whom have waited over three decades for legal redress.

With Yasin Malik already in custody, the chargesheet opens the path for formal trial proceedings. Legal observers note that successfully prosecuting a decades-old case of this nature will depend heavily on the strength of witness accounts and forensic documentation assembled in the 737-page document.
