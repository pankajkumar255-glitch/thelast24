Names of 6 Soldiers Killed in Operation Sindoor Released for the First Time

Key facts

  • Six soldiers killed in action during Operation Sindoor
  • Names released publicly for the first time by the government
  • Information confirmed by Hindustan Times and NDTV

The government has for the first time publicly released the names of six soldiers killed in action during Operation Sindoor, ending weeks of official silence over the human toll of the military campaign.

The names were made public on Thursday, according to reports by Hindustan Times and NDTV. Until now, the identities of the soldiers had not been officially confirmed, even as Operation Sindoor dominated national conversation. The decision to name them marks a notable shift in how the government has handled information about casualties from the operation.

The disclosure was met with widespread public attention. Families of the fallen soldiers now have formal government acknowledgement of their loss, a step that many had been awaiting. The move also opens the door for formal state honours and commemorations for those who died.

The release of the names underscores the broader reckoning with the costs of Operation Sindoor, even as the government has sought to project the mission as a success. For the Indian public, the names humanise what had until now remained a largely abstract accounting of military sacrifice — and are likely to intensify both tributes and scrutiny of how the operation was conducted and communicated.
