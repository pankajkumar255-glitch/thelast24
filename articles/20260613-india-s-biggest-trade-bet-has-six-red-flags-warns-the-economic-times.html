India's Biggest Trade Bet Has Six Red Flags, Warns The Economic Times

What happened

The Economic Times reports that India's most consequential ongoing trade negotiation — widely understood to be the India-US bilateral trade agreement — comes with six identifiable red flags. While the specific risks are not detailed in the headline alone, the framing suggests concerns spanning market access, intellectual property, agricultural imports, digital trade, and labour standards — the classic pressure points in India-US trade history. The report positions this as a significant cautionary note at a moment when the government is under pressure to deliver a deal quickly.

The context

India and the US have been negotiating a bilateral trade agreement with renewed urgency since the Trump administration's April 2025 tariff announcement. India faces a reciprocal tariff that, if reimposed after the 90-day pause, would significantly hurt its exports. Past India-US trade negotiations have repeatedly stalled over disagreements on agriculture (India's red line), digital services taxation, pharmaceutical pricing, and market access for US dairy and poultry. India has historically been protective of its farm sector given the political sensitivity around agricultural livelihoods.

Why it matters

A deal that concedes too much on agriculture could affect the incomes of hundreds of millions of Indian farmers. Intellectual property concessions could raise the cost of generic medicines that millions of Indians depend on. Conversely, failing to strike any deal means Indian exporters continue to face punishing tariffs in their most important foreign market. The stakes make this arguably the most consequential trade decision of Modi's third term.

What's next

Expect intensified scrutiny from domestic industry lobbies and opposition parties as details of the negotiating positions emerge. Watch for any official response from the Commerce Ministry addressing the concerns raised by analysts about the deal's potential red flags.
