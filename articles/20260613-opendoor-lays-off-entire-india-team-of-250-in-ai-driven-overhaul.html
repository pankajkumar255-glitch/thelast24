OpenDoor Lays Off Entire India Team of 250 in AI-Driven Overhaul

What happened

India Today reports that OpenDoor, a US-based real estate technology firm, has laid off all 250 members of its India team as part of an AI-driven operational overhaul. The company is restructuring its workforce to rely more heavily on artificial intelligence tools in place of human employees. This is a complete elimination of OpenDoor's India presence rather than a partial reduction. The specific functions performed by the India team were not detailed in the headline.

The context

OpenDoor is a prominent US proptech company that uses technology to simplify home buying and selling. Like many technology firms, it had built an India team likely for engineering, data, and support functions — a common model for US tech companies seeking cost-effective talent. The broader global tech industry has been undergoing significant layoffs since 2022, with AI automation increasingly cited as a driver alongside cost pressures. India's IT and GCC (Global Capability Centre) sector employs millions and has been watching AI-driven job displacement with anxiety.

Why it matters

The complete elimination of a 250-person India team attributed directly to AI automation is a concrete, high-profile data point in the debate about AI and jobs. It signals that offshore support and technology roles are not immune to AI displacement. For India's large IT services sector, which employs millions in roles that could be automated, this is a cautionary development worth close attention.

What's next

Expect the affected employees to face a challenging job market given that similar AI automation pressures are affecting many firms simultaneously. Industry bodies and policymakers are likely to face renewed calls to address AI-driven job displacement with reskilling programmes.
