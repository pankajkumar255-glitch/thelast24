OpenDoor Lays Off Entire India Team of 250 in AI-Driven Overhaul

What happened

India Today reports that OpenDoor, a US real-estate technology firm, has eliminated its entire India team of 250 people as part of what the company describes as an AI-driven overhaul. The layoffs affect the entirety of its Indian operations, suggesting the company is replacing functions previously performed by the team — likely data processing, customer operations, or technology support — with AI tools. The scale and totality of the cuts, affecting every single India-based employee, makes this unusual even by the standards of recent global tech layoffs.

The context

OpenDoor is a US-listed proptech company that pioneered the 'iBuyer' model — using algorithms to make instant cash offers on homes. Like many US tech firms, it had built an India team to handle back-office, data, and engineering functions at lower cost. The broader global tech industry has seen a wave of layoffs since 2022 as companies cut costs and, more recently, pivot resources toward AI development. Several large technology firms, including Google, Microsoft, and Salesforce, have cited AI as a reason for workforce reductions. India's IT and business-process outsourcing sectors, which employ millions, are watching such decisions closely as a signal of structural shifts in global hiring.

Why it matters

The complete elimination of a 250-person India team signals that AI is now capable enough to replace entire offshore operational units, not just augment them. For India's large BPO and IT services workforce, this is a tangible illustration of the threat that automation poses to jobs that were considered relatively safe a few years ago. It may accelerate pressure on Indian workers to upskill toward AI-resistant roles in design, strategy, and model oversight. Companies and policymakers will need to respond faster to reskilling demands if such layoffs become a trend.

What's next

Watch for similar announcements from other US-based firms with large India back-office teams as AI automation matures. Expect industry bodies such as Nasscom to respond with data on net job creation versus destruction in the sector over the coming quarters.
