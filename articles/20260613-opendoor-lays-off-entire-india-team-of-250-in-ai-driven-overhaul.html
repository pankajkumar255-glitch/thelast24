OpenDoor Lays Off Entire India Team of 250 in AI-Driven Overhaul

What happened

India Today reports that OpenDoor, a US-based real-estate technology platform, has laid off its complete India team of 250 people as part of what the company describes as an AI-driven overhaul. The entire India operation has been wound down, with AI tools cited as the replacement for the functions the team performed. The report does not specify which precise roles — engineering, operations, customer support, or data — were affected, but the scale of the cut, covering the full India headcount, indicates a wholesale strategic decision rather than a targeted reduction. India Today frames this as a direct consequence of the company deploying AI to handle work previously done by human employees.

The context

OpenDoor is a prominent US prop-tech firm that uses technology to facilitate quick home buying and selling. Like many tech companies, it established an India team to handle engineering, operations, and support functions at lower cost. The global tech industry has seen a wave of layoffs since 2022 as companies cut costs and, more recently, as AI automation reduces the need for certain categories of workers. India has historically been a major beneficiary of offshore outsourcing, hosting hundreds of thousands of roles for global technology and services firms. The trend of AI replacing offshore teams has been discussed extensively but concrete large-scale examples have been relatively rare until now.

Why it matters

The loss of 250 jobs in one announcement is significant in human terms, but the larger concern is what it signals for India's vast IT and BPO sector, which employs several million people. If AI can wholesale replace an offshore team's functions, companies across industries may re-examine their India headcount. This development will intensify debate among Indian policymakers, educators, and industry bodies about reskilling and the future of services exports, which are a crucial source of foreign exchange for India. Workers in similar roles at other companies will rightly ask whether their positions face the same risk.

What's next

Expect industry bodies like Nasscom to respond with assessments of how widespread AI-driven offshore layoffs could become. Watch whether other US tech and prop-tech firms announce similar India team restructurings in coming months, which would confirm a broader pattern rather than an isolated case.
