Rio de Janeiro Unveils Rio 3.5 Open 397B AI Model to Challenge ChatGPT and Claude

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that Rio de Janeiro has unveiled the Rio 3.5 Open 397B AI model, which it is positioning as a challenger to ChatGPT and Claude. The model, with 397 billion parameters, is described as open, meaning it can be downloaded and used freely. The launch represents an unusual entry into frontier AI development by a sub-national government.

The context

The global AI landscape has been dominated by a handful of US companies — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind and Meta — with China's Baidu and others offering alternatives. The open-source AI movement, led by models like Meta's Llama, has democratised access to capable AI, but a 397B-parameter model from a city government is unprecedented. Brazil has been investing in digital governance and AI, and Rio's move may reflect national ambitions channelled through a city administration.

Why it matters

The release of a genuinely large open-source model by a non-US, non-Big Tech entity demonstrates that frontier AI is becoming more accessible, which could inspire and inform India's own national AI model efforts. Indian developers and enterprises could potentially use or fine-tune such open models without geopolitical risk.

What's next

Expect scrutiny of the model's actual benchmark performance against ChatGPT and Claude, and watch for whether Indian research institutions or government bodies explore building on or partnering around this open release.
