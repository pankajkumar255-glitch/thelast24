Shreyanka Patil reveals she was 'depressed and wanted to quit cricket' during injury battle

What happened

India Today reports that Shreyanka Patil has recalled a dark phase during a serious injury, saying she was depressed and wanted to quit cricket altogether. Patil shared her personal struggle as she reflected on the challenging period that tested her resolve to continue playing.

The context

Shreyanka Patil emerged as a promising talent in India women's cricket, particularly in T20 formats, known for her off-spin bowling and lower-order contributions. Injuries are a persistent challenge for cricketers, and the mental health dimension of long-term injuries in sport has gained growing global attention in recent years.

Why it matters

Patil's openness could encourage more athletes — and young Indians in general — to acknowledge and seek help for mental health struggles, reducing the stigma around depression in competitive sports. Her comeback story also adds to the narrative of resilience that inspires the next generation of women cricketers.

What's next

Expect Patil's story to spark broader conversations around mental health support systems for athletes within the BCCI and Indian sports bodies.
