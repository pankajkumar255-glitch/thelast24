Imtiaz Ali recalls being 'jokingly' asked how he got into Hindu College as a Muslim

What happened

The Indian Express reports that filmmaker Imtiaz Ali has shared a personal anecdote in which someone jokingly questioned how he, as a Muslim, was admitted to Hindu College — one of Delhi University's most prestigious colleges — reflecting on experiences of religious identity and social belonging he faced during his formative years.

The context

Hindu College is one of the oldest and most academically prestigious colleges of Delhi University, founded in 1899; its name derives from its founding community and has no exclusionary admission policy. Imtiaz Ali grew up in Jamshedpur and studied in Delhi before entering the film industry. His recounting of the anecdote comes in the context of promoting Main Vaapas Aaunga, which itself engages with themes of identity and belonging.

Why it matters

Public figures sharing personal experiences of religious othering in educational settings contribute to a wider national conversation about inclusion and secular values that affects students, educators, and institutions across India.

What's next

Expect the remarks to generate continued social media discussion about the experience of minority communities in Indian educational institutions.
