Lorcan Tucker named permanent Ireland T20I captain ahead of India series; three uncapped players called up

Key facts

  • Lorcan Tucker named permanent Ireland T20I captain
  • Three uncapped players included in Ireland squad
  • Squad named ahead of T20I series vs India

Lorcan Tucker has been named Ireland's permanent T20I captain, the country's cricket board confirmed ahead of the upcoming T20I series against India. The wicketkeeper-batter, who has been one of Ireland's standout white-ball performers in recent seasons, takes the armband on a full-time basis after previously serving in a caretaker capacity.

The squad announcement also revealed three uncapped players in the group, underlining Ireland's intent to inject fresh talent as they prepare for a competitive bilateral series against one of the world's top T20 sides. The new faces represent a significant opportunity for Ireland to assess their next generation in a high-profile setting.

Tucker's elevation to the permanent captaincy is widely seen as a reward for consistent performances and leadership shown on the field. His experience behind the stumps and as a top-order batter gives Ireland a versatile, experienced leader at the top.

The India series, with its schedule and venues confirmed by The Indian Express, is set to give Irish cricket another chance to test itself against a top-ranked side. For India, the series provides useful T20I preparation and game time in the lead-up to more high-stakes engagements.
