Lutnick's Anthropic Crackdown Claims New US Government Power Over AI Models

Key facts

  • US Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick asserting new power over AI models
  • Anthropic is the focal point of the federal crackdown
  • Marks shift from hardware export controls to direct AI model oversight
  • Could affect global access to US-developed frontier AI platforms

Bloomberg reports that Howard Lutnick, the US Commerce Secretary, is using an Anthropic-related crackdown to claim sweeping new powers over AI models, representing a significant assertion of federal authority over the AI industry. The development marks what appears to be a deliberate expansion of the US government's role in directly overseeing how frontier AI labs like Anthropic develop and deploy their technology.

Anthropicl, one of the leading AI safety companies in the United States, has become the focal point of this regulatory move. Lutnick, who heads the Commerce Department, is leveraging existing trade and commerce authorities to stake out new oversight terrain, at a moment when Washington is increasingly treating advanced AI as a matter of national security.

This follows earlier US moves such as chip export restrictions targeting China, but represents a qualitative shift: rather than simply limiting foreign access to AI hardware, the government is now seeking influence over the AI models themselves. The exact scope of the authority being claimed — whether it covers training, deployment, export, or all three — is still emerging.

For India, which has been deepening its technology partnership with the US and hosts a large base of companies using US-made AI tools, an expansion of American government control over AI models could eventually affect Indian firms' access to and terms of use for leading AI platforms.
