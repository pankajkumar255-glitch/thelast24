Trump Dismisses Concerns About Anthropic Security Threat After AI Curbs

Key facts

  • Trump said Anthropic is not a national security threat
  • US Commerce Secretary Lutnick's actions claimed new power over AI models
  • Anthropic makes the Claude AI model series
  • Amazon and Google are major investors in Anthropic

Business Standard reports that US President Donald Trump has publicly stated that Anthropic does not represent a national security threat, seeking to cool tensions after a series of restrictions were placed on the AI startup. The comments come amid a broader regulatory tightening in Washington around frontier AI development.

Separately, the Economic Times reports that Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick's intervention in the Anthropic matter has effectively claimed new governmental power over AI models — a significant move that could set precedents for how US federal authorities oversee private AI companies going forward.

Anthropicis the maker of the Claude series of AI assistants and is one of the most well-funded AI safety-focused startups in the world, backed by billions in investment from Amazon and Google, among others. The regulatory attention it is now receiving reflects Washington's growing anxiety about who controls cutting-edge AI systems.

For Indian readers, the developments matter because Anthropic's Claude models are widely used by Indian enterprises and developers; any US government restrictions on the company's operations could affect access to these tools and influence how India's own AI policy conversation evolves.
