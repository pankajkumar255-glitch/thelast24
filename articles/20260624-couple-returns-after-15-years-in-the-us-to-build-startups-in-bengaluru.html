Couple returns after 15 years in the US to build startups in Bengaluru

Key facts

  • Couple returned to Bengaluru after 15 years working in the US
  • They are building startups in Bengaluru's tech ecosystem
  • Part of a broader reverse-migration trend among Indian tech professionals

After spending 15 years building careers in the United States, a couple has returned to Bengaluru to launch their own startups — a choice that puts them at the centre of one of the more quietly significant trends reshaping India's technology landscape. NDTV reported their story as emblematic of a broader reverse-migration movement gathering pace among Indian professionals who once saw the US as the only arena for serious ambition.

The reasons driving this kind of return are multiple. India's startup ecosystem has matured dramatically over the past decade, with Bengaluru in particular offering world-class co-working infrastructure, a deep pool of engineering talent, and access to a domestic consumer market of continental scale. For founders, the combination of lower burn rates, local market knowledge, and the prestige that now attaches to building in India makes the case for returning increasingly compelling.

There is also a pull factor rooted in a sense of timing. Many returning entrepreneurs believe they are catching India at an inflection point — a moment when the country's digital infrastructure, from UPI to cloud penetration, has made it possible to build category-defining companies that simply could not have existed a decade ago. Fifteen years of US experience in product development, fundraising norms, and scaling operations is seen as a distinct advantage when brought back to an Indian context.

For Bengaluru, each such return adds to a compounding effect: experienced founders mentor others, create employment, and signal to global investors that the city can retain and attract serious talent. Whether this couple's ventures succeed or not, their choice itself is the story — and it is one India's startup establishment is eager to tell.
