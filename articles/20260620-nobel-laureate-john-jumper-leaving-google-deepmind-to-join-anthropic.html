Nobel laureate John Jumper leaving Google DeepMind to join Anthropic

Key facts

  • John Jumper shared the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for AlphaFold
  • Jumper is leaving Google DeepMind for Anthropic
  • AlphaFold predicts protein 3D structures, aiding drug discovery
  • Anthropic is backed by Amazon and founded by ex-OpenAI researchers

Bloomberg reports that John Jumper, the Nobel Prize-winning scientist who helped build AlphaFold at Google DeepMind, is departing to join Anthropic. Jumper shared the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry alongside DeepMind founder Demis Hassabis for AlphaFold, the AI system that solved one of biology's grand challenges by accurately predicting the three-dimensional structure of proteins — a breakthrough with sweeping implications for drug discovery and disease research.

The move is one of the most high-profile talent transfers in recent AI history, taking a laureate from Alphabet's flagship AI research division to Anthropic, the safety-focused AI company founded by former OpenAI executives and heavily backed by Amazon. Anthropic is the maker of the Claude family of AI assistants and has been rapidly expanding its research capabilities.

For Indian users and researchers, the shift matters because the competitive battle for top AI talent directly shapes which companies build the most capable and safe AI systems. Anthropic's Claude models are increasingly used across Indian enterprises and startups, and landing a researcher of Jumper's calibre could accelerate the lab's scientific ambitions well beyond language AI into biology and medicine.
