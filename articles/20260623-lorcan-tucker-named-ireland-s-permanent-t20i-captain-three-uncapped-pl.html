Lorcan Tucker named Ireland's permanent T20I captain; three uncapped players called up for India series

Key facts

  • Lorcan Tucker named permanent Ireland T20I captain
  • Three uncapped players included in Ireland's squad
  • Squad announced for home T20I series against India
  • Tucker had previously led Ireland in interim capacity

Lorcan Tucker has been confirmed as Ireland's permanent T20 International captain, with Cricket Ireland formally handing him the role ahead of the home series against India. Tucker, who has been one of Ireland's most reliable performers behind the stumps and with the bat, had previously led the side in an interim capacity and now gets the job on a full-time basis.

Three uncapped players have been named in the squad, underlining Ireland's deliberate effort to broaden their T20 talent pool. The inclusions are a calculated risk ahead of facing a full-strength India side, but the series provides the kind of high-pressure exposure that accelerates player development at international level.

The India series is a landmark occasion for Irish cricket. Hosting one of the sport's biggest nations, with the attendant global viewership and commercial attention, gives Ireland a platform that goes well beyond the result on the field. For Tucker, it is a defining first assignment as permanent skipper — a chance to stamp his authority and set the tone for the side's T20 ambitions ahead of the next World Cup cycle.

Cricket Ireland's decision to commit to Tucker for the long term reflects confidence in his leadership qualities and his ability to galvanise a squad that has shown it can compete with stronger nations on its day. The series schedule and venue details have been confirmed, with Irish fans expected to turn out in force to witness the clash.
