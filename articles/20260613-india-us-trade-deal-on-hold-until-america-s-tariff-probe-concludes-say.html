India-US Trade Deal On Hold Until America's Tariff Probe Concludes, Says Official

What happened

Reuters reports that an official stated the India-US trade deal can only be finalised after the United States concludes its tariff probe. The comment confirms that the two countries, while in active negotiations, are constrained by a formal US trade law process that must run its course first. No timeline for the probe's conclusion was specified in the report. The statement effectively puts a ceiling on how quickly any bilateral agreement can be signed, regardless of progress at the negotiating table.

The context

India and the US have been in intensive trade talks since the Trump administration imposed sweeping reciprocal tariffs in early 2025, paused for 90 days to allow negotiations. India faces tariffs that threaten its key export sectors including gems and jewellery, pharmaceuticals, textiles, and engineering goods. A separate NDTV report highlighted that a specific US trade law — likely referencing Section 301 or a related probe mechanism — is creating a procedural bottleneck. Both governments had publicly expressed hope for a deal within the 90-day pause window, making this official admission a notable setback.

Why it matters

Indian exporters face prolonged uncertainty about the tariff regime applicable to their goods in the US market, making forward planning difficult. Industries like textiles and pharmaceuticals, which employ millions of Indians, are directly exposed. A delayed deal also means India's competitive position relative to other countries also negotiating with Washington could weaken. For investors, the lack of a clear timeline adds risk to trade-sensitive sectors on Indian markets.

What's next

Expect both governments to continue back-channel negotiations even as the US tariff probe runs its course. Watch for any announcement on the probe's conclusion date, which would set a hard deadline for when a deal could realistically be signed.
