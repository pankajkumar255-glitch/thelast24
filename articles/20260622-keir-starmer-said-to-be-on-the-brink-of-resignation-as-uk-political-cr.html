Keir Starmer Said to Be on the Brink of Resignation as UK Political Crisis Deepens

Key facts

  • Starmer's resignation said to be expected imminently
  • Labour won a historic majority in the July 2024 UK general election
  • Resignation would trigger Labour leadership contest
  • India-UK Free Trade Agreement negotiations could be affected

Keir Starmer is reportedly on the brink of resignation as British Prime Minister, with an announcement expected imminently — a stunning potential collapse for a leader who came to power in July 2024 with one of the largest parliamentary majorities in Labour's history.

Starmer's tenure has been marked by mounting internal party discontent, a series of controversial policy reversals, and plummeting public approval ratings. The Labour government faced fierce criticism over welfare reform cuts, energy bill decisions, and a perception that the administration had failed to deliver on its transformational promises to British voters.

The prospect of Starmer's exit has thrown Westminster into turmoil, with Labour MPs scrambling to assess succession options. Senior cabinet members and party figures are said to be in urgent consultations, while the opposition Conservative Party and Reform UK have seized on the reports to demand an immediate general election.

For India, the stakes are considerable. The India-UK Free Trade Agreement — long in negotiation — could face delays or a fundamental renegotiation under a new prime minister. The status of the large Indian-origin diaspora in Britain and bilateral ties on defence, technology, and education partnerships would all come under fresh scrutiny depending on who succeeds Starmer.
