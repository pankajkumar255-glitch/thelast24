Kunal Shah named WhatsApp CEO, sparking conversation about his educational background

Key facts

  • Kunal Shah, CRED founder, named CEO of WhatsApp
  • Shah studied philosophy at Wilson College, Mumbai
  • He dropped out of an MBA programme
  • WhatsApp is a Meta-owned platform used by hundreds of millions of Indians

Kunal Shah, the Indian entrepreneur who founded the rewards and credit platform CRED, has been appointed the new CEO of WhatsApp, a development that has set off a wave of discussion across India about what his ascent says about the country's entrepreneurial talent on the global stage.

Perhaps as striking as the appointment itself is the conversation it has sparked about Shah's academic record. Shah studied philosophy at Wilson College in Mumbai and famously dropped out of an MBA programme — a background that stands in contrast to the Ivy League or IIT pedigrees typically associated with leaders of global technology giants. The fact that he rose to lead one of the world's most widely used messaging apps — part of Meta's core portfolio — has made his story a talking point well beyond the business pages.

WhatsApp is not merely a global product; it is deeply woven into everyday Indian life, used by hundreds of millions of people for everything from family communication and payments to small business transactions. The choice of an Indian founder to lead the platform carries symbolic weight in a country that has produced a generation of startup builders who are increasingly visible on the world stage.

Shah's appointment follows a period of significant evolution at WhatsApp, as Meta has pushed the platform further into commerce and business messaging. His experience building CRED — which carved out a unique niche in India's competitive fintech landscape — may prove directly relevant to WhatsApp's ambitions in payments and the creator economy.
