Technology at the heart of India-France strategic convergence

Key facts

  • Technology identified as core driver of India-France strategic convergence
  • Domains include defence, space, digital infrastructure, semiconductors
  • Reflects India's broader push for Western tech partnerships

What happened

The Hindu reports that technology is now the defining force behind strategic convergence between India and France, covering domains such as defence co-production, space collaboration, digital infrastructure, and semiconductor supply chains. The story highlights how both governments are actively structuring bilateral agreements around technology transfer and co-development.

The context

India and France have maintained a historically strong strategic partnership since the 1998 nuclear tests, when France stood by India. In recent years, the Rafale fighter jet deal and joint space projects have deepened ties. France is also a key voice within the EU on India's proposed trade and tech partnerships with the bloc.

Why it matters

A technology-driven India-France axis could give Indian firms preferential access to European deep-tech ecosystems and accelerate domestic capability in areas like aerospace manufacturing and advanced semiconductors. It also strengthens India's multi-alignment strategy by diversifying away from US-only tech dependencies.

What's next

Expect formal technology-transfer agreements and joint R&D announcements to follow, likely timed around the next India-France summit or an upcoming state visit.
