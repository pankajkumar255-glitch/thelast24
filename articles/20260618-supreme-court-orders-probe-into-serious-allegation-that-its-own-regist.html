Supreme Court orders probe into 'serious' allegation that its own Registry misplaced case records

Key facts

  • Supreme Court orders probe into its own Registry
  • Allegation: Registry misplaced case records
  • Court described the allegation as 'serious'
  • Could affect pending cases and judicial administration

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has called for a probe into what it termed a 'serious' allegation that its own Registry had misplaced case records. The court made this observation while hearing a matter in which the disappearance of documents from the Registry was brought to its attention, prompting the bench to treat the issue with considerable gravity.

The Supreme Court Registry is the administrative backbone of the apex court, responsible for receiving, maintaining, and routing all case files and legal documents. Any systemic failure in this function can have cascading consequences, potentially stalling or compromising proceedings in multiple cases.

The court's willingness to order an internal probe signals judicial accountability at the highest level. For ordinary litigants, many of whom wait years for their matters to be listed and heard, the misplacement of records represents a serious threat to the timely delivery of justice. The outcome of the probe is expected to determine whether corrective reforms to the Registry's document management systems will follow.
