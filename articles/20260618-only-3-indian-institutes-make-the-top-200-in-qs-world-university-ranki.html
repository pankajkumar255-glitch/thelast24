Only 3 Indian institutes make the top 200 in QS World University Rankings 2027

Key facts

  • Only 3 Indian institutes ranked in QS World University Rankings 2027 top 200
  • IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, and IISc Bengaluru are India's historically top-ranked institutions
  • Rankings assess research, reputation, faculty ratio, and international outlook
  • India has invested in Institutions of Eminence scheme to boost global standing

India Today reports that only three Indian institutions have featured in the top 200 of the QS World University Rankings 2027, a widely tracked global benchmark for academic reputation, research output, faculty-to-student ratios, and international outlook. Historically, IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, and the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, have been India's highest-placed institutions in QS rankings.

The result is a sobering data point for India's higher education sector, which has seen significant government investment through initiatives like the Institutions of Eminence scheme and the National Education Policy 2020, both aimed at propelling Indian universities into global top tiers. Despite a large and growing student population, Indian institutions continue to be held back by factors including research output, international faculty, and citations per paper.

For everyday Indians, the rankings carry real consequences — they influence where students apply, how employers value degrees, and whether international academic collaborations flow to India. With millions of Indian students spending tens of thousands of crores annually to study abroad, improving the global standing of domestic institutions remains both an economic and a national prestige priority.
