Pegasystems opens new Bengaluru office with ₹60-crore investment

Key facts

  • Pegasystems opens new Bengaluru office
  • Investment of ₹60 crore committed
  • Pegasystems specialises in AI-powered CRM and automation software

Pegasystems, the US-based enterprise software company known for its AI-driven CRM and business process automation platforms, has opened a new office in Bengaluru with a ₹60-crore investment, Business Standard reported.

The new facility underscores India's growing importance as both a development hub and a market for enterprise technology. Bengaluru has consistently attracted major global tech firms looking to build engineering capacity, and Pegasystems' commitment adds to a steady stream of such investments in the city in recent years.

Pegasystems' software is used by large enterprises and financial institutions worldwide for customer engagement, intelligent automation, and decision-making workflows. India has become a critical node in its global operations, and the expanded office is expected to support both product development and client-facing functions for the region.

The ₹60-crore outlay reflects confidence in India's talent pool and long-term growth potential. For Bengaluru's tech ecosystem, such investments continue to sustain demand for specialised engineering and consulting skills even as parts of the broader global IT services sector navigate uncertain macroeconomic conditions.
