25% of Indian tech services firms have moved AI experiments into production: Nasscom

Key facts

  • 25% of Indian tech services firms have moved AI to production level
  • Finding from Nasscom industry body report
  • Signals shift from AI experimentation to live deployment in India's IT sector

A quarter of Indian technology services firms have moved their artificial intelligence experiments out of the lab and into full production deployments, according to new data from industry body Nasscom. The figure, reported by The Hindu, marks a significant maturation point for India's IT sector in its AI adoption journey.

The transition from experimentation to production is a critical inflection — it means AI is no longer a side project for these companies but an active component of the services and solutions they are delivering to clients. For an industry that employs millions and generates hundreds of billions of dollars in annual exports, the shift has real implications for how work is structured, what skills are in demand, and how competitive Indian firms are globally.

Nasscom's finding also comes against a backdrop of intense global competition in AI services, with companies in the US, Europe, and China racing to embed AI into enterprise workflows. Indian IT firms — which have historically excelled at execution and scale — are now positioning AI-powered delivery as a key differentiator in contract negotiations with large multinational clients.

The remaining 75% of firms are likely at varying stages of experimentation and pilots, suggesting the wave of AI production deployments has significant room to grow. For India's tech workforce, the Nasscom data is both an opportunity and a prompt: those who upskill in AI tooling and deployment stand to ride the next major growth cycle in the country's most important export sector.
