India's exports hit record $863 billion despite global trade shocks

Key facts

  • India's total exports reached a record $863 billion
  • Figure covers both goods and services exports
  • Achieved despite global trade shocks and tariff disruptions

Business Standard reports that India's total exports have touched a record $863 billion, according to an official statement, even as global trade faced significant disruptions over the past year. The figure encompasses both merchandise and services exports and represents India's strongest external trade performance to date.

The achievement comes against a backdrop of geopolitical tensions, Western tariff actions, and supply-chain realignments that have squeezed many export-dependent economies. India has benefited from a diversification of its export basket and a boom in services, particularly IT and business process management.

For everyday Indians, record exports translate into stronger demand for domestic labour across sectors from textiles and pharmaceuticals to software, while also supporting the rupee and giving the government more fiscal room. It also reinforces India's pitch to global companies looking to shift supply chains away from China.
