India World's Fastest-Growing Major Economy Under Modi, Says Union Minister Nadda

What happened

ANI News reports that Union Minister JP Nadda stated that India has emerged as the world's fastest-growing major economy under Prime Minister Modi. The claim echoes data from the IMF and World Bank that have consistently ranked India as the fastest-growing among the G20 major economies in recent years.

The context

India's GDP growth has averaged over 7% in recent years, outpacing China, the US, and other major economies. The government has pointed to infrastructure investment, digital public infrastructure, and manufacturing incentives as key drivers. However, critics have raised concerns about unemployment, income inequality, and the quality of growth.

Why it matters

India's macroeconomic growth story directly influences foreign investment flows, the value of the rupee, and market sentiment — all of which affect savings, jobs, and consumer prices for everyday Indians.

What's next

Expect the government to continue highlighting growth metrics in the run-up to state elections, while opposition parties are likely to counter with data on employment and real wages.
