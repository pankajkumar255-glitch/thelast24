Akshay Kumar dismisses Welcome To The Jungle salary rumours and addresses retirement

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Akshay Kumar has dismissed circulating rumours about his remuneration for Welcome To The Jungle, an upcoming large ensemble Bollywood comedy. In the same interaction, Kumar opened up about the question of retirement, offering his perspective on how long he intends to continue working in films. The actor did not confirm the rumoured salary figures, effectively denying their accuracy. The comments come at a time when Kumar's commercial standing has been questioned following several consecutive box-office failures.

The context

Akshay Kumar has historically been among the highest-paid actors in Bollywood and one of its most prolific, regularly releasing three to four films a year. However, a prolonged run of box-office underperformers — including films like Ram Setu, Selfie, and Bade Miyan Chote Miyan — has significantly dented his image as a reliable commercial draw. Welcome To The Jungle is a sequel in the Welcome franchise, which was a major hit in the late 2000s. Salary rumours around top stars are common in Bollywood and often relate to debates about whether their fees are justified given recent performance.

Why it matters

Akshay Kumar's commercial viability directly affects the investment decisions of producers and studios who back expensive Bollywood productions, with his films typically involving budgets of hundreds of crores. If top stars command high fees regardless of recent performance, it squeezes margins across the industry and filters down to smaller productions. His comments on retirement, however casual, will be closely parsed by fans, producers, and the trade.

What's next

Expect Welcome To The Jungle's release to be a major litmus test for whether Kumar can reclaim his box-office standing. It is likely that salary and budget details will continue to circulate as the film approaches its release date.
