The context

The ICC Women's T20 World Cup is the premier global tournament in women's cricket, held every two years. India and Pakistan have one of sport's most intense bilateral rivalries, and their meetings at World Cups — whether men's or women's — consistently rank among the most-watched cricket matches globally. India women have historically performed strongly against Pakistan in T20 World Cups, winning the majority of their head-to-head encounters in the tournament. The Women's T20 World Cup has been a vehicle for the rapid growth of women's cricket in India, with the BCCI significantly increasing investment in the women's game over the past five years.

Why it matters

A strong start for India women against Pakistan would boost the team's net run rate and psychological confidence for the rest of the tournament. For the growth of women's cricket in India, high-profile wins in marquee matches attract viewership, sponsorships, and grassroots interest that feed into the talent pipeline. For millions of Indian fans, the India-Pakistan World Cup fixture — regardless of format or gender — is a national event that transcends cricket.

What's next

Expect record viewership numbers for the Women's T20 World Cup on Indian broadcast platforms off the back of the India-Pakistan fixture.
