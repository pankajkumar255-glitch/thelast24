Modi and Carney Set 2026 Deadline for India-Canada Trade Deal at G7

What happened

NDTV reports that Prime Minister Modi and Canadian PM Carney met at the G7 and set a 2026 deadline for finalising a trade deal between India and Canada. The meeting signals a formal restart of trade negotiations that had stalled amid diplomatic tensions between the two nations.

The context

India-Canada relations hit a historic low in 2023 after Canada alleged Indian involvement in the killing of Khalistani separatist Hardeep Singh Nijjar, a charge India denied. Trade negotiations had been suspended. Mark Carney, who became PM in 2025, has signalled a more pragmatic approach to relations with India.

Why it matters

A completed trade deal could significantly boost Indian exports in sectors like pharmaceuticals, IT, and textiles, while also easing visa and immigration pathways for the large Indian-origin community in Canada. For businesses on both sides, a 2026 deadline creates a concrete planning horizon after years of uncertainty.

What's next

Expect formal trade negotiation rounds to resume quickly, with both governments likely to announce working groups and sectoral priorities in the coming months.
