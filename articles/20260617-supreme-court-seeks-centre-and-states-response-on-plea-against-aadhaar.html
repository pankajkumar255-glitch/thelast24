Supreme Court seeks Centre and states' response on plea against Aadhaar being used as citizenship proof

What happened

The Hindu and The Indian Express both report that the Supreme Court has sought responses from the Centre and states on a petition challenging the use of Aadhaar as proof of citizenship. The court's notice signals it considers the question legally substantial enough to warrant a formal government reply.

The context

Aadhaar is India's biometric identity system covering over a billion residents, but the Aadhaar Act explicitly states it cannot be used as proof of citizenship or domicile. Concerns about its misuse have grown in the context of citizenship verification exercises, particularly in Assam and other northeastern states where the National Register of Citizens process has been contested.

Why it matters

If courts or authorities treat Aadhaar as citizenship proof, it could affect the legal status of residents — particularly migrants and marginalised communities — who hold Aadhaar but may face citizenship challenges. A clear ruling could reshape how identity documents are used in citizenship-linked processes across India.

What's next

Expect the Centre and state governments to file their responses, after which the Supreme Court will decide whether to proceed with a full hearing on the matter.
