Soni Razdan defends Alia Bhatt: 'It's not her fault she is the daughter of industry insiders'

Key facts

  • Soni Razdan says Alia Bhatt is not at fault for being born into a film family
  • Alia Bhatt is daughter of filmmaker Mahesh Bhatt and Soni Razdan
  • Remarks come amid ongoing Bollywood nepotism debate

Soni Razdan has entered the long-running Bollywood nepotism debate directly, stating that her daughter Alia Bhatt should not be blamed for being the child of industry insiders. The actor and filmmaker made clear that Alia's background is not something the star chose, and that holding her personally responsible for the circumstances of her birth is unfair.

Alia Bhatt is among the most prominent names invoked whenever the subject of filmy families and insider privilege comes up in Hindi cinema. The daughter of filmmaker Mahesh Bhatt and Soni Razdan, she made her debut as a teenager and has since grown into one of Bollywood's leading actors, winning critical acclaim and commercial success in equal measure. Yet the question of whether that trajectory was smoothed by family connections has followed her throughout her career.

Razdan's intervention is notable because it comes from within the family rather than from a publicist or a supportive co-star. By framing the issue as one of circumstance rather than culpability, she makes a straightforward moral argument: children do not choose their parents, and the advantages or disadvantages that come with a particular birth are not something any individual can be held accountable for.

The nepotism conversation in Bollywood was reignited sharply after the death of Sushant Singh Rajput in 2020 and has never fully subsided. For star kids who have built genuine careers on their own talent, the debate presents a difficult bind — one that Soni Razdan's comments attempt, at least partially, to untangle.
