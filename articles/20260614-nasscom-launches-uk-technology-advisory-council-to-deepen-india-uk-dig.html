Nasscom Launches UK Technology Advisory Council to Deepen India-UK Digital Ties

What happened

The Hindu reports that Nasscom has launched a UK Technology Advisory Council to strengthen the India-UK digital partnership. The council is intended to serve as a structured platform for technology industry leaders from both countries to align on digital policy and business opportunities.

The context

Nasscom represents India's $250 billion IT-BPM industry, which counts the UK as one of its largest export markets. The India-UK relationship in technology has grown rapidly, with Indian IT firms operating major delivery centres in Britain and UK companies increasingly sourcing talent and services from India.

Why it matters

A formal advisory council could accelerate deal flow and policy alignment between the two countries' tech sectors, benefiting Indian IT companies and professionals seeking UK-linked opportunities. It also provides a platform to navigate post-Brexit digital trade rules that affect Indian service exports.

What's next

Expect the council to convene its first formal sessions in the months ahead, likely timed to complement any India-UK FTA finalisation.
