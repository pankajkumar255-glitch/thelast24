Supreme Court orders probe into 'serious' allegation that Registry misplaced case records

Key facts

  • Supreme Court calls allegation of Registry misplacing records 'serious'
  • Court orders a formal probe into the matter
  • Registry is responsible for maintaining all SC case files and orders
  • Such internal accountability scrutiny is rare at the apex court

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has said a probe is necessary into a 'serious' allegation that its own Registry misplaced case records. The bench's observation is notable because it involves an internal accountability concern at the country's apex court rather than a matter concerning external parties.

The Registry of the Supreme Court is the administrative backbone of the institution, responsible for maintaining all case files, orders, and documentation. Allegations of records being misplaced — whether through negligence or other causes — are considered grave because they can delay or prejudice the rights of litigants who depend on the integrity of those records.

The Supreme Court's willingness to publicly acknowledge such an allegation and call for a formal investigation reflects a degree of institutional self-scrutiny that is relatively rare in Indian judicial history. The exact case in which this issue arose was not specified in the available information.

For ordinary litigants, particularly those with long-pending matters, the episode underscores the vulnerability of cases to administrative failures even at the highest court in the land — and the importance of holding judicial institutions accountable for their own functioning.
