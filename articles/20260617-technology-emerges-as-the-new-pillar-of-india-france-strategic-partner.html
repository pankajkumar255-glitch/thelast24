Technology emerges as the new pillar of India-France strategic partnership

What happened

The Hindu reports that technology is emerging as the central driver of strategic convergence between India and France. The story covers growing collaboration across defence technology, space exploration, digital infrastructure, and emerging technologies. Both governments appear to be institutionalising this tech axis as a pillar of their broader strategic partnership.

The context

India and France have maintained a strong strategic partnership since the 1990s, anchored initially by Rafale jets and nuclear cooperation. In recent years, both countries have aligned on the Indo-Pacific, and France has been one of the strongest European backers of India's rise. Technology co-development fits into India's broader push under initiatives like Atmanirbhar Bharat and its semiconductor mission.

Why it matters

Deeper India-France technology ties could accelerate domestic manufacturing of advanced components, open European markets for Indian tech firms, and reduce India's reliance on US or Chinese technology ecosystems.

What's next

Expect formal government-to-government agreements on joint technology development — particularly in semiconductors and space — to be announced at upcoming bilateral summits.
