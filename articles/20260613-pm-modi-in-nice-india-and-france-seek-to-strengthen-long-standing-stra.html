PM Modi in Nice: India and France seek to strengthen long-standing strategic partnership

What happened

The Times of India reports that Prime Minister Narendra Modi is in Nice, France, for discussions focused on strengthening the long-standing strategic partnership between India and France. The visit involves bilateral talks that are expected to cover defence cooperation, civil nuclear energy, space collaboration, and trade and investment. France is one of India's most important strategic partners and a major supplier of defence equipment. The specific outcomes or agreements from the Nice meeting had not been finalised at the time of reporting.

The context

India and France established a strategic partnership in 1998, making France one of the first Western nations to enter such an arrangement with India. The relationship has been anchored by major defence deals, most notably the Rs 59,000-crore agreement for 36 Rafale fighter jets signed in 2016, and ongoing negotiations for additional Rafale-Marine jets for the Indian Navy and Scorpene-class submarines. France has also been a partner in India's civil nuclear programme and in space cooperation through ISRO-CNES collaboration. President Emmanuel Macron and PM Modi have cultivated a personal rapport that has kept the bilateral relationship dynamic through multiple diplomatic cycles.

Why it matters

Defence deals emerging from the Modi-France talks could involve enormous public expenditure and shape India's military capability for decades, directly affecting national security calculus with respect to China and Pakistan. French investment in Indian manufacturing under the defence offset clause supports the Make in India programme and creates high-skilled jobs. For Indian businesses, a strengthened France partnership also opens doors to European Union market access and technology transfer opportunities.

What's next

Watch for formal announcements of defence or commercial agreements from the Nice visit, particularly regarding additional Rafale jets or submarine contracts. Expect joint statements on the Iran conflict and broader West Asia stability, given both nations' energy security interests. PM Modi may also use the European visit to shore up diplomatic support for India's positions at multilateral forums.
