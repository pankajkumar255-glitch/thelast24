HCLTech Buys 10.5% Stake in Sarvam AI as Indian Startup Turns Unicorn at $1.5 Billion Valuation

What happened

Reuters reports that HCLTech has agreed to buy a 10.5% stake in Sarvam AI at a valuation of $1.5 billion. NDTV and The New Indian Express further report that Sarvam AI has raised $234 million in the first tranche of its Series B round, crossing the unicorn threshold. The funding and strategic investment together mark a significant milestone for the Indian AI ecosystem.

The context

Sarvam AI was founded to build large language models tailored for Indian languages and use cases, positioning itself as an alternative to Western AI platforms. HCLTech is one of India's largest IT services companies, with deep enterprise relationships and the scale to deploy AI solutions across industries. The deal reflects a broader trend of established Indian tech companies investing in domestic AI startups rather than relying solely on global providers like OpenAI or Google.

Why it matters

For everyday Indians, a well-funded Sarvam AI could accelerate the availability of AI tools in regional languages, making technology more accessible across the country. For investors and the Indian tech industry, a $1.5 billion valuation for a homegrown AI firm validates the sector and could attract further capital into Indian AI ventures.

What's next

Expect Sarvam AI to deploy the fresh capital toward expanding its language model capabilities and enterprise integrations, with HCLTech likely among its first large-scale commercial partners.
