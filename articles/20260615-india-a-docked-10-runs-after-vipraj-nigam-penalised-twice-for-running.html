India A docked 10 runs after Vipraj Nigam penalised twice for running on pitch

What happened

India Today reports that Vipraj Nigam was penalised twice during an India A match for running on the pitch, a violation of ICC playing conditions. As a consequence, India A were docked 10 runs, a steep and uncommon punishment that directly affected their match total.

The context

ICC playing conditions explicitly prohibit fielders from running on the protected area of the pitch, with umpires empowered to award penalty runs to the batting side for repeat infringements. India A matches serve as the primary preparation and selection ground for players seeking a full Indian cap. Vipraj Nigam is a young cricketer who has been part of India's emerging talent pipeline.

Why it matters

The 10-run penalty could have a direct bearing on India A's match result, and the incident is a reminder that mental lapses — even in non-Test cricket — carry real consequences. It may affect Nigam's selection prospects at a critical stage of his career.

What's next

Expect the Board of Control for Cricket in India's selectors and India A management to address fielding discipline with the squad following this embarrassing episode.
