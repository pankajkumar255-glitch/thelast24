Supreme Court Values a Homemaker's Labour at Rs 30,000 a Month — Here's Why It's a Landmark

What happened

The Hindu and India Today report that the Supreme Court of India has ruled that the labour of homemakers must be quantified, setting a benchmark figure of Rs 30,000 per month as the value of domestic care work. The court described homemakers as 'nation builders' and directed that this valuation be applied in relevant legal and compensation proceedings. The ruling addresses a long-standing gap in how Indian courts have treated unpaid household labour.

The context

Indian courts have historically struggled to assign monetary value to the work of homemakers, particularly in motor accident compensation cases under the Motor Vehicles Act, where the death or injury of a non-earning spouse was often awarded minimal damages. The Supreme Court has periodically revisited this issue, and this ruling represents one of the clearest monetary benchmarks set by the apex court for domestic labour. The decision aligns with a broader global movement to formally account for unpaid care work in economic and legal frameworks.

Why it matters

For millions of Indian families, this ruling sets a concrete floor for compensation claims in accident and insurance cases involving homemakers, potentially increasing payouts significantly. It also carries symbolic weight by placing the state's highest court on record as recognising domestic work as economically productive labour.

What's next

Expect lower courts and insurance tribunals across India to begin applying the Rs 30,000 monthly benchmark in compensation cases, and likely challenges from insurers seeking clarity on how the figure will be indexed or adjusted over time.
