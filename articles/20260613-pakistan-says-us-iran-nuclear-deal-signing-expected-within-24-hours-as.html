Pakistan says US-Iran nuclear deal signing expected within 24 hours as Iran confirms talks near conclusion

What happened

Business Standard reports that Pakistan has said the signing of a US-Iran peace deal is expected within 24 hours. The BBC separately reports that Iran has claimed a deal with the United States is near, even as Israel carried out air strikes on Lebanon around the same time. The near-simultaneous military and diplomatic signals underscore the volatile and fast-moving nature of the situation. Pakistan's role as a messenger or interlocutor in relaying this timeline adds a notable regional dimension to the development.

The context

The United States and Iran have been locked in a nuclear standoff for years, intensified after former President Donald Trump withdrew from the 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action in 2018 and reimposed sweeping sanctions on Tehran. Iran responded by progressively exceeding the uranium enrichment limits set by that agreement. Indirect and direct talks have been attempted multiple times since, including in Oman, without a final breakthrough. Iran's oil exports, constrained by US sanctions, have been a persistent source of tension affecting global energy supply. India, which once imported significant volumes of Iranian crude, has a direct stake in any change to the sanctions regime.

Why it matters

A signed deal would likely lead to the easing or lifting of US sanctions on Iran, freeing up Iranian oil to re-enter global markets and putting downward pressure on crude prices — a major relief for India, which is heavily dependent on oil imports. It could also reduce the geopolitical risk premium baked into energy prices. For Indian businesses and consumers, lower fuel costs cascade into cheaper transportation, goods, and inflation moderation. Strategically, it could reshape India's options for resuming Iranian crude imports and reviving the Chabahar port trade corridor.

What's next

Expect intense international scrutiny over the next 24-48 hours to confirm whether a formal signing actually occurs, given how many previous rounds of talks have stalled at the final stage. Watch for Israel's reaction, as it has consistently opposed any deal that does not permanently end Iran's nuclear programme, and its air strikes on Lebanon signal continued regional assertiveness.
