BCCI weighing bilateral series feasibility; IPL 2026 likely to begin from early March

What happened

India Today reports that the BCCI is currently assessing the practicality of scheduling bilateral international series alongside its existing commitments. According to the publication, IPL 2026 is expected to begin from early March, which would mark an earlier start than many recent editions. The board is reportedly weighing the logistical and cricketing demands of accommodating both bilateral fixtures and the IPL window. This planning is taking place as India manages a busy international schedule that includes bilateral series with multiple nations.

The context

The IPL, now in its late teens as a tournament, is the world's richest T20 cricket league and the BCCI's largest revenue generator. In recent years, the IPL window has typically run from late March through May, though the precise dates shift annually based on the international calendar and venue availability. BCCI's scheduling decisions carry enormous consequences for broadcasters, franchises, sponsors, and players. Bilateral series with visiting nations are also significant revenue earners but must be balanced against player workloads and franchise interests.

Why it matters

An early March IPL start means fans get more cricket sooner, but it also compresses preparation time for franchises and players returning from international duty. For broadcasters and fantasy gaming platforms, the IPL start date is a major commercial trigger. Clarity on bilateral series also affects touring nations' planning and India's international commitments.

What's next

Expect the BCCI to formalise the IPL 2026 schedule and announce bilateral fixtures in the coming weeks. Franchise owners and team management will be watching for official confirmation so pre-season preparations and player auctions can be planned accordingly.
