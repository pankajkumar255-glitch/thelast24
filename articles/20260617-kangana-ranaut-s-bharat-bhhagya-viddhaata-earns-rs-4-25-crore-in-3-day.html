Kangana Ranaut's Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata earns Rs 4.25 crore in 3 days, less than half of Emergency's opening

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata, starring Kangana Ranaut, has earned Rs 4.25 crore in its first three days at the box office. The collection is less than half of what Emergency, Kangana's previous release, collected over the same period. The film's opening performance marks a notable disappointment for the actress-filmmaker.

The context

Kangana Ranaut has been one of Bollywood's most prominent and controversial figures, increasingly taking on directing duties alongside starring roles. Emergency, her biopic on Indira Gandhi, also faced a troubled release with delays and moderate box-office returns. Consecutive underperformers put significant pressure on her standing as a bankable solo draw in Hindi cinema.

Why it matters

Weak box-office returns affect the financing and greenlit prospects of future projects, and signal to producers the risks of attaching high-cost talent without guaranteed returns. For audiences, it reflects a broader pattern of mid-budget Bollywood films struggling to fill seats.

What's next

Expect trade analysts to watch the film's weekday collections closely to determine whether it can recover costs through OTT rights and satellite deals.
