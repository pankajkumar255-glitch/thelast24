Fans Slam US Visa Restrictions Ahead Of 2026 FIFA World Cup

What happened

BBC reports that football fans are voicing significant anger over US travel bans and visa restrictions that could bar them from attending the 2026 FIFA World Cup. The frustration is being expressed across fan communities, with supporters feeling the host nation's immigration policies are incompatible with the inclusive, global spirit of the tournament. BBC notes the sentiment is summed up in the phrase 'A World Cup for them not us', reflecting a sense of exclusion among international fans.

The context

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is scheduled to be jointly hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico — the first time three nations will co-host the tournament. It will also be the first World Cup to feature 48 teams, meaning more nations than ever before will qualify and send fans. The Trump administration has imposed travel bans on citizens of several countries and tightened visa issuance broadly, creating uncertainty for fans from affected nations. FIFA awarded the tournament to the North American bid in 2018.

Why it matters

Indian football fans, as well as fans from countries on US visa watchlists, may find themselves unable to attend matches even if their national team qualifies. Beyond individual fans, the controversy could pressure FIFA and host organisers to address access issues, potentially affecting the commercial and cultural success of the tournament. It also highlights a broader tension between the US administration's immigration stance and its role as host of a global event.

What's next

Expect FIFA to face mounting calls to clarify travel guarantees for fans of all participating nations. Watch for whether the US State Department issues any specific World Cup visitor guidelines or exemptions in the months ahead.
