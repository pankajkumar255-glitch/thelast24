Supreme Court seeks Centre and Punjab response on failure to implement 25% RTE quota in schools

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has sought responses from the Centre and the Punjab government on a petition alleging that the 25% RTE quota in private unaided schools has not been implemented. The court's notice puts both the federal and state governments on notice to explain their compliance record with a statutory obligation.

The context

The Right to Education Act, 2009 requires private unaided schools to admit at least 25% of their intake from economically weaker sections and disadvantaged groups, with the government reimbursing tuition fees. Implementation has been uneven across states, with many schools and state administrations accused of ignoring or diluting the mandate over the past decade.

Why it matters

For lower-income families, the 25% RTE quota is often the only route to quality private schooling; non-implementation directly denies children that opportunity. A Supreme Court order compelling compliance could force thousands of private schools nationwide to open their gates to students they have long excluded.

What's next

Expect the Centre and Punjab government to file their affidavits in response, after which the court is likely to set a compliance timeline or consider further directions.
