Supreme Court seeks Centre and Punjab response on failure to implement 25% RTE quota in schools

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has sought responses from the Centre and the Punjab government on a petition highlighting the failure to implement the 25% RTE quota for economically weaker sections in private schools. The court has issued formal notice to both governments to explain the non-compliance.

The context

The Right to Education Act, 2009 mandates that all private unaided schools reserve 25% of seats in entry-level classes for children from economically weaker sections and disadvantaged groups, with the state reimbursing schools for these admissions. Implementation has been patchy across several states, with disputes over reimbursement rates and identification of beneficiaries frequently cited as reasons for non-compliance.

Why it matters

Failure to enforce the RTE quota directly denies thousands of poor children in Punjab access to better-resourced private schools, perpetuating educational inequality. A Supreme Court order mandating compliance could force state governments nationwide to accelerate implementation and clear backlogs of eligible applicants.

What's next

Expect the Centre and Punjab to file affidavits detailing the current status of RTE seat allocation and reimbursement, with the court likely setting a compliance deadline.
