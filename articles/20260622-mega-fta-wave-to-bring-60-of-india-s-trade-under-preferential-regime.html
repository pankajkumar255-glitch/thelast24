Mega FTA wave to bring 60% of India's trade under preferential regime

Key facts

  • 60% of India's trade to come under preferential tariff regime
  • FTAs being pursued with U.S., EU, UK, GCC and others
  • Marks sharp shift from India's historically protectionist trade policy
  • Could benefit exporters in textiles, pharma, IT and engineering

India is on the cusp of a sweeping transformation in its trade architecture, with a cluster of free trade agreements set to bring 60% of the country's total trade under preferential tariff arrangements, according to a new analysis. The scale of the FTA push marks a decisive break from the cautious, go-slow approach that defined Indian trade policy for decades.

The deals in various stages of negotiation or conclusion span India's most consequential trading relationships. Talks with the United States are nearing an interim agreement, negotiations with the European Union — long stalled — have been revived with renewed urgency, and the India-UK FTA has been under discussion for years but is edging toward a conclusion. Deals with Gulf Cooperation Council countries, Canada, and several Indo-Pacific partners are also in the pipeline.

The cumulative effect, if all major agreements are concluded, would be to fundamentally alter the tariff landscape for Indian exporters and importers alike. Indian goods in sectors like textiles, pharmaceuticals, IT services, and engineering products would gain preferential access to some of the world's largest consumer markets, while domestic industries — including agriculture and manufacturing — would face stiffer competition from imports entering at lower or zero duties.

The shift also has strategic dimensions. By diversifying its FTA network, India is reducing dependence on any single trade partner and positioning itself as a hub in global supply chains at a time when geopolitical realignments are redrawing trade flows worldwide. Whether the gains materialise will depend heavily on how well Indian industry adapts to the opportunities — and pressures — that a more open trade regime will bring.
