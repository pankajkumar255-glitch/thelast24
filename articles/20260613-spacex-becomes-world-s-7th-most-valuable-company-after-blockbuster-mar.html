SpaceX becomes world's 7th most valuable company after blockbuster market debut

What happened

The Economic Times reports that SpaceX has become the world's seventh most valuable company following its blockbuster market debut. The Times of India separately covers Uday Kotak's pointed reaction to the historic IPO, asking whether it is a 'mega bubble or fairy tale'. The listing represents one of the most closely watched public offerings in recent memory, given SpaceX's dominance in commercial launch services and its Starlink satellite internet business. The scale of the valuation has prompted debate among global investors and financial leaders about whether the price reflects fundamentals or speculative excess.

The context

SpaceX, founded by Elon Musk, is the world's leading private launch provider and operates the Starlink broadband satellite constellation, which has millions of subscribers globally. The company had long remained private despite its enormous implied valuation in secondary markets, making its public debut one of the most anticipated in tech history. Uday Kotak, founder of Kotak Mahindra Bank, is one of India's most respected voices in finance and his public scepticism carries weight in domestic investment circles. India's own space sector is undergoing rapid liberalisation, and SpaceX's valuation will inevitably shape how Indian space startups and ISRO's commercial arm are valued going forward.

Why it matters

Indian mutual funds and retail investors increasingly hold global equity exposure, and SpaceX's debut will influence portfolio strategies and benchmark comparisons. Starlink already has regulatory approvals pending in India, and the company's listed status could accelerate commercial negotiations with the government. Uday Kotak's 'bubble' warning is a signal for Indian investors to scrutinise sky-high valuations before chasing momentum in global tech stocks.

What's next

Expect Indian market analysts to reassess global tech allocation strategies in the wake of SpaceX's debut valuation. Starlink's India rollout timeline and pricing are likely to come under sharper focus now that the company is publicly listed and accountable to shareholders.
