How OTT is Reimagining '90s Leading Ladies: Sonali Bendre, Madhuri Dixit, Karisma Kapoor

Key facts

  • Sonali Bendre, Madhuri Dixit and Karisma Kapoor finding renewed careers on OTT
  • Streaming platforms offering more complex roles than 1990s Bollywood did
  • Trend reflects growing appetite for women-led narratives in Indian streaming

Three decades after they ruled the Hindi film industry, Sonali Bendre, Madhuri Dixit and Karisma Kapoor are finding new, more complex creative lives on streaming platforms — and audiences are taking notice.

OTT platforms have emerged as an unlikely second act for the biggest female stars of 1990s Bollywood. Where the commercial cinema of that era often cast these women in song-and-dance roles with limited dramatic range, streaming productions are giving them the space to inhabit layered, morally complex characters. Madhuri Dixit's work on Netflix's Finding Anamika, Karisma Kapoor's turn in Mentalhood, and Sonali Bendre's appearances in digital projects have all been cited as evidence of this transformation.

The phenomenon speaks to a structural shift in Indian entertainment. OTT platforms, competing fiercely for subscribers, have an incentive to develop content that appeals across age groups — and that includes viewers who grew up idolising these stars and are now eager to see them in stories that reflect adult realities. The stars themselves have spoken about valuing creative freedom over commercial formulas.

For film historians and gender critics, the trend also raises a pointed question: why did it take streaming to unlock the full potential of actresses who were always capable of more? The answer, many suggest, lies in the patriarchal structures of mainstream Bollywood that OTT, for all its imperfections, is quietly beginning to dismantle.
