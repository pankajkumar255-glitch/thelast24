Indian Man Venkatesh Doppalaud From Andhra Pradesh Found Dead In Kansas Floods

Key facts

  • Venkatesh Doppalaud from Andhra Pradesh found dead in Kansas, US
  • His car was swept away by floodwaters; locals witnessed it
  • Andhra Pradesh MLA had sought help tracing the missing youth

Venkatesh Doppalaud, an Indian national from Andhra Pradesh, has been found dead in the state of Kansas after his car was swept away by raging floodwaters, according to multiple Indian news reports. Witnesses at the scene reportedly saw the vehicle being carried into the water, leaving little chance of survival in the severe flooding that has struck parts of the United States in recent days.

The tragedy had attracted political attention in Andhra Pradesh even before his body was recovered, with a local MLA seeking help from authorities to trace the missing youth. His death has now been confirmed, bringing grief to his family and the wider Telugu community both in India and in the United States, which has one of the largest concentrations of Telugu-speaking immigrants anywhere in the world.

The incident comes amid broader flooding events across parts of the US that have claimed multiple lives. For Indian families who send children abroad for education or employment — particularly to states like Kansas with significant Indian student and professional populations — the news serves as a stark reminder of the dangers of extreme weather.

The Andhra Pradesh government and diaspora organisations are expected to extend support to the bereaved family. Consular officials from India are likely to be involved in coordinating the repatriation of the deceased.
