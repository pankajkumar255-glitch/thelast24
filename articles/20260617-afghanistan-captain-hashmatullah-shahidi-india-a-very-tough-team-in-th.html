Afghanistan captain Hashmatullah Shahidi: 'India a very tough team in their own conditions'

What happened

Rediff and India Today report that Afghanistan captain Hashmatullah Shahidi has called India a very tough team in their own conditions, while also urging his players to take ownership and make India grind for every result. His comments were made in the context of the ongoing ODI series being played in India. Shahidi's dual message — respect for India combined with a rallying call for his own team — underlines Afghanistan's growing competitive confidence.

The context

Afghanistan cricket has made remarkable strides since earning Test status in 2017, regularly producing upsets against established sides in white-ball cricket. Hashmatullah Shahidi is an experienced Afghanistan batter and captain who has overseen several of his country's landmark wins. Playing in Indian conditions remains one of the toughest challenges for visiting teams due to spin-friendly pitches and partisan home crowds.

Why it matters

Afghanistan's competitive mindset in this series ensures it will not be a walkover for India, keeping the contest engaging for fans while also genuinely testing India's squad depth and selections. A strong Afghanistan performance would also boost the global narrative of cricket's expanding competitive landscape.

What's next

Expect Afghanistan to adopt an aggressive strategy built around their spin attack in the remaining ODIs as they look to level or win the series.
