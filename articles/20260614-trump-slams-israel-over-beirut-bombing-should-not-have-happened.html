Trump Slams Israel Over Beirut Bombing: 'Should Not Have Happened'

What happened

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump publicly condemned Israel's bombing of Beirut, stating it 'should not have happened.' The remarks represent an unusually sharp public criticism of Israel from Trump, who has historically been a strong supporter of the Israeli government.

The context

Israel has been conducting an extended military campaign in Lebanon targeting Hezbollah, the Iran-backed militant group. The US has broadly backed Israel's right to self-defence, making Trump's public rebuke notable. Lebanon's capital Beirut has faced repeated Israeli strikes, raising international concern about civilian harm.

Why it matters

A public rift between the US and Israel could complicate ceasefire negotiations and influence how the conflict in Lebanon unfolds, with consequences for regional stability. India, which has a large diaspora in the Gulf and close ties with both Israel and Arab nations, watches any escalation closely.

What's next

Expect the Israeli government to respond to Trump's comments and international mediators to test whether the US position shifts in upcoming diplomatic talks.
