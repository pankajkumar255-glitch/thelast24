Bharat Innovates opens doors to European customers and investors for Indian deeptech startups

Key facts

  • Bharat Innovates creates corridor for Indian deeptech startups to access Europe
  • Programme targets European customers, investors, and partners
  • Aimed at startups in hardware, deep science, and advanced technology sectors

Moneycontrol.com reports that Bharat Innovates has opened a dedicated corridor connecting Indian deeptech startups with European customers, investors, and partners. The initiative is structured to help Indian technology companies — particularly those working on hardware, space, defence, climate, and advanced manufacturing — find market traction and funding in Europe, a region known for its strong industrial base and appetite for deep-technology solutions.

India's deeptech startup ecosystem has grown significantly in recent years, but many founders face the challenge of limited domestic market size and long commercial cycles. Access to European customers can accelerate product validation, while European institutional investors have shown increasing interest in backing global deeptech ventures.

The Bharat Innovates programme adds a meaningful international dimension to India's startup support infrastructure. For everyday Indians, the broader implication is more homegrown deeptech companies achieving global scale — creating high-skilled jobs, generating export revenues, and raising India's profile as a serious player in the next generation of global technology.
