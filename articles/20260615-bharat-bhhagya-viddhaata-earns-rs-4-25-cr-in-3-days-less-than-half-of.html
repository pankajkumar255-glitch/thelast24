Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata earns Rs 4.25 cr in 3 days — less than half of Kangana's Emergency

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata, starring Kangana Ranaut, earned just Rs 4.25 crore in its first three days, a figure that is less than half of what her previous film Emergency collected in the same window. The performance makes it one of the weaker openings for a high-profile Bollywood release this year.

The context

Kangana Ranaut has positioned herself as both an actor and director in recent years, with Emergency being a politically charged biopic on Indira Gandhi. Both films arrived with significant publicity and controversy, yet neither has translated that attention into ticket sales. The Indian film industry has increasingly seen a gap between marketing buzz and actual footfalls.

Why it matters

Poor box office returns mean distributors and theatre chains lose money, making them more cautious about booking future big-banner films. For audiences, it reflects a broader shift in viewing habits, with OTT platforms drawing viewers away from cinemas.

What's next

Expect trade analysts to watch the film's weekday collections closely to determine whether it can recover enough to break even on its production and marketing costs.
