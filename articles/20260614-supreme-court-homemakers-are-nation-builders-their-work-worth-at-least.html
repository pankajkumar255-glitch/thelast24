Supreme Court: Homemakers are 'nation builders', their work worth at least ₹30,000 a month

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has ruled that the work of homemakers is worth at least Rs 30,000 a month, describing them as 'nation builders' whose contribution to society is immense but routinely undervalued. The judgment was delivered in the context of a compensation matter and sets a legal floor for how courts must value domestic labour going forward. India Today NE also covered the ruling, confirming the ₹30,000 monthly benchmark.

The context

Indian courts have historically struggled to assign monetary value to the work of homemakers in compensation cases, often awarding negligible amounts that failed to reflect the true economic contribution of domestic labour. Women's rights groups and legal scholars have long argued for a more just framework. This ruling aligns with global conversations around unpaid care work and its recognition in national accounting and legal systems.

Why it matters

Families of homemakers involved in accident or negligence cases can now expect significantly higher compensation from courts and insurance bodies. Beyond legal proceedings, the ruling sends a powerful social signal that domestic work — predominantly done by women — has real, quantifiable economic value.

What's next

Expect lower courts and insurance tribunals to begin applying the ₹30,000 benchmark in compensation cases, and anticipate renewed legislative debate on formally recognising unpaid domestic labour.
