BSE Sensex surges around 800 points at close — falling oil prices and Asian gains drive the rally

Key facts

  • BSE Sensex closed around 800 points higher on June 25
  • Nifty 50 opened above 24,100 in morning trade
  • IndiGo shares rose 3% in early trade
  • Rally driven by falling oil prices and positive Asian equity trends

The BSE Sensex closed around 800 points higher on Wednesday, delivering one of its stronger single-session gains in recent weeks as multiple tailwinds converged to lift Indian equities. The rally was broad-based, reflecting renewed optimism among domestic and foreign investors.

A sharp drop in global crude oil prices was among the primary catalysts, according to The Times of India. Lower oil prices are a significant positive for India — the country imports a large share of its energy needs, and cheaper crude reduces the import bill, eases inflation pressure, and improves the current account outlook. Positive trading sessions across Asian equity markets through the day provided additional momentum, giving Indian traders early confidence that was sustained into the close.

The market had already signalled its direction at the opening bell. The Nifty 50 began the session above 24,100 and IndiGo shares rose as much as 3 percent in morning trade, with aviation stocks benefiting directly from the slide in jet fuel costs linked to lower crude prices. The broader market followed suit, with buying visible across sectors.

Improving sentiment around the India-US trade negotiations — which both governments described as showing 'substantial progress' — also contributed to the positive mood, even as a final deal remained elusive. For everyday investors, the session served as a reminder of how interconnected global commodity markets, geopolitics, and domestic equities have become.
