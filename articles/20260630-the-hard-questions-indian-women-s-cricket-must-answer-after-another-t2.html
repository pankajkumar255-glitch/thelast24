The hard questions Indian women's cricket must answer after another T20 World Cup failure

Key facts

  • India women exited the T20 World Cup with a disappointing campaign
  • Structural issues: selection inconsistency and bench depth flagged
  • Asian Games 2026 and England series are next opportunities to rebuild

Indian women's cricket faces yet another reckoning after yet another early exit from the T20 World Cup, and the questions being asked now are sharper and more urgent than ever before. Despite having a generation of talented players, the team has consistently failed to translate promise into ICC tournament victories, and patience among fans and analysts is wearing thin.

Moneycontrol's analysis points to a range of structural concerns — inconsistency in selection policy, a lack of robust bench depth, and the mental and tactical gaps that separate India from the top T20 sides. The team has often relied heavily on a handful of performers, and when those players underperform, the entire campaign unravels with startling speed.

The timing of this reflection is critical. With the Asian Games 2026 squad already announced and the India vs England bilateral calendar looming, the women's team and its management have a series of competitive opportunities to course-correct before the next major ICC event. Each match in this period will be scrutinised for evidence of genuine progress.

For Indian women's cricket to truly break through, analysts argue that a more settled selection approach, greater investment in domestic structures, and a willingness to back young players through difficult patches will all be essential. Another T20 World Cup disappointment of the same kind, many believe, would simply be unacceptable.
