India's startup count surges from a few hundred in 2016 to 2 lakh in 2025

Key facts

  • India's startups grew from a few hundred (2016) to 2 lakh (2025)
  • Growth driven by Startup India initiative launched in 2016
  • India now among top 3 global startup ecosystems
  • Over 100 unicorns produced in this period

What happened

NDTV reports that India's officially recognised startup count has risen from a few hundred in 2016 to 2 lakh in 2025, tracking the dramatic expansion of the country's entrepreneurial ecosystem over nine years. The growth is attributed to the Startup India programme launched by PM Modi, increased domestic and foreign venture capital, and the proliferation of digital payments and internet access.

The context

India launched the Startup India initiative in January 2016 with the goal of building a strong entrepreneurial ecosystem. DPIIT recognition grants startups tax benefits and easier compliance. India has produced over 100 unicorns and is home to major global startups including Flipkart, Zomato, and Razorpay.

Why it matters

Two lakh startups represent a significant engine of job creation, innovation, and foreign investment inflows at a time when India needs to generate high-quality employment for its demographic dividend. It also signals growing confidence in India as a destination for risk capital.

What's next

Expect the government to highlight this milestone in the upcoming Union Budget discussions and potentially announce new incentives targeted at deep-tech and manufacturing startups.
