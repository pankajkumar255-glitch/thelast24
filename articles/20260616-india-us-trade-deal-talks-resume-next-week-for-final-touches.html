India-US trade deal talks resume next week for final touches

What happened

The Times of India reports that India and the US are scheduled to hold talks next week with the goal of finalising a bilateral trade agreement, described as giving 'final touches' to the deal. The report comes amid a broader push by both governments to formalise trade terms that have been under negotiation for an extended period.

The context

India and the US have been negotiating a trade deal as part of efforts to deepen economic ties and reduce friction caused by tariffs imposed under the Trump administration. India has been seeking better market access for its goods and services, while the US has pushed for reduced tariffs on American agricultural and industrial products.

Why it matters

A finalised deal could lower import duties on American goods sold in India and open up larger US markets for Indian exporters including textiles, pharmaceuticals and IT services, with real implications for prices and jobs. It would also signal geopolitical alignment at a time of global supply chain shifts.

What's next

Expect an announcement or at least a framework agreement to emerge from the talks next week, with details on which sectors are included in the initial phase.
