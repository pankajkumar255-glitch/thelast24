Cocktail 2 Crosses Rs 76 Crore Globally in Three Days

Key facts

  • Cocktail 2 grosses Rs 76.25 crore globally in first three days
  • Shahid Kapoor leads the film
  • Sequel to the 2012 hit Cocktail

Shahid Kapoor's Cocktail 2 has powered to a Rs 76.25 crore global box office haul in just three days, cementing its status as one of Bollywood's stronger openings of the year. The figures, tracked through Day 3, reflect healthy footfalls across domestic multiplexes and a solid overseas showing from Indian diaspora markets.

The original Cocktail, released in 2012, was a turning point in Kapoor's career, and its sequel has evidently carried that brand equity into a new decade. Early audience response appears to have driven strong word-of-mouth over the opening weekend, with collections building rather than declining sharply — a pattern that augurs well for the film's total run.

For the Hindi film industry, the number matters beyond just this one title. Several mid-range productions have struggled to find traction in theatres since the industry reset post-pandemic, and a clear Rs 76-crore global weekend from a single-hero commercial film reinforces the case for theatrical investment at scale.

The coming weekdays will be the real test: whether Cocktail 2 sustains or drops sharply will determine whether it crosses the crucial Rs 150-crore mark that typically defines a solid commercial success for a film at this budget level.
