India-US Trade Talks Set for June 23-24 as First-Phase Deal Nears Finish Line

What happened

India Today and Moneycontrol report that India and the US will hold fresh trade talks on June 23-24, with US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer visiting India as both sides push to lock in the first phase of a bilateral trade agreement. Reuters separately confirmed Greer's planned visit. The negotiations are described as entering their final stage for the first tranche of the deal.

The context

India and the US have been negotiating a bilateral trade agreement for several months, driven by the threat of elevated American tariffs under the Trump administration's reciprocal tariff regime. India was among the countries granted a 90-day pause on higher tariffs, making the current window crucial for reaching at least a partial agreement. USTR Greer's personal visit signals that talks have reached a decisive stage.

Why it matters

A successful first-tranche deal could protect Indian exporters of goods like gems, textiles, and pharma from steep US tariffs, safeguarding hundreds of thousands of jobs. For consumers and businesses, a deal would also shape import costs on American goods entering India.

What's next

Expect close attention to whether the June 23-24 talks produce a framework agreement or at minimum a joint statement locking in key terms of the first phase.
