Modi and Macron to jointly inaugurate Bharat Innovates 2026 in Nice on June 14

What happened

PIB announced that Prime Minister Narendra Modi and French President Emmanuel Macron will jointly inaugurate Bharat Innovates 2026 on June 14, 2026, in Nice, France. The event is designed to spotlight India's technology, startup, and innovation ecosystem to an international audience. The joint inauguration by two heads of state elevates the event well beyond a typical industry showcase.

The context

India and France have steadily deepened their strategic partnership across defence, space, nuclear energy, and digital technology over recent years. Bharat Innovates is conceived as a platform to project India's growing startup and deep-tech credentials to global investors and policymakers. The choice of Nice — which will host leaders around the G7 season — adds diplomatic visibility to the initiative.

Why it matters

A Modi-Macron co-inauguration can accelerate European venture capital interest in Indian startups and open bilateral technology collaboration agreements. For Indian innovators, it signals government backing for global market access at the highest diplomatic level.

What's next

Expect a slate of India-France technology partnership announcements and investment commitments to be unveiled around the June 14 event in Nice.
