Delhi's EV Policy 2.0 cleared: What it means for two-wheelers and repair shops

Key facts

  • Delhi EV Policy 2.0 cleared by the city government
  • Policy focuses primarily on two-wheelers as the key adoption category
  • Raises questions about legacy petrol two-wheelers and repair shop livelihoods
  • Aimed at addressing Delhi's chronic air pollution crisis

Delhi's EV Policy 2.0 has been cleared, with the city government placing two-wheelers at the centre of its electric vehicle transition strategy. The policy reflects an acknowledgement that two-wheelers constitute the single largest category of vehicles on Delhi's roads and that electrifying them offers the fastest route to meaningful reductions in tailpipe emissions.

The new framework raises immediate practical questions for owners of existing petrol two-wheelers and the repair ecosystem that supports them. Hundreds of thousands of mechanics and spare-parts dealers whose livelihoods depend on internal combustion engine servicing could face a significant shift in demand as the city pushes adoption of electric alternatives.

Delhi's earlier EV policy had already established the capital as one of the more progressive states on electric mobility, with incentives for buyers and charging infrastructure investment. Policy 2.0 is expected to sharpen those incentives, though the detailed financial provisions were still being assessed at the time of reporting.

With Delhi consistently ranking among the world's most polluted cities, the success of the EV push will be closely watched both as an air quality intervention and as a model for other Indian megacities considering similar transitions.
