OTT this week: Ali Fazal's crime series Raakh and Jason Statham's Shelter arrive on streaming

What happened

The Indian Express recommends two OTT titles for the weekend: Raakh, an Indian crime series featuring Ali Fazal in the lead role, and Shelter, described as a survival thriller starring Jason Statham. The publication frames both as the standout streaming choices of the week across platforms. Raakh represents a significant Indian original production given Fazal's profile, while Shelter adds Hollywood star power to the weekend lineup. No specific platform names are confirmed from the headline alone, but both are presented as currently available for Indian viewers.

The context

Ali Fazal is one of the few Indian actors to have successfully built a profile in both Bollywood and international productions, appearing in the Hollywood hit Furious 7 and the critically acclaimed British film Victoria & Abdul alongside Dame Judi Dench. His return to a lead role in an Indian crime series is therefore a high-expectation project. Jason Statham is one of Hollywood's most consistent action stars, known for the Transporter and Mechanic franchises. The Indian OTT market has exploded in scale, with platforms like Netflix, Prime Video, JioCinema, and SonyLIV competing fiercely for subscriber attention with a mix of Indian originals and international acquisitions.

Why it matters

For Indian OTT subscribers, the simultaneous availability of a credible Indian original crime series and a mainstream Hollywood thriller represents strong value in a market where content quality is the primary driver of subscription decisions. Ali Fazal's involvement in Raakh could set a benchmark for Indian crime drama production values. The weekend lineup also illustrates how Indian and global content increasingly compete for the same viewer's attention on the same platforms.

What's next

Expect reviews and social media reactions for both Raakh and Shelter to drive or dampen viewership significantly within the first 48 hours of release. It is likely that Raakh's performance will influence commissioning decisions for future Indian crime originals on its platform.
