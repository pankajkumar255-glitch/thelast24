Persistent Systems stock crashes 10% as Nagarro acquisition rattles markets

Key facts

  • Persistent Systems stock fell 10%, top midcap loser
  • CEO Sandeep Kalra called Nagarro deal a 'God-given opportunity'
  • Deal aims to build AI engineering capability
  • Brokerages divided on deal's merits

Persistent Systems shares fell sharply — crashing 10% to become the day's top midcap loser — after the company announced the acquisition of Nagarro, even as CEO Sandeep Kalra publicly championed the deal as a 'God-given opportunity' for the firm.

Kalra framed the Nagarro acquisition as central to Persistent's ambition of building a leading AI engineering capability, a differentiation he believes will become decisive as enterprise clients demand more intelligent, automated technology solutions. The company's leadership has positioned the deal as transformative rather than merely additive to its existing services portfolio.

However, the market's verdict was sharply negative. Brokerages flagged concerns around valuation, integration risk, and whether the acquisition dilutes near-term earnings at a time when global IT spending remains under pressure. Several analysts noted that while the strategic rationale is sound, the timing and the price paid will face scrutiny over the coming quarters.

The Persistent-Nagarro deal underscores a broader trend among mid-tier Indian IT firms seeking inorganic growth to break into higher-value AI and engineering services. Whether the market's scepticism proves correct or Kalra's conviction is vindicated will likely depend on how quickly synergies materialise — and what the final integration costs look like.
