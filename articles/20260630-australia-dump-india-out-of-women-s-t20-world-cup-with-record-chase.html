Australia dump India out of Women's T20 World Cup with record chase

Key facts

  • Australia beat India with a record chase in the Women's T20 World Cup
  • India eliminated from the tournament
  • Australia's chase described as record-breaking

Australia eliminated India from the ICC Women's T20 World Cup with a record chase, handing the Indian side a defeat that ended their tournament run. The Australians, one of the most formidable sides in women's cricket, chased down their target in record fashion, leaving India with no path further in the competition.

The defeat was a sobering reminder of the gap that still exists between India and the world's best T20 sides. Australia's batting lineup dismantled the Indian bowling attack with an efficiency that exposed defensive frailties, while their own bowlers kept India's batters in check throughout the match.

For Indian cricket, the loss stings not just in terms of the World Cup campaign but also in what it reveals about the team's readiness for elite-level T20 competition. The selectors and coaching staff will need to assess whether the current template — in terms of batting order, bowling combinations, and overall game plan — is fit for purpose against the strongest opposition.

Ironically, the exit did not cost India their broader ambitions entirely. By virtue of their ICC rankings and the qualification pathway for cricket's debut at the 2028 LA Olympics, India's women's team still secured an Olympic quota place, offering some consolation from an otherwise disappointing campaign.

Coach and captain will face hard questions in the coming days, with calls already growing for a thorough review of India's approach to the shortest format of the game.
