The 90-minute White House ultimatum that killed Anthropic's most powerful AI model

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that Anthropic was handed a 90-minute White House ultimatum and faced an intervention by Amazon before it pulled its most powerful AI model, Fable 5, shortly after launch. The report details how US government pressure, linked to export-control concerns, drove the rapid shutdown decision. NDTV also covered the story, confirming the model was disabled within days of its release.

The context

Anthropic is one of the world's leading AI safety companies, backed heavily by Amazon. The US government has been tightening export controls on advanced AI models and chips, part of a broader effort to keep cutting-edge AI technology out of adversaries' hands. Fable 5 was understood to be among the most capable models Anthropic had released publicly.

Why it matters

The incident shows that even commercially released AI products can be pulled at 24 hours' notice on government orders, exposing businesses worldwide — including in India — that have built workflows or products on such platforms to sudden disruption. It also underscores the growing role of geopolitics in AI access.

What's next

Expect Anthropic to clarify what export restrictions now apply to its model lineup, and for Indian enterprises dependent on its API to urgently assess alternative or domestically sourced AI solutions.
