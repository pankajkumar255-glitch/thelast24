How OpenAI, Google and Anthropic Are Using AI in Their Own Daily Operations

Key facts

  • OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic all use AI in their own daily operations
  • Use cases include coding, research, and document assistance
  • Story reported by NDTV

The world's leading AI companies — OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic — are not just selling artificial intelligence; they are using it extensively in their own internal operations, offering a revealing window into the real-world utility of the technology they build.

According to NDTV's report, all three organisations deploy AI tools across a range of daily functions. This includes using large language models for coding assistance, document summarisation, research support, and internal knowledge management. For companies that employ thousands of engineers and researchers, AI-assisted coding and documentation alone can represent enormous productivity gains.

The practices of these companies matter beyond curiosity. When AI labs themselves rely on their tools to operate, it provides credibility to the broader enterprise AI market and signals which specific use cases have matured enough to be trusted in high-stakes environments. It also helps calibrate expectations — if even Anthropic's internal teams rely on human review for certain decisions, that tells businesses something important about where human oversight remains essential.

For Indian enterprises considering large-scale AI adoption, understanding how the most sophisticated AI users in the world deploy these tools — and where they still exercise caution — provides a practical benchmark. The gap between AI's marketing promise and its operational reality is often best illustrated by how its creators actually behave.

The report does not suggest the three companies have fully automated their operations, but rather that AI has become a genuine productivity layer across their organisations — a finding that has direct implications for businesses globally.
