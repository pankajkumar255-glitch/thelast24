Trump says Netanyahu 'almost derailed' US-Iran peace deal

What happened

NDTV reports that Trump publicly stated Netanyahu was 'very difficult' and came close to derailing the US-Iran peace negotiations. BBC separately notes that while Trump's Iran announcement may ease regional pressure, significant uncertainty lingers over the agreement's scope and implementation. BBC also reports it remains unclear whether the deal will provide any relief to Lebanon.

The context

The United States and Iran have been locked in a decades-long standoff over Iran's nuclear programme, with previous agreements — notably the 2015 JCPOA — collapsing after Trump withdrew in his first term. Israel has consistently opposed any deal it sees as leaving Iran with nuclear capability. Iran is a major oil exporter and a key supplier to India.

Why it matters

A successful US-Iran deal could ease global oil supply tensions and lower crude prices, providing relief to India's import bill and potentially reducing fuel costs for Indian consumers. Conversely, a collapse could trigger fresh sanctions and a price spike.

What's next

Expect close monitoring of whether Iran and the US can finalise and ratify the agreement's terms, and whether Israel's opposition will ultimately derail the deal.
