Trump Says Netanyahu 'Almost Derailed' US-Iran Peace Deal

What happened

NDTV reports that President Trump has said Israeli PM Netanyahu almost derailed the US-Iran peace deal, describing the process as 'very difficult'. Trump's public remarks highlight serious disagreement between the two leaders over the diplomatic breakthrough with Tehran.

The context

Israel has long opposed any diplomatic accommodation with Iran, viewing Tehran's nuclear programme and regional proxies as existential threats. Netanyahu had previously pushed for maximum pressure and military options against Iran. Trump's willingness to publicly criticise Netanyahu signals that the US prioritised the deal over Israeli objections, a notable shift in the traditional Washington-Tel Aviv dynamic.

Why it matters

A durable US-Iran deal depends partly on whether Israel accepts or actively undermines it; Israeli military action against Iran could instantly unravel the agreement and send oil prices soaring. For India, which imports significant quantities of Gulf crude, the stability of this deal has direct consequences for fuel prices and inflation.

What's next

Expect Israel to clarify its position on the deal and watch for whether Netanyahu publicly accepts or contests Trump's characterisation of events.
