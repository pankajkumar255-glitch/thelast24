BBC Explains: How Trump's Iran Approach Differs Fundamentally from Obama's

What happened

BBC reports an explainer examining the differing approaches taken by Presidents Obama and Trump toward Iran's nuclear programme. The analysis highlights how Trump's current deal-making differs in method and framing from the 2015 Obama-era Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action, with Trump having previously exited that agreement in 2018.

The context

The 2015 JCPOA, negotiated under Obama with the P5+1 group, was designed to cap Iran's nuclear enrichment in exchange for sanctions relief. Trump withdrew the US from it in 2018, reimposing severe sanctions. Iran subsequently accelerated enrichment. A new Trump-Iran framework, if agreed, would be the third major diplomatic pivot on this issue in a decade.

Why it matters

A durable US-Iran deal could unlock Iranian oil exports and ease global crude prices, benefiting India significantly. A fragile or contested agreement, however, could unravel quickly and trigger fresh volatility.

What's next

Expect intense scrutiny from Congress, Israel, and Gulf states as details of any new Trump-Iran framework emerge.
