Israel Strikes Lebanon, Killing 5, Despite Active Ceasefire With Hezbollah

Key facts

  • At least 5 people killed in Israeli strikes on Lebanon
  • Ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah was still nominally in effect
  • Strikes risk collapsing the 2024-brokered truce

NDTV reports that Israeli forces struck Lebanese territory, killing at least 5 people, despite an active ceasefire agreement between Israel and Hezbollah. The strikes mark a serious breach of the truce that had halted months of devastating cross-border warfare between the two sides.

The ceasefire, brokered with US and French mediation in late 2024, was intended to halt Israeli operations in Lebanon and pull back Hezbollah forces from the border zone. Repeated Israeli strikes since the agreement came into force have drawn international criticism, with Lebanon accusing Israel of systematically violating the terms.

For India, the stability of Lebanon remains a direct concern. Tens of thousands of Indian nationals, many of them workers and nurses, are based in the country. A full collapse of the ceasefire would not only endanger them but could also destabilise the broader Middle East, affecting oil prices and remittance flows that matter to millions of Indian households.
