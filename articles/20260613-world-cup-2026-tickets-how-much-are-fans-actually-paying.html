World Cup 2026 Tickets: How Much Are Fans Actually Paying?

What happened

BBC reports on the range of prices fans are paying for FIFA World Cup 2026 tickets, highlighting significant cost variation depending on the match, the stage of the tournament and the host city. The report illustrates the financial commitment required to attend what will be the largest football World Cup in history. Secondary market prices for premium fixtures appear to be substantially above official face-value prices, reflecting strong global demand.

The context

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is a landmark tournament, being the first to feature 48 national teams rather than the previous 32, resulting in a larger number of matches spread across 16 cities in three countries. FIFA has introduced a tiered ticketing system, with prices varying by match category and seating area. Demand from global football fans has been exceptionally high since the expanded format was announced, and the tournament's North American host cities — including New York, Los Angeles, Dallas and Toronto — are among the world's most expensive destinations. India does not have a team in the tournament but has a large and passionate football fan base, particularly in West Bengal, Kerala and Goa.

Why it matters

For the growing number of Indian football fans who aspire to attend a World Cup, the pricing reality — combined with US visa uncertainty — makes 2026 a particularly challenging proposition. The story also reflects broader trends of sports event commercialisation, where ticket prices on secondary markets increasingly put live attendance out of reach for ordinary fans worldwide.

What's next

Expect ticket prices on the secondary market to rise further as the tournament draws closer and the official allocation sells out. Watch for FIFA and host nation governments to announce any fan support schemes or additional ticket releases for the general public.
