India cannot delay building AI sovereignty, analysts warn

What happened

Deccan Herald argues that India cannot afford to delay developing AI sovereignty, warning that the country's growing dependence on foreign AI platforms exposes it to geopolitical risk. The piece comes in the context of the US government restricting access to Anthropic's Claude model, demonstrating how quickly foreign governments can cut off AI access. India's own AI Mission and compute infrastructure investments are still in early stages relative to the scale of the challenge.

The context

India launched its National AI Mission in 2024 with Rs 10,371 crore in funding, aiming to build indigenous AI compute and model capabilities. However, most Indian enterprises and government departments still rely heavily on US-origin AI platforms such as those from OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic. The global race for AI dominance has made sovereign AI capability a strategic priority comparable to defence self-reliance.

Why it matters

India's dependence on foreign AI tools means that any geopolitical disruption — sanctions, export controls, or platform suspensions — could instantly paralyse government services, banking, and enterprise operations that run on those tools. Building sovereign AI is not just about national pride; it directly protects Indian jobs, data, and digital infrastructure from external shocks.

What's next

Expect the Indian government to accelerate indigenisation targets under the AI Mission, with pressure on domestic players like TCS, Infosys, and CDAC to fast-track homegrown large language models.
