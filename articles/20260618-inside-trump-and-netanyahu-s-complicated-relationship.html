Inside Trump and Netanyahu's Complicated Relationship

Key facts

  • Trump-Netanyahu relationship examined amid Gaza war and Iran deal
  • Trump's first term included moving US embassy to Jerusalem
  • Netanyahu congratulated Biden in 2020, straining ties with Trump
  • New US-Iran agreement adds fresh tension to Israel-US dynamic

Hindustan Times takes an in-depth look at the layered and at times contradictory relationship between US President Donald Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, two leaders who have projected a strong alliance but whose bond is more complicated beneath the surface.

Trump's first term saw landmark decisions in Israel's favour — recognising Jerusalem as Israel's capital, moving the US embassy there, and brokering the Abraham Accords. Yet Netanyahu reportedly frustrated Trump at key moments, including congratulating Joe Biden on his 2020 election win before Trump had conceded. The current context — an ongoing war in Gaza and the freshly signed US-Iran agreement — adds new stress to the relationship, as Israel views any deal with Tehran through the lens of existential security concern.

For India, the Trump-Netanyahu dynamic is not merely a geopolitical curiosity. The US stance on Iran, shaped partly by this relationship, affects global oil markets and India's energy import strategy. India also maintains strong ties with both Israel and Iran, and must navigate carefully as this relationship evolves.
