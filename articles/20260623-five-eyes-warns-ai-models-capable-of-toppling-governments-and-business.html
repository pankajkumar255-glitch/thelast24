Five Eyes warns AI models capable of toppling governments and businesses are months away

Key facts

  • Five Eyes: US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand issued joint warning
  • AI models capable of destabilising governments said to be months away
  • Rare public statement from five intelligence agencies simultaneously
  • Threat framed as near-term, not hypothetical

Five Eyes intelligence agencies — the security services of the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand — have issued a rare joint public warning that AI systems capable of taking down governments and major corporations are now only months away from deployment. The statement, published by The Guardian, represents one of the most alarming and concrete assessments of near-term AI risk ever made in a coordinated, official format by Western intelligence bodies.

The warning goes beyond the usual academic or think-tank caution about long-term AGI risk. The Five Eyes statement specifically identifies advanced frontier AI models as a near-term threat to institutional stability — suggesting that the capabilities in question are not speculative but are visible in current development pipelines being monitored by these agencies. The joint nature of the statement underscores that this is a shared, cross-border threat assessment, not the concern of a single nation.

For India, which is neither part of the Five Eyes alliance nor a significant producer of frontier AI models, the warning carries layered implications. Indian critical infrastructure, financial institutions, and democratic processes could be vulnerable to AI-enabled disruption originating from models developed and controlled elsewhere — a point that intersects sharply with the ongoing domestic debate about AI sovereignty.

The statement is expected to accelerate calls in multiple countries for tighter regulation of frontier AI development, mandatory safety evaluations, and international governance frameworks. India's own AI policy discussions — currently centred on the IndiaAI Mission — may face renewed pressure to address not just opportunity but existential risk in its roadmap.
