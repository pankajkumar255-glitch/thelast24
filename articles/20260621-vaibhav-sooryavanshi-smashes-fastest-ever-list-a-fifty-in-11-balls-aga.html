Vaibhav Sooryavanshi smashes fastest-ever List A fifty in 11 balls against Sri Lanka A

Key facts

  • Sooryavanshi hit a fifty off just 11 balls — fastest in List A cricket history
  • Innings came in the India A vs Sri Lanka A final
  • Sooryavanshi is 16 years old
  • Previous world record for fastest List A fifty broken

Vaibhav Sooryavanshi has rewritten cricket's record books, smashing a half-century off just 11 balls during the India A vs Sri Lanka A final to register the fastest fifty in List A cricket history. The 16-year-old's assault was breathtaking in its ferocity — a succession of boundaries and sixes that left Sri Lanka A bowlers with nowhere to hide and onlookers scrambling for the record books.

Sooryavanshi, who burst onto the national consciousness during the 2025 IPL season with similarly explosive knocks for Rajasthan Royals, has now confirmed that his power-hitting is no fluke confined to T20 cricket. Breaking the previous List A world record in a high-stakes final only amplifies the scale of the achievement — this was not a dead-rubber cameo but a defining innings under pressure.

The teenager's rise has been meteoric. A product of Bihar cricket and the BCCI's development pipeline, Sooryavanshi was already being spoken of as a future India opener. Saturday's innings will accelerate those conversations dramatically. The record invites comparison with the great hitters of the game, and Sooryavanshi is only at the beginning of his career.

For Indian cricket fans, this is a moment to savour. At a time when the sport is searching for the next generation of match-winners, a 16-year-old has answered with a world record. Selectors and coaches at every level will have taken careful note.
