Sensex surges 1,000 points as investors cheer Iran-US peace deal; RIL up 2%

What happened

India Today reports that the Sensex surged 1,000 points on June 15, 2026, driven by investor euphoria over an Iran-US peace deal. Reliance Industries rose 2% as markets priced in reduced energy supply risks and smoother trade through the Strait of Hormuz.

The context

India imports over 85% of its crude oil, making it acutely sensitive to West Asian geopolitical tensions. The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint through which a significant share of India's energy supplies pass, and any conflict premium on oil has direct knock-on effects on fuel prices, inflation, and the rupee.

Why it matters

A sustained rally could lift retail investor portfolios and boost business confidence, while lower oil prices would reduce India's import bill and ease pressure on the rupee and inflation. For ordinary Indians, this could eventually translate to more stable petrol and cooking gas prices.

What's next

Expect markets to watch for formal confirmation and implementation details of the Iran-US deal, with sustained gains likely only if crude oil prices fall meaningfully in the coming sessions.
