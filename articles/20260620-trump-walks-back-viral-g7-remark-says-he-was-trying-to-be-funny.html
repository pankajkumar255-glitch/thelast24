Trump Walks Back Viral G7 Remark, Says He Was 'Trying to Be Funny'

Key facts

  • Trump said he was 'trying to be funny, not the boss' about a G7 remark
  • The original comment had gone viral internationally
  • Clarification aimed at reducing diplomatic fallout from the statement

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump sought to walk back a viral remark made at the G7 summit, telling reporters he was "trying to be funny, not the boss" — framing his earlier comment as a joke rather than a declaration of dominance over allied leaders. The original remark had spread widely on social media, prompting considerable international scrutiny.

Trump has frequently used the G7 as a stage for high-profile friction with allies, including past disputes over trade tariffs, NATO contributions, and climate commitments. His relationship with G7 partners has been characterised by unpredictability, making any statement at the summit subject to intense parsing by diplomats and markets worldwide.

For India, the temperature inside G7 has practical consequences. The bloc's cohesion — or lack of it — influences the shape of global trade rules, sanctions regimes, and technology-sharing arrangements that directly affect Indian industry and diplomacy. How the US conducts itself with allies also signals to New Delhi the reliability of Washington as a strategic partner.
