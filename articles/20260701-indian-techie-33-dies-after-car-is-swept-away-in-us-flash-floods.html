Indian Techie, 33, Dies After Car Is Swept Away In US Flash Floods

Key facts

  • Victim was a 33-year-old Indian technology professional
  • Car was swept away in flash floods in the US
  • Flash flooding is a leading cause of weather-related deaths in the US

A 33-year-old Indian technology professional has died in the United States after the car they were travelling in was swept away by flash floodwaters, NDTV reported. The incident highlights the deadly power of flash floods, which can engulf vehicles within seconds and leave little opportunity for escape, even for alert drivers.

Flash flooding has become an increasingly frequent and deadly hazard across parts of the United States, with extreme rainfall events intensifying in frequency and severity. Cars caught in floodwaters are among the most common causes of flood-related fatalities, as water levels can rise faster than drivers can react, and the force of moving water is sufficient to carry away even heavy vehicles.

The victim was among India's large and growing community of technology workers in the US — a cohort of hundreds of thousands of young professionals who have made America home, primarily in technology hubs. The death will resonate deeply within that community and among families in India who send their children abroad in pursuit of careers in the global tech industry.

The incident comes as Indian families across the country keep a close watch on news of their relatives abroad, and as weather-related risks in the US increasingly claim the lives of young Indian expatriates in circumstances that feel sudden and preventable.
