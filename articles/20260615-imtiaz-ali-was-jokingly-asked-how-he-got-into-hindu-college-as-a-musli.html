Imtiaz Ali was 'jokingly' asked how he got into Hindu College as a Muslim

What happened

The Indian Express reports that filmmaker Imtiaz Ali shared an anecdote in which he was jokingly asked how he, being Muslim, secured admission to Hindu College in Delhi. The comment, framed as humour, has drawn wider attention for what it reveals about assumptions around religious identity in institutional spaces.

The context

Hindu College is one of Delhi University's most prestigious institutions and has a diverse student body despite its name, which refers to its founding community rather than a religious restriction. The anecdote comes as Imtiaz Ali is in the public eye for his new film Main Vaapas Aaunga, and it touches on longstanding conversations about Muslim identity in Hindu-majority public spaces in India.

Why it matters

When a prominent public figure shares an experience of casual religious profiling, it amplifies a conversation about the everyday experiences of minorities in Indian institutions that rarely makes headlines.

What's next

Expect the anecdote to fuel further commentary on social media and in op-ed columns about religious identity and belonging in Indian educational and cultural spaces.
