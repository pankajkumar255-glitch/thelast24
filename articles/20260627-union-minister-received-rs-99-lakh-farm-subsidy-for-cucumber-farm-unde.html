Union Minister Received Rs 99 Lakh Farm Subsidy for Cucumber Farm Under Ministry's Own Scheme

Key facts

  • Union Minister received Rs 99 lakh subsidy for cucumber farm
  • IAS officer's family also among farm subsidy beneficiaries
  • Scheme is administered by the Centre's Agriculture Ministry
  • Raises conflict-of-interest concerns about subsidy allocation

A Union Minister personally received a subsidy of Rs 99 lakh under a Central government farm scheme for a cucumber farm, The Indian Express has reported, in a disclosure that raises immediate questions about conflict of interest at the highest levels of government.

The minister's family is not the only politically connected beneficiary flagged. The report also identifies the family of an IAS officer among those who benefited from the Centre's farm subsidy scheme — a programme ostensibly designed to support agricultural activity and incentivise horticulture. The identities of the minister and the IAS officer were reported in the briefing, marking a significant accountability story.

The revelations come amid longstanding concerns about the targeting of agricultural subsidies in India. Critics of such programmes have long argued that well-connected landowners and politically influential families are better positioned to navigate the paperwork and approval processes, effectively diverting funds intended for marginal and small farmers.

The Ministry of Agriculture, which administers the scheme, is yet to respond publicly to the disclosures. The report is likely to draw parliamentary scrutiny and calls for a review of how beneficiaries for the scheme are selected and verified, particularly when applicants have direct or indirect influence over the scheme's administrative apparatus.
