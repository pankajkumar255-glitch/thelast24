Marco Rubio Says US Is 'Hoping' for Trump Visit to India 'Early Next Year'

Key facts

  • Marco Rubio says US is 'hoping' Trump visits India 'early next year'
  • No formal confirmation of the visit yet — Rubio used 'we're hoping'
  • A Trump India visit would follow Modi's Washington trip earlier in the term
  • Comes amid near-complete India-US trade deal negotiations

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio has publicly indicated that Washington hopes President Donald Trump will visit India early next year, NDTV reported, providing the first concrete timeframe attached to speculation about a potential Trump state visit to New Delhi. Rubio's use of 'we're hoping' signals that a visit is under active discussion, though not yet formally confirmed.

A Trump visit to India would be a high-stakes diplomatic moment. State visits by sitting US presidents to India are rare and consequential events — they typically anchor major agreements on trade, defence, and technology cooperation, and send strong signals about the strategic direction of the bilateral relationship. Prime Minister Narendra Modi visited Washington earlier in the Trump term, and a reciprocal visit would round out the personal diplomatic exchange.

The timing of Rubio's statement is significant. It comes as India and the US are described as being close to finalising a bilateral trade deal, with the US envoy calling only a 'handful of issues' remaining. A Trump visit in early 2026 would provide the ideal backdrop — and deadline — for completing those negotiations and announcing outcomes.

For India, the prospect of hosting Trump also carries domestic political significance, underscoring Modi's standing as a global statesman capable of cultivating relationships with major world leaders. The 'Howdy Modi' and 'Namaste Trump' events during Trump's first term set high-profile precedents that both sides may seek to replicate.
