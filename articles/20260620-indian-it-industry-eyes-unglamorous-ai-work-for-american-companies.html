Indian IT industry eyes 'unglamorous' AI work for American companies

Key facts

  • Indian IT firms targeting data labelling, model fine-tuning, AI ops work
  • Work is for American tech companies seeking to offshore AI pipeline tasks
  • Strategy mirrors India's earlier capture of global software outsourcing
  • Leverages India's large English-speaking, process-skilled workforce

Scroll.in reports that the Indian IT industry is actively seeking to capture what it calls 'unglamorous' AI work from American technology companies — the essential but less celebrated tasks that power modern AI systems, including data annotation, model training support, quality evaluation, and AI operations management.

This work, while not headline-grabbing, is enormous in scale and aligns well with India's established strengths in large-scale outsourcing, process management, and a vast English-proficient workforce. Indian IT majors and mid-tier firms alike are reportedly pitching themselves to US clients as reliable partners for this layer of the AI pipeline.

The move reflects a pragmatic strategy: rather than competing with Silicon Valley on cutting-edge AI research, India's IT industry is doubling down on execution-layer work — the kind of high-volume, process-intensive tasks that American companies are eager to offshore. This mirrors how India captured the global software services boom of the 1990s and 2000s.

For everyday Indians working in or entering the IT sector, this represents a real and near-term employment opportunity in the AI economy, offering a bridge between current skills and the demands of an AI-driven world.
