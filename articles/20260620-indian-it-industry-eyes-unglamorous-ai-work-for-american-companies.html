Indian IT Industry Eyes 'Unglamorous' AI Work for American Companies

Key facts

  • Indian IT industry targeting AI data labelling, model training, and testing work for US firms
  • Sector employs over five million people in India
  • India's cost advantage and outsourcing scale cited as key strengths
  • Opportunity driven by generative AI adoption surge among American companies

Scroll.in reports that India's IT industry is making a deliberate push to capture the back-end, infrastructure-heavy work that powers artificial intelligence for American corporations — tasks such as data annotation, model training, AI testing, and system maintenance that are essential but rarely in the spotlight. Industry voices describe this as 'unglamorous' AI work, yet argue it represents a massive, durable opportunity that aligns well with India's established outsourcing capabilities.

The pitch is that India's IT sector — already a dominant force in global technology services — can replicate its decades-long success in business process and software outsourcing by pivoting large parts of its workforce toward AI-enabling tasks. Competitive labour costs, scale, and a large English-proficient talent pool are cited as India's core advantages in this race.

The stakes are significant: with generative AI transforming how American companies operate, the volume of supporting work required is expected to grow sharply. For India's IT workforce of over five million, success in this segment could translate into sustained hiring and wage growth, particularly in tech hubs like Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune, even as traditional software services face disruption from automation.
