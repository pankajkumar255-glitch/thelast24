Mercedes-Benz Sees India Playing a Bigger Role in Software and Sourcing

Key facts

  • Mercedes-Benz expanding India's role in software development and sourcing
  • Part of broader shift to software-defined vehicle strategy
  • India already hosts Mercedes engineering and IT operations

Bloomberg reports that Mercedes-Benz sees India playing a significantly larger role in its global software development and sourcing operations. The German luxury automaker, which already maintains engineering and technology centres in India, is looking to deepen its presence as it accelerates a shift toward software-defined vehicles and diversifies its supply chains.

The move reflects a wider trend among global automakers: as cars become increasingly software-driven, technology talent hubs like India — with large pools of engineers and relatively competitive costs — are becoming central to product development strategies, not just back-office support.

For Indian readers, this matters on two levels. First, it signals the potential for more premium engineering and software jobs in a sector that has traditionally been dominated by services outsourcing. Second, it is part of a broader pattern of multinationals — from Apple suppliers to semiconductor firms — steadily raising India's role in global value chains, with real implications for industrial growth and employment.
