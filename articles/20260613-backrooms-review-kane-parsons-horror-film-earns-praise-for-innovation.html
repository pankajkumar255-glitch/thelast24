Backrooms review: Kane Parsons' horror film earns praise for innovation

What happened

The Indian Express has published a review of The Backrooms, directed by Kane Parsons, calling it an innovative horror film and praising its ability to meet audience expectations smartly. The review credits Parsons with bringing originality to the genre. The Backrooms is based on a widely known internet horror mythology involving endless, liminal office-like spaces. The film has been released and is available for Indian audiences to access, making the review timely guidance for viewers considering it.

The context

The Backrooms originated as an internet creepypasta — a piece of user-generated horror fiction — that described an unsettling, infinite maze of yellow-wallpapered rooms as a kind of psychological terror. The concept went massively viral on platforms like Reddit and YouTube, spawning countless fan films, the most notable of which were made by then-teenager Kane Parsons, whose short films accumulated millions of views. A24, the prestigious American independent film studio known for elevated horror, later backed Parsons to develop a full feature film, an extraordinary leap for a self-taught teenage filmmaker. The project attracted significant attention as an example of social-media talent being fast-tracked into professional Hollywood production.

Why it matters

For Indian horror fans and filmmakers, The Backrooms represents a case study in how internet-native storytelling can translate into commercially and critically viable cinema. A positive review from a major Indian publication will likely drive streaming viewership among the significant Indian horror audience. The film also raises interesting questions about whether Indian OTT platforms and studios might similarly scout and develop internet-native talent.

What's next

Expect The Backrooms to perform strongly on whichever streaming platform carries it in India following this critical endorsement. It is likely that Parsons' success will be cited as a model in Indian film industry conversations about discovering new directorial talent from online spaces.
