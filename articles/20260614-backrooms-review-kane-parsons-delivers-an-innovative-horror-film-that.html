Backrooms Review: Kane Parsons Delivers an Innovative Horror Film That Knows Its Audience

What happened

The Indian Express has published a review of The Backrooms, directed by Kane Parsons, calling it an innovative horror film. The review characterises the film as one that successfully delivers on the expectations of its target audience. Parsons is a young American filmmaker who became an internet sensation through his short Backrooms horror videos on YouTube, which collectively garnered massive global viewership including in India. The film represents a full-length feature adaptation of the viral Backrooms concept, which originated as internet folklore. Specific details about the film's cast, release platform, or Indian distributor were not provided in the headline.

The context

The Backrooms is a creepypasta internet mythology that originated on 4chan in 2019, describing an unsettling liminal space of endless yellow-carpeted rooms that people can supposedly fall into. Kane Parsons, known online as Kane Pixels, adapted this concept into a series of found-footage horror short films on YouTube that went massively viral globally, earning hundreds of millions of views. The films were notable for their technical sophistication despite being made by a then-teenage creator, and they sparked widespread internet discussion and fan creativity. A24, known for prestige horror films including Hereditary, Midsommar, and Everything Everywhere All at Once, acquired the project, lending it significant industry credibility. The transition from viral online content to feature films is an increasingly common pathway in contemporary cinema.

Why it matters

For Indian horror fans and cinephiles, The Backrooms represents the arrival of a genuinely new kind of filmmaker — one whose creative credibility was built entirely on the internet before any studio involvement. The film's success or failure will influence how the industry approaches other creator-to-cinema transitions. It also speaks to the globalisation of horror as a genre, with Indian audiences increasingly engaging with international horror content through OTT and theatrical releases.

What's next

Expect the film to build word-of-mouth among Indian horror audiences online, given the Backrooms mythology's strong existing following in India. A strong critical reception could encourage wider theatrical or streaming distribution in the Indian market.
