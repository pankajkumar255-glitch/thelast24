Zhipu Surges 33% as Wall Street Raises Bets on China AI After Anthropic Curbs

What happened

CNBC reports that Zhipu, a Chinese AI company, surged 33% as Wall Street investors raised their bets on China AI stocks following the US government's imposition of curbs on Anthropic's models. The market reaction reflects investor expectations that restrictions on US AI could open doors for Chinese AI providers in global markets. The move is part of a broader re-rating of Chinese AI companies by international investors.

The context

Zhipu is one of China's leading large language model developers, backed by significant state and private investment as part of Beijing's push to become a global AI power. The US has been progressively restricting the export and access of advanced AI technologies to adversaries and, increasingly, to broader markets. China's AI industry, which includes companies like Baidu, Alibaba and Zhipu, has been positioning itself as an alternative ecosystem.

Why it matters

A stronger Chinese AI industry, buoyed by US restrictions, could accelerate the bifurcation of the global AI market into US and China-led blocs, forcing Indian companies and the government to pick sides. Indian startups and enterprises that have built on US AI APIs may find their options narrowing.

What's next

Expect more capital to flow into Chinese AI stocks and partnerships if the US continues to widen access restrictions, intensifying pressure on India to develop its own sovereign AI alternatives.
