Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata earns just Rs 4.25 crore in 3 days — less than half of Kangana's Emergency

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata, starring Kangana Ranaut, earned Rs 4.25 crore in its first three days at the box office. The paper notes this is less than half the opening-three-day collection of Kangana's previous film, Emergency. The numbers mark a significant underperformance for the film.

The context

Kangana Ranaut has faced a difficult run at the box office in recent years, with several of her films — including Tejas and Emergency — failing to find large audiences despite high-profile releases. Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata continues that trend, raising questions about the commercial viability of her recent projects.

Why it matters

Weak box-office numbers influence how distributors and multiplex chains schedule and promote Bollywood films, ultimately shaping what content gets greenlit and funded. For audiences, a struggling star vehicle can signal broader shifts in what Indian viewers are willing to pay to watch in cinemas.

What's next

Expect the film's theatrical run to be cut short as multiplexes likely reduce screen count in the coming days if collections do not pick up over the weekend.
