OpenAI Unveils Sol, Terra and Luna AI Models — US Government Blocks Wide Release

Key facts

  • OpenAI announces three new AI models: Sol, Terra and Luna
  • US government blocks wide public release of the models
  • OpenAI reportedly briefed US authorities before release due to models' power
  • Restricted rollout rather than open availability

OpenAI has unveiled three new artificial intelligence models — Sol, Terra and Luna — but the US government has moved to block their wide release, India Today reported on Thursday. The announcement marks one of the more striking instances of Washington exercising direct control over the public deployment of frontier AI, intervening before the models could reach a broad user base.

The development comes amid a broader moment of reckoning in the US over how to handle increasingly powerful AI systems. NDTV earlier reported that OpenAI's new AI was considered so powerful that the company had to brief the US government before its release — suggesting the Sol, Terra and Luna models, or a closely related system, triggered heightened regulatory attention. The government's decision to block wide release while permitting a limited or staged rollout reflects the emerging framework Washington is applying to the most capable AI systems.

For OpenAI, the intervention is a significant moment. The company has long maintained a complex relationship with policymakers, positioning itself as a safety-conscious developer while racing to the frontier of AI capability. Having the government constrain a product launch — even partially — represents a new kind of check on the industry's pace.

For users and developers globally, including in India, the practical consequence is a delay in accessing what may be among the most capable AI models yet released. The extent of the restrictions, and the timeline for any broader rollout, remained unclear as of Thursday.
