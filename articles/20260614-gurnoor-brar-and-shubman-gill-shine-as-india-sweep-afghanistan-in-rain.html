Gurnoor Brar and Shubman Gill shine as India sweep Afghanistan in rain-hit Dharamsala ODI

What happened

India Today reports that India swept Afghanistan in the first ODI of their series at Dharamsala, with the match reduced by rain. Pacer Gurnoor Brar was the standout bowler, generating sharp pace to trouble the Afghan batting lineup, while Shubman Gill anchored the chase with a fluent innings. ESPNcricinfo's match report also confirms India's win in the rain-hit fixture. The result gives India a commanding 1-0 lead in the series.

The context

India regularly host Afghanistan for bilateral ODI series as Afghanistan continues to develop as a Test and ODI nation. Dharamsala's high-altitude pitch is known to assist fast bowlers, making it a favourable venue for a pacer like Gurnoor Brar. Shubman Gill has been one of India's most consistent ODI batters in recent years and is widely seen as a central figure in India's white-ball future. Afghanistan have improved considerably as a side and have caused upsets against top nations in recent years.

Why it matters

A clean series win would boost India's ODI rankings and provide valuable preparation time for upcoming global tournaments. The emergence of Gurnoor Brar as an effective pace option gives the selectors more firepower to consider. For fans, a dominant home win is an encouraging sign of the team's depth and bench strength.

What's next

Expect India to look for a series sweep in the remaining ODIs, with selectors likely monitoring fringe players for form and fitness. Gurnoor Brar's performances will be closely watched to see if he can cement a regular spot in the side.
