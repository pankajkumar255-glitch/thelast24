India's startups surge from a few hundred in 2016 to 2 lakh in 2025

What happened

NDTV reports that India's registered startup count has risen from a few hundred in 2016 to 2 lakh in 2025, tracking nearly a decade of growth in the country's entrepreneurship ecosystem. The data point is being cited at Bharat Innovates 2026, a summit focused on India's technology and innovation ambitions. The figure covers DPIIT-recognised startups across sectors.

The context

India's Startup India initiative, launched in January 2016, kickstarted systematic government recognition and support for new ventures. Over the following years, the combination of cheap data, smartphone penetration, UPI, and a maturing venture capital market created fertile ground. India is now home to over 100 unicorns, making it the third-largest startup ecosystem after the US and China.

Why it matters

The 2-lakh milestone signals that entrepreneurship is no longer confined to metros — startups from smaller cities are increasingly contributing to job creation, innovation, and export earnings across sectors from agritech to deeptech.

What's next

Expect the government to announce further policy measures at Bharat Innovates 2026 aimed at pushing startup quality metrics — profitability, exports, and deep-tech focus — alongside raw count growth.
