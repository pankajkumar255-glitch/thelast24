England name 17-man T20I squad for India series; Harry Brook leads, James Coles gets maiden call-up

Key facts

  • Harry Brook named England T20I captain for India series
  • James Coles — uncapped Sussex all-rounder — gets maiden call-up
  • England squad is 17 members for the T20I series
  • Series is part of England's tour of India in 2026

England have named a 17-man squad for the upcoming T20I series against India, with Harry Brook captaining the side and uncapped Sussex all-rounder James Coles earning his first senior call-up. Coles, a right-handed bat and right-arm seam-bowling all-rounder, has been rewarded for consistent county performances that marked him as one of English cricket's most promising young talents.

Brook's appointment as T20I captain underlines England's succession planning following the end of Jos Buttler's white-ball era. The 17-member squad blends experienced campaigners with emerging names, a hallmark of England's 'Bazball' philosophy that has extended from Tests into their white-ball setup under new management.

The England squad announcement sets the stage for what promises to be one of the marquee bilateral T20I series of 2026. India, reigning T20 World Cup champions, will be formidable hosts, and England's selectors have opted for depth and flexibility in a large squad that gives the touring side options across formats and conditions.

Coles' inclusion is the story that will draw the most scrutiny. Uncapped players called into squads for India tours face an immediate examination under intense pressure, and how England manage his introduction — whether as a finisher, a middle-over option, or both — will be keenly watched. The full series schedule and match dates are expected to be confirmed shortly by the respective boards.
