JPMorgan Chase Blocks Anthropic AI Access for Hong Kong Employees, Following Goldman Sachs

Key facts

  • JPMorgan Chase blocked Anthropic access for Hong Kong employees
  • Goldman Sachs enacted a similar restriction previously
  • Anthropic known for its Claude AI models
  • Data security and Hong Kong regulations cited as drivers

NDTV reports that JPMorgan Chase has blocked its Hong Kong employees from accessing Anthropic's AI assistant, following a near-identical move by Goldman Sachs. The back-to-back restrictions from two of Wall Street's most powerful institutions point to deepening concerns about data security, regulatory compliance, and the use of third-party AI tools in tightly governed financial environments.

Hong Kong operates under a distinct legal and regulatory framework, and financial firms there face specific obligations around data handling and cross-border information flows that complicate the use of external generative AI platforms. Anthropic, the AI safety company backed by Google and Amazon and known for its Claude models, has been positioning itself as an enterprise-grade AI provider, making the blocks a reputational and commercial challenge.

For India, where large domestic banks and regulators are beginning to formulate their own guardrails around AI deployment in finance, the decisions by JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs offer an early signal of how compliance demands may override the push for AI productivity gains — a tension that Indian financial institutions are likely to face sooner rather than later.
