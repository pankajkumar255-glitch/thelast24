US envoy Sergio Gor says Trump's H-1B visa changes 'not targeted at India'

Key facts

  • US envoy Sergio Gor says H-1B changes are not targeted at India
  • H-1B is the primary US work visa for Indian tech professionals
  • India is the largest source of H-1B visa petitions annually
  • Changes being made as part of broader US immigration reform

US envoy Sergio Gor has sought to reassure India that the Trump administration's modifications to the H-1B visa programme are not aimed at Indian applicants or workers, telling The Times of India that the changes are not "targeted at India." The statement comes as Indian technology workers and students monitoring the US immigration landscape have grown increasingly anxious about the direction of American visa policy under the current administration.

The H-1B visa is the single most important immigration pathway for Indian professionals, particularly those in the information technology, engineering, and finance sectors. India consistently accounts for the largest share of H-1B petitions filed each year, meaning that any structural changes to eligibility, wage requirements, or processing rules hit the Indian diaspora and IT sector more acutely than any other nationality.

The Trump administration has taken a more restrictive posture on immigration broadly, with changes announced or under consideration that could affect renewal timelines, dependent visa rights, and the selection lottery. Indian IT firms — both domestic companies with large US operations and multinational employers — have been closely tracking these developments, as H-1B restrictions could force costly restructuring of their US workforce models.

Gor's public defence of the policy as non-discriminatory is diplomatically significant, particularly given the warm tone US officials have adopted toward India in recent weeks. Whether the assurances translate into concrete policy relief for Indian H-1B holders remains to be seen.
