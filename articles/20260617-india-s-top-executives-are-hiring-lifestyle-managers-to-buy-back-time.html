India's top executives are hiring lifestyle managers to buy back time

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that startup founders and CXOs in India are increasingly turning to lifestyle managers — personal aides who manage non-work tasks including travel, scheduling, household needs, and wellness — to reclaim productive time. The trend is accelerating in cities like Bengaluru, Mumbai, and Delhi, with several startups and agencies now offering these services professionally. Demand is reportedly rising fast among executives at growth-stage companies.

The context

India's startup and corporate leadership ecosystem has grown significantly over the past decade, creating a layer of time-poor, cash-rich executives. Similar trends emerged earlier in the US and UK, where personal concierge and chief-of-staff services became mainstream for the executive class. In India, rising disposable incomes among senior professionals and the growth of the gig economy are making such services economically viable for both supply and demand.

Why it matters

The trend is quietly seeding a new urban service economy — lifestyle management roles are emerging as a viable, well-paying career path in metros, while also reflecting how the definition of executive productivity is changing in India's fast-moving startup culture.

What's next

Expect specialised lifestyle management startups to raise venture funding and expand beyond the top four metros as the addressable market of high-earning Indian executives continues to grow.
