Why Agent Vinod remains Sriram Raghavan's most fascinating failure

Key facts

  • Agent Vinod (2012) directed by Sriram Raghavan, starring Saif Ali Khan
  • Film underperformed critically and commercially despite strong ambition
  • Raghavan later directed Andhadhun (2018), a major critical success
  • Indian Express retrospective examines the film's structural and tonal shortcomings

Twelve years on, 'Agent Vinod' continues to fascinate — not for what it achieved, but for the gap between its ambition and its execution. Sriram Raghavan's 2012 spy thriller, starring Saif Ali Khan as a RAW agent navigating a globe-trotting mission, was a project of considerable scale and stylistic intention, yet it underperformed both critically and commercially in a way that still invites analysis.

The Indian Express takes a deep dive into why the film qualifies as Raghavan's 'most fascinating failure' — a phrase that itself says much. Raghavan, who had already demonstrated his noir instincts with 'Ek Haseen Thi' and would later deliver the near-universally acclaimed 'Andhadhun' (2018) and 'Merry Christmas' (2024), is a filmmaker with a clear and distinctive sensibility. That makes 'Agent Vinod' interesting precisely because the ingredients were present — a charismatic lead, an international canvas, genre affection — and yet the whole did not cohere.

The retrospective points to structural and tonal issues that plagued the film: a runtime that stretched its narrative thin, a Cold War-era spy aesthetic that felt more referential than alive, and a central performance that struggled to anchor the episodic plotting. Kareena Kapoor Khan co-starred in the film, and Pritam's music — particularly the title track — became arguably more enduring than the film itself.

For students of Indian cinema, the piece is a reminder that a director's journey is rarely linear. Raghavan's subsequent work suggests he absorbed the lessons of 'Agent Vinod' well — and that understanding a filmmaker's failures is often the most honest way to appreciate their eventual mastery.
