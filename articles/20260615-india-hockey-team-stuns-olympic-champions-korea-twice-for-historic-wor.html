India hockey team stuns Olympic champions Korea twice for historic World Cup double

What happened

Business Standard reports that India stunned Olympic champions Korea twice in the Hockey World Cup, describing the result as historic. The headline characterises both victories as a 'World Cup double', suggesting consecutive wins against Korea in the tournament.

The context

South Korea is one of Asia's premier hockey nations and a reigning Olympic champion, making any victory over them significant. Indian hockey has been rebuilding its global standing and a double win in a World Cup setting would rank among the team's greatest recent achievements.

Why it matters

Beating Olympic champions twice in a single World Cup boosts India's chances of advancing deep into the tournament, while also inspiring a new generation of hockey players across the country. It reinforces investment in Indian hockey infrastructure and could draw greater public and sponsorship attention to the sport.

What's next

Expect India to carry strong momentum into the knockout rounds of the Hockey World Cup, with the team likely emerging as a medal favourite.
