Smart Cities Mission Turns 10: How Technology Is Transforming Urban India

What happened

The Times of India reports on the 10th anniversary of the Smart Cities Mission, examining the role technology has played in transforming urban governance and infrastructure across India's designated smart cities over the past decade.

The context

The Smart Cities Mission was launched in June 2015 under the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, selecting 100 cities for targeted infrastructure upgrades funded jointly by central and state governments. The programme focused on area-based development and pan-city solutions using technology, including integrated traffic management, smart meters, and digital citizen grievance systems. It has been both celebrated for visible urban improvements and criticised for uneven implementation.

Why it matters

Urban infrastructure quality directly affects the daily lives of hundreds of millions of Indians — from commute times and water supply to civic transparency. The mission's progress (or shortfalls) will shape how India plans its next generation of urban development.

What's next

Expect a policy review and possible announcement of a successor urban technology programme as the mission formally concludes its current phase.
