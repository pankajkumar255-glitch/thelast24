Anthropic Halts Access to New AI Models After US Order — Indian Tech Leaders Respond

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Anthropic has suspended access to its new AI models following a US government order. Indian tech leaders have begun publicly responding to the halt, highlighting concerns about supply-chain dependency on American AI platforms. The report focuses on the immediate operational and strategic impact on the Indian technology sector.

The context

Anthropic, the US-based AI safety company backed by Amazon and Google, is one of the leading providers of large language models, including the widely used Claude series. Indian startups, enterprises, and developers have increasingly integrated Anthropic's models into their products. US export-control and national-security orders can restrict access to American AI tools for foreign users, a risk that has now materialised.

Why it matters

Indian companies relying on Anthropic's models for customer service, coding assistance, or content generation may face immediate service disruptions or be forced into costly and time-consuming migrations to alternative platforms. The episode underlines the broader risk of AI dependency on foreign vendors subject to geopolitical decisions beyond India's control.

What's next

Expect Indian tech firms and policymakers to accelerate conversations around sovereign AI infrastructure and diversifying AI model providers away from US-only platforms.
