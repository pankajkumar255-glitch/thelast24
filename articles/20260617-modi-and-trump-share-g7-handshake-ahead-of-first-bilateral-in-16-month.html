Modi and Trump Share G7 Handshake Ahead of First Bilateral in 16 Months

What happened

NDTV reports that PM Modi and President Trump exchanged a handshake at the G7 summit ahead of a formal bilateral meeting, the first between the two leaders in 16 months. The encounter marks a significant diplomatic moment as both countries have been navigating trade tensions and strategic realignments.

The context

India and the US share one of the world's most consequential bilateral relationships, spanning defence, technology, trade, and the Indian diaspora. Trump's return to the presidency has introduced fresh uncertainty around tariffs on Indian exports and H-1B visa policies, making this face-to-face meeting particularly significant.

Why it matters

Outcomes from this bilateral could directly affect Indian exporters facing US tariff pressure and thousands of Indian professionals on H-1B visas. It also sets the tone for the India-US strategic partnership going into the second half of 2025.

What's next

Expect official readouts from both sides on trade, defence, and immigration that could signal concrete policy shifts in the weeks ahead.
