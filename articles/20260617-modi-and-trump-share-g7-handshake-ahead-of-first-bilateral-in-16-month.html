Modi and Trump Share G7 Handshake Ahead of First Bilateral in 16 Months

What happened

NDTV reports that Prime Minister Narendra Modi and President Donald Trump met at the G7 summit, with their handshake captured ahead of a formal bilateral discussion. This is described as their first direct meeting in 16 months. The encounter takes place amid ongoing tensions over US tariffs on Indian goods and the broader realignment of global alliances.

The context

The G7 summit has brought together leaders of the world's major democracies at a moment of significant geopolitical flux — from the US-Iran nuclear agreement to Middle East conflicts. India, though not a G7 member, is frequently invited as a partner nation. Modi and Trump previously met during Trump's first term and during Modi's US state visit, but relations have been tested by tariff disputes and differing positions on Iran.

Why it matters

Any outcomes from this bilateral could directly affect the US tariffs currently hitting Indian exports, with implications for Indian industry and jobs. Strategic alignment between India and the US also shapes India's security environment, from the Indo-Pacific to its western borders.

What's next

Expect official statements from both governments on trade, defence cooperation, and regional security in the hours following the bilateral.
