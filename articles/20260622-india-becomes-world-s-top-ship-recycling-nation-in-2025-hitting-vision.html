India becomes world's top ship recycling nation in 2025, hitting Vision 2030 target five years early

Key facts

  • India ranked world's No. 1 ship recycling nation in 2025
  • Vision 2030 maritime target achieved five years ahead of schedule
  • Alang yards in Gujarat are the world's largest ship-breaking facility
  • Industry supplies significant steel scrap to India's domestic market

India has emerged as the world's leading ship recycling nation in 2025, surpassing Bangladesh, Turkey, and Pakistan to claim the top position — and doing so five years before its own Vision 2030 target had envisaged. The announcement, reported by Mint, marks a transformative moment for India's maritime and steel industries.

The bulk of India's ship recycling activity is concentrated at the Alang yards in Gujarat, which stretch along roughly 10 kilometres of coastline and are already the largest ship-breaking facility on the planet by area. In recent years, Indian operators have invested in upgrading environmental compliance, worker safety standards, and recycling efficiency in response to international regulations — including the Hong Kong Convention on ship recycling — which has helped India win business from shipowners who require certified, responsible dismantling.

The economic significance is substantial. Ship recycling generates large volumes of recovered steel scrap, which feeds directly into India's secondary steel production chain and reduces dependence on iron ore and imported scrap. It also supports hundreds of thousands of direct and indirect jobs along the Gujarat coast.

Achieving the Vision 2030 milestone by 2025 reflects a confluence of favourable factors: a global shipping supercycle that has now produced a wave of ageing vessels ready for retirement, India's competitive cost base, and improving regulatory credibility. The government is expected to use the milestone to advocate for India's inclusion in higher-tier international recycling certification frameworks, potentially opening up an even larger share of the global market.
