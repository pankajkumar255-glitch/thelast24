India's Startups Grew from a Few Hundred in 2016 to 2 Lakh in 2025

Key facts

  • Startups grew from a few hundred (2016) to 2 lakh (2025)
  • Growth driven by Startup India programme and digital infrastructure
  • India has produced 100+ unicorns in this period

NDTV reports that India's startup ecosystem has grown from a few hundred registered entities in 2016 to 2 lakh — that is 200,000 — by 2025, charting a remarkable decade of entrepreneurial expansion. The milestone reflects the combined impact of the government's Startup India initiative, easier access to digital payments infrastructure, and growing pools of domestic and foreign venture capital.

The journey from 2016 also coincides with the rapid spread of affordable smartphones and cheap mobile data, which lowered the barrier to building and scaling digital businesses. India has produced over 100 unicorns — startups valued at $1 billion or more — during this period, across sectors from fintech and edtech to health tech and logistics.

For everyday Indians, the 2-lakh milestone matters because startups have become a primary creator of new-age employment, particularly for young graduates. The spread of entrepreneurship beyond Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru into smaller cities also signals a broader democratisation of economic opportunity across the country.
