OpenAI, Anthropic and Google joined Trump and world leaders at G7 — 'a signal of where power sits'

Key facts

  • OpenAI, Anthropic, Google executives present at G7 alongside world leaders
  • Described as 'a signal of where power sits' by observers
  • G7 summit increasingly focused on AI governance and access
  • Unprecedented presence of private AI firms at heads-of-state forum

CNBC reports that executives from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google were present at the G7 summit alongside US President Donald Trump and other world leaders, an unprecedented blending of state power and corporate AI interests at the world's most exclusive diplomatic forum. The report quotes observers describing this as 'a signal of where power sits', reflecting how much geopolitical weight AI companies have accumulated.

The G7 — comprising the US, UK, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, and Japan — has increasingly turned its agenda toward AI governance, export controls, and the question of which countries get access to frontier models. Having the makers of the world's most powerful AI systems in the room effectively gives them a direct hand in shaping those rules.

For India, which has been building its own AI ecosystem and is in active technology partnership talks with the US, the composition of the G7 AI discussions is significant. The decisions made by this small circle of governments and companies will determine the global framework within which Indian AI policy, access, and competitiveness will have to operate.
