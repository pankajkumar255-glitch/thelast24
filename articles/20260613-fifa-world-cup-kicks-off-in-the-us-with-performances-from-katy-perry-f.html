FIFA World Cup kicks off in the US with performances from Katy Perry, Future and Tyla

What happened

BBC reports that the FIFA World Cup 2026 has commenced in the United States with a glittering opening ceremony featuring international artists Katy Perry, Future, and Tyla. The tournament is jointly hosted by the US, Canada, and Mexico — a first in World Cup history — and adopts the expanded 48-team format introduced by FIFA for this edition. The opening ceremony set the stage for what is expected to be the most-watched football tournament in history given the expanded team count and North American host markets. India is not participating as a national team but the tournament draws massive viewership from Indian football fans.

The context

The FIFA World Cup is the world's most-watched sporting event, drawing billions of viewers globally. The 2026 edition is the first to feature 48 national teams, up from the traditional 32, giving more nations — particularly from Africa, Asia, and North/Central America — a path to qualification. The United States last hosted the World Cup in 1994, when it set attendance records. India, despite having over a billion people, has never qualified for the World Cup finals, though interest in the tournament among Indian viewers has grown substantially with the rise of sports broadcast platforms and fantasy gaming.

Why it matters

For Indian broadcasters and OTT platforms holding World Cup rights, the tournament is a major revenue event driven by advertising and subscriptions. The expanded format and prime-time US scheduling means many matches will air during Indian morning and afternoon hours, making viewing more accessible. The tournament also stimulates India's growing sports merchandise, fantasy sports, and sports-betting-adjacent gaming industries.

What's next

Expect Indian broadcasters to roll out extensive coverage packages and fantasy sports platforms to see record user engagement over the tournament's month-long run. Watch for whether any Asian teams make deep runs in the expanded format, which could further grow football's profile in India. The group stage results over the next two weeks will set the tournament's narrative.
