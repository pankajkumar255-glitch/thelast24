Indian-American Sriram Krishnan Quits as White House AI Adviser After 18 Months

What happened

NDTV reports that Sriram Krishnan has stepped down as the White House's senior AI policy adviser after serving in the role for 18 months. Krishnan, an Indian-American technologist with a background in senior roles at major US tech companies, was appointed to help shape the federal government's approach to artificial intelligence. No specific reason for his departure was given in the headline. His exit comes at a pivotal moment for US AI policy amid ongoing regulatory debates and geopolitical competition with China.

The context

Sriram Krishnan previously held senior roles at companies including Microsoft, Twitter, and Andreessen Horowitz before joining the White House. He was appointed as AI adviser in early 2024 and became a recognisable figure in US technology policy circles. The US government's AI policy apparatus has been evolving rapidly, navigating questions of regulation, national security, and international competitiveness. Indian-American technologists have played an increasingly prominent role in shaping Silicon Valley and US government technology strategy.

Why it matters

The departure of a senior AI policy architect from the White House signals potential shifts in US AI governance priorities. For India, which has been deepening technology cooperation with the United States — including on AI and semiconductors — changes in key personnel can affect the momentum of bilateral tech diplomacy. It also highlights the revolving door between government AI roles and the private sector.

What's next

Expect the White House to name a successor in the coming weeks, and analysts will watch whether the new appointee continues or redirects existing US AI policy priorities. Krishnan is likely to return to the private sector or venture capital, where his policy experience will be highly valued.
