Supreme Court seeks Centre and Punjab government response on failure to implement 25% RTE quota in schools

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has sought responses from the Centre and the Punjab government on a plea alleging the failure to implement the 25% EWS quota mandated under the Right to Education Act in private schools.

The context

Section 12(1)(c) of the Right to Education Act, 2009 requires private unaided schools to admit at least 25% of their entry-class seats from EWS and disadvantaged group children, with reimbursement from the state. Compliance has been patchy across states, with many private schools resisting the mandate through legal challenges or administrative non-cooperation.

Why it matters

Effective implementation of the RTE quota could open doors to quality education for millions of children from low-income families who would otherwise be confined to under-resourced government schools. Continued non-compliance entrenches educational inequality at the very first rung.

What's next

Expect the Centre and Punjab government to file affidavits detailing the state of RTE quota implementation, potentially prompting the court to issue compliance directives.
