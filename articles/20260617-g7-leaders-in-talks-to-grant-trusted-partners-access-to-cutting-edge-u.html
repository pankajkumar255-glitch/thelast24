G7 leaders in talks to grant 'trusted partners' access to cutting-edge US AI models

Key facts

  • G7 leaders discussing 'trusted partners' fast-track access to US AI models
  • Talks triggered by US AI diffusion rule that blocked even allied nations
  • Framework would create tiered global access to frontier US AI systems
  • India is not a G7 member but attends as a guest nation

Reuters reports, citing sources, that G7 leaders are actively discussing a 'trusted partners' mechanism that would grant vetted allied nations privileged access to cutting-edge US AI models. The talks are a direct response to the sweeping US AI diffusion rule, which imposed export restrictions so broad that even close allies found themselves blocked from accessing frontier American AI systems developed by companies like Anthropic and OpenAI.

The proposed framework would effectively create a two-tier global AI access system: a fast lane for trusted G7 and allied partners, and restricted or no access for others. The discussions reflect growing alarm among wealthy democracies that US export controls, designed to keep advanced AI out of adversarial hands, are also handicapping their own technology sectors and AI ambitions.

For India, the outcome of these G7 talks carries significant implications. India is not a G7 member, though it participates as a guest nation. If a closed 'trusted partners' club is formalised without India's inclusion, Indian companies, academic institutions, and government AI projects could face barriers in accessing the most powerful US AI platforms — adding a geopolitical dimension to an already competitive global AI landscape.
