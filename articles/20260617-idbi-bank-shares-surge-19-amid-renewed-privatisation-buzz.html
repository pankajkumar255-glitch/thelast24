IDBI Bank shares surge 19% amid renewed privatisation buzz

Key facts

  • IDBI Bank shares surged up to 19% in intraday trade
  • Rally driven by renewed privatisation optimism
  • Government and LIC hold majority stake in IDBI Bank
  • Privatisation process has faced repeated delays

India Today reports that shares of IDBI Bank surged as much as 19% in intraday trading, making it one of the top gainers on the exchanges. The sudden buying frenzy is being attributed to renewed speculation that the government's long-delayed plan to privatise the bank is gaining momentum.

The Centre and Life Insurance Corporation (LIC) together hold a dominant stake in IDBI Bank, and the government had invited expressions of interest from strategic buyers as part of its disinvestment agenda. However, the process has seen repeated delays due to regulatory and structural complexities. Any fresh signal of progress tends to trigger sharp rallies in the stock.

For retail investors who hold IDBI Bank shares — a widely tracked public-sector bank stock — a 19% move in a single session is highly significant. More broadly, a successful IDBI Bank privatisation would mark a landmark moment in India's disinvestment programme and could unlock significant capital for the government.
