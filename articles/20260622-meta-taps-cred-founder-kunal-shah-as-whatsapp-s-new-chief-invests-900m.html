Meta taps CRED founder Kunal Shah as WhatsApp's new chief, invests $900M in his startup

Key facts

  • Kunal Shah, founder of CRED, named new head of WhatsApp
  • Meta investing $900 million in CRED alongside the appointment
  • WhatsApp has over 500 million users in India
  • Appointment marks an Indian founder leading a top global tech platform

Kunal Shah, the Bengaluru-based entrepreneur who built CRED into one of India's most prominent fintech brands, has been named the new chief of WhatsApp by Meta, the company announced late Sunday. Alongside the appointment, Meta is committing $900 million to CRED, an investment that would rank among the largest ever made into an Indian startup and that values Shah's company at a new landmark.

Shah built CRED around a deceptively simple premise — rewarding creditworthy Indians for paying their bills on time — and turned it into a lifestyle platform with millions of high-income users. His profile in Silicon Valley and among Indian tech circles has been rising steadily, making him a recognisable figure well beyond the domestic startup scene. The appointment places an Indian founder at the helm of a platform that has become as fundamental to daily communication in India as any utility.

For Meta, the move is strategic on multiple levels. WhatsApp is its fastest-growing revenue opportunity through the Business API, and India — where the app has over 500 million users — is central to that growth story. Bringing in a leader who understands the Indian consumer deeply, and who has already built payment and credit infrastructure, could accelerate WhatsApp Pay and WhatsApp Business in ways a traditional Silicon Valley executive might not.

The $900 million investment in CRED simultaneously strengthens Meta's ties to the Indian fintech space and gives CRED the firepower to expand. For India's startup ecosystem, the announcement is a landmark: it confirms that Indian founders are no longer just building companies to be acquired, but are ascending to lead global platforms from the top.
