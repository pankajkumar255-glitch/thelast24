Taare Zameen Par's child star Yohaan quit Bollywood, is now a gold-medallist dentist and pilot in the UK

What happened

The Indian Express reports that the actor who played Yohaan in the 2007 Bollywood film Taare Zameen Par has moved to the UK, where he has earned a gold medal in dentistry and also qualified as a pilot, having stepped away from the film industry after his childhood acting career.

The context

Taare Zameen Par, directed by Aamir Khan and released in 2007, was a landmark Hindi film dealing with dyslexia and the pressure on children, and became a cultural touchstone for a generation of Indian viewers. Child actors from that era faced the notoriously difficult transition from juvenile to adult roles in Bollywood. Many child stars from the 2000s have since pursued careers outside the industry.

Why it matters

The story resonates with millions of Indian parents and young people navigating the tension between creative ambitions and conventional professional success, offering a feel-good counterpoint to more cautionary tales of child stardom. It also prompts reflection on the entertainment industry's long-term support structures for young performers.

What's next

Expect this story to circulate widely on social media, potentially prompting broader conversations about the welfare and future prospects of child actors in Indian cinema.
