Bollywood producers pocket only Rs 40 crore when a film earns Rs 100 crore: Rajkumar Hirani

Key facts

  • Producers earn Rs 40 crore when a film grosses Rs 100 crore at box office
  • Remaining Rs 60 crore goes to taxes, distributors, and exhibitors
  • Rajkumar Hirani made the disclosure publicly

Bollywood producers walk away with just Rs 40 crore for every Rs 100 crore their film earns at the box office, director Rajkumar Hirani has revealed — a figure that lays bare how thin the margins of Hindi mainstream cinema truly are.

The revelation comes from one of Bollywood's most commercially successful filmmakers, the director behind blockbusters like the Munna Bhai series and 3 Idiots. Hirani's candour is significant precisely because he speaks from experience at the top of the industry, not from the fringes. The remaining Rs 60 crore is absorbed by GST, distributor commissions, and the exhibitor's share — costs that are baked into every theatrical release regardless of its scale.

The disclosure arrives at a moment when the Hindi film industry is already under pressure, grappling with a string of high-profile box-office disappointments and a growing audience preference for OTT platforms. For producers who spend heavily on stars, marketing, and spectacle, the realisation that a triple-digit gross translates to barely Rs 40 crore in hand explains the persistent anxiety around theatrical performance.

Hirani's comments are likely to reopen conversations about the fee structures demanded by A-list stars and the industry's dependence on minimum-guarantee deals. When the economics are this compressed, even a film that the public perceives as a hit can leave its producer scrambling to recover costs — a systemic issue the industry has long acknowledged but rarely quantified so plainly in public.
