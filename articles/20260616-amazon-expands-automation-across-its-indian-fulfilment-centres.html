Amazon expands automation across its Indian fulfilment centres

What happened

Indiatimes reports that Amazon is expanding automation within its fulfilment centres in India, deploying advanced robotics and automated warehousing systems across its logistics network. The move is aimed at improving operational efficiency and reducing order processing times.

The context

Amazon operates dozens of fulfilment centres across India and employs hundreds of thousands of workers directly and indirectly in its logistics chain. Globally, Amazon has been a leader in warehouse robotics, deploying systems from its Robotics division to reduce manual handling. India has been a key growth market for Amazon's e-commerce business, and improving fulfilment efficiency is critical to competing with Flipkart and the rising quick-commerce players.

Why it matters

Increased automation could mean faster deliveries and lower costs for Indian shoppers, but it also poses a displacement risk for the large number of warehouse and logistics workers employed across Amazon's Indian operations. The scale of rollout will determine how significant the employment impact is.

What's next

Expect Amazon to face increased scrutiny from labour groups and policymakers as the automation rollout becomes more visible, particularly around worker retraining commitments.
