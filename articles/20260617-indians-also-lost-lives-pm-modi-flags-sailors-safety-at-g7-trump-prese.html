'Indians Also Lost Lives': PM Modi Flags Sailors' Safety At G7, Trump Present

What happened

NDTV reports that Prime Minister Modi, speaking at the G7 summit with President Trump present, flagged the safety of sailors and specifically mentioned that Indians have also lost their lives in maritime incidents. Modi used the high-profile platform to ensure that Indian casualties in sea-related incidents receive international attention. The statement was made during what appears to be a session addressing global maritime security.

The context

India is one of the world's largest suppliers of seafarers, with hundreds of thousands of Indian nationals working aboard international vessels. Maritime security has become a pressing global issue, particularly following Houthi attacks on shipping in the Red Sea, which have disrupted global trade routes and affected Indian-crewed ships. Modi's participation in G7 sessions as an invited partner has become a regular feature of India's global diplomacy.

Why it matters

India's explicit flagging of Indian sailor deaths at G7 could lead to stronger international protocols to protect seafarers of all nationalities, directly benefiting Indian maritime workers. It also signals India's intent to use global platforms to advocate for its citizens rather than remain a passive observer.

What's next

Expect India to follow up this statement with formal diplomatic proposals on maritime safety frameworks within international bodies such as the IMO.
