India-US trade deal created uncertainty between the two nations, says India's foreign secretary

Key facts

  • India's foreign secretary admitted the India-US trade deal caused bilateral uncertainty
  • India and the US are negotiating a comprehensive bilateral trade agreement
  • US is one of India's largest export markets, covering pharma, IT, and textiles

Reuters reports that India's foreign secretary has publicly stated that the India-US trade deal negotiations created uncertainty between the two nations, marking one of the most candid official acknowledgements of strain in the bilateral economic relationship. The remarks reflect the complexity of a deal that has been in discussion for years, touching on tariffs, market access for goods like agricultural products and electronics, and rules on data and intellectual property.

The India-US trade relationship is among the most significant for India, with the United States being one of India's largest export destinations. Tensions have periodically flared over tariffs — including US duties on Indian steel and aluminium and India's retaliatory measures — complicating the path to a comprehensive deal. The foreign secretary's statement suggests that despite recent momentum, deep differences remain.

For everyday Indians, the outcome of this deal has real consequences: a successful agreement could boost exports in sectors like pharmaceuticals, textiles, and IT services, supporting jobs, while a prolonged stalemate keeps uncertainty alive for businesses and investors on both sides.
