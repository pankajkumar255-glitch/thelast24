Sridhar Vembu Backs Debate on Why India Hasn't Built Its Own ChatGPT

What happened

India Today reports that Infosys co-founder Sridhar Vembu has backed the argument explaining why India has not yet built a ChatGPT-equivalent, weighing in on a debate that has gained fresh urgency amid global AI developments including Claude's Mythos and the pause around Microsoft's Fable 5. Vembu's position suggests structural and ecosystem gaps that go beyond mere funding or intent.

The context

India has a vast pool of software engineers and an active startup ecosystem, yet no Indian organisation has produced a globally competitive foundational large language model. Critics point to a lack of compute infrastructure, limited high-quality multilingual training data, and a culture of building on top of existing foreign models rather than from scratch. Vembu himself has long advocated for India building deep technology stacks rather than remaining a services economy.

Why it matters

Without a sovereign foundational AI model, India remains dependent on American and European platforms for critical AI infrastructure, raising concerns about data privacy, national security, and the ability to customise AI for India's diverse languages and needs. The debate directly affects the careers of hundreds of thousands of Indian AI and machine learning engineers who could either build world-class products domestically or continue supporting foreign platforms.

What's next

Expect the debate to intensify as the government's IndiaAI Mission rolls out compute and dataset initiatives, with pressure growing on both the private sector and academia to deliver a credible homegrown large language model.
