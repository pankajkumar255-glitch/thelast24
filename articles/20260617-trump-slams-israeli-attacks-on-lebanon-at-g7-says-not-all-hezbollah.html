Trump slams Israeli attacks on Lebanon at G7, says 'Not all Hezbollah'

Key facts

  • Trump criticised Israeli attacks on Lebanon at the G7 Summit
  • Quote: 'Not all Hezbollah' — implying civilian targets are being hit
  • Iran separately threatened a 'harsh response' to Israeli attacks in Lebanon
  • Remarks represent a rare public US rebuke of Israeli military action

What happened

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump criticised Israeli attacks on Lebanon during the G7 Summit, stating 'Not all Hezbollah' to suggest that strikes are going beyond the militant group. The remarks were made publicly at the summit, marking an unusual departure from Washington's standard defence of Israeli military action.

The context

Israel has been conducting military operations in Lebanon targeting Hezbollah, the Iran-backed militant group, following the broader regional conflict that escalated after the October 2023 Gaza war. The G7 is a forum of the world's seven largest advanced economies, and US statements there carry outsized diplomatic weight. Trump had previously positioned himself as strongly pro-Israel but has shown growing impatience with the scope of Israeli military campaigns.

Why it matters

A US presidential rebuke of Israel at a G7 summit could reshape diplomatic pressure on the conflict, affecting ceasefire negotiations and oil prices that directly impact India's economy and the safety of its large diaspora in the Gulf region.

What's next

Expect Israeli officials to respond to Trump's remarks and watch for whether G7 nations issue a joint statement calling for restraint in Lebanon.
