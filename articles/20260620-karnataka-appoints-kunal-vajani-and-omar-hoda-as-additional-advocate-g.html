Karnataka appoints Kunal Vajani and Omar Hoda as Additional Advocate Generals in Supreme Court

Key facts

  • Karnataka appoints Kunal Vajani as Additional Advocate General, Supreme Court
  • Omar Hoda also appointed Additional Advocate General, Supreme Court
  • Appointments strengthen Karnataka's legal team at the apex court

The New Indian Express reports that the Karnataka government has appointed Kunal Vajani and Omar Hoda as Additional Advocate Generals to represent the state before the Supreme Court of India. The move expands Karnataka's legal team at the apex court level, adding two senior lawyers to handle the state's growing docket of cases.

Additional Advocate Generals play a key role in presenting state governments' positions in constitutional, civil, and inter-state disputes before the Supreme Court. Karnataka is a party to several prominent and sensitive matters at the apex court, including the long-running Cauvery river water-sharing dispute with neighbouring Tamil Nadu.

The appointments reflect the Karnataka government's focus on ensuring robust legal representation at the national level, particularly as states increasingly contest central policies and resource-sharing arrangements before the Supreme Court.
