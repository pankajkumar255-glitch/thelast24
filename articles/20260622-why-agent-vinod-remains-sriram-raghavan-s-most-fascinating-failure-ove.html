Why Agent Vinod remains Sriram Raghavan's most fascinating failure, over a decade on

Key facts

  • Agent Vinod released in 2012, directed by Sriram Raghavan
  • Starred Saif Ali Khan; underperformed commercially on release
  • Raghavan later directed acclaimed Andhadhun (2018)

More than a decade after its release, Agent Vinod — Sriram Raghavan's ambitious 2012 spy thriller starring Saif Ali Khan — is getting a fresh critical look, with The Indian Express arguing that the film remains the director's most fascinating failure: a project that reached for something genuinely different in Hindi commercial cinema and came up short in ways that are worth examining.

Raghavan, who had previously made Johnny Gaddaar and would go on to direct the widely acclaimed Andhadhun, conceived Agent Vinod as a big-budget homage to classic Cold War spy cinema — think less Salman Khan action and more John le Carré filtered through Bollywood glamour. Shot across multiple countries, the film had an unusually sprawling canvas for a Hindi production of its era. Yet on release, it was met with mixed reviews and underperformed commercially, leaving behind an odd legacy: beloved by a small but vocal subset of Hindi film enthusiasts, largely forgotten by the mainstream.

The reassessment positions the film's failure not as straightforward incompetence but as a symptom of the structural tensions Raghavan was navigating — between the demands of a star vehicle, the expectations of a mass entertainer, and his own instincts as a genre craftsman. The film tried to do too many things at once, the argument goes, and its messiness is inseparable from its genuine moments of invention.

With Raghavan's reputation now at a high point, the piece serves as a reminder that even the most acclaimed directors in Indian cinema have complicated roads behind them — and that revisiting those detours can illuminate both the artist and the industry.
