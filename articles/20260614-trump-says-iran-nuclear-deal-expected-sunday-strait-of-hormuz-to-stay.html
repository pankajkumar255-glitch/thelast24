Trump Says Iran Nuclear Deal Expected Sunday, Strait of Hormuz to Stay Open

What happened

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump has stated a deal with Iran is expected on Sunday and confirmed that the Strait of Hormuz will be open to all nations. The announcement appears aimed at reassuring global energy markets amid heightened US-Iran tensions.

The context

The Strait of Hormuz, located between Iran and Oman, is the transit route for roughly 20% of the world's oil supply. Tensions between the US and Iran over Tehran's nuclear programme have periodically threatened to disrupt passage through the strait, causing global oil price spikes. India, as one of the world's largest crude oil importers, is acutely sensitive to any disruption in Gulf shipping lanes.

Why it matters

A successful US-Iran deal that guarantees free passage through the Strait of Hormuz would help stabilise global crude prices, offering relief to India's import bill and keeping petrol and diesel prices from rising further. For Indian households already managing tight budgets, stable fuel prices matter for everything from commuting costs to food inflation driven by transport expenses.

What's next

Expect close market attention on Sunday for any formal announcement of a US-Iran deal, with oil prices likely to react sharply to either confirmation or collapse of the agreement.
