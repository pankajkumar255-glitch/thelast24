India can absorb oil at $90 a barrel, but growth and inflation risks rise sharply beyond that: Avendus

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that Avendus has drawn a clear line at $90 per barrel as India's comfort zone for absorbing crude oil prices. Beyond that level, the investment bank flags rising risks to economic growth and inflation, two variables that the Reserve Bank of India monitors closely in setting interest rate policy. The report does not specify an exact growth or inflation figure but frames the $90 threshold as a turning point for macro stability. The analysis is particularly timely given recent fluctuations in Brent crude driven by OPEC+ decisions and Middle East tensions.

The context

India imports roughly 85 percent of its crude oil needs, making it acutely sensitive to global price swings. The government partially controls retail fuel prices through state-owned oil marketing companies — Indian Oil, HPCL, and BPCL — which absorb losses during price spikes before passing them on selectively to consumers. High oil prices simultaneously widen the current account deficit, weaken the rupee, and stoke headline inflation, creating a challenging trilemma for policymakers. The RBI has already been navigating sticky core inflation, and an oil shock would complicate its rate-cut calculus significantly.

Why it matters

Petrol and diesel prices feed directly into transport and logistics costs, which in turn raise prices of vegetables, manufactured goods, and services across India. A crude spike beyond $90 would likely delay any RBI interest rate cuts, keeping home loan and vehicle loan EMIs elevated for millions of borrowers. The fiscal deficit could also widen if the government chooses to subsidise fuel, squeezing spending on infrastructure and social programmes.

What's next

Expect the RBI's next monetary policy communication to reference global oil price trajectories as a key risk factor. Markets will watch OPEC+ meetings and Middle East developments closely for signals on whether Brent crude approaches or breaches the $90 level.
