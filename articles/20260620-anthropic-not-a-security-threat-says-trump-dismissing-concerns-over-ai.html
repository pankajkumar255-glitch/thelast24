Anthropic Not A Security Threat, Says Trump, Dismissing Concerns Over AI Curbs

Key facts

  • Trump declares Anthropic is not a US national security threat
  • Statement comes amid wider US AI export control debates
  • Anthropic backed by Amazon and Google; founded by ex-OpenAI team
  • Anthropic's Claude models compete with ChatGPT and Gemini

Business Standard reports that US President Donald Trump has dismissed concerns about Anthropic being a national security risk, stating the AI safety company does not pose a threat to the United States. The remarks were made in the context of broader debates over AI technology curbs and export controls that have gathered pace in Washington.

Anthropic, founded by former OpenAI researchers Dario and Daniela Amodei, has emerged as one of the world's leading frontier AI labs. It has received significant investment from Amazon and Google, and its Claude family of AI models competes directly with OpenAI's ChatGPT and Google's Gemini. The company has been positioning itself on AI safety, which has brought it both credibility and scrutiny from policymakers.

Trump's comments effectively clear the political air around Anthropic at a time when the company is on a hiring spree — having just attracted Nobel Prize-winning scientist John Jumper from Google DeepMind. For Indian enterprises and developers increasingly adopting Claude-based tools, the US government's stance on Anthropic's legitimacy removes a layer of geopolitical uncertainty from their technology choices.
