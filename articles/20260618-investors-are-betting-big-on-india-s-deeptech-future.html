Investors are betting big on India's deeptech future

Key facts

  • Investors increasing bets on Indian deeptech: semiconductors, space, defence, drones
  • Shift away from consumer internet toward engineering-intensive startups
  • Government Semiconductor Mission and space liberalisation acting as catalysts
  • Deeptech seen as key to reducing India's strategic import dependence

Moneycontrol's Startup Street reports that investors are making increasingly large bets on India's deeptech ecosystem, channelling capital into startups working on semiconductors, space technology, defence applications, drones, robotics, and advanced materials. The shift marks a notable evolution from the consumer internet and fintech-dominated investment cycles of the previous decade.

Policy tailwinds have played a significant role. The Indian government's Semiconductor Mission, the liberalisation of the space sector to allow private players, and increased defence procurement from domestic sources have all created commercial pathways for deeptech ventures that did not exist five years ago. Startups in this space typically require longer gestation periods and larger upfront capital, making institutional and patient capital crucial.

For India, the stakes are high. Dependence on imported semiconductors, foreign satellites, and overseas defence hardware has long been identified as a strategic vulnerability. A well-funded domestic deeptech sector could address those gaps while also generating high-value engineering jobs and reducing import bills — outcomes with direct economic relevance for millions of Indians beyond the startup ecosystem itself.
