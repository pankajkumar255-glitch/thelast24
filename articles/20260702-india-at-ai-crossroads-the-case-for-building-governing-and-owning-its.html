India at AI Crossroads: The Case for Building, Governing, and Owning Its Intelligent Future

Key facts

  • Frontline argues India must build, govern, and own its AI future
  • India has AI talent and data but lacks frontier compute capacity
  • IndiaAI Mission signals government ambition in the sector

India faces a defining moment in the global artificial intelligence landscape, Frontline Magazine argues in a sweeping analysis — one in which the decisions made in the next few years will determine whether the country shapes AI on its own terms or becomes dependent on technology built and governed by others.

The piece frames the challenge across three imperatives: building indigenous AI capability through research, talent, and compute infrastructure; governing AI through regulatory frameworks that reflect Indian values and protect citizens; and owning the resulting systems, data, and intellectual property rather than licensing them from foreign corporations. Each dimension, the analysis contends, reinforces the others — governance without capability is toothless, and capability without ownership creates new forms of technological dependency.

India's position is genuinely complex. The country has world-class AI researchers, a massive data pool generated by its digital economy, and a government that has signalled ambition through initiatives like the IndiaAI Mission. Yet it currently lacks the semiconductor manufacturing base, large-scale compute clusters, and frontier model development that the United States and China have invested in heavily.

The Frontline argument lands at a moment when global AI governance debates are accelerating, with major economies rushing to set standards that will shape the technology for decades. For India, the stakes are not merely economic — they extend to how AI will be used in public services, law enforcement, healthcare, and elections, making the question of who builds and governs these systems one of the most consequential policy choices of the decade.
