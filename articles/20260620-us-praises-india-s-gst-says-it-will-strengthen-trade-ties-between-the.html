US praises India's GST, says it will strengthen trade ties between the two countries

Key facts

  • US praises India's GST as a trade-enabling reform
  • US described as 'bullish' on India's overall economy
  • Remarks made amid active India-US bilateral trade negotiations

NDTV reports that the United States has publicly praised India's GST structure, calling it a positive development that will help boost trade ties between the two nations. The remarks reflect a broadly bullish American stance on the Indian economy as bilateral trade negotiations continue.

The US endorsement of GST — India's unified indirect tax system that replaced a complex web of state and central levies — is notable because American businesses had previously cited regulatory complexity as a barrier to deeper engagement with India. Washington's positive assessment suggests that India's economic reforms are increasingly being viewed favourably by its largest trading partner.

The comments come alongside ongoing negotiations for a bilateral trade deal and against the backdrop of US tariff actions. For Indian businesses and consumers, a closer US-India trade relationship could mean expanded export opportunities for Indian goods and greater foreign investment inflows, with knock-on effects on employment and economic growth.
