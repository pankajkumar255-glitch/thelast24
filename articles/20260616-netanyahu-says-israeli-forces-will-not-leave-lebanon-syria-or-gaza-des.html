Netanyahu Says Israeli Forces Will Not Leave Lebanon, Syria Or Gaza Despite US-Iran Deal

What happened

NDTV reports that Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stated Israeli forces would not withdraw from Lebanon, Syria or Gaza following the US-Iran deal announced by Donald Trump. The declaration was made publicly after Trump's announcement of the agreement.

The context

Israel has maintained military deployments in southern Lebanon following its campaign against Hezbollah, controls territory in Syria near the Golan Heights, and continues operations in Gaza. Netanyahu has consistently argued that Iran's regional proxies pose an existential threat to Israel.

Why it matters

An Israeli refusal to de-escalate even after a US-Iran deal risks reigniting wider regional conflict, which could spike oil prices and endanger the millions of Indians working across Gulf states neighbouring these conflict zones.

What's next

Expect international pressure on Israel to clarify its military intentions, and watch for Iran's reaction to Netanyahu's declaration in the context of its new agreement with Washington.
