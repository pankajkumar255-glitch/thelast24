OpenAI Unveils GPT-5.6 Series: Meet Sol, Terra and Luna — But Access Remains Limited

Key facts

  • GPT-5.6 series includes three models: Sol, Terra, and Luna
  • Sol features a new reasoning mode and enhanced safety features
  • Rollout is limited — most users cannot access any of the three models yet
  • OpenAI is managing a phased release rather than an open launch

OpenAI has unveiled its GPT-5.6 series, introducing three distinct AI models — Sol, Terra, and Luna — in what amounts to one of its most ambitious multi-model launches to date. Sol, the first model to see a limited rollout, comes equipped with a new reasoning mode and enhanced safety features, signalling OpenAI's continued push to make its frontier models both more capable and more responsible.

Despite the fanfare, access to all three models remains tightly restricted. The Indian Express reported that almost no one can use them yet, with OpenAI managing a careful, staged release rather than an open launch. Moneycontrol noted that Sol's debut also includes two additional AI capabilities, though the full scope of features is being introduced gradually.

The GPT-5.6 series represents a significant architectural moment for OpenAI: rather than a single flagship update, the company is segmenting its offering into specialised models — a strategy that mirrors how cloud providers tier compute and services. Sol appears positioned as the reasoning-heavy option, while Terra and Luna's distinct roles are expected to become clearer as access widens.

For the Indian AI ecosystem — which encompasses a fast-growing developer community, enterprise adopters, and a wave of AI-native startups — the staggered rollout means that planning around these tools requires patience. The models' eventual wide release will be closely watched, both for their raw capability and for how OpenAI prices and distributes access in markets like India.
