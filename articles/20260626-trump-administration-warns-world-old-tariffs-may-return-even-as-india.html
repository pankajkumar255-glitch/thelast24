Trump Administration Warns World: 'Old Tariffs May Return' Even as India-US Talks Continue

Key facts

  • Trump administration warns old higher tariffs may return without deals
  • Warning issued during active India-US trade talks
  • India insisting on tariff advantage over rivals as precondition
  • Key Indian exports at stake include pharma, textiles, IT and engineering

The Trump administration has put the world on notice: countries that do not conclude trade deals with Washington risk seeing the pre-pause tariff levels restored, a threat that lands with particular force given that India and the US are currently in active negotiations. The Times of India reported the fresh warning, which effectively sets a ticking clock on trade talks with multiple partners simultaneously.

For India, the timing is pointed. New Delhi is engaged in what officials have described as substantive but challenging bilateral trade discussions with American counterparts, even as Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal has made clear that India will only accept a deal that gives it a tariff edge over competitors. The twin pressures — a US threat to revert to higher duties and India's own high bar for what constitutes an acceptable agreement — create a complex negotiating environment.

The Trump administration's approach mirrors the broader transactional stance it has adopted in global trade: using the threat of tariff restoration as leverage to extract concessions from partners on market access, agriculture and digital trade. India, alongside other major trading partners, must weigh the cost of an imperfect deal against the risk of higher duties on its exports.

Analysts note that India's exports to the United States span high-value sectors including pharmaceuticals, information technology services, textiles and engineering goods. Any significant tariff escalation would ripple through these industries, affecting employment and corporate earnings. The next few weeks of negotiations are therefore being watched closely by Indian industry and policymakers alike.
