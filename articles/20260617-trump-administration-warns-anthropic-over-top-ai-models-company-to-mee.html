Trump administration warns Anthropic over top AI models, company to meet White House

What happened

BBC and Bloomberg report that US Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick sent a formal letter to Anthropic warning of potential curbs on its top AI models, and that the Trump administration directed Anthropic to limit the reach of certain tools. CNBC notes that prediction market traders are speculating Anthropic will restore access quickly after the directive. Business Standard and Bloomberg both confirmed the contents of Lutnick's letter.

The context

Anthropic, founded by former OpenAI researchers, is one of the most prominent AI safety-focused labs in the world and makes the Claude family of AI models. The Trump administration has taken an increasingly hands-on approach to regulating and directing US technology companies, particularly around AI exports and access. This is the first publicly known instance of a direct government warning to a major AI lab about restricting its own domestic model access.

Why it matters

Any restrictions on Anthropic's advanced models could limit access for the growing number of Indian startups and enterprises that use Claude-based APIs for products and services. It also signals that AI model availability is increasingly subject to geopolitical and regulatory risk, a concern for any business building on top of US AI platforms.

What's next

Expect Anthropic's White House meeting to determine whether the curbs are formalised or walked back, with the outcome likely to set a template for how the US government engages with other AI firms.
