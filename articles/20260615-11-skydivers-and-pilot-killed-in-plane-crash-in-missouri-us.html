11 Skydivers And Pilot Killed In Plane Crash In Missouri, US

What happened

NDTV reports that a plane carrying 11 skydivers and a pilot crashed in Missouri in the United States, killing all 12 on board. The incident is being investigated by US authorities.

The context

Skydiving aircraft are typically small, propeller-driven planes that make multiple ascents per day at drop zones. Fatal accidents involving skydiving planes, while rare, do occur and often attract significant attention due to the number of casualties involved. Missouri is home to several active skydiving operations.

Why it matters

The scale of the disaster — 12 deaths in a single skydiving flight — will likely prompt a review of safety regulations for jump planes in the US, and serves as a sobering story for the global adventure sports community including India's growing skydiving scene.

What's next

Expect the US National Transportation Safety Board to open a formal investigation, with preliminary findings likely within days.
