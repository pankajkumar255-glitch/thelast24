US Trade Representative Greer to Visit India on June 23-24 for Trade Deal Talks

What happened

Reuters reports that US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer will visit India on June 23-24 for bilateral trade talks. Moneycontrol adds that the first tranche of a trade deal is nearing its final stage, with both sides aiming to lock in an agreement. NDTV Profit notes the visit could also occur on June 22, suggesting scheduling flexibility as both governments coordinate.

The context

India and the United States have been in trade negotiations for several months, accelerated by the Trump administration's imposition of reciprocal tariffs earlier in 2025. India faces a 26% tariff on its exports to the US under those measures, creating urgency to secure a deal. A first-tranche agreement would likely cover select goods categories and could serve as a template for broader trade liberalisation between the two nations.

Why it matters

A successful first-tranche deal could reduce tariffs on key Indian export sectors such as textiles, pharmaceuticals, and engineering goods, boosting revenues and employment in those industries. For Indian consumers, it could also mean lower prices or greater availability of American goods such as agricultural produce and technology products.

What's next

Expect a clearer picture of the deal's contours — including which goods categories are included — to emerge after the June 23-24 meeting in New Delhi.
