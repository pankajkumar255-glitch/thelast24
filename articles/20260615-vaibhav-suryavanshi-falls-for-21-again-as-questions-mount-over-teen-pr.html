Vaibhav Suryavanshi falls for 21 again as questions mount over teen prodigy's consistency

What happened

The Times of India reports that Vaibhav Suryavanshi was dismissed for 21 against Sri Lanka A, marking yet another match in which the young batsman made a start but could not go on to a significant score. The dismissal continues a concerning pattern in his India A appearances.

The context

Suryavanshi became a household name after his record-breaking IPL performance for Rajasthan Royals, becoming one of the youngest cricketers to score a T20 fifty at elite level. His India A inclusion was seen as a fast-tracking of his development. However, converting T20 instincts into Test-match patience and concentration is a well-known challenge for young stroke-makers.

Why it matters

Repeated low scores at India A level may slow his path to a senior Test call-up, as selectors typically demand proven ability to bat long in first-class cricket before elevation. His development arc is being watched closely by fans and analysts as a potential future cornerstone of Indian batting.

What's next

Expect selectors to give Suryavanshi more time in India A fixtures to find consistency, though another series of starts-without-scores will intensify the debate about his readiness.
