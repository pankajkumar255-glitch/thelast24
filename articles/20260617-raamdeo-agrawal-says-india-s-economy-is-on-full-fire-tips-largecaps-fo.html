Raamdeo Agrawal says India's economy is 'on full fire', tips largecaps for leadership

Key facts

  • Raamdeo Agrawal calls India's economy 'on full fire'
  • Predicts largecap stocks could regain leadership over mid/smallcaps
  • Agrawal is co-founder of Motilal Oswal Financial Services

CNBC TV18 reports that Raamdeo Agrawal, co-founder and chairman of Motilal Oswal Financial Services, has declared India's economy to be "on full fire", offering one of his most bullish assessments of the macroeconomic environment in recent memory. Speaking to the channel, he said the fundamentals underpinning India's growth story remain firmly intact.

Agrawal made a specific market call that largecap stocks could reclaim leadership from mid and smallcap stocks, which have dominated returns for much of the recent market cycle. This shift, if it materialises, would favour investors in index funds, large-cap mutual funds, and blue-chip direct equity portfolios over those concentrated in smaller companies.

Agrawal is considered one of India's most respected long-term equity investors, known for his framework of identifying wealth-creating businesses over decades. His views carry weight with both institutional and retail investors. For the growing base of Indian retail investors tracking markets through SIPs and direct platforms, his largecap thesis is a timely pointer as they review asset allocation in a market that has seen significant volatility.
