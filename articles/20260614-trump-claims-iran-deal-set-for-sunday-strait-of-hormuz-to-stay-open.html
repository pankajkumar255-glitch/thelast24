Trump Claims Iran Deal Set for Sunday, Strait of Hormuz to Stay Open

What happened

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump stated a deal with Iran is expected on Sunday and pledged that the Strait of Hormuz will be open to all nations. Trump made the announcement publicly, signalling active diplomatic engagement between Washington and Tehran.

The context

The US and Iran have been locked in a prolonged standoff over Iran's nuclear programme, with Washington imposing sweeping sanctions and Tehran gradually increasing uranium enrichment. The Strait of Hormuz is vital to global energy supply, and any threat to it — or resolution easing that threat — has immediate consequences for oil markets worldwide, including India, which is one of the largest importers of Iranian and Gulf oil.

Why it matters

A successful deal could ease geopolitical risk in the Gulf, potentially softening global crude prices and reducing India's import costs. Conversely, if talks collapse, renewed tensions could spike oil prices and widen India's trade deficit.

What's next

Expect global oil markets and Indian energy policymakers to closely watch whether the announced Sunday deadline produces a concrete agreement or breaks down.
