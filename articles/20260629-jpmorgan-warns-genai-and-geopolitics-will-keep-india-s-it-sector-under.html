JPMorgan warns GenAI and geopolitics will keep India's IT sector under pressure

Key facts

  • JPMorgan flags GenAI and geopolitics as twin growth headwinds for Indian IT
  • Generative AI is automating core IT services tasks
  • Geopolitical uncertainty is causing client budget deferrals in key Western markets

India's IT sector faces a prolonged period of subdued growth, with JPMorgan warning that generative AI and geopolitical headwinds will keep the industry under pressure. The American investment bank's assessment points to two structural forces reshaping demand for traditional IT services in ways that are difficult to reverse in the short term.

On the technology side, generative AI is automating coding, testing, documentation and customer-support tasks that have long been the bread and butter of Indian IT services firms. While companies like TCS, Infosys and Wipro are pivoting to offer AI-enabled services, the transition is compressing billing volumes and squeezing margins on legacy work faster than new AI-driven revenues can fill the gap.

Geopolitics adds a second layer of complexity. Uncertainty around US trade policy, visa restrictions and client budget freezes in key Western markets is prompting enterprises to defer or scale back outsourcing decisions. Indian IT exporters, heavily reliant on North American and European clients, are directly exposed to this caution.

JPMorgan's note reinforces concerns that the sector's post-pandemic growth surge has definitively cooled. For a country where IT exports are a major source of dollar earnings and the sector remains one of the most sought-after employers for engineering graduates, the outlook has real consequences — for hiring plans, campus recruitment and wage growth across the technology workforce.
