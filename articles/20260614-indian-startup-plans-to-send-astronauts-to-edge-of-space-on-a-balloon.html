Indian startup plans to send astronauts to edge of space on a balloon

What happened

India Today reports that an Indian startup is working on a system to carry astronauts to the edge of space using a balloon, from which they would descend back to Earth. The venture represents an unconventional approach to near-space human access, bypassing the enormous cost and complexity of rocket propulsion for altitude-based missions. Specific technical or funding details were not elaborated in the headline.

The context

India's private space sector has expanded rapidly since IN-SPACe, the government's space promotion body, began licensing private players following policy reforms in 2020. Startups such as Skyroot Aerospace and Agnikul Cosmos have already conducted rocket launches, while others are exploring satellites, propulsion, and now near-space tourism. High-altitude balloon-based near-space travel has precedents globally, including projects by companies like Space Perspective in the United States.

Why it matters

Success would validate India's growing private space innovation and could attract international scientific and commercial interest in cost-effective near-space access. It also broadens the scope of what India's startup ecosystem is attempting beyond software and consumer tech.

What's next

Expect the startup to announce test flights or seek IN-SPACe regulatory clearances as it moves from concept toward demonstration.
