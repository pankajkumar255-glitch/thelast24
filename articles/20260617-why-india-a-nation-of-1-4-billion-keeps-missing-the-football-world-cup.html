Why India, a nation of 1.4 billion, keeps missing the football World Cup

What happened

BBC reports on the structural and systemic reasons behind India's continued absence from the FIFA World Cup, despite having a population of 1.4 billion. The piece highlights issues including inadequate grassroots infrastructure, administrative failures, and cricket's dominance in attracting talent and sponsorship money. India last appeared in a World Cup in 1950, when they withdrew without playing.

The context

India's football federation (AIFF) has faced repeated governance crises, including a FIFA suspension in 2022 due to third-party interference. The national team, ranked around 126 globally, has struggled to compete consistently even within Asia, where nations like Japan, South Korea, and Australia dominate World Cup qualification.

Why it matters

For the millions of Indian children who play football recreationally, the absence of a credible World Cup pathway means fewer professional opportunities, less investment in coaching, and continued brain drain of talent to other sports. It also reflects a broader policy failure to diversify India's sporting identity beyond cricket.

What's next

Expect the debate to intensify as the 2026 FIFA World Cup qualification rounds progress and India faces elimination from the Asian bracket.
