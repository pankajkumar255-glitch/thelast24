China's Meituan Releases Massive Open-Source AI Model Built Entirely Without Foreign Chips

Key facts

  • Meituan releases large open-source AI model
  • Model trained using zero foreign chips
  • Demonstrates China's AI progress amid US chip export restrictions
  • Meituan is China's leading food delivery and tech conglomerate

Meituan, China's dominant food delivery and technology conglomerate, has released a massive open-source AI model built entirely without foreign chips, in what is being seen as a landmark demonstration of China's progress in AI self-reliance. The model's release underlines how Chinese tech giants are navigating — and in some cases overcoming — the constraints imposed by American semiconductor export controls.

The US has progressively restricted the export of high-end AI chips, particularly Nvidia's advanced GPUs, to Chinese entities, aiming to slow the country's AI development. Meituan's achievement suggests those restrictions, while disruptive, have not halted China's AI ambitions. Instead, they appear to have accelerated investment in domestic chip development and alternative hardware stacks.

By open-sourcing the model, Meituan also enters a global conversation alongside the likes of Meta's Llama series and China's own DeepSeek, making powerful AI accessible to developers worldwide and potentially influencing the direction of open AI development. The move is as much a geopolitical statement as a technical one.

For observers in India, the development is noteworthy on multiple fronts. It demonstrates the pace at which China is closing the gap in frontier AI capability, and it raises strategic questions about technology supply chains, chip dependencies, and the importance of building independent AI infrastructure — challenges India is only beginning to seriously grapple with.
