India-U.S. interim trade deal needs only 'final touches'; USTR Greer to visit New Delhi on June 23-24

Key facts

  • India-U.S. interim deal described as needing only 'final touches'
  • USTR Jamieson Greer to visit New Delhi on June 23-24
  • Deal aims to pre-empt expiry of 90-day U.S. tariff pause
  • Covers select goods/services where both sides have agreed

An interim trade deal between India and the United States is on the verge of conclusion, with both sides describing it as needing only 'final touches', and U.S. Trade Representative Jamieson Greer set to travel to New Delhi on June 23-24 to wrap up negotiations. The development marks a major step forward in trade ties between the world's largest democracies, coming after months of difficult back-and-forth over tariffs, market access, and non-tariff barriers.

Greer's visit is widely seen as a closing mission rather than an exploratory one — a signal that negotiators on both sides have bridged the most contentious gaps. India and the U.S. have been racing to conclude a deal before the 90-day pause on reciprocal tariffs imposed by the Trump administration expires, which has added urgency to the talks.

The interim agreement is expected to cover select goods and services where both countries have found common ground, potentially lowering duties on American exports like agricultural and industrial products, while securing relief for Indian goods — particularly textiles, gems and jewellery, and engineering products — from the steep U.S. tariffs announced earlier this year.

For India, the deal would also be a diplomatic win, signalling that the Modi government has managed to navigate the turbulent trade environment engineered by Washington without major concessions on sensitive sectors. The announcement comes on the heels of talks between Prime Minister Modi and President Trump at the G7 summit, where trade, tariffs and strategic trust dominated the bilateral agenda. If Greer's New Delhi visit delivers a signed framework, it could be the most consequential trade development for India in years.
