American Whiskey Pushes for Fairer Market Access in India Amid Trade Talks

Key facts

  • US spirits industry lobbying for tariff reduction on American whiskey in India
  • India has high import duties on spirits, limiting US market access
  • Push comes amid active India-US trade deal negotiations
  • India is one of the world's largest and fastest-growing whiskey markets

American whiskey producers are pressing India for a fairer deal on import tariffs as the two countries negotiate a bilateral trade agreement, with the US spirits lobby pushing to dismantle a tariff structure they argue has long made the Indian market effectively inaccessible to American brands.

India is one of the world's largest and fastest-growing whiskey markets, yet American products command a tiny fraction of it — largely because of high import duties that make US spirits prohibitively expensive for most Indian consumers. The industry's tariff and taste argument is straightforward: without a level playing field, American whiskey cannot compete on price with domestically produced Indian-made foreign liquor brands.

The timing of the push is deliberate. With US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer having just concluded two days of talks in Delhi and both sides signalling progress toward an interim deal, the spirits industry sees a narrow window to secure meaningful tariff concessions. American trade negotiators have historically included alcohol market access as a standard ask in trade talks with India.

For Indian consumers, any deal that reduces duties on imported American whiskey could eventually bring down prices on popular US brands. For domestic producers, it would represent a competitive challenge. The outcome will depend on how far India is willing to go on agricultural and consumer goods tariffs — always one of the most politically sensitive elements of any trade negotiation.
