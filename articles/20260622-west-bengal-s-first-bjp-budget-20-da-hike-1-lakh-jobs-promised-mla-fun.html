West Bengal's first BJP budget: 20% DA hike, 1 lakh jobs promised, MLA funds raised

Key facts

  • DA hiked 20%, taking Bengal state employees' total DA to 38%
  • 1 lakh jobs promised in BJP's first Bengal budget
  • MLA local area funds raised by Rs 30 lakh per constituency
  • Budget targets 1.7 lakh total employment opportunities

West Bengal's first BJP government delivered its maiden state budget, rolling out a clutch of major announcements anchored by a 20% dearness allowance hike for state employees — raising their DA to 38% — and a pledge to create 1 lakh jobs in the year ahead. The budget also raised MLA Local Area Development funds by Rs 30 lakh per constituency, and set an overall employment generation target of 1.7 lakh positions.

The DA increase was the headline relief measure, addressing a longstanding grievance of state government employees who had seen their allowances lag behind central government norms during the Trinamool Congress years. For the BJP, delivering on this promise in the very first budget is a pointed political statement, aimed at consolidating support among government workers — a key constituency in the state.

The budget's jobs focus reflects the challenging economic realities the new government inherited. Bengal's industrial investment climate and unemployment rates have been subjects of sustained political debate, and the BJP's state leadership will be judged on whether the promised positions materialise rather than remain on paper.

Opposition leaders, including remnants of the Trinamool Congress, were quick to question the budgetary arithmetic, pointing to the state's significant debt burden and questioning how the spending would be financed. Analysts noted that the budget is as much a political document as a fiscal one — the BJP's opportunity to signal a clean break from the previous administration and set expectations for its tenure in Kolkata's Writers' Building.
