5 Killed as Israel Strikes Lebanon Despite Active Ceasefire with Hezbollah

Key facts

  • 5 people killed in Israeli strikes on Lebanon
  • Strikes occurred despite an active ceasefire with Hezbollah
  • Ceasefire was brokered to end months of cross-border hostilities
  • India has a large diaspora community in Lebanon

NDTV reports that Israeli strikes on Lebanon have killed at least 5 people, occurring despite an active ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah. The ceasefire, brokered with international involvement, was intended to halt months of cross-border hostilities that had escalated sharply in 2024. The latest strikes suggest the truce is being violated and may be on the verge of breaking down entirely.

The ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah had been seen as a partial de-escalation in the broader Middle East conflict that has consumed the region since the Hamas attacks of October 2023. However, mutual accusations of violations have persisted, and the latest Israeli military action raises serious questions about the durability of the agreement and international efforts to maintain it.

India has approximately 90,000 citizens living and working in Lebanon, making the security situation a matter of direct concern. Beyond the diaspora angle, a renewed escalation would likely push up global crude oil prices — of which India is the world's third-largest importer — and further complicate New Delhi's diplomatic balancing act between Israel and the Arab world.
