US Curbs on Anthropic Models Reignite Calls for Sovereign AI in India

What happened

Business Standard reports that the US government has imposed curbs on Anthropic's AI models, restricting access for certain users and geographies. Indiatimes notes that the ban on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models is expected to put Indian IT services firms at a competitive disadvantage. The restrictions have reignited calls from industry and policy circles for India to build sovereign AI capabilities rather than remain reliant on US-developed models.

The context

Anthropic, one of the leading US AI startups behind the Claude family of models, has been seen as a key partner for enterprise and IT services globally. India's IT sector — a multi-billion-dollar export industry — has increasingly integrated large language models into its service offerings. Geopolitical tensions around AI technology access, similar to earlier restrictions on semiconductors, are now beginning to directly affect software and AI services.

Why it matters

Indian IT firms losing access to top-tier US AI models could weaken their competitiveness against rivals in countries with unrestricted access, potentially affecting revenues and jobs in the sector. The episode gives concrete urgency to India's push for a domestic AI ecosystem, which could attract significant government and private investment.

What's next

Expect the Indian government and industry bodies to accelerate discussions around funding and building sovereign large language models as a direct response to this supply-side vulnerability.
