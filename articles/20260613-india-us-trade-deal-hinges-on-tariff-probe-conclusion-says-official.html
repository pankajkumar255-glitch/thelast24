India-US Trade Deal Hinges on Tariff Probe Conclusion, Says Official

What happened

Reuters reports that a senior Indian official stated the India-US trade deal cannot be finalised until the United States completes its ongoing tariff probe. NDTV separately reports that a specific US trade law is holding up the agreement, adding another layer of complexity to negotiations. The dual disclosures confirm that the deal, long anticipated as a landmark economic pact, faces both legal and procedural hurdles on the American side. No revised timeline for conclusion has been officially stated by either government.

The context

India and the United States have been negotiating a bilateral trade deal for several years, with talks gaining fresh momentum after Prime Minister Narendra Modi's meetings with US leadership. The US has periodically launched tariff investigations under domestic trade laws — including Section 301 and similar statutes — that can restrict the conclusion of new trade agreements until probes are resolved. India is one of the US's largest trading partners, with bilateral trade exceeding $190 billion annually. American tariffs on Indian goods, including the 'reciprocal tariffs' announced in 2025, have added urgency to sealing a deal. Previous deadlines for an early harvest or interim agreement have slipped multiple times.

Why it matters

For Indian exporters in sectors like IT services, gems and jewellery, and pharmaceuticals, a trade deal would mean lower tariffs and better market access in the world's largest economy. Every month of delay costs Indian businesses competitive ground against rivals from other countries that may secure preferential US access first. Indian consumers and the broader economy also stand to benefit from reciprocal access, as a deal could attract more US investment into India's manufacturing sector.

What's next

Expect both governments to continue back-channel negotiations even as the US tariff probe runs its course, with officials likely to signal progress through diplomatic statements. Watch for the conclusion of the US probe as the clearest trigger for a fresh round of high-level trade talks and a possible deal announcement.
