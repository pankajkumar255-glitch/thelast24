Supreme Court Halts All High Court Proceedings on Transgender Amendment Act

What happened

NDTV, The New Indian Express, Hindustan Times, and Deccan Herald all report that the Supreme Court has stayed High Court proceedings challenging the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Amendment Act 2026 and issued notices on transfer pleas. The bench noted "scattered opinion" across courts as justification for centralising the matter. The court will now examine whether challenges to the amendment should be heard directly by the Supreme Court.

The context

The Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act was originally passed in 2019 and has been repeatedly criticised by activists and legal scholars for falling short of the Supreme Court's landmark 2014 NALSA judgment, which recognised transgender persons as a third gender. Subsequent amendments have continued to draw legal challenges. Multiple High Courts hearing simultaneous petitions risked producing divergent interpretations of the same law.

Why it matters

The amendment directly affects the legal identity, welfare entitlements, and protection from discrimination of India's transgender community. A Supreme Court-led consolidation could either strengthen or weaken those protections depending on the eventual ruling.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to set a hearing schedule for the consolidated transfer petitions and determine whether it will examine the constitutional validity of the 2026 amendment.
