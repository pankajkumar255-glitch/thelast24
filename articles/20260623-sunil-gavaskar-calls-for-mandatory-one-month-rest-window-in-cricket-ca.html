Sunil Gavaskar calls for mandatory one-month rest window in cricket calendar to address player burnout

Key facts

  • Gavaskar calls for a mandatory one-month rest window in the cricket calendar
  • He links player rotation directly to the 'cheapening' of cricket
  • His comments target the congested international cricket schedule

Sunil Gavaskar has called for a structured one-month rest window to be made mandatory in the cricket calendar, arguing that the punishing schedule is directly responsible for the player rotation policies that he believes are cheapening the game. The batting great's intervention brings renewed attention to one of modern cricket's most contentious issues.

Gavaskar linked the two problems explicitly — the absence of a built-in rest period forces team managements to rotate players to protect their fitness, which in turn leads to weakened line-ups and a dilution of the competition's prestige. The iconic opener argued that without a genuine break enforced at the administrative level, nothing will change for players or for the integrity of the sport.

The concern about player burnout is not new, but it has intensified as the cricket calendar has grown more congested with bilateral series, multi-team tournaments and franchise leagues running back-to-back across the year. Several Indian stars have been rested for key series in recent years, drawing both understanding and criticism from fans who want to see the best players on the field at all times.

Gavaskar's proposal — a formal, calendar-wide one-month window — goes beyond individual board decisions and would require coordination between the ICC and member boards. Whether administrators heed the advice of one of cricket's most respected voices remains to be seen, but the call is likely to reopen debate about how the sport manages its biggest assets.
