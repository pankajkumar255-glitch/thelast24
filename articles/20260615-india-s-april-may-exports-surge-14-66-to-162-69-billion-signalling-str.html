India's April-May Exports Surge 14.66% to $162.69 Billion, Signalling Strong Start to FY27

What happened

PIB (Press Information Bureau) released government estimates showing cumulative merchandise and services exports for April-May 2026-27 at $162.69 billion, compared to $141.89 billion in April-May 2025-26, representing a growth of 14.66%. The release highlights the strong early momentum in India's trade performance for FY27.

The context

India's export sector encompasses merchandise goods such as engineering products, gems and jewellery, chemicals, and pharmaceuticals, as well as a large services component dominated by IT and software exports. The government tracks these figures monthly as part of its foreign trade policy assessment. Double-digit export growth in the first two months of a financial year is considered a strong indicator for the full year.

Why it matters

Strong export growth boosts India's foreign exchange reserves, supports the rupee, and sustains employment in export-oriented industries from textiles to technology. It also strengthens India's negotiating hand in ongoing trade talks with the US and other partners.

What's next

Expect the government to cite these numbers as evidence of export resilience when the June trade data is released next month, and in the context of the India-US trade negotiations.
