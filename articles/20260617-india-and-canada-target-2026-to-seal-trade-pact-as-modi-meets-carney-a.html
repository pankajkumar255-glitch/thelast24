India and Canada Target 2026 to Seal Trade Pact as Modi Meets Carney at G7

Key facts

  • Modi and Carney met at G7 summit
  • Both sides target 2026 to conclude India-Canada FTA talks
  • Talks had been suspended since September 2023 diplomatic fallout
  • Canadian PM Carney replaced Trudeau in 2025

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that Prime Minister Modi met Canadian PM Carney at the G7 summit, with both sides agreeing to aim to conclude free trade agreement talks by 2026. The meeting represents the most significant diplomatic engagement between the two leaders since India-Canada relations deteriorated sharply in late 2023.

The context

India-Canada FTA negotiations, known as the Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA), were launched in 2022 but were suspended in September 2023 after Canadian PM Trudeau alleged Indian government involvement in the killing of Khalistani separatist Hardeep Singh Nijjar — allegations India strongly denied. Mark Carney replaced Trudeau as Canadian PM in 2025, opening a window for reset.

Why it matters

Canada is a significant destination for Indian exports and a major source of foreign investment, particularly in the insurance and resources sectors; a trade deal could open up new channels for both. For the large Indian diaspora in Canada, improved ties would ease business and consular relations.

What's next

Expect formal resumption of CEPA negotiating rounds between Indian and Canadian trade officials in the coming months, with both sides working toward the 2026 deadline.
