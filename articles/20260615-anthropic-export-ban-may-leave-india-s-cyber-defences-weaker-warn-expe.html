Anthropic Export Ban May Leave India's Cyber Defences Weaker, Warn Experts

What happened

Indiatimes reports that experts have raised alarms that the US 'export ban' on Anthropic models could weaken India's cyber defence ecosystem. The restrictions limit access to some of the most capable AI tools currently used in threat detection and security operations, potentially creating gaps that adversaries could exploit.

The context

India ranks among the most targeted countries globally for cyberattacks, with critical sectors including banking, healthcare, and power grids facing frequent intrusions. AI-powered security tools, including those built on Anthropic's Claude, have become integral to enterprise and government cybersecurity strategies in recent years. Export controls that cut off access to state-of-the-art AI models can degrade the effectiveness of these defences.

Why it matters

Downgraded cyber defences leave Indian financial systems, personal data, and critical infrastructure more exposed to sophisticated attacks. The episode also highlights how geopolitical AI restrictions can create unintended national security risks for third-party nations like India.

What's next

Expect India's CERT-In and cybersecurity agencies to assess alternative AI-powered security platforms and expedite procurement or development of domestically built solutions.
