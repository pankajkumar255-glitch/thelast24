Is India producing more graduates than its economy can absorb?

Key facts

  • India produces millions of graduates annually across universities and colleges
  • Graduate unemployment and underemployment remain persistently high
  • Skills mismatch between graduates and available jobs is a core concern
  • Slower hiring in IT, banking, and manufacturing compounds the problem

The Hindu examines a pressing structural challenge facing India's economy: whether the country is producing far more university graduates than the job market can meaningfully absorb. India has dramatically expanded its higher education infrastructure over the past two decades, adding hundreds of universities and engineering and management colleges, resulting in millions of new graduates entering the workforce every year.

Yet data on graduate unemployment and underemployment — where degree-holders take up jobs well below their qualification level — suggests a significant mismatch. Sectors that typically absorb large numbers of graduates, such as IT services, banking, and manufacturing, have seen slower hiring or, in some cases, layoffs, while the informal economy continues to dominate actual employment. The quality of graduates from many institutions is also a recurring concern raised by industry.

For an everyday Indian reader, the stakes are immediate: families across income levels sacrifice significantly to fund children's college education in the belief that it secures a better livelihood. If the economy cannot generate enough quality jobs for the educated, the return on that investment diminishes sharply, with consequences not just for individual households but for India's broader demographic dividend ambitions.
