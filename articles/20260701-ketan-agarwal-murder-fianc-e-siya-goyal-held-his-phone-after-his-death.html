Ketan Agarwal Murder: Fiancée Siya Goyal Held His Phone After His Death, Police Probe Tampering

Key facts

  • Siya Goyal retained Ketan Agarwal's phone after his murder
  • Police suspect possible evidence tampering with the phone
  • Agarwal had suspected an affair and sought a background check on Goyal
  • Gait analysis technology being used to identify suspects
  • Accused Chetan Chaudhary taken to Lohagad fort for crime scene recreation

The murder investigation of Ketan Agarwal has taken a critical turn, with police revealing that his fiancée Siya Goyal retained his mobile phone for a period after his death — a detail that investigators are now treating as a potential evidence-tampering angle. The phone, which could contain crucial communications and location data, is at the centre of what appears to be a rapidly widening probe.

Earlier revelations from the investigation suggest the relationship between Agarwal and Goyal was under strain well before his killing. Police sources indicate that Agarwal had suspected his fiancée of having an affair and had even sought a background check on her, pointing to deep mistrust in the months leading up to his death. A video from roughly a month before the murder, showing Agarwal dancing with Goyal's parents, has also surfaced — highlighting the outwardly normal facade of the relationship.

In a forensically significant development, investigators are deploying gait analysis technology to help identify suspects captured on surveillance footage. The technique, which involves matching an individual's distinctive walking pattern against camera recordings, could provide key evidence that is harder to manipulate than conventional digital trails.

Pune police have also taken another accused, Chetan Chaudhary, to the Lohagad fort to recreate the crime scene — a standard investigative procedure used to verify and challenge the accounts of accused individuals. With multiple forensic and investigative threads running in parallel, the Ketan Agarwal case is developing into one of the most closely watched criminal investigations in recent months.
