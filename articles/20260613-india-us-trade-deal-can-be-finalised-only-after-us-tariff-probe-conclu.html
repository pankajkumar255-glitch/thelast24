India-US Trade Deal Can Be Finalised Only After US Tariff Probe Concludes, Says Official

What happened

Reuters reports that an official has stated the India-US trade deal can only be finalised after a US tariff probe concludes. The statement signals that a structural legal process in the United States must run its course before any bilateral agreement can be formally signed. NDTV had earlier reported on a specific US trade law holding up the deal, providing further context to the official's remarks. The probe in question relates to US trade law provisions that require investigation before trade concessions or agreements can be formalised. No specific timeline for the probe's conclusion was given in the reports.

The context

India and the United States have been engaged in bilateral trade negotiations for several years, with both governments expressing intent to significantly expand two-way trade. The US has deployed several trade law tools — including Section 232 (national security tariffs) and Section 301 (unfair trade practices) — that can complicate or delay free trade agreements. India has historically been cautious about opening its agricultural and dairy sectors, while the US has pushed for greater market access in these and other areas. The two countries had a prolonged dispute at the World Trade Organisation over retaliatory tariffs before partially resolving it. PM Modi's Washington visits and US presidential engagement have repeatedly highlighted trade as a priority bilateral issue.

Why it matters

A finalised India-US trade deal could be transformative for Indian exporters in sectors like IT services, gems and jewellery, textiles, and pharmaceuticals, potentially generating significant employment. Delays caused by US legal processes mean Indian businesses face continued tariff uncertainty, affecting pricing and competitiveness in the American market. For Indian consumers, a deal could eventually lower prices on imported American goods. The probe's conclusion — and what emerges from it — could also set the terms of the deal in ways that either favour or constrain Indian industry.

What's next

Expect the Indian trade ministry and commerce officials to monitor the US tariff probe timeline closely and continue back-channel negotiations in the interim. The deal's finalisation will likely be announced at a high-level bilateral summit, making the scheduling of any Modi-Trump meeting a key signal to watch.
