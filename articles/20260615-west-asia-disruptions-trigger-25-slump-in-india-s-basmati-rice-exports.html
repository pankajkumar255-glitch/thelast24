West Asia disruptions trigger 25% slump in India's basmati rice exports; Gulf holds 40-lakh tonne share

What happened

The Indian Express reports that West Asia disruptions have triggered a 25% slump in India's basmati rice exports. The Gulf accounts for 40 lakh tonnes of India's 60 lakh tonne basmati trade, underlining how concentrated the export market is. The disruption has hit the farm-to-export supply chain hard during an already sensitive agricultural period.

The context

India is the world's largest exporter of basmati rice, with the Gulf Cooperation Council countries being the dominant buyers. Punjab and Haryana are the primary growing states, and basmati exports are a crucial source of income for millions of farmers and rice millers. Any prolonged West Asian instability directly shrinks the market for this premium export crop.

Why it matters

A sustained export slump could depress farm-gate prices for basmati paddy, squeezing farmer incomes in north India's key agrarian states. It also dents India's agricultural export earnings at a time when the government is trying to widen the trade surplus.

What's next

Expect basmati export volumes to recover if the Iran-US peace deal stabilises Gulf trade routes and shipping resumes normally in the coming weeks.
