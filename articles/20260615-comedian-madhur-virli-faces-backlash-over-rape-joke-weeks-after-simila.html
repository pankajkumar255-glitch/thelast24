Comedian Madhur Virli faces backlash over rape joke, weeks after similar row over Pranit More

What happened

The Indian Express reports that comedian Madhur Virli has been slammed on social media for making a rape joke during a performance, drawing widespread criticism. The report notes this follows a recent similar controversy involving fellow comedian Pranit More, indicating a broader pattern of such incidents in the Indian stand-up comedy space.

The context

Indian stand-up comedy has grown into a major industry over the last decade, with comedians building large online followings. However, the space has repeatedly faced controversies over offensive jokes, particularly those targeting women. These incidents have sparked ongoing public debates about the limits of free speech versus the normalisation of harmful attitudes, and have at times led to legal action.

Why it matters

Repeated incidents of comedians making jokes about sexual violence contribute to public discourse on gender safety and can have real social consequences, particularly for younger audiences who consume such content online.

What's next

Expect further social media pressure on Virli and potentially on the platforms hosting his content, with calls for an apology or removal of the material likely to mount.
