Aamir Khan to Attend IFFM Curtain Raiser in Melbourne as Lagaan Turns 25

Key facts

  • Aamir Khan to attend IFFM curtain raiser in Melbourne
  • Lagaan completes 25 years since its 2001 release
  • Lagaan was nominated for the Oscar for Best Foreign Language Film

Aamir Khan will travel to Melbourne to attend the Indian Film Festival of Melbourne's curtain raiser event, marking 25 years of Lagaan — the film that brought Indian cinema one of its most celebrated Oscar nominations and captured audiences across the world.

Released in 2001, Lagaan was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film and became a phenomenon far beyond Bollywood's usual reach. The film's sweeping narrative of a village challenging British colonial rule through a cricket match resonated with audiences globally, cementing Aamir Khan's reputation as one of Hindi cinema's most ambitious and meticulous storytellers.

The Indian Film Festival of Melbourne, one of the most prominent platforms for Indian cinema in the Southern Hemisphere, has chosen the Lagaan anniversary as its marquee occasion for the curtain raiser, reflecting the film's enduring significance. Aamir Khan's in-person attendance signals the importance both the festival and the star attach to this milestone.

The 25th anniversary comes at a moment when nostalgia for early-2000s Bollywood classics is running high among Indian audiences at home and in the diaspora. For millions who grew up watching Lagaan, the occasion is as much a celebration of an era as of a film.
