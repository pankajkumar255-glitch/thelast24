Jemimah Rodrigues on India's T20 World Cup and Olympic ambitions: 'We believe we can do it'

What happened

Olympics.com reports that Jemimah Rodrigues has spoken about the 'strong belief' fuelling India's ambitions at the T20 World Cup and as cricket prepares for its Olympic debut. Rodrigues highlighted the team's collective confidence as a driving force behind their goals.

The context

Women's T20 cricket is set to make its Olympic debut at the 2028 Los Angeles Games, marking a watershed moment for the sport. India's women's team has been a rising force in global cricket, with players like Rodrigues, Smriti Mandhana, and Deepti Sharma among its marquee names. The T20 World Cup remains the sport's premier tournament ahead of that Olympic milestone.

Why it matters

An Olympic medal in cricket would be a historic first for India and could dramatically boost funding, viewership, and infrastructure for women's cricket across the country. Rodrigues's public optimism signals a confident, motivated squad heading into marquee tournaments.

What's next

Expect India's women's cricket team to be under intense scrutiny in upcoming T20 internationals as selectors finalize squads for the World Cup and Olympic qualifying rounds.
