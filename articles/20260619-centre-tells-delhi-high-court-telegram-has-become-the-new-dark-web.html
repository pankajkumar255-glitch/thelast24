Centre tells Delhi High Court: Telegram has become the 'new dark web'

Key facts

  • Centre told Delhi High Court Telegram is the 'new dark web'
  • Government flagged Telegram's use for drug trafficking and financial fraud
  • Telegram has historically resisted sharing user data with governments
  • Statement made during ongoing Delhi High Court proceedings

The Hindu reports that the Centre told the Delhi High Court that Telegram has become the 'new dark web', marking one of the Indian government's sharpest public condemnations of the messaging platform to date. The characterisation came during proceedings in the High Court, with the government highlighting how the platform is allegedly being used for drug trafficking, financial scams, and the spread of illegal content beyond the reach of law enforcement.

Telegram, which boasts hundreds of millions of users globally and a large Indian user base, has long been criticised by governments for its resistance to sharing user data with authorities. Unlike WhatsApp or other platforms that have engaged with Indian regulators under the IT Rules 2021, Telegram has maintained a more opaque stance toward government requests.

The Centre's 'new dark web' framing before a High Court dramatically raises the stakes for how Indian regulators approach the platform. It could foreshadow demands for stricter compliance, potential restrictions, or legal action — similar to pressure that has been applied to other Big Tech firms. For everyday Indians who rely on Telegram for news channels, community groups, and even business communication, the outcome of these proceedings could have tangible consequences for access and privacy.
