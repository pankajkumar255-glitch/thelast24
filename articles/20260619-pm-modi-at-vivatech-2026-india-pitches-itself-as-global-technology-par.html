PM Modi at VivaTech 2026: India pitches itself as global technology partner, calls for democratising AI

Key facts

  • Modi spoke at VivaTech 2026 in Paris, a major European tech conference
  • Declared AI for India means 'All Inclusive' — technology must be democratised
  • Called on global partners to 'work with India' in technology
  • Framed India as a trusted global technology partner with growing digital scale

NDTV and The Hindu report that Prime Minister Narendra Modi addressed VivaTech 2026 in Paris, one of Europe's largest technology conferences, making a high-profile pitch for India as a global technology collaborator. Modi declared that for India, AI stands for 'All Inclusive', framing artificial intelligence as a tool for broad-based social progress rather than elite adoption. He called on the world to 'work with India' and stressed that technology can lead to genuine progress only when it is democratised.

Modi underscored India's emergence as a trusted global partner, pointing to the country's rapid digital growth — from UPI's scale to its expanding startup ecosystem — as evidence of India's readiness to co-shape the global technology order. The address at VivaTech came amid India's active courtship of European investment and technology partnerships, with France being a key strategic ally.

The Firstpost also noted Modi's framing of India's global role, highlighting that the speech positioned India not merely as a technology consumer but as a co-creator of global digital standards. For everyday Indians, the significance lies in whether such high-level diplomacy translates into real investment in domestic AI infrastructure, jobs in the technology sector, and affordable access to emerging technologies.
