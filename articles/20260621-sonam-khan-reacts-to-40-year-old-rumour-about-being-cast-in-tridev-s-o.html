Sonam Khan Reacts to 40-Year-Old Rumour About Being Cast in Tridev's 'Oye Oye'

Key facts

  • Rumour claims Sonam Khan was originally cast in 'Oye Oye' from Tridev (1989)
  • The rumour is approximately 40 years old
  • 'Oye Oye' is one of Bollywood's most iconic late-1980s dance numbers
  • Sonam Khan addressed the rumour publicly via The Indian Express

Sonam Khan has broken her silence on one of Hindi film industry's more persistent backstage rumours — that she was originally meant to feature in 'Oye Oye', the electrifying dance number from the 1989 hit Tridev that became a defining pop-culture moment of late-1980s Bollywood.

The rumour, apparently four decades old, has continued to circulate in film trivia circles and on social media, prompting Khan to finally react. While the specifics of her response — whether she confirmed, denied, or offered a more nuanced account — were not fully detailed in the headline, the fact that she chose to address it publicly suggests the claim has been persistent enough to warrant a direct reply.

'Oye Oye' from Tridev, directed by Rajiv Rai, is one of the most recognisable tracks of its era, associated in popular memory with Sangeeta Bijlani's on-screen performance. Behind-the-scenes casting decisions from that golden period of Bollywood are rarely documented, making such recollections particularly valuable for film historians and enthusiasts.

The exchange is a reminder of how long the rumour mill of Hindi cinema's golden era continues to churn, with social media now giving veterans a direct platform to correct — or complicate — the record on stories that have taken on a life of their own over decades.
