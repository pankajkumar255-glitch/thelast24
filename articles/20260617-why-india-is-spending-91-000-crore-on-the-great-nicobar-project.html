Why India Is Spending ₹91,000 Crore on the Great Nicobar Project

What happened

The Hindu reports on India's ₹91,000 crore development project for Great Nicobar Island, examining the strategic, economic, and environmental reasoning behind one of the country's most ambitious and controversial infrastructure undertakings. The project is being executed under the Andaman and Nicobar Islands Integrated Development Corporation.

The context

Great Nicobar Island sits just 90 nautical miles from the Strait of Malacca, one of the world's busiest shipping lanes, and close to the Sunda Strait — making it a position of immense naval and commercial value. India's government has positioned the project as critical to establishing a presence in the Indian Ocean Region at a time of intensifying competition with China, which has been deepening port and base access across Sri Lanka, Myanmar, and Pakistan. The project has also drawn significant criticism from environmentalists over its impact on a biodiversity-rich, protected ecosystem.

Why it matters

The ₹91,000 crore outlay represents a major sovereign bet on maritime infrastructure and strategic positioning that will shape India's naval reach and trade connectivity in the Indian Ocean for generations. It also sets a precedent for how India balances developmental ambitions against environmental protection in ecologically sensitive zones.

What's next

Expect continued legal and environmental scrutiny of the project's clearances alongside accelerated construction timelines as India pushes to establish operational capability at Great Nicobar before the end of the decade.
