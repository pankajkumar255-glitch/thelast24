Imtiaz Ali's New Show Main Vaapas Aaunga Explores India's Crisis of Identity and Belonging

What happened

The Indian Express reviews Imtiaz Ali's Main Vaapas Aaunga, describing it as a work that chronicles India's danger of forgetting its own cultural and social identity. The review positions the project as one of Ali's more ambitious and thematically weighty undertakings.

The context

Imtiaz Ali is one of Hindi cinema's most celebrated auteurs, known for films like Jab We Met, Rockstar, and Highway, which frequently explore rootlessness and self-discovery. Main Vaapas Aaunga marks his foray into a format or story that directly interrogates collective Indian identity, a theme that carries particular resonance in the current socio-political climate.

Why it matters

A major Indian filmmaker engaging seriously with questions of national identity and cultural amnesia through popular media can shape how audiences reflect on these issues. It also signals a growing trend of Indian OTT and screen content taking on deeper social themes.

What's next

Expect audience and critical response to the show to fuel debate about the cultural and political ideas it raises, with streaming numbers likely to be closely watched.
