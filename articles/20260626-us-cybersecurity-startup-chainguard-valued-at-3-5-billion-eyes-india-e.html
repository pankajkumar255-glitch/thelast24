US cybersecurity startup Chainguard, valued at $3.5 billion, eyes India expansion with new hires and partnerships

Key facts

  • Chainguard valued at $3.5 billion
  • US cybersecurity startup expanding hiring in India
  • Focuses on software supply chain security

Chainguard, the US cybersecurity startup valued at $3.5 billion, has set its sights on India as a key growth market, announcing plans to expand hiring and develop local partnerships as it looks to capitalise on surging demand for software supply chain security across the subcontinent, CNBC TV18 reported.

Chainguard's core focus — securing the software components, containers and open-source dependencies that underpin modern applications — addresses one of the fastest-growing threat vectors globally. High-profile software supply chain attacks in recent years have forced enterprises worldwide to scrutinise the security of third-party code embedded in their systems, and Indian firms operating in banking, technology and critical infrastructure are no exception.

By expanding into India, Chainguard joins a wave of global cybersecurity firms that have identified the country as both a significant customer base and a talent reservoir. India's large pool of software engineers and cloud architects, combined with growing regulatory pressure on data protection and cybersecurity compliance, creates a fertile environment for enterprise security vendors.

The company's $3.5 billion valuation reflects strong investor conviction in the software supply chain security market globally. Its India push — through local hiring and partnership-building — suggests it is moving beyond a sales presence toward embedding itself more deeply in the country's technology ecosystem, positioning for long-term growth as Indian enterprises mature their security practices.
