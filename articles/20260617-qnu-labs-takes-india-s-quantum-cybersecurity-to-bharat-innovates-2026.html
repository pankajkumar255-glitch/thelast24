QNu Labs Takes India's Quantum Cybersecurity to Bharat Innovates 2026, Signs Research Deal with Eindhoven University

What happened

Raksha Anirveda reports that QNu Labs represented India's quantum cybersecurity sector at the Bharat Innovates 2026 event in Nice, France, where it signed a research partnership deal with Eindhoven University of Technology. The collaboration is aimed at advancing quantum-secure communication technologies.

The context

QNu Labs, founded in Bengaluru, is a pioneer in quantum-safe cybersecurity in India, developing quantum key distribution (QKD) and post-quantum cryptography solutions. The Bharat Innovates 2026 event in Nice is an India-Europe deep-tech platform facilitating partnerships between Indian startups and European research institutions. India's National Quantum Mission, backed by over Rs 6,000 crore, has elevated quantum technology as a strategic priority.

Why it matters

Quantum cybersecurity will underpin the security of India's critical digital infrastructure — including financial systems, defence networks, and government data — in the coming decade. Indian firms building global research partnerships now are positioning India to be a technology creator rather than just a consumer in this space.

What's next

Expect more Indian quantum startups to forge European research and commercialisation partnerships as the National Quantum Mission funding flows through.
