Reliance unveils AI-first strategy at AGM 2026; Mukesh Ambani says India must become a global leader

Key facts

  • Reliance announced AI-first strategy at AGM 2026
  • Mukesh Ambani called for India to become a global AI leader
  • AI integration planned across Jio, retail, and energy verticals

The Indian Express reports that Reliance Industries used its AGM 2026 platform to announce a sweeping AI-first strategy, with Chairman Mukesh Ambani declaring that India must become a global leader in artificial intelligence. The pivot signals that AI will now be woven into the core of Reliance's operations — spanning Jio's telecom network, its vast retail arm, and its energy businesses.

Ambani's statements at the AGM underline a broader ambition: rather than merely adopting AI tools developed elsewhere, Reliance intends to help build indigenous AI capabilities for India. The AGM has historically served as a launchpad for major Reliance announcements — from Jio's 2016 telecom disruption to subsequent 5G rollouts — making this AI declaration a significant strategic signal.

For hundreds of millions of Indians who use Jio for connectivity and Reliance Retail for everyday shopping, an AI-driven transformation at Reliance could translate into new consumer services, smarter logistics, and potentially more affordable access to AI-powered tools. It also raises the stakes in India's competition with global tech giants seeking to dominate the AI era.
