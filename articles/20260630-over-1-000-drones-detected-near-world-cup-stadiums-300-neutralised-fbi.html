Over 1,000 Drones Detected Near World Cup Stadiums, 300 Neutralised: FBI

Key facts

  • Over 1,000 drones detected near World Cup stadiums, per FBI
  • Around 300 drones were neutralised by security forces
  • Highlights growing drone threat at major international events

The FBI has disclosed that more than 1,000 drones were detected in the vicinity of World Cup stadiums, with approximately 300 of them neutralised by counter-drone systems deployed for the tournament. The revelation offers an unprecedented glimpse into the scale of aerial security threats facing one of the world's largest sporting events.

The 2025 FIFA World Cup, being hosted across cities in the United States, Canada, and Mexico, has required a massive mobilisation of security resources — on the ground, in the air, and in cyberspace. The drone threat represents a particular challenge because commercially available unmanned aerial vehicles have become cheap, easily modified, and difficult to intercept at scale without sophisticated detection and jamming technology.

Security agencies have been deploying a combination of radar-based detection systems, radio-frequency jammers, and in some cases kinetic countermeasures to neutralise drones that violate restricted airspace around stadium perimeters. The fact that over 1,000 were detected — and that 300 required active neutralisation — suggests that the threat is not merely theoretical but an active and ongoing operational challenge.

The disclosure carries lessons well beyond American borders. As India prepares to host the 2036 Olympic Games and continues to stage major cricket and other sporting events, the drone security framework being developed and tested at the World Cup will become an important reference point for Indian security planners.
