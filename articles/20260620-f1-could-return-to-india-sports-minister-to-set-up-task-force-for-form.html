F1 could return to India: Sports Minister to set up task force for Formula 1 revival

Key facts

  • Sports Minister to set up task force for F1 revival in India
  • India last hosted F1 at Buddh International Circuit, Greater Noida, in 2013
  • F1 was dropped over entertainment tax disputes and infrastructure issues
  • India hosted 3 F1 races between 2011 and 2013

India Today reports that the Sports Minister has decided to establish a dedicated task force to explore and fast-track the revival of Formula 1 racing in India. The announcement marks the most concrete government step toward bringing the sport back since India's F1 chapter ended over a decade ago.

India hosted three Formula 1 Grand Prix races at the Buddh International Circuit in Greater Noida between 2011 and 2013, with Sebastian Vettel winning all three. The event was subsequently removed from the F1 calendar following disputes over entertainment tax levied on teams and organisers, as well as unresolved infrastructure concerns around the circuit.

A government-backed task force lends institutional weight to revival efforts that have surfaced periodically in recent years. For Indian motorsport fans and the broader economy, an F1 return would represent a massive boost — the race generates significant tourism revenue, international media attention, and positions India among elite global sporting destinations. The task force's composition and timeline will be keenly watched by promoters and F1's commercial rights holders.
