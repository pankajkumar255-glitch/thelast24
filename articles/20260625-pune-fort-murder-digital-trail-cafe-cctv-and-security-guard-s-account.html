Pune Fort Murder: Digital Trail, Cafe CCTV and Security Guard's Account Deepen Case Against Siya Goyal

Key facts

  • Siya Goyal accused of pushing fiancé Ketan Agarwal off Pune fort
  • CCTV footage shows Siya and boyfriend at cafe before the killing
  • Digital trail included 'secret signals' and a 'ghost point'
  • Siya's mother publicly says 'hang her if she's guilty'
  • Police questioned Siya and her lover together

New and damning details have emerged in the Pune fort murder case, in which 20-year-old Siya Goyal stands accused of pushing her fiancé Ketan Agarwal off a fort cliff. CCTV footage recovered from a cafe shows Siya and her boyfriend meeting just before the killing — footage that investigators say captures the pair in what appeared to be a deliberate pre-murder rendezvous.

The digital trail unearthed by police has added a chilling layer of premeditation to the alleged crime. Investigators say they tracked 'secret signals' and a location referred to as a 'ghost point' in the couple's digital communications, which they believe led directly to the execution of the alleged plot. The accused and her lover have since been questioned together by police.

In a remarkable turn, Siya Goyal's own mother told media that she had no inkling of any problem — the family had finalised the wedding after speaking to Siya — and added starkly that her daughter should be hanged if found guilty. The victim's father, equally devastated, questioned publicly why Siya could not simply have called off the engagement rather than resorting to murder. The security guard posted at the fort also revealed what Siya said immediately after Ketan fell, evidence that is likely to feature prominently in trial proceedings.

The case has drawn nationwide attention and reignited debate about digital surveillance as an investigative tool, the pressures of arranged marriages, and the justice system's capacity to swiftly try such cases. Ketan Agarwal's family and the public are now watching the legal proceedings closely.
