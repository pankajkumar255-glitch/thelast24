India-US Trade Deal Not Imminent at G7; USTR Greer to Visit India for Further Talks

What happened

Reuters, NDTV Profit, Moneycontrol, Times of India, and Deccan Herald all report that a trade deal between India and the United States will not be finalised or announced at the G7 summit. US officials confirmed that while Presidents Trump and PM Modi will discuss trade at the summit, no agreement is imminent. USTR Jamieson Greer is expected to travel to India following the G7 meeting to advance negotiations. A senior US official told Moneycontrol that a deal remains possible but requires more rounds of talks. Multiple outlets attribute this to outstanding differences on key trade issues between the two countries.

The context

India and the United States have been negotiating a bilateral trade deal for several years, with talks gaining fresh urgency after the Trump administration's renewed push for reciprocal tariffs in 2025. The US is India's largest trading partner, with bilateral trade exceeding $190 billion annually. India has faced US tariffs on steel, aluminium, and several other goods, and American officials have long pressed India to open its markets further, particularly in agriculture and digital trade. The G7 summit has been widely watched as a potential venue for a high-profile announcement given the presence of both leaders.

Why it matters

Without a trade deal, Indian exporters in sectors like gems and jewellery, textiles, IT services, and pharmaceuticals continue to operate under tariff uncertainty, affecting their global competitiveness. Domestic industries that fear import competition from a liberalised pact, especially agriculture, remain on edge. For everyday Indians, a deal could eventually lower prices on some imported goods and create new export-driven jobs, making its delay economically significant.

What's next

Expect USTR Greer's post-G7 visit to India to be the next critical milestone, with both governments likely to signal progress on specific sectors. Markets and industry bodies will watch closely for any announcement on tariff concessions or a framework agreement in the weeks following the summit.
