10 Years of Smart Cities Mission: How Technology Is Transforming Urban India

What happened

The Times of India reports on the 10th anniversary of the Smart Cities Mission, reviewing how technology-driven infrastructure has transformed urban governance and civic services in the 100 cities selected under the programme. The assessment covers a range of digital and physical interventions deployed over the decade.

The context

Launched in 2015 under the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs, the Smart Cities Mission allocated over Rs 48,000 crore to upgrade urban infrastructure using technology. The mission selected 100 cities through a competitive process, with projects spanning surveillance, mobility, water management, and e-governance. It remains one of the most ambitious urban technology programmes in the developing world.

Why it matters

A decade of smart city investment has directly improved traffic flow, reduced water wastage, and made civic services more accessible for hundreds of millions of urban Indians. The lessons from this mission will shape the next generation of urban infrastructure spending as India's cities continue to grow rapidly.

What's next

Expect the government to announce a refreshed urban technology policy building on Smart Cities Mission learnings, potentially with expanded funding for AI-driven city management.
