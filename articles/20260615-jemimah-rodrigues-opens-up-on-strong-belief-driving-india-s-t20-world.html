Jemimah Rodrigues opens up on strong belief driving India's T20 World Cup and Olympic dreams

What happened

Olympics.com has published an interview with Jemimah Rodrigues in which she discusses the 'strong belief' within the Indian women's cricket team as they pursue T20 World Cup glory and set their sights on competing at the Olympics. Rodrigues articulates the team's ambitions during what is a pivotal period for women's cricket globally.

The context

Cricket was approved for inclusion at the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics, marking a historic milestone for the sport. The Indian women's team has grown significantly in global stature, consistently competing in World Cup finals and semifinals. Jemimah Rodrigues is known for her aggressive batting and vocal presence both on and off the field.

Why it matters

India's participation in cricket at the Olympics would be one of the most watched sporting events in the country's history, given cricket's unrivalled popularity. A successful World Cup campaign would also further accelerate investment and viewership in women's cricket in India.

What's next

Expect Rodrigues and India's women's team to be closely followed through the T20 World Cup 2026, with Olympic squad selection discussions likely to intensify as 2028 approaches.
