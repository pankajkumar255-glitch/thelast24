Chiranjeevi, Not Manoj Bajpayee, Was Original Choice for The Family Man, Reveals Director Raj Nidimoru

Key facts

  • Chiranjeevi was original choice for The Family Man's lead role
  • Manoj Bajpayee was eventually cast as Srikant Tiwari
  • Revealed by director Raj Nidimoru (Raj & DK)
  • The Family Man premiered on Amazon Prime Video in 2019

The Indian Express reports that filmmaker Raj Nidimoru, one half of the Raj & DK duo behind The Family Man, has revealed that Telugu megastar Chiranjeevi was the original choice for the lead role of intelligence officer Srikant Tiwari in the Amazon Prime Video series — a role that ultimately went to Manoj Bajpayee.

The casting revelation highlights how different the show's creative trajectory could have been. Chiranjeevi is one of the biggest stars in Telugu cinema, with a mass-hero screen presence quite distinct from Bajpayee's grounded, middle-class portrayal of Tiwari. The show, which premiered in 2019, became a landmark moment for Indian OTT content and ran for two successful seasons.

Manoj Bajpayee's performance as the harried, underpaid NIA officer balancing national security missions with a struggling domestic life earned widespread critical acclaim and multiple awards. The Family Man is widely credited with establishing the viability of high-budget, serialised spy drama on Indian streaming platforms.

For fans of both stars, the disclosure raises compelling questions about how the show's tone, reach, and identity might have differed had the original casting gone ahead — underscoring how pivotal a single casting decision can be to a series' legacy.
