Dozens Protest Outside Iran Foreign Ministry Against Peace Deal With US

What happened

NDTV reports that dozens of protesters demonstrated outside Iran's Foreign Ministry, opposing a potential peace deal between Iran and the United States. The protests reflect the hardline opposition within Iran to any diplomatic rapprochement with Washington.

The context

Iran's political landscape is deeply divided between pragmatists who favour engagement with the West to ease crippling sanctions, and hardliners — including elements of the Revolutionary Guards — who regard the US as an existential adversary. Public protests outside the Foreign Ministry are relatively rare and signal the intensity of opposition to the current talks, even as President Trump expresses optimism about a deal being imminent.

Why it matters

If domestic opposition in Iran scuttles or significantly delays a deal, the resulting uncertainty could keep oil prices elevated — a direct concern for India, which imports a large share of its energy needs from the wider Gulf region.

What's next

Expect Iranian government officials to face mounting pressure to either defend the negotiations publicly or scale back their ambitions in any deal to placate domestic hardliners.
