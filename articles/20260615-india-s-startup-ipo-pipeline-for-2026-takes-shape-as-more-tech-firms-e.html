India's Startup IPO Pipeline for 2026 Takes Shape as More Tech Firms Eye Public Markets

What happened

Inc42 reports on its ongoing Indian Startup IPO Tracker for 2026, documenting the pipeline of technology startups that have filed or are preparing to file draft red herring prospectuses with SEBI for public listings on Indian exchanges this year. The tracker reflects a broad trend of venture-backed startups from the 2015-2020 boom era reaching the stage where founders and investors are seeking liquidity through public markets.

The context

India's startup IPO market has been active since Zomato's landmark 2021 listing, which was followed by a mix of successes and high-profile stumbles such as Paytm's troubled debut. SEBI has since tightened disclosure norms for new-age tech companies, requiring clearer paths to profitability. The 2026 pipeline is expected to be shaped by improved profitability metrics across the sector and a more discerning retail investor base that learned hard lessons from earlier listings.

Why it matters

Startup IPOs give ordinary retail investors access to fast-growing technology companies at an earlier stage than was previously possible in India, but they also carry significant risk if companies are not yet profitable. The 2026 pipeline will test whether Indian startups have genuinely matured financially or are again rushing to market to give early investors an exit.

What's next

Expect SEBI to scrutinise 2026 DRHP filings closely for profitability disclosures, with the first major listings likely to set the tone for retail investor sentiment toward the entire startup IPO wave.
