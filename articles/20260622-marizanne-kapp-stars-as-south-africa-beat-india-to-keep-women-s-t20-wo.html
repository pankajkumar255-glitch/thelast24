Marizanne Kapp stars as South Africa beat India to keep Women's T20 World Cup hopes alive

Key facts

  • South Africa beat India — India's first loss of Women's T20 World Cup 2026
  • Marizanne Kapp was South Africa's standout performer
  • Win keeps South Africa's semifinal qualification hopes alive
  • India had been unbeaten in the tournament before this match

South Africa ended India's unbeaten run at the Women's T20 World Cup 2026, beating them in a group-stage match that shifts the semifinal picture for both sides. Marizanne Kapp was the architect of South Africa's victory, delivering a match-winning performance that gave her team a lifeline in the tournament.

India had entered the fixture as the stronger side on paper, having won their earlier group games. But South Africa produced a disciplined and clinical display, with Kapp's contribution — whether with bat, ball, or both — proving the decisive difference. The defeat is India's first of the tournament and comes at a critical juncture in the group stage.

For South Africa, the win is more than a confidence boost — it keeps their semifinal qualification bid firmly alive. A loss would have effectively ended their campaign, making this a must-win that they delivered on under pressure. The result injects fresh uncertainty into what had looked like a straightforward group for India.

India now need to reassess their qualification arithmetic. Depending on other results in the group, they may need to win their remaining match and hope other fixtures fall their way. The loss also serves as a warning that the Indian women's side cannot afford complacency in a competitive tournament field.

The match underlines South Africa's growing strength in women's cricket, with Kapp — one of the most experienced and dangerous all-rounders in the game — reminding everyone why she remains a match-winner on the biggest stages.
