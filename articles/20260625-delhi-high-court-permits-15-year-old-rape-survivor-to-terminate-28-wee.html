Delhi High Court Permits 15-Year-Old Rape Survivor to Terminate 28-Week Pregnancy

Key facts

  • Delhi High Court allowed a 15-year-old rape survivor to terminate pregnancy
  • Pregnancy was at 28 weeks — beyond standard statutory limit
  • Court exercised exceptional jurisdiction for minor rape victims
  • Termination to be conducted at a government hospital under medical supervision

The Delhi High Court has allowed a 15-year-old survivor of rape to terminate her 28-week pregnancy, exercising its constitutional powers to grant relief beyond the standard thresholds under Indian abortion law. The Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act sets limits on gestational age for terminations, but courts have consistently held that in exceptional cases — particularly involving minors and rape survivors — judicial intervention is warranted in the interest of the woman's physical and mental health.

The case highlights the ongoing challenge in India's legal and healthcare systems of ensuring that vulnerable survivors access timely reproductive healthcare. Delays in reporting, fear of social stigma, and procedural bottlenecks frequently mean that pregnancies resulting from sexual violence are discovered or acted upon later than would be ideal, pushing cases into the higher gestational range where judicial sanction becomes necessary.

The High Court's order, which followed a medical examination to assess the viability and risks of the procedure, provides the teenager with immediate access to the termination at a government hospital under medical supervision. Such orders are typically accompanied by directions for anonymity to protect the minor's identity.

Child rights advocates and legal experts have welcomed the ruling while noting that the systemic delays that force minors into High Court proceedings must be addressed. Faster identification, legal aid, and medical response for minor rape survivors remain urgent priorities if such cases are to be resolved without courts having to intervene at advanced stages of pregnancy.
