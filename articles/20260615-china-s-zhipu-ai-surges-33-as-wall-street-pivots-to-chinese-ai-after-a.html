China's Zhipu AI Surges 33% as Wall Street Pivots to Chinese AI After Anthropic Curbs

What happened

CNBC reports that Zhipu AI's stock surged 33% as Wall Street began raising bets on Chinese AI companies following US export curbs on Anthropic's models. Investors see the restrictions as opening a competitive window for Chinese AI developers who can now offer frontier model capabilities to markets Anthropic has been barred from serving.

The context

Zhipu AI is one of China's best-funded AI startups, backed by Tencent and Alibaba, and a developer of the GLM series of large language models. The US-China technology rivalry has intensified around AI, with Washington using export controls as a key tool. The Anthropic curbs mark an escalation of this policy beyond chips and hardware into AI software and models themselves.

Why it matters

If Chinese AI platforms gain ground in markets vacated by US companies, it shifts the global AI competitive landscape and raises questions about data privacy and security for Indian businesses that may turn to those alternatives. It also signals that US export policy could accelerate rather than slow China's AI global footprint.

What's next

Expect further volatility in global AI stocks and Indian policymakers to watch closely whether Chinese AI vendors begin actively courting Indian enterprise clients left without Anthropic access.
