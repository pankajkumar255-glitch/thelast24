India's Growth Story Faces Its Toughest Test Yet in Modi's Third Term, Says CNBC

What happened

CNBC reports that India's economic growth trajectory is facing what it characterises as its toughest test in Modi's third term. The assessment draws attention to the intersection of global trade disruptions, slowing domestic investment, and consumption pressures that are simultaneously weighing on the Indian economy. The report from a globally watched financial broadcaster adds international heft to concerns that domestic analysts have also been raising. It comes at a time when multiple forecasters, including BMI, have trimmed India's medium-term growth projections.

The context

Modi's third term, which began in June 2024, inherited an economy that had been one of the world's fastest-growing but was already showing signs of fatigue at the household consumption level. Private investment revival has remained elusive despite strong government capital expenditure. Externally, the US tariff shock of 2025 has added a significant headwind. India's growth model has historically relied on a combination of domestic consumption, services exports (particularly IT), and more recently, manufacturing ambitions under the Production Linked Incentive scheme.

Why it matters

A sustained slowdown in India's growth trajectory would have cascading effects: fewer formal jobs, pressure on government finances, weaker corporate earnings, and reduced capacity to fund the social and infrastructure spending that underpins quality of life for ordinary Indians. International perception of India's growth story also affects foreign direct investment inflows, which fund factories and create employment.

What's next

Expect the government to accelerate policy responses, potentially including additional fiscal stimulus, interest rate cuts by the RBI, and efforts to fast-track the US trade deal. Watch for the Union Budget mid-year review and RBI's next Monetary Policy Committee meeting as key signposts.
