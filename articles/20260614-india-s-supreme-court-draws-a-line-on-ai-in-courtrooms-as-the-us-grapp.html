India's Supreme Court Draws a Line on AI in Courtrooms as the US Grapples with Crisis

What happened

NDTV Profit reports that India's Supreme Court is in the process of drawing up formal guidelines to regulate the use of AI in courtroom proceedings, prompted in part by high-profile incidents in the United States where AI tools produced false legal citations that were submitted in court. Deccan Herald also flags the broader concern that while AI can assist judicial processes, human judgment must remain central to legal decision-making. The Supreme Court's intervention signals institutional awareness of the risks at the highest level of India's judiciary.

The context

Globally, the integration of AI into legal systems has accelerated, with tools being used for legal research, drafting, and even predictive judgments. However, incidents in the US — where lawyers using ChatGPT submitted briefs containing entirely fictitious case citations — have alarmed judiciaries worldwide. India's Supreme Court has already experimented with AI for transcription and case management, making clear guidelines on boundaries both timely and necessary.

Why it matters

For lawyers and litigants, clear rules on AI use will determine the reliability and integrity of court documents and legal arguments — with direct consequences for the fairness of trials and the speed of case resolution across India's overburdened court system. For the broader public, it sets a precedent for how state institutions will govern AI before crises, not after.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to release draft guidelines on AI use in judicial proceedings in the coming months, likely inviting responses from bar councils and legal technology stakeholders before finalising the framework.
