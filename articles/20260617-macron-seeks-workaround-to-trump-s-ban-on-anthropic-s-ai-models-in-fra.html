Macron seeks workaround to Trump's ban on Anthropic's AI models in France

Key facts

  • Macron seeking workaround to Trump's ban on Anthropic AI models in France
  • US export controls restrict allied nations' access to frontier US AI systems
  • Anthropic makes the Claude series of advanced AI models
  • France has positioned itself as Europe's primary AI investment hub

Bloomberg reports that French President Emmanuel Macron is personally seeking a way around the Trump administration's ban that has cut off France's access to Anthropic's AI models. The restrictions are part of sweeping US export controls on advanced AI systems, limiting which countries — even allied ones — can access frontier models developed by American companies like Anthropic, the maker of the Claude series.

The move has alarmed European governments, who see unrestricted access to leading AI as critical to their economic competitiveness and national security. France, which has positioned itself as Europe's AI hub and hosted the Paris AI Action Summit earlier this year, is particularly exposed given its ambitions to build a domestic AI ecosystem partly in partnership with US firms.

Macron's diplomatic scramble underscores a broader fracture in transatlantic tech relations. The US diffusion rule, introduced in the final days of the Biden administration and not rescinded by Trump, effectively creates a tiered global system where even G7 partners must negotiate special access to American AI. For India and other nations watching from the sidelines, the episode is a warning that access to the world's most powerful AI tools is increasingly a matter of geopolitics, not just commerce.
