CBSE Frames New Class 12 Assessment Policy for Students in Gulf Countries

Key facts

  • CBSE has framed a new Class 12 assessment policy for Gulf-based students
  • Policy disclosed to the Supreme Court during legal proceedings
  • Gulf region hosts a large CBSE-affiliated Indian student population

The Central Board of Secondary Education has informed the Supreme Court that it has framed a new assessment policy for Class 12 students enrolled in CBSE-affiliated schools in the Gulf countries. The disclosure, reported by India Today, was made during ongoing legal proceedings before the apex court.

The Gulf region is home to one of the largest concentrations of the Indian diaspora in the world, and CBSE-affiliated schools there cater to hundreds of thousands of Indian students. Questions around how these students are assessed — particularly during disruptions such as the COVID-19 pandemic — have previously generated significant concern among parents and students.

The new policy signals CBSE's acknowledgement that students in Gulf countries may face distinct challenges compared to those in India, and that a tailored framework is needed to address their circumstances fairly. The Supreme Court's involvement suggests the matter had escalated to a point where judicial oversight was deemed necessary.

Details of what specifically the new policy entails were not available from the source, but its framing and formal disclosure before the Supreme Court indicate that it carries official weight. Students and parents in the Gulf can expect clarity on the new assessment norms through official CBSE channels.
