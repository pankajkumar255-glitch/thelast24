Trump turns 80 as age debate he once weaponised comes full circle

What happened

NDTV reports that Trump, now 80 years old, finds himself on the other side of the age debate that he himself weaponised against political opponents. The story notes the reversal of fortunes for a president who made his rivals' age and mental fitness a central campaign talking point.

The context

Trump repeatedly and publicly questioned Joe Biden's cognitive fitness during the 2020 and 2024 election cycles, making age a defining political attack line. Biden ultimately withdrew from the 2024 race partly due to concerns over his age. Trump, who defeated Biden's successor Kamala Harris, is now the oldest person ever to serve as US President.

Why it matters

Questions about a sitting US president's health and decision-making capacity matter globally, as American foreign policy, trade decisions, and military posture under Trump have direct consequences for India's economy, geopolitics, and bilateral ties.

What's next

Expect the age question to remain a recurring theme in US political coverage as Trump's second term progresses toward the 2026 midterm elections.
