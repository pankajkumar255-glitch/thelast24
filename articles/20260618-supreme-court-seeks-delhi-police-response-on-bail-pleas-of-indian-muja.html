Supreme Court seeks Delhi Police response on bail pleas of Indian Mujahideen operatives

Key facts

  • Supreme Court issues notice to Delhi Police on Indian Mujahideen bail pleas
  • Accused are operatives of the banned terror outfit Indian Mujahideen
  • Case involves balance between national security and constitutional bail rights

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has issued a notice to Delhi Police seeking its response to bail pleas filed by individuals described as operatives of Indian Mujahideen, the banned domestic terror organisation. The court's move indicates it is willing to examine whether the continued detention of the accused is justified under law, even in cases involving serious national security charges.

Indian Mujahideen has been linked to multiple serial bomb blasts across Indian cities in the 2000s and early 2010s, and its operatives have faced charges under stringent anti-terror laws including UAPA. Convictions and prolonged pre-trial detentions have been a feature of these cases, making bail applications rare and closely watched.

The Supreme Court seeking a reply from Delhi Police is significant because it compels the prosecution to formally justify ongoing detention. For everyday Indians, it is a reminder that even in terror-related matters, the judiciary acts as a check on executive power, ensuring that detentions cannot be indefinite without accountability to the courts.
