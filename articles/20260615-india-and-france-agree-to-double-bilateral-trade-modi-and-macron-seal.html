India and France Agree to Double Bilateral Trade, Modi and Macron Seal Closer Ties

What happened

India Today and The Times of India report that India and France have agreed to double bilateral trade during a high-level meeting between PM Modi and President Macron. The New Indian Express reports the two nations are elevating their ties with a specific focus on AI, defence, and trade. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal pitched India's manufacturing scale and talent to French investors as part of the engagement.

The context

India and France already share a strong strategic partnership covering defence, space, and nuclear energy. France is a key European ally for India, and bilateral trade has been growing steadily. This latest agreement comes as India aggressively courts foreign investment under its Make in India and manufacturing push, with France seen as a critical partner in defence co-production and emerging technologies like AI.

Why it matters

Doubling trade with France and attracting French manufacturing investment could generate significant employment and accelerate technology transfer, particularly in defence and advanced industries. For everyday Indians, this signals stronger economic ties with a G7 nation that could influence everything from defence procurement to job creation in high-skill sectors.

What's next

Expect formal announcements of specific investment commitments and trade targets in the coming weeks as both governments translate summit-level agreements into actionable frameworks.
