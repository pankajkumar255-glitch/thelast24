India gets its first homegrown AI video model

What happened

The Times of India reports that India has developed and launched a homegrown AI video model, making it one of very few countries outside the US and China to achieve this capability. The report marks the development as a landmark moment for India's domestic AI ecosystem. Specific details about the developer — whether a government body, academic institution, or private company — are reported by the Times of India but not fully elaborated in the headline. The launch coincides with growing alarm in India about dependence on American AI platforms following the Anthropic model suspension.

The context

Generative AI video models — systems that can create realistic or stylised video from text or image prompts — have until now been dominated by US companies such as OpenAI (Sora), Google (Veo), and Chinese firms. India has been investing in AI through the IndiaAI Mission, which includes funding for compute infrastructure and model development, but frontier model creation has remained elusive for most countries outside the US and China. The Indian government has pushed for domestic AI development partly to avoid the kind of access disruptions now seen with Anthropic. India's large pool of AI researchers and competitive software talent makes it a credible contender in this space.

Why it matters

A homegrown AI video model gives Indian developers, filmmakers, advertisers, and content creators a domestically controlled alternative to foreign platforms, reducing the risk of sudden access cuts. It also signals that India's AI investment is beginning to produce tangible results at the frontier, not just in applications built on top of foreign models. For the broader economy, domestic AI model development can anchor high-value research jobs and intellectual property within India rather than ceding them to Silicon Valley or Beijing.

What's next

Expect more details about the model's developer, capabilities, and public or commercial availability to emerge in coming days. Watch whether this launch influences government AI procurement policy and whether Indian startups begin building products on top of this domestic model.
