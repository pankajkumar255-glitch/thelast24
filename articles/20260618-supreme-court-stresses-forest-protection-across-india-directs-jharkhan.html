Supreme Court stresses forest protection across India, directs Jharkhand to preserve natural ecosystems

Key facts

  • Supreme Court directs Jharkhand to preserve its natural ecosystems
  • Court stresses forest protection as a nationwide imperative
  • Jharkhand is among India's most forested and mineral-rich states

Telegraph India reports that the Supreme Court, during a hearing on forest protection, stressed the need for India-wide preservation of natural ecosystems and specifically called out Jharkhand, directing the state to take measurable steps to safeguard its forests. The bench made clear that its concern was not limited to one jurisdiction but reflected a broader national imperative for environmental stewardship.

Jharkhand is one of India's most heavily forested states and also among the most mineral-rich, making it a perpetual flashpoint between conservation imperatives and industrial development. Encroachment, illegal mining, and infrastructure projects have historically threatened its forest cover, affecting indigenous Adivasi communities who depend on forests for their livelihoods and cultural identity.

The Supreme Court's intervention is significant because judicial directions on forest protection carry binding force on state governments. For ordinary Indians — particularly in states with large forest cover — such rulings determine whether green buffers, water catchments, and biodiversity corridors survive or are lost to unchecked development.
