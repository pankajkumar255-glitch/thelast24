India, Australia, Great Britain and South Africa Secure First Women's Cricket Quotas for LA28 Olympics

Key facts

  • Australia, Great Britain, India and South Africa are first four LA28 women's cricket qualifiers
  • Confirmed by olympics.com
  • India qualified despite early T20 World Cup exit
  • West Indies did not qualify under current pathway
  • Cricket returns to Olympics at LA28 after over a century

India have officially secured a quota place for women's cricket at the 2028 Los Angeles Olympic Games, with olympics.com confirming that Australia, Great Britain, India and South Africa are the first four nations to lock in their berths for the sport's return to the Games.

The confirmation is a landmark moment for Indian women's cricket. Cricket makes its return to the Olympics at LA28 after more than a century, and India's inclusion as one of the first automatic qualifiers underscores the team's standing in the global T20 rankings. The qualification pathway, based on ICC rankings rather than a single tournament result, meant India's early exit from the Women's T20 World Cup — where they were eliminated by Australia — did not derail their Olympic ambitions.

The Indian Express reported that India are among the automatic qualifiers, while West Indies did not make the cut under the current qualification structure. The absence of a powerhouse Caribbean side and the presence of Great Britain — who field a combined England, Scotland and Wales side — reflects how the ICC and IOC have shaped the field for the inaugural Olympic women's T20 competition.

For the broader cricket ecosystem in India, the development carries enormous significance. An Olympic appearance for the women's team will attract new sponsorships, viewership and institutional support. Every ICC-sanctioned T20 international from here on will carry Olympic ranking points, meaning the stakes in women's cricket fixtures have risen sharply — a reality that will reshape how the BCCI and selectors approach bilateral series and tournaments in the years ahead.
