A US Trade Law Is Blocking Progress on India-US Deal, Reports NDTV

What happened

NDTV reports that a US trade law is specifically holding up the finalisation of a deal between India and the United States. The report points to a legislative or regulatory requirement on the American side that must be satisfied before any trade agreement can be formally concluded. This corroborates the Reuters report from later in the day in which an official confirmed the deal cannot be finalised until a US tariff probe concludes. Together, the two reports paint a picture of a structural impediment, not merely a negotiating disagreement, standing in the way of a deal.

The context

The US has several trade laws that govern how agreements can be negotiated and implemented, including requirements for Congressional notification periods, Section 301 investigations, and International Trade Commission reviews. Any of these mechanisms can create mandatory waiting periods that no amount of diplomatic goodwill can shortcut. India has been one of several countries in active trade negotiations with Washington during the 90-day tariff pause declared in April 2025. The procedural constraint is particularly problematic given the hard deadline that pause creates.

Why it matters

If the legal process on the US side cannot be completed within the tariff pause window, Indian exporters could find themselves subject to full reciprocal tariffs with no deal in place, a scenario that would sharply increase costs for Indian goods in the American market and potentially trigger job losses in export-dependent industries.

What's next

Watch for any US government announcement on accelerating or concluding the relevant legal process. If the probe timeline cannot be compressed, expect both governments to explore interim arrangements — such as a framework agreement or tariff rate quotas — to provide some relief while the formal deal process continues.
