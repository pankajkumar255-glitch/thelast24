'From Rs 45 lakh salary to losing it all': Founder shares brutal startup failure story

Key facts

  • Founder left a Rs 45 lakh annual salary to start a company
  • The startup ultimately failed, resulting in total financial loss
  • Story reported by NDTV and widely shared online

A startup founder's unflinching account of walking away from a Rs 45 lakh salary only to lose everything has gone viral, striking a chord in India's entrepreneurship community and sparking a wider conversation about the unglamourised reality of building a company.

The founder's story, reported by NDTV, follows a trajectory that is more common than India's startup success narrative suggests: a well-paying corporate career abandoned for the promise of building something original, followed by the grinding reality of fundraising, market competition, execution challenges, and ultimately, failure. The specificity of the Rs 45 lakh figure — a salary that places the founder firmly in the upper-middle-class professional bracket — makes the loss tangible and relatable for a generation of Indian professionals weighing similar bets.

India's startup ecosystem has done a remarkable job celebrating its winners, from billion-dollar unicorns to scrappy bootstrapped successes. But the failure rate for startups globally is high, and India is no exception. Most ventures do not survive their first few years, yet the stories of those who tried and fell are rarely told with the same prominence as those who succeeded. This founder's willingness to speak openly about the losses — financial, professional, and personal — fills an important gap in the public discourse.

The viral spread of the story reflects a broader appetite within India's youth for honest conversations about risk, failure, and the real cost of entrepreneurship. Mental health, financial ruin, and identity loss are dimensions of startup culture that rarely make headlines.

For the next generation of founders, accounts like this serve as both cautionary tale and validation — a reminder that failure is not an aberration, but an inherent part of the entrepreneurial journey.
