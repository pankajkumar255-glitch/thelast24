Strait of Hormuz reopening to ease oil supply risks for India

What happened

The Hindu reports that the anticipated reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, following the Iran-US peace deal, is expected to significantly ease oil supply risks for India. The strait is a critical chokepoint for crude oil shipments heading to Indian refineries.

The context

India relies on the Gulf region for the bulk of its crude oil imports, with shipments from Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Iraq, and Iran all passing through or near the Strait of Hormuz. Any closure or blockade of the strait forces India to seek alternative, costlier supplies and pay elevated shipping and insurance costs.

Why it matters

Easing supply risks could help stabilise domestic fuel prices and reduce pressure on the government to subsidise petroleum products, benefiting both the fiscal deficit and household budgets. It also strengthens the rupee by narrowing India's trade deficit.

What's next

Expect India's oil ministry and state refiners like Indian Oil and BPCL to reassess their crude procurement strategies and potentially lock in lower-cost Gulf supplies as the strait reopens.
