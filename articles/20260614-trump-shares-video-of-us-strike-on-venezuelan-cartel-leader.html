Trump Shares Video of US Strike on Venezuelan Cartel Leader

What happened

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump shared footage of a strike on a Venezuelan cartel leader. The public release of the strike video is part of Trump's wider crackdown campaign against Latin American criminal networks, which he has framed as a national security priority.

The context

Since returning to office, Trump has taken an aggressive stance against drug cartels in Latin America, designating groups including Venezuelan gang Tren de Aragua as foreign terrorist organisations. This has included diplomatic pressure on Mexico and Venezuela, deportation operations, and now apparent direct military action. The public sharing of strike footage represents an unusual degree of transparency — or political signalling — around the use of lethal force.

Why it matters

The US deployment of lethal force against non-state actors in Latin America without formal declarations of war sets a precedent with broad implications for international law and regional stability — and reflects the Trump administration's willingness to act unilaterally in ways that reverberate across global diplomacy.

What's next

Expect Venezuelan government condemnation and intensified debate in the US Congress and among international legal experts about the authority and legality of such strikes.
