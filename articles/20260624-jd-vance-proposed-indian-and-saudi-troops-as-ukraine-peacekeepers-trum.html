JD Vance Proposed Indian and Saudi Troops as Ukraine Peacekeepers — Trump Rejected It

Key facts

  • Vance proposed Indian and Saudi troops as Ukraine peacekeepers
  • Trump rejected the proposal, saying they 'won't do that'
  • Revelation comes from a new book on White House deliberations
  • India has maintained strategic autonomy on the Russia-Ukraine conflict

US Vice President JD Vance privately suggested that Indian and Saudi Arabian troops be deployed as peacekeepers in Ukraine as part of an effort to broker a ceasefire in the Russia-Ukraine war, according to a new book detailing internal White House deliberations. President Donald Trump rejected the proposal, reportedly saying that India and Saudi Arabia simply "won't do that."

The revelation offers a rare window into the Trump administration's internal debates over Ukraine policy and the creative — if ultimately rejected — options being floated to end Europe's most devastating conflict in decades. Vance's suggestion reflects a broader US interest in bringing in neutral or non-aligned powers to provide credibility to any peacekeeping arrangement, given the deep mistrust between Russia and NATO member states.

For India, the story carries particular diplomatic weight. New Delhi has consistently maintained a position of strategic autonomy on the Ukraine conflict — abstaining on key UN votes, continuing trade with Russia, and positioning itself as a potential mediator rather than a participant. The idea that Washington was even internally discussing a role for Indian troops underlines the expectations some in the US government have of India as a rising global security actor.

India has shown no public appetite for such a deployment, and the government's stated position emphasises dialogue and diplomacy over military involvement. Trump's swift dismissal of the idea may have spared New Delhi an awkward conversation — but the episode is unlikely to be the last time India's potential role in the Ukraine conflict is raised in global capitals.
