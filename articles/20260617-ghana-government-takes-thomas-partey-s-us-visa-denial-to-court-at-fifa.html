Ghana Government Takes Thomas Partey's US Visa Denial to Court at FIFA World Cup 2026

What happened

BBC reports that the government of Ghana has taken the matter of Thomas Partey's US visa denial to court, seeking to challenge the decision. Partey had reportedly been denied a visa to enter the United States for the FIFA World Cup 2026, which is currently underway. The Ghanaian government's legal challenge is described as an active court proceeding.

The context

Thomas Partey is a key midfielder for Ghana and plays club football for Arsenal in England. The FIFA World Cup 2026 is being co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The US has faced criticism for strict visa policies, and FIFA had secured commitments from host nations to allow all registered players and officials to enter. A visa denial for a prominent player creates a rare conflict between immigration law and international sporting agreements.

Why it matters

The case could set a precedent for how visa disputes are handled at the World Cup, potentially affecting other players, officials, and fans — including thousands of Indians planning to travel to North America for matches.

What's next

Expect FIFA to be drawn into mediating between the US government and Ghana, with a court ruling likely before Ghana's next fixture.
