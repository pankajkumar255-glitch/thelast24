Technology emerges as a new pillar of India-France strategic partnership

What happened

The Hindu reports that technology has emerged as a key driver of strategic convergence between India and France, with both governments focusing on cooperation in areas such as digital infrastructure, AI, and semiconductors. The development reflects a broadening of bilateral ties beyond defence and diplomatic relations.

The context

India and France have historically maintained strong ties through defence deals — notably the Rafale jet purchase — and cultural diplomacy. In recent years, as geopolitical competition over technology supply chains has intensified, both nations have sought to build partnerships that offer alternatives to US and Chinese dominance in critical tech sectors. France is also a leading voice within the EU for tech sovereignty.

Why it matters

Strategic tech alignment with France could help India gain access to European semiconductor and AI ecosystems, diversifying its supply chains and reducing geopolitical risk. This also positions India as a preferred technology partner for the European bloc.

What's next

Expect formal agreements or joint working groups on AI and semiconductor collaboration to be announced at upcoming India-France or India-EU summits.
