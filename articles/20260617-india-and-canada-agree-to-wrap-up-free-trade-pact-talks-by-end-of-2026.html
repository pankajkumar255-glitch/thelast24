India and Canada agree to wrap up free trade pact talks by end of 2026 as Modi meets Carney at G7

What happened

The Economic Times and Moneycontrol both report that India and Canada have agreed to conclude free trade pact negotiations by the end of 2026, with the commitment made as Prime Minister Narendra Modi met Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney on the sidelines of the G7 summit. The deal would cover goods, services, and potentially investment.

The context

India-Canada trade relations had been severely strained since September 2023 when Canada alleged Indian government involvement in the killing of Khalistani separatist Hardeep Singh Nijjar, leading to diplomatic expulsions. Trade talks had been suspended during this period. The G7 meeting provided both leaders a platform to reset ties and put negotiations back on track.

Why it matters

A completed FTA with Canada would give Indian exporters — particularly in IT services, pharmaceuticals, and textiles — preferential access to a high-income market, potentially generating thousands of jobs. Indian consumers and businesses could also benefit from competitively priced Canadian commodities, including pulses and energy inputs.

What's next

Expect formal negotiating rounds to resume in the coming weeks, with both sides likely to release a joint roadmap detailing the timeline and key sectors under discussion.
