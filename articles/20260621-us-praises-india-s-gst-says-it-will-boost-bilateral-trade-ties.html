US praises India's GST, says it will boost bilateral trade ties

Key facts

  • US praised India's GST as a bilateral trade booster
  • US described as 'bullish' on the Indian economy overall
  • Remarks come during ongoing India-US trade deal negotiations
  • India still demands 'competitive advantage' before signing any deal

The United States has publicly praised India's Goods and Services Tax system, declaring it will serve as a positive force for bilateral trade ties — a significant diplomatic signal as the two countries work toward a trade agreement, NDTV reported Thursday.

The endorsement represents a meaningful evolution in Washington's posture. For years, American companies and trade bodies flagged India's multi-rate GST structure as a compliance complexity that complicated doing business in India. The US government's current framing — that GST will boost trade — suggests that after nearly eight years of the tax regime's operation, Washington now views it as a stable, comprehensible framework rather than a barrier.

The broader US assessment of the Indian economy was described as bullish, aligning with recent IMF and World Bank projections that place India among the fastest-growing major economies. This positive framing matters to the negotiating dynamic: a US that sees India as a reliable, reform-oriented economy may be more willing to offer meaningful tariff concessions, particularly in sectors like gems, jewellery, and engineering goods where India seeks better access.

However, as Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal made clear in separate remarks Thursday, India will not sign a deal until it secures a genuine competitive advantage — meaning positive rhetoric from Washington will need to be matched by concrete movement on market access and the restoration of GSP benefits before a pact is clinched.
