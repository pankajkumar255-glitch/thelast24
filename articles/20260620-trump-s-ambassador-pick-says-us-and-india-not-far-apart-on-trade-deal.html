Trump's Ambassador Pick Says US and India 'Not Far Apart' on Trade Deal

Key facts

  • Trump's US ambassador nominee said US and India are 'not far apart' on trade deal
  • Indian government says farmers will be 'fully protected' under any deal
  • Remarks made at US Senate confirmation proceedings

NDTV reports that the Trump administration's ambassador pick for India told the US Senate that the two countries are 'not far apart' on a bilateral trade deal, offering one of the most optimistic public assessments yet from the American side. The statement came during Senate confirmation proceedings and signals that Washington views a deal as achievable in the near term.

Concurrently, NDTV also reports that the Indian government has offered reassurance that Indian farmers will be 'fully protected' under any India-US trade agreement, addressing domestic concerns that an opening of agricultural markets could expose farmers to cheaper American imports. The government's position suggests New Delhi is drawing clear red lines in negotiations even as both sides express optimism.

A trade deal between the world's largest and fifth-largest economies would have wide-ranging consequences — from tariff relief for Indian IT services and pharmaceuticals entering the US, to potential pressure on sensitive sectors like agriculture and dairy. For everyday Indians, the outcome will influence the cost of imported goods, job creation in export industries, and the long-term trajectory of India's economic relationship with its largest trading partner.
