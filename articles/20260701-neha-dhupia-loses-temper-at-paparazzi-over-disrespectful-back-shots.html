Neha Dhupia Loses Temper at Paparazzi Over 'Disrespectful' Back Shots

Key facts

  • Neha Dhupia confronted paparazzi over 'disrespectful' back shots
  • She objected to being photographed from behind without consent
  • Incident reignites debate over ethics of celebrity photography in India

Neha Dhupia publicly lost her temper at paparazzi photographers after they took what she called 'disrespectful' back shots — photographs taken from behind, without her engagement or consent. The incident, which quickly circulated on social media, has sparked a fresh debate about the ethics and limits of celebrity photography in India.

Dhupia made her displeasure clear in no uncertain terms, confronting the photographers on the spot. Her objection centred on the nature of the images — taken from behind and, in her view, demeaning — rather than simply the act of being photographed. The episode highlights a tension that simmers constantly between Bollywood celebrities and the paparazzi ecosystem that surrounds them.

While stars routinely engage with photographers at events and airports, many have long expressed discomfort with unsolicited or invasive shots. Dhupia's willingness to call it out publicly, using the word 'disrespectful', brings an ethical dimension to what is often dismissed as a routine celebrity-media friction.

The moment is part of a broader reckoning in the industry about boundaries, consent and how public figures — particularly women — are photographed and represented. Dhupia's reaction has drawn both support and debate online, with many women in and outside the industry voicing solidarity.
