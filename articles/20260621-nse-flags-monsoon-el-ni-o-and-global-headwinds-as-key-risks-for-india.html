NSE flags monsoon, El Niño and global headwinds as key risks for India's economy in 2026

Key facts

  • NSE flags monsoon variability, El Niño and global trends as top 2026 risks
  • El Niño can suppress Indian monsoon, lifting food inflation and hurting rural demand
  • Global market volatility cited as external risk for Indian equities and the rupee
  • Assessment comes as India targets sustained high economic growth into 2026

The National Stock Exchange has put monsoon risk, El Niño and evolving global market trends at the top of its watch-list for risks that could shape India's economic fortunes in 2026, in an assessment that underlines how vulnerable the country's growth story remains to factors beyond policymakers' control.

El Niño weather patterns, which suppress rainfall over the Indian subcontinent, have historically been associated with below-average monsoons that hit agricultural output, drive up food inflation and squeeze rural consumption — a chain reaction with wide macroeconomic consequences. With forecasters watching the Pacific Ocean's temperature anomalies closely, the NSE's inclusion of this risk reflects real uncertainty about the 2026 kharif and rabi seasons.

Beyond the weather, the NSE identified global market trends as a significant external variable. Volatility in US interest rates, commodity price swings and geopolitical disruptions to trade flows all have the potential to affect Indian equities, the rupee and capital flows into the country. These concerns mirror warnings issued by the International Monetary Fund and the Reserve Bank of India in recent months.

For investors and policymakers alike, the NSE's risk matrix is a timely reminder that India's strong near-term growth narrative must be stress-tested against structural vulnerabilities. The government's ability to manage food prices through buffer stocks and its fiscal headroom to absorb an economic slowdown will be closely watched as these risks evolve through the year.
