CSIR Licenses Seven Technologies to Industry; Quantum Sensing Components Delivered to DRDO

What happened

PIB reports that CSIR has licensed seven technologies to industry, released Bharatiya Nirdeshak Dravyas for quality standards, and delivered quantum sensing components to DRDO. The announcements represent a cluster of technology-transfer milestones from India's premier scientific research body.

The context

CSIR, under the Ministry of Science and Technology, operates 37 laboratories across India and is a key node in translating Indian scientific research into industrial and defence applications. Quantum technology has been identified as a national priority under the National Quantum Mission, launched in 2023 with a budget of Rs 6,003 crore, with defence sensing among its key application areas.

Why it matters

Technology licensing to industry creates commercial products and jobs, while delivering quantum sensing to DRDO advances India's defence self-reliance under the Atmanirbhar Bharat initiative. Both outcomes reduce India's dependence on expensive foreign imports in critical sectors.

What's next

Expect DRDO to begin integration testing of the quantum sensing components, with further CSIR technology transfers likely as the National Quantum Mission ramps up through 2026.
