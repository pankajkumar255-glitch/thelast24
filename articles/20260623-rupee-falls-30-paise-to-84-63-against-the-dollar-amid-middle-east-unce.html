Rupee falls 30 paise to 84.63 against the dollar amid Middle East uncertainty

Key facts

  • Rupee fell 30 paise to 84.63 against the US dollar
  • Trigger: rising Middle East geopolitical tensions
  • India imports over 85% of its crude oil, amplifying currency risk
  • RBI bulletin separately flagged fragile global economic conditions

The Indian rupee fell 30 paise to 84.63 against the US dollar on Wednesday, weighed down by escalating uncertainty in the Middle East that prompted investors globally to move toward safer assets and away from emerging-market currencies. The Times of India reported the decline, which comes against a backdrop of fragile global sentiment.

The Middle East tensions have pushed crude oil prices higher, compounding pressure on the rupee. India imports over 85 percent of its crude oil requirements, making it acutely sensitive to any spike in global energy prices. A weaker rupee combined with costlier oil is a double blow for the current account deficit and for domestic fuel pricing.

The Reserve Bank of India has in recent months attempted to manage rupee volatility through periodic interventions in the foreign exchange market. However, sustained geopolitical shocks of the kind currently unfolding test the central bank's capacity to defend the currency without depleting foreign exchange reserves.

For ordinary Indians, a sustained depreciation in the rupee translates into higher prices for imported goods, from electronics to edible oils, and can stoke inflation even as the RBI attempts to engineer a soft landing for the economy. The RBI's own bulletin, separately released Wednesday, flagged that the global economic landscape remains fragile — a warning that currency and market volatility may persist.
