Tamil Nadu Moves Supreme Court Against High Court Order in Deepam Scheme Row

Key facts

  • Tamil Nadu moves Supreme Court against High Court's Deepam order
  • Deepam scheme provides free cooking gas cylinders to eligible households
  • State contests the High Court directive at the apex court

The Tamil Nadu government has escalated the legal dispute over its Deepam free cooking gas scheme, approaching the Supreme Court to challenge a High Court order that has complicated the programme's implementation. The state's decision to seek apex court intervention underscores how central the scheme is to the TVK government's welfare agenda and its political identity.

The Deepam scheme, which provides free LPG cylinders to eligible households, was a flagship promise and has been positioned as a direct benefit to lower-income families in the state. Any High Court order that threatens its rollout is therefore not merely an administrative inconvenience but a political flashpoint, particularly as the TVK government seeks to establish its credentials in office.

The specifics of what the High Court directed — and on whose petition — were not detailed in the available report, but Tamil Nadu's swift move to the Supreme Court indicates the state views the lower court's order as a significant obstacle. The Supreme Court will now examine whether to stay or modify the High Court's direction.

The outcome will be watched closely by welfare scheme beneficiaries, opposition parties and legal observers. It also fits into a broader pattern of state governments using the Supreme Court to protect locally popular schemes from judicial interference at the High Court level — a strategy that has had mixed results across different states and programmes.
