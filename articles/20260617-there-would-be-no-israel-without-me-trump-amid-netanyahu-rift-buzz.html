'There Would Be No Israel Without Me': Trump Amid Netanyahu Rift Buzz

What happened

NDTV reports that President Trump made the claim that there would be no Israel without him, even as reports of a rift between Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu circulate. The statement was made publicly and reflects tension within one of the world's most closely watched bilateral relationships. The context appears to be broader disagreements over the direction of US policy toward Israel and the conflict in Gaza.

The context

Trump and Netanyahu have historically been close allies, but tensions have reportedly emerged over disagreements on the Gaza conflict's conduct, hostage negotiations, and the broader shape of any post-war settlement. Trump's first term saw him deliver major favourable moves for Israel, including recognising Jerusalem as its capital. His current public boasting about Israel's existence being tied to him signals both political posturing and genuine friction.

Why it matters

A visible US-Israel rift could reshape ceasefire negotiations in Gaza and alter the regional security calculus in the Middle East, affecting Indian workers and diaspora in Gulf countries as well as India's oil supply chains. Any instability in US-Israel ties also has wider implications for global diplomatic alignments that India must navigate.

What's next

Expect close scrutiny of the next Trump-Netanyahu interaction and whether the reported rift translates into any concrete shift in US military or diplomatic support for Israel.
