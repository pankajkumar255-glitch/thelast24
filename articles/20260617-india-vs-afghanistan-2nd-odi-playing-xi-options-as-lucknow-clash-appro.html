India vs Afghanistan 2nd ODI: Playing XI options as Lucknow clash approaches

What happened

The Indian Express reports on the likely Playing XI for India ahead of the second ODI against Afghanistan in Lucknow, analysing whether the team management will make changes after the first match. India enter the game having won the series opener, with the second game offering an opportunity to seal the bilateral series. The report weighs selection options across batting and bowling departments.

The context

India's ODI series against Afghanistan is part of their ongoing preparation cycle for multi-team tournaments. Afghanistan, ranked in the top 10 of ICC ODI rankings and featuring spinners like Rashid Khan and Mohammad Nabi, are no longer a pushover side and have beaten top teams in recent years.

Why it matters

A series win at home keeps India's ODI momentum intact ahead of future ICC events, while fringe players fighting for spots need performances in these games to press their case to selectors.

What's next

Expect the official Playing XI to be announced at the toss in Lucknow, with all eyes on whether any new faces are handed opportunities.
