12-year-old beheaded, skull dumped in sewer; three minors detained for murder

Key facts

  • 12-year-old child beheaded; skull dumped in sewer
  • Three minors detained in connection with the murder
  • Case to be handled under juvenile justice system

A 12-year-old child was beheaded in a horrific killing that has shocked the country, with the victim's skull discovered dumped in a sewer. Police have detained three minors in connection with the murder, making the case all the more disturbing given the ages of those allegedly involved.

The extreme violence of the crime — a child killed and decapitated, apparently by peers or near-peers — has raised urgent questions about what drove the accused to commit such an act. Investigators are working to establish the motive and reconstruct the sequence of events leading up to the killing.

The detention of minors means the case will be handled through the juvenile justice system, which limits the punishments that can be imposed regardless of the severity of the crime. Experts and child rights advocates are likely to use this case to renew calls for stronger intervention programmes targeting at-risk youth.

For the victim's family and the wider community, the case has left a trail of trauma. Law enforcement is under pressure to ensure a thorough investigation and to address the systemic failures that may have allowed such violence to occur.
