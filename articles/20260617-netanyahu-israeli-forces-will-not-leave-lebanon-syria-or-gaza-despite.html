Netanyahu: Israeli Forces Will Not Leave Lebanon, Syria, or Gaza Despite US-Iran Deal

What happened

NDTV reports that Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stated that Israeli forces would not leave Lebanon, Syria, or Gaza following the US-Iran nuclear deal. The announcement came as a firm rebuttal to any expectation that a broader diplomatic settlement would prompt Israeli military withdrawal from territories it has occupied or operates in following recent conflicts.

The context

Israel expanded its military footprint significantly during the 2024 Gaza war, including operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon and strikes in Syria. A US-Iran nuclear deal, if finalised, would ease sanctions on Tehran and potentially shift the regional balance of power — something Israel has consistently opposed. Netanyahu's government has long argued that Iran's proxies across the region pose an existential threat.

Why it matters

Prolonged Israeli military presence across multiple fronts risks wider regional escalation, which could disrupt global oil supplies and push up fuel costs for Indian consumers. India also has a large diaspora in the Gulf region whose safety and livelihoods are tied to Middle East stability.

What's next

Expect heightened diplomatic pressure from the US on Israel and possible counter-moves by Iran-backed groups in Lebanon and Syria.
