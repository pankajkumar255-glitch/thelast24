India must invest in deep tech and build globally competitive products, says Neelkanth Mishra

Key facts

  • Neelkanth Mishra calls for urgent investment in deep tech capabilities
  • India must build globally competitive products, not just services
  • India has large startup count but few globally dominant tech products
  • Deep tech includes semiconductors, AI research, advanced manufacturing

Moneycontrol reports that economist Neelkanth Mishra has made a pointed call for India to invest heavily in deep technology capabilities and develop globally competitive products, rather than continuing to rely primarily on IT services and outsourcing. Mishra argued that India's current technology posture — skilled at execution and services but weak on original product innovation — leaves the country exposed as AI and automation reshape global demand for traditional outsourcing.

Mishra's comments come at a moment when India's technology ambitions are under sharp debate. The country has built one of the world's largest startup ecosystems by count, but critics note that very few Indian startups have produced globally dominant technology products. Most remain domestically focused or serve as service extensions of foreign platforms.

For India to move up the value chain, Mishra's argument implies the need for sustained government and private investment in areas like semiconductors, AI research, advanced manufacturing, and biotech — areas where India currently lags behind China, South Korea, and even smaller technology nations. The stakes are high: deep tech leadership determines long-term economic competitiveness and the quality of jobs available to India's 1.4 billion people.
