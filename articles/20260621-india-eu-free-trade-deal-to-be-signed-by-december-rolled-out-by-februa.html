India-EU Free Trade Deal to Be Signed by December, Rolled Out by February-March

Key facts

  • India-EU FTA signing targeted for December 2025
  • Implementation expected February-March 2026: Piyush Goyal
  • Negotiations have been ongoing for nearly two decades
  • Deal covers trade, investment and services between the two blocs

India and the European Union are set to sign their free trade agreement by December, with the pact coming into force as early as February or March next year, Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal confirmed on Tuesday. The announcement marks one of the most significant milestones in India's trade diplomacy, bringing to a close negotiations that have stretched across nearly two decades and multiple failed attempts.

The India-EU Bilateral Trade and Investment Agreement, if implemented on schedule, would link two of the world's largest economies in a formal preferential trading arrangement. The EU is already one of India's top trading partners, with bilateral merchandise trade running into hundreds of billions of dollars annually. A deal would substantially lower tariffs on Indian exports such as textiles, garments, leather goods and pharmaceuticals entering Europe, while making European automobiles, wines, spirits and machinery more competitively priced in India.

Goyal's timeline is notably precise, and coming from a senior cabinet minister it signals genuine political momentum on both sides. Indian negotiators have been working to wrap up outstanding differences on issues including government procurement rules, data protection standards and market access in services — areas that have historically proven contentious.

The announcement comes as India is simultaneously managing trade negotiations with the United States and looking to diversify its export relationships amid global uncertainty. Signing the EU deal would represent a major strategic as well as commercial win, cementing India's position as a preferred trade partner for Western economies.
