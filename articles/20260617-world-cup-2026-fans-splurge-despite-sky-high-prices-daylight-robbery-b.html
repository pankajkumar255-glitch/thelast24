World Cup 2026 Fans Splurge Despite Sky-High Prices: 'Daylight Robbery But Worth It'

What happened

BBC reports that fans attending the FIFA World Cup 2026 are spending exceptionally high amounts on tickets, travel, and accommodation despite widespread complaints about prices. Fan interviews and spending data paint a picture of a tournament that is breaking cost records even as it breaks attendance enthusiasm.

The context

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the first edition to be held across three nations — the US, Canada, and Mexico — and the first to feature 48 teams, making it the largest in the tournament's history. Hosting costs and commercial demand have driven prices to record levels, with US cities in particular commanding premium rates.

Why it matters

The World Cup economy offers a lens into the commercialisation of global sport, with implications for how India — which is bidding to host future FIFA events — plans event infrastructure, ticketing, and fan affordability. Indian fans following the tournament abroad are also navigating these steep costs.

What's next

Expect further reporting on whether FIFA and host cities introduce any price intervention mechanisms as public complaints mount ahead of the knockout rounds.
