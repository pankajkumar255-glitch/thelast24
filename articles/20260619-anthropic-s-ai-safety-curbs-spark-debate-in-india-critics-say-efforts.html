Anthropic's AI safety curbs spark debate in India — critics say efforts are 'too slow, way too small'

Key facts

  • Anthropic released a new AI safety vision for its Claude models
  • Indian critics call the effort 'too slow, way too small'
  • India has no comprehensive AI regulation currently in place
  • AI safety debate has direct implications for Indian users across key sectors

CNBC's Inside India newsletter reports that Anthropic's announcement of a new safety framework for its AI models has ignited a pointed debate within India's technology and policy circles. Critics quoted in the newsletter describe the company's efforts as 'too slow, way too small,' reflecting frustration that voluntary safety commitments by major AI labs are not keeping pace with the speed at which these tools are being deployed globally and in India.

Bloomberg and Moneycontrol separately reported on Anthropic's vision, which focuses on making its Claude AI models more interpretable and resistant to misuse. The company is among the most prominent voices in the global AI safety movement, and its framework is being watched as a potential benchmark for the broader industry.

The Indian dimension of this debate is significant. India has no comprehensive AI regulation in place yet, and the government has largely encouraged industry self-governance. With AI tools increasingly embedded in sectors such as banking, healthcare, education, and public service delivery, the adequacy of safety measures developed by foreign AI companies has direct consequences for Indian users. The debate underscores growing pressure on both global AI labs and Indian policymakers to move faster on governance frameworks.
