The Modi Government Wants To Reverse India's Brain Drain

Key facts

  • Modi government planning policy to reverse India's brain drain
  • India loses skilled engineers, scientists, and entrepreneurs to US, UK, Canada
  • Initiative targets improving research infrastructure and career opportunities
  • Diaspora repatriation seen as key to AI, semiconductor ambitions

NDTV reports that the Modi government is actively formulating a strategy to reverse India's brain drain, a decades-old challenge that has seen millions of highly skilled engineers, doctors, scientists, and entrepreneurs leave for opportunities abroad. The initiative reflects growing recognition in New Delhi that retaining and repatriating top talent is essential if India is to compete in emerging fields such as artificial intelligence, semiconductor manufacturing, and advanced research.

While specific policy instruments have not been detailed in the headline, such efforts typically involve improving research funding, creating world-class institutional infrastructure, offering competitive compensation in government and public-sector research roles, and potentially providing incentives to diaspora professionals who return to set up ventures or join academia.

India's diaspora — particularly in Silicon Valley, the UK, and the Gulf — represents one of the country's most valuable but underutilised assets. A successful repatriation push could catalyse a virtuous cycle: returning professionals bring global networks, capital, and cutting-edge expertise that strengthen local institutions, making India an even more attractive destination for the next generation of talent.
