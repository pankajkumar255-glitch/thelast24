Supreme Court asks Centre and states to respond on plea against Aadhaar being used as citizenship proof

What happened

The Hindu and The Indian Express both report that the Supreme Court has issued notices to the Centre and states seeking their response on a plea challenging the use of Aadhaar as proof of citizenship. The court's intervention signals it considers the issue of sufficient constitutional importance to warrant a formal government reply.

The context

Aadhaar, administered by the Unique Identification Authority of India, is issued to all residents regardless of citizenship status, and the Aadhaar Act explicitly states it is not proof of citizenship. However, in practice, government agencies and officials have frequently treated it as such, creating legal and humanitarian complications especially around issues like the National Register of Citizens.

Why it matters

If the court rules that Aadhaar cannot be used as citizenship proof, it could disrupt countless government processes and welfare schemes that currently rely on it for verification. Conversely, establishing clearer boundaries on Aadhaar's legal status could protect genuine citizens from arbitrary exclusion.

What's next

Expect the Centre and states to file their affidavits in the coming weeks, with the case likely drawing submissions from civil liberties groups and setting the stage for a landmark ruling.
