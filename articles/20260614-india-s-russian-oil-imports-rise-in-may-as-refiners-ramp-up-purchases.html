India's Russian Oil Imports Rise in May as Refiners Ramp Up Purchases

What happened

The Hindu reports that India's imports of Russian crude oil rose in May, with refiners stepping up purchases compared to the previous month. The report does not specify exact volumes but attributes the increase to refiners actively boosting procurement. Russia has remained India's largest single source of crude oil for several months running.

The context

Since Western nations imposed sanctions on Russia following the 2022 invasion of Ukraine, India has dramatically increased its Russian crude purchases, attracted by steep discounts compared to benchmark prices. Indian state and private refiners, including Indian Oil Corporation and Reliance Industries, have been key buyers, helping India diversify away from Middle Eastern suppliers.

Why it matters

Cheaper Russian crude reduces India's overall import bill, easing pressure on the current account deficit and helping the government manage retail fuel prices. However, heavy dependence on a single sanctioned supplier carries geopolitical and logistical risks that could affect energy security.

What's next

Expect Indian refiner procurement patterns to remain closely watched as global oil prices fluctuate and Western pressure on countries buying Russian oil persists through diplomatic channels.
