iCreate and France's Hauts-de-France Launch India-France Deep Tech Innovation Corridor

What happened

ANI News reports that iCreate, India's International Centre for Entrepreneurship and Technology, and the Hauts-de-France regional authority in France have formally announced a bilateral Deep Tech Innovation Corridor. The partnership is designed to boost startup collaboration and accelerate technology innovation between the two countries.

The context

iCreate, based in Ahmedabad and backed by the Government of Gujarat, is one of India's premier startup incubators with a focus on deep technology. Hauts-de-France is a major industrial and innovation region in northern France, home to significant advanced manufacturing and research clusters. The announcement aligns with deepening India-France strategic ties, which have expanded rapidly under the partnership framework between Prime Ministers Modi and Macron.

Why it matters

The corridor gives Indian deep-tech startups a structured pathway into European innovation networks and funding, while offering French firms access to India's large engineering talent base and growing domestic market.

What's next

Expect the first cohort of startups selected under the India-France Deep Tech Innovation Corridor to be announced within the coming months, with joint pilot projects in AI and clean energy likely to be prioritised.
