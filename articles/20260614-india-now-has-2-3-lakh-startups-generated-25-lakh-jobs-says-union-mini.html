India now has 2.3 lakh startups, generated 25 lakh jobs, says Union minister Jitendra Singh

What happened

The Economic Times reports that Union Minister Jitendra Singh announced India now has 2.3 lakh startups that have collectively generated 25 lakh jobs. The minister cited these figures as evidence of the success of India's startup promotion policies. The numbers reflect growth tracked under the government's DPIIT-recognised startup framework.

The context

India's Startup India initiative, launched in 2016, has provided tax benefits, easier compliance, and funding access to recognised startups. The country is home to over 100 unicorns and is globally ranked third in startup count after the United States and China. Startup employment has increasingly drawn young graduates away from traditional government and corporate job tracks.

Why it matters

25 lakh jobs represent a significant dent in youth unemployment and demonstrate that startups are no longer a fringe employment source but a mainstream economic contributor. For investors and policymakers, the scale of the ecosystem justifies continued regulatory and financial support.

What's next

Expect the government to use these figures to anchor further startup-friendly policy announcements, potentially in the upcoming Union Budget discussions.
