Harshit Rana added to India's ODI squad after recovering from knee surgery

Key facts

  • Harshit Rana added to India's ODI squad
  • Return follows recovery from knee surgery
  • Delhi pacer made international debut in 2024
  • Inclusion bolsters India's pace bowling depth

ESPN reports that fast bowler Harshit Rana has been added to India's ODI squad after successfully recovering from a knee surgery that had kept him out of action. The inclusion comes as a boost to India's seam bowling resources, with Rana having impressed selectors during his initial outings in international cricket after making his debut in 2024.

Rana, who plays domestic cricket for Delhi and was picked up by Kolkata Knight Riders in the IPL, is known for his ability to generate sharp pace and movement. His return to the squad indicates the BCCI medical team has cleared him fully for competitive cricket at the highest level.

For Indian cricket fans, the return of a young, promising pace bowler is significant at a time when India is managing workloads across formats. Rana's availability gives the national selectors more flexibility in building a well-rounded attack, particularly in the 50-over format where India are looking to strengthen their bench strength ahead of future ICC events.
