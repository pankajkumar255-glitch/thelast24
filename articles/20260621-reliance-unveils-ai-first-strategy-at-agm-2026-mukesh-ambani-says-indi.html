Reliance unveils AI-first strategy at AGM 2026; Mukesh Ambani says India must become a global AI leader

Key facts

  • Reliance announced AI-first strategy at AGM 2026
  • Mukesh Ambani called for India to become a global AI leader
  • Strategy covers Jio telecom, Reliance Retail, and energy businesses
  • Reliance is India's largest private conglomerate by market capitalisation

Reliance Industries has formally announced an AI-first strategy at its Annual General Meeting 2026, with Chairman Mukesh Ambani positioning the conglomerate at the vanguard of India's artificial intelligence ambitions. Ambani declared that India must become a global leader in AI, framing the shift as not just a corporate priority but a national imperative.

The announcement marks a significant strategic evolution for the Rs 17-lakh-crore conglomerate, which spans telecom through Jio, retail through Reliance Retail, and energy and petrochemicals. An AI-first approach suggests these businesses will increasingly be powered by, and will sell, AI-driven services — from personalised retail experiences to smarter network management across Jio's roughly 500 million subscribers.

Ambani's remarks come as global technology giants including Google, Meta, and Microsoft have already committed billions of dollars to AI infrastructure in India. By flagging an AI-first posture at the AGM — Reliance's most-watched annual event — the company is signalling that it intends to compete at that scale domestically rather than cede the ground to foreign platforms.

The strategy dovetails with broader calls, including from economists like Neelkanth Mishra, for India to invest in deep technology rather than simply riding global trends. For Reliance, which built its consumer dominance through Jio's data revolution in 2016, an AI pivot could represent a second, potentially larger, transformation — one that will be watched closely by investors, rivals, and policymakers alike.
