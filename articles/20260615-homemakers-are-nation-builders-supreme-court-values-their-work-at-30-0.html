Homemakers Are 'Nation Builders': Supreme Court Values Their Work At ₹30,000 A Month

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court declared homemakers 'nation builders' and put a monetary value of at least ₹30,000 per month on their unpaid domestic work. The ruling came as the court sought to establish a concrete benchmark for valuing housewives' labour in legal and compensation proceedings. The judgment addresses a long-standing gap in how Indian courts have treated the economic contribution of those who work within the home.

The context

Indian courts have historically struggled to quantify the financial value of a homemaker's contribution when awarding compensation in motor accident or insurance cases, often arriving at inconsistently low figures. The Supreme Court has periodically revisited this issue, recognising that unpaid domestic labour underpins household and national economic productivity. This ruling is among the most explicit attempts to set a floor figure for such valuations.

Why it matters

The ₹30,000-per-month benchmark could significantly raise compensation payouts to families of deceased or injured homemakers in accident and insurance litigation. It also signals a broader legal recognition of unpaid domestic work as economically measurable labour, with potential ripple effects on social security and policy debates.

What's next

Expect lower courts and insurance tribunals to begin applying this benchmark in compensation cases, and legal advocates to push for its incorporation into formal insurance regulations.
