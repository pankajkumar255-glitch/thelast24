Ahmed Khan reveals why Sanjay Dutt quit Welcome To The Jungle mid-shoot

Key facts

  • Sanjay Dutt exited 'Welcome To The Jungle' after beginning shoot
  • Director Ahmed Khan revealed the reasons behind the departure
  • The film is part of the long-running 'Welcome' franchise
  • The Indian Express published Khan's account

Director Ahmed Khan has publicly explained why Sanjay Dutt exited 'Welcome To The Jungle' after having already started shooting for the film — a departure that sent ripples through the production and forced a rethink of the ensemble cast.

Khan, who helmed the big-budget comedy, revealed the circumstances in an interview with The Indian Express, offering the clearest account yet of one of Bollywood's more surprising mid-production exits. The film, part of the long-running 'Welcome' franchise, was already a crowded production featuring a large ensemble, and Dutt's exit added an unexpected layer of complication to its already ambitious scope.

The 'Welcome' franchise has been a reliable crowd-puller, and the third instalment was expected to raise the scale significantly. Dutt's involvement had been a much-publicised selling point, making his departure all the more jarring for fans and trade observers. Productions of this size carry enormous financial stakes, and a star exit mid-shoot typically demands reshoots, renegotiations, and creative restructuring.

Khan's willingness to speak openly about the episode is notable, as Bollywood productions rarely air internal conflicts publicly. The revelation adds context to months of speculation that followed Dutt's exit, and underscores the complex logistical and personal negotiations that define big Hindi film productions.
