Why BCCI Allowed Vaibhav Sooryavanshi's Parents to Travel With Him on India Tours

Key facts

  • BCCI made a special exception allowing Sooryavanshi's parents on India tours
  • Vaibhav Sooryavanshi is one of India's youngest-ever international cricketers
  • Arrangement reflects BCCI duty of care for a minor player

India Today reports on the background to the BCCI's decision to allow the parents of young cricket prodigy Vaibhav Sooryavanshi to travel with him during India's international tours. Sooryavanshi made headlines when he became one of the youngest players to represent India, and his age meant that standard touring arrangements were simply not appropriate for him without parental support.

The BCCI, which ordinarily does not facilitate family travel for players on official tours, made a specific exception given Sooryavanshi's status as a minor. The decision underscores the board's responsibility toward the welfare of young athletes who are thrust into the demanding environment of international cricket before they have reached adulthood.

Sooryavanshi's emergence is part of a broader trend of India blooding teenagers at the highest level, backed by a deep domestic pipeline through age-group cricket and the IPL. His parents' presence on tour is intended to provide emotional and personal stability while he adjusts to the rigours of international schedules, travel, and media attention at a very young age.

For Indian cricket followers, the story highlights the human side of managing child prodigies and the institutional framework the BCCI is building to ensure their long-term wellbeing alongside on-field development.
