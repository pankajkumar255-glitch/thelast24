A year on, Zuckerberg must now sell the Meta AI model Alexandr Wang was hired to build

What happened

CNBC reports that approximately one year after Meta brought in Alexandr Wang, the co-founder and CEO of Scale AI, to help develop a new AI model, Mark Zuckerberg is now actively working to sell that model to enterprises and partners. The report suggests the internal development phase is largely complete and the focus has shifted to adoption and commercialisation. This marks a significant moment for Meta's AI ambitions as it competes with OpenAI's GPT models and Google's Gemini.

The context

Meta has been investing heavily in open-weight AI models under its Llama series, positioning itself as an alternative to proprietary models from OpenAI and Google. Alexandr Wang's Scale AI is a leading data-labelling and AI evaluation company, and his involvement signalled Meta's intent to build enterprise-grade AI. Zuckerberg has publicly staked Meta's future on AI as its next major growth platform after social media.

Why it matters

If Meta successfully commercialises its new AI model, Indian developers and startups could gain access to a powerful, potentially open-weight model that reduces dependence on US-export-restricted proprietary AI services. Enterprise adoption would also accelerate AI integration across Indian industries.

What's next

Expect Meta to announce enterprise partnerships and pricing tiers for the new model in the coming weeks, with Indian cloud and software firms likely among early adopters if open access is maintained.
