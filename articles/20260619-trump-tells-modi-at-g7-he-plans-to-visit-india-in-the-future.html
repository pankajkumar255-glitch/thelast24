Trump tells Modi at G7 he plans to visit India in the future

Key facts

  • Trump told Modi he plans to visit India, no date given
  • Remarks made during bilateral meeting at G7 summit
  • India-US trade deal negotiations currently ongoing
  • No timeline specified for the visit

BBC reports that US President Donald Trump told Prime Minister Narendra Modi during their meeting on the sidelines of the G7 summit that he plans to visit India in the future. The remark came as the two leaders held bilateral talks, with trade negotiations between the two countries currently ongoing and a broader tariff framework yet to be finalised.

India and the United States have been engaged in discussions over a bilateral trade deal since early 2025, as Trump's sweeping tariff regime placed pressure on multiple trading partners including India. Modi and Trump have met several times this year, signalling a degree of personal rapport that both governments have sought to leverage in negotiations.

A formal Trump visit to India would carry significant symbolic and diplomatic weight, potentially fast-tracking trade discussions and defence cooperation. However, no date or specific timeline was announced, leaving the commitment largely aspirational for now. For Indian businesses and exporters, progress in the India-US trade relationship remains closely watched given the potential impact on sectors like pharmaceuticals, IT services, and manufacturing.
