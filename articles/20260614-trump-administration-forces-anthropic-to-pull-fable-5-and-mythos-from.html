Trump Administration Forces Anthropic to Pull Fable 5 and Mythos from Foreign Users — India's AI Plans in Question

What happened

Hindustan Times and The Economic Times report that the Trump administration intervened to pull Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos AI models from foreign access, marking the first time a US government action has directly forced an AI company to suspend model availability internationally. Moneycontrol and Indiatimes report that Amazon CEO Andy Jassy discussed risks associated with these Anthropic models with US officials ahead of the suspension. The Economic Times specifically flags that Indian IT services firms now face a competitive disadvantage as a result of the ban.

The context

Anthropic, backed heavily by Amazon, is one of the leading frontier AI labs globally and a direct rival to OpenAI. Its models had gained traction with enterprise and IT services clients in India. The Trump administration has signalled an aggressive posture on controlling advanced AI exports, mirroring earlier semiconductor restrictions, and this intervention marks a significant escalation into software and model access.

Why it matters

Indian IT firms and startups that had built workflows or client pitches around Anthropic's most capable models must now find alternatives, potentially ceding ground to competitors with access. India's stated ambition to become a global AI hub faces a structural challenge if access to frontier US models remains restricted by geopolitical decisions.

What's next

Expect Indian industry bodies and the government to push for clarity on exemptions or bilateral technology-access arrangements with the US as pressure mounts from the IT sector.
