Supreme Court halts all High Court proceedings on Transgender Persons Act, citing scattered rulings

What happened

NDTV reports that the Supreme Court has halted proceedings across various High Courts that were hearing pleas against the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, with the top court noting 'scattered opinion' across benches. The Supreme Court will now consolidate and hear all challenges to the Act centrally.

The context

The Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019 was intended to codify protections for transgender individuals, but activists and legal experts have long argued that it is regressive — requiring a district screening committee to certify gender identity, which critics say violates the NALSA judgment of 2014 that recognised self-identification as a fundamental right.

Why it matters

A unified Supreme Court ruling could either strengthen legal protections for transgender persons or uphold contested provisions, affecting the daily lives, legal identity, and welfare access of an estimated 4-5 lakh transgender people in India. The outcome will also shape how future legislation addresses gender identity.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to consolidate all petitions and likely constitute a larger bench, with hearings that could set a landmark precedent on gender identity rights in India.
