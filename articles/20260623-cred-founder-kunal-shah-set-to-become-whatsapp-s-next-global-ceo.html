CRED founder Kunal Shah set to become WhatsApp's next global CEO

Key facts

  • Kunal Shah is founder of CRED and previously co-founded FreeCharge
  • WhatsApp has over 2 billion users globally; India is its largest market
  • Appointment not yet officially confirmed by Meta or Shah

Kunal Shah, the Mumbai-born entrepreneur best known for founding CRED, the rewards-based credit card payments platform, is reportedly set to become the next global CEO of WhatsApp, according to The Times of India. If the appointment is confirmed, Shah would take charge of one of the world's most widely used communication platforms, with over two billion active users globally and an unmatched presence in India.

Shah built CRED into one of India's most prominent fintech startups after earlier founding FreeCharge, which he sold to Snapdeal and subsequently Axis Bank. His profile as a deeply analytical, product-focused thinker has earned him considerable respect in Silicon Valley and Indian startup circles alike. A move to WhatsApp would be his most consequential leadership role yet.

WhatsApp, owned by Meta, has been expanding aggressively in India — its largest market — with features including UPI-linked payments, business messaging, and AI integrations. An India-rooted CEO would align with that strategic priority and could accelerate the platform's push into financial services and enterprise communication across emerging markets.

The reported appointment has sent ripples through India's tech ecosystem, with many founders and investors hailing it as validation of the Indian entrepreneurial class on the global stage. It would place Shah alongside a handful of Indian-origin executives — including Sundar Pichai and Satya Nadella — who have risen to lead the world's most powerful technology companies.

Neither Meta nor Shah has officially confirmed the appointment, and the transition timeline remains unclear. The story will be closely watched by India's startup and tech communities in the days ahead.
