From Google intern to $60B SpaceX deal: the rise of 25-year-old Michael Truell

Key facts

  • Michael Truell is 25 years old, co-founder of Cursor (AI coding tool)
  • Secured a $60 billion deal with SpaceX
  • Previously interned at Google
  • Cursor competes with GitHub Copilot in AI developer tools

What happened

India Today reports on Michael Truell, a 25-year-old who began his career as a Google intern and has since co-founded Cursor, an AI coding assistant, and secured a $60 billion deal with SpaceX. The profile highlights his rapid ascent as emblematic of the current AI startup boom where developer-tools companies are attracting exceptional valuations.

The context

Cursor, built by Truell's company Anysphere, has become one of the most widely used AI coding assistants globally, competing with GitHub Copilot. The AI developer-tools space has seen explosive investment as enterprises rush to automate software engineering workflows. SpaceX, valued at roughly $350 billion, is one of the world's most valuable private companies.

Why it matters

The story resonates strongly in India, which produces one of the largest pools of software engineers globally, signalling that AI coding tools are creating vast new wealth opportunities for developers willing to pivot to product entrepreneurship.

What's next

Expect Cursor and similar AI coding assistants to accelerate adoption in Indian IT firms and startups as the SpaceX deal draws mainstream attention to the category.
