Lagaan was shot in extreme heat, injuries and jet interruptions, recalls actor Yashpal Sharma on the film's anniversary

What happened

The Indian Express reports that actor Yashpal Sharma recalled that Lagaan was shot under extreme weather conditions, with the cast and crew facing physical injuries and repeated interruptions from jets flying over the location during filming. Sharma shared these memories apparently in connection with a milestone anniversary of the film. No specific anniversary year was mentioned in the headline.

The context

Lagaan (2001), produced by Aamir Khan and directed by Ashutosh Gowariker, was shot primarily in the village of Bhuj, Gujarat, in punishing summer heat. The film was a massive critical and commercial success and received an Academy Award nomination for Best Foreign Language Film, making it only the third Indian film to achieve that feat. It is widely regarded as one of the greatest Indian films ever made.

Why it matters

Yashpal Sharma's testimony adds to the documented legacy of a film that remains a source of national pride, and such stories humanise the immense effort behind landmark Indian cinema. For younger audiences discovering Lagaan on OTT platforms, these accounts deepen appreciation for the film's achievement.

What's next

Expect renewed streaming interest in Lagaan as anniversary coverage and cast interviews circulate across entertainment media and social platforms.
