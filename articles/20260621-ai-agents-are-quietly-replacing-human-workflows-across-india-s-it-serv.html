AI agents are quietly replacing human workflows across India's IT services sector

Key facts

  • AI agents autonomously execute multi-step tasks replacing human IT workflows
  • India's IT sector employs over 5 million people directly
  • Shift moves billing from time-and-material to outcome-based contracts
  • Entry-level process roles face sharpest displacement risk

Artificial intelligence agents are fundamentally altering the workforce model inside India's IT services industry, with companies increasingly deploying autonomous software systems to handle tasks that once required large human teams, Business Standard reports.

Unlike simple automation tools, AI agents can plan, reason, and execute multi-step workflows with minimal human supervision — covering functions from code generation and testing to customer support triage and data processing. India's top IT firms, which built their global dominance on labour-arbitrage models, are now integrating these agents into client delivery pipelines at scale, effectively compressing the headcount required per project.

The consequences for workforce composition are already visible. Entry-level and process-heavy roles — traditionally the entry point for hundreds of thousands of engineering graduates each year — face the sharpest displacement risk. Firms are reconfiguring billing structures too, moving from time-and-material models toward outcome-based contracts that reward efficiency over headcount.

Industry observers note that while AI agents create new roles in agent supervision, prompt engineering, and AI quality assurance, the net employment arithmetic remains deeply uncertain. The speed of adoption means that workforce reskilling programmes may struggle to keep pace with the structural change underway.

For a sector that directly employs over five million people and supports millions more in ancillary roles, the shift represents one of the most consequential technology transitions in Indian industry in a generation — one that will test the resilience of the IT talent pipeline for years to come.
