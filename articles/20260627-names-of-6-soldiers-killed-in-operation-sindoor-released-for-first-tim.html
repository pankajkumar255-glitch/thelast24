Names of 6 Soldiers Killed in Operation Sindoor Released for First Time

Key facts

  • 6 soldiers killed in action during Operation Sindoor named officially
  • First time names have been publicly released since the operation
  • Disclosure reported by NDTV

Six soldiers who lost their lives during Operation Sindoor have been named publicly for the first time, the government confirmed, ending weeks of anonymity around the human cost of India's cross-border military strike against terror camps.

The names were released by official channels as reported by NDTV, representing the first formal acknowledgement of individual casualties from the operation. Operation Sindoor, launched in response to a major terror attack on Indian soil, targeted militant infrastructure and drew widespread national attention for its scale and strategic intent.

The decision to release the names carries deep significance — in Indian military tradition, naming the fallen is both an honour and an obligation to their kin. Families of the deceased had been waiting for this formal recognition, and advocacy from veterans' groups had been building pressure on the government to make the information public.

What comes next will likely include state honours, compensation processes for the next of kin, and possibly public tributes. The release of names also opens the door to fuller parliamentary and public scrutiny of the operation's conduct and its costs — a conversation India's defence establishment will now need to engage with more openly.
