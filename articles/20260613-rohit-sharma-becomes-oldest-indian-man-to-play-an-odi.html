Rohit Sharma becomes oldest Indian man to play an ODI

What happened

India Today reports that Rohit Sharma has become the oldest man to play a One-Day International for India, setting a new record in the course of the India vs Afghanistan ODI series. The publication highlights this as a historic personal milestone for the Indian captain. Rohit, who has been a central figure in Indian cricket across all three formats for well over a decade, surpassed a record that had previously stood in the annals of Indian cricket. The achievement was noted ahead of or during the first ODI against Afghanistan in Dharamsala.

The context

Rohit Sharma made his international debut in 2007 and has since become one of India's most decorated batters, holding the record for most centuries in T20 internationals and being renowned for his three double-centuries in ODI cricket. He was appointed India's full-time white-ball captain and later Test captain, leading the side to the T20 World Cup title in 2024. Longevity records in Indian cricket are particularly significant given the fierce competition for places and the physical demands of a packed international calendar. Rohit has continued to defy age, maintaining his fitness and form across formats.

Why it matters

Rohit's record underscores that India can rely on their captain's experience and class even in a transitional period for the squad. For fans, watching a legend extend his own legacy in real time is a rare treat. It also signals to selectors and younger players alike that merit and fitness, not age, determine selection.

What's next

Expect Rohit's performance in the Afghanistan ODI series to be scrutinised closely, with analysts watching whether his batting output matches the historic milestone. His continued presence will also keep conversations about succession planning for Indian cricket's white-ball captaincy simmering.
