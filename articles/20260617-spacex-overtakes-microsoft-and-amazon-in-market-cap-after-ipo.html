SpaceX Overtakes Microsoft and Amazon in Market Cap After IPO

What happened

The Economic Times reports that SpaceX has overtaken Microsoft and Amazon in market capitalisation after its IPO. The development places Elon Musk's company among the most valuable publicly listed firms globally. No specific valuation figure was provided in the headline, but the overtaking of two of the world's largest technology giants underlines the scale of investor appetite for the listing.

The context

SpaceX, founded by Elon Musk, is best known for its Falcon 9 and Starship rockets and its Starlink satellite internet service. It has for years been the most valuable private company in the United States. Microsoft and Amazon are trillion-dollar companies, so surpassing both in market cap places SpaceX in rarefied territory that few companies have ever reached.

Why it matters

The IPO redefines how global capital values space infrastructure, potentially drawing institutional money toward the sector at a moment when India is opening its own space industry to private players. Indian mutual funds and ETFs with global equity exposure will feel the ripple effects of SpaceX entering public indices.

What's next

Expect index providers such as MSCI and S&P to evaluate SpaceX's inclusion in major benchmarks, which would compel passive funds worldwide — including India-focused international funds — to buy the stock.
