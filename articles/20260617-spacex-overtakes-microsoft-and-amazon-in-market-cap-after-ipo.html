SpaceX Overtakes Microsoft and Amazon in Market Cap After IPO

What happened

The Economic Times reports that SpaceX has overtaken Microsoft and Amazon in market capitalisation following its IPO. The listing marks SpaceX as one of the most valuable publicly traded companies in the world. The development comes as Elon Musk's company has expanded rapidly across launch services and its Starlink satellite internet business.

The context

SpaceX, founded by Elon Musk, has been the dominant force in commercial launch services and is the operator of the Starlink broadband constellation. Its IPO was one of the most closely watched in recent memory, given the company's private valuation had already exceeded hundreds of billions of dollars. Microsoft and Amazon are two of the largest companies in the world by market cap.

Why it matters

The listing reorders the global technology hierarchy and could influence how Indian investors and fund managers allocate capital toward space-tech bets. It also intensifies scrutiny on India's own emerging private space sector, including companies backed by ISRO's liberalisation push.

What's next

Expect global institutional investors to re-evaluate space-sector allocations, and Indian regulators and startups alike to benchmark themselves against SpaceX's public-market valuation.
