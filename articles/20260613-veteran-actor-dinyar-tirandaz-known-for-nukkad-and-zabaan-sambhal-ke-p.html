Veteran actor Dinyar Tirandaz, known for Nukkad and Zabaan Sambhal Ke, passes away at 69

What happened

The Indian Express and Hindustan Times both report the death of actor Dinyar Tirandaz at the age of 69. He was widely recognised for his role in the celebrated Doordarshan serial Nukkad, and had more recently appeared in Zabaan Sambhal Ke. Neither report details the cause of death or the precise location where he passed away. The news has prompted an outpouring of tributes from the Indian entertainment community.

The context

Nukkad, produced by Kundan Shah and Saeed Mirza and first aired on Doordarshan in the 1980s, is regarded as one of the most socially conscious and beloved serials in Indian television history. It depicted the lives of ordinary people in a street-side setting and made stars of its ensemble cast. Dinyar Tirandaz was part of that ensemble and carried his television career across several decades, appearing in both serious drama and comedy formats. Zabaan Sambhal Ke, a more recent comedy series, reintroduced him to newer audiences.

Why it matters

The passing of Tirandaz is a reminder of the deep cultural footprint left by Doordarshan-era television on generations of Indian viewers. Actors from that period shaped how Indians experienced storytelling on screen before the satellite and OTT revolution. His death leaves a smaller pool of surviving cast members from that foundational era of Indian TV.

What's next

Expect tributes and retrospective coverage of Nukkad and Tirandaz's career across Indian entertainment media in the coming days. There may also be renewed interest in streaming or re-broadcasting classic Doordarshan serials as audiences remember his work.
