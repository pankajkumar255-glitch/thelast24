'Get Multiple Wives': Online Backlash Follows Supreme Court's ₹30,000 Homemaker Valuation

What happened

The Times of India reports that the Supreme Court's landmark ruling valuing homemakers' work at ₹30,000 per month triggered a wave of shocking misogynistic comments on social media, with some men suggesting the ruling incentivised marrying multiple women. The reactions went viral and sparked debate about entrenched attitudes toward women's unpaid labour in India. The episode has drawn attention to the gap between judicial progressivism and prevailing social attitudes.

The context

The Supreme Court's ruling was intended to secure better compensation for families of homemakers in legal proceedings, particularly accident and insurance cases. However, the public reception on social media exposed how progressive legal judgments can be met with hostility or deliberately misread. Women's rights organisations have long highlighted that legal recognition of domestic labour must be accompanied by broader social and educational change.

Why it matters

The backlash underlines that landmark court rulings on gender equity face an uphill battle in changing ground-level attitudes, raising questions about enforcement and the cultural messaging needed to support judicial progress. It also puts public pressure on platforms, policymakers, and civil society to respond to online misogyny targeting women's rights milestones.

What's next

Expect women's rights groups and public commentators to amplify the conversation around the ruling's intent, and calls for social media platforms to act against misogynistic content are likely to grow.
