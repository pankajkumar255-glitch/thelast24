Harshit Rana recalled to India's ODI squad for third Afghanistan game

Key facts

  • Harshit Rana recalled to India's ODI squad for 3rd Afghanistan game
  • Third ODI is a series decider
  • Rana is a 23-year-old Delhi fast bowler

Outlook India reports that fast bowler Harshit Rana has been recalled to India's ODI squad ahead of the third and final match against Afghanistan. The development comes as the series reaches a decisive stage, with India looking to seal the contest. Rana, a 23-year-old right-arm quick from Delhi, has been one of the more exciting pace prospects in Indian cricket, known for his ability to generate sharp pace and movement.

His return to the squad suggests that the team management wants to strengthen the pace bowling options after the second ODI, where India's attack combination may have left questions unanswered. The inclusion fits the pattern of India rotating and testing their bowling resources in bilateral series, particularly when a series decider is at stake.

For Indian cricket fans following the Afghanistan series, Rana's recall is a concrete signal of selection intent going into the final match. It also reflects the broader conversation around India's ODI bowling setup — one that former captain Mithali Raj has noted requires significant thought and planning before bigger tournaments.
