US Firm Signa Enters India to Fight Counterfeiters Using AI

Key facts

  • US firm Signa enters India with AI-based anti-counterfeiting platform
  • Counterfeiting affects pharma, FMCG, automotive, and other sectors in India
  • Platform targets product authentication and supply chain tracking

US technology firm Signa has entered India, bringing an artificial intelligence-driven platform designed to identify and curb counterfeit products, The Hindu reported. The move marks one of the more specific applications of AI entering the Indian market — targeting a problem that costs industries and consumers billions of rupees annually.

Counterfeiting is a pervasive challenge in India, affecting a wide range of sectors including pharmaceuticals, consumer goods, automotive parts, agrochemicals, and apparel. Fake products not only cause financial losses for legitimate manufacturers but also pose serious health and safety risks to end consumers — particularly in the case of spurious medicines or substandard industrial components.

Signa's platform is understood to use AI for product authentication and supply chain tracking, enabling brands and enforcement agencies to identify fake goods at various points in the distribution chain. The company's India entry suggests growing commercial appetite for tech-driven anti-counterfeiting tools in the country, as brands scale up and e-commerce makes fake product distribution easier.

The foray comes as Indian authorities and industry bodies have been stepping up efforts to address counterfeiting, and as global technology firms increasingly see India as both a large market for their products and a geography where counterfeiting problems create real demand for solutions. How quickly Signa can build partnerships with brands and regulators will determine its early traction in the market.
