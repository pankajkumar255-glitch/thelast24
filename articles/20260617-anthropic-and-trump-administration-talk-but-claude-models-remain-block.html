Anthropic and Trump Administration Talk, but Claude Models Remain Blocked

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that Anthropic and the Trump administration held talks regarding the restriction on Claude AI models but no agreement has been reached. CNBC reports that prediction market traders are speculating Anthropic will restore access quickly after the Trump administration directed it to limit the models' reach. The situation remains unresolved and fluid.

The context

Anthropic is the maker of Claude, one of the leading large language models used by enterprises and developers worldwide, including in India. The Trump administration's directive to restrict the model's reach is unprecedented and reflects growing US government intervention in AI deployment and access. This follows broader geopolitical debates around AI sovereignty and export controls.

Why it matters

Indian companies and startups that have integrated Claude into their products or workflows face potential disruption if access remains restricted. It also raises questions about the reliability of depending on foreign AI infrastructure for critical business operations.

What's next

Expect further negotiations between Anthropic and the Trump administration, with markets and Indian AI users watching closely for any restoration of access or formal policy announcement.
