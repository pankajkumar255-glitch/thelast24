Anthropic and Google DeepMind CEOs call for U.S.-led AI coalition at G7

Key facts

  • Anthropic and Google DeepMind CEOs addressed G7 leaders
  • Called for a United States-led international AI coalition
  • OpenAI chief separately warned against ceding AI responsibility to tech giants
  • PM Modi called for inclusive AI access at the same summit

CNBC reports that the CEOs of Anthropic and Google DeepMind appeared before G7 leaders to make a joint case for a United States-led international coalition on artificial intelligence. The appeal underscores how the world's most powerful AI laboratories are actively lobbying governments to structure global AI governance around Western — and specifically American — leadership.

The call came alongside separate remarks from OpenAI's chief, who warned G7 governments not to 'cede responsibilities' to AI giants, creating a nuanced picture: AI companies want governments engaged, but also want the rules written in ways that favour their home jurisdictions. Together, these interventions at the G7 represent the most concentrated lobbying effort by major AI labs at a heads-of-government forum to date.

For India, the formation of a U.S.-led AI bloc has direct implications. It could shape access to frontier AI models, semiconductor supply chains, and data-sharing arrangements. Prime Minister Modi's separate call at the same G7 for broad and inclusive AI access reflects India's awareness that the governance architecture being built now could lock in advantages for a small group of nations, making this summit a pivotal moment in the global AI order.
