Govind Namdev says he was 'kept in the dark' about Salman Khan blackbuck film

What happened

The Indian Express reports that actor Govind Namdev has distanced himself from a film based on Salman Khan's blackbuck poaching case, stating publicly that he was 'kept in the dark' about the nature of the project when he was approached for it.

The context

Salman Khan was convicted by a Jodhpur court in 2018 for poaching two blackbucks — a protected species — in Rajasthan in 1998 and was sentenced to five years in jail, though he was granted bail shortly after. The case, which also implicated other Bollywood actors, remains one of the most high-profile wildlife crime convictions in India. Any film dramatising the episode risks legal challenges and controversy.

Why it matters

The incident underscores the legal and reputational risks for actors and filmmakers who associate with projects based on unresolved or contentious legal cases involving major celebrities. It also highlights the lack of transparency that can exist during film casting processes.

What's next

Expect the filmmakers to respond to Namdev's statement and for legal scrutiny around the project's rights and content to intensify.
