Govind Namdev says he was 'kept in the dark' about Salman Khan blackbuck film

What happened

The Indian Express reports that actor Govind Namdev has publicly stated that he was 'kept in the dark' about a film related to Salman Khan's blackbuck poaching case, and is distancing himself from the project. Namdev's remarks suggest he was not fully informed about the film's content or intent before participating.

The context

Salman Khan was convicted in 2018 by a Jodhpur court for poaching blackbucks, an endangered species, during the 1998 filming of Hum Saath-Saath Hain in Rajasthan. The case has dragged through the courts for decades and remains deeply controversial. Any film dramatising the episode would be legally and reputationally sensitive, given Khan's massive fan base and continued legal proceedings.

Why it matters

The episode raises concerns about transparency and informed consent in Indian film productions, and highlights how commercially and legally risky it is to make content about ongoing or unresolved celebrity legal cases.

What's next

Expect the film's producers to respond to Namdev's claims, and watch for any legal notices or further statements from those associated with the project.
