Trump Says Iran Has Agreed To Never Have Nuclear Weapons After Historic Deal

What happened

NDTV reports that Donald Trump stated Iran has 'agreed' to never acquire nuclear weapons, and separately rejected claims related to a $300 million fund. The development follows intensive diplomatic negotiations between the US and Iran whose details are still emerging.

The context

Iran's nuclear ambitions have been a flashpoint since the US withdrew from the 2015 JCPOA deal under Trump's first term. A new agreement, if durable, would be a major geopolitical realignment and could ease sanctions that have affected global oil supply for years.

Why it matters

A stable US-Iran deal could lower global crude oil prices, providing relief to India which imports a significant share of its oil from the Gulf region. It would also reduce security risks for the roughly nine million Indians living and working in Middle Eastern countries.

What's next

Expect close scrutiny of the deal's verification mechanisms and Iran's formal response, as well as reactions from Israel and Saudi Arabia in the coming days.
