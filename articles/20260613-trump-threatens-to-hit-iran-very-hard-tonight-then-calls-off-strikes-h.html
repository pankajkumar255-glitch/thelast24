Trump Threatens to Hit Iran 'Very Hard Tonight', Then Calls Off Strikes Hours Later

What happened

NDTV reports that President Donald Trump publicly threatened to strike Iran 'very hard tonight' and stated his intention to seize control of Iran's oil reserves in a manner similar to US actions in Venezuela. The threat was issued via social media and represented one of the most explicit military warnings Trump has directed at Tehran. However, NDTV subsequently reported — just hours later — that Trump called off the planned strikes before any action was taken. A separate NDTV report also noted that Iran has not yet approved any deal with the United States, indicating nuclear negotiations remain deadlocked.

The context

US-Iran tensions have escalated sharply since Trump reimposed 'maximum pressure' sanctions on Iran after withdrawing from the 2015 nuclear deal during his first term. Negotiations over a new nuclear agreement have proceeded fitfully, with multiple rounds of talks in Oman producing no breakthrough. Iran's oil sector is already under heavy US sanctions, and any military confrontation would threaten the Strait of Hormuz, through which a significant share of the world's oil supply passes. India imports a large portion of its crude oil from the Gulf region, making any West Asian conflict directly consequential for the Indian economy.

Why it matters

Any US military strike on Iran would almost certainly trigger a sharp rise in global crude oil prices, which would feed directly into higher fuel, transport and food costs for Indian consumers. India's strategic and energy ties with Iran — including the Chabahar port project — would also come under pressure. The episode underlines the extreme volatility in US foreign policy that supply chains and financial markets globally must now price in.

What's next

Expect continued volatility in global oil markets as long as the US-Iran standoff remains unresolved. Watch for whether formal nuclear negotiations resume or whether Trump issues further ultimatums that could escalate into actual military action.
