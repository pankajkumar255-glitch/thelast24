Imtiaz Ali's Main Vaapas Aaunga reviewed as a meditation on a country forgetting itself

What happened

The Indian Express has reviewed Imtiaz Ali's Main Vaapas Aaunga, describing it as a film that chronicles a country in danger of forgetting itself — suggesting the film engages with themes of Indian identity, memory and cultural continuity rather than following a conventional narrative format.

The context

Imtiaz Ali is known for films like Jab We Met, Rockstar, Highway, and Tamasha — works that blend personal journeys with broader emotional or social landscapes. Main Vaapas Aaunga marks his return to directing after a gap and arrives at a moment when questions of cultural and national identity are particularly resonant in Indian public discourse.

Why it matters

Films that engage seriously with India's social and cultural fabric matter beyond entertainment — they shape public conversation and reflect how creative industries process the country's rapid changes. A strong critical reception can extend the film's theatrical life through word-of-mouth.

What's next

Expect audience reception and opening week collections to determine whether the film's critical appreciation translates into commercial viability for future similar projects.
