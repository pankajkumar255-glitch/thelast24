World Music Day: Shankar Mahadevan says Indian musicians are 'doing wonders on the world stage'

Key facts

  • Shankar Mahadevan spoke on World Music Day (June 21)
  • Said Indian musicians are 'doing wonders on the world stage'
  • Reflects growing global reach of Indian artists across genres

Shankar Mahadevan used World Music Day on June 21 to celebrate what he sees as a landmark era for Indian musicians, telling Hindustan Times that artists from India are 'doing wonders on the world stage.' The acclaimed composer and singer, himself a towering figure in Indian music, spoke with evident pride about the expanding global ambitions of his peers and the younger generation of Indian artists.

Mahadevan pointed to a growing trend of Indian musicians breaking into international markets and earning recognition well beyond the subcontinent. The shift is visible across genres — from classical and Hindustani traditions to contemporary fusion, film music and independent artists who have built substantial international audiences through streaming platforms and global tours.

World Music Day, observed across the world every June 21, is a moment when the industry pauses to reflect on music's cultural power. This year, conversations in India have centred on how the country's artists are no longer merely following global trends but helping to shape them — a sentiment Mahadevan's comments reinforced strongly.

For a country with one of the world's most diverse and prolific music cultures, the recognition on the global stage represents both an artistic and economic opportunity. Mahadevan's voice carries weight in this conversation, given his own career spanning Bollywood blockbusters, Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy collaborations, and a vocal style that has defined eras of Indian film music.
