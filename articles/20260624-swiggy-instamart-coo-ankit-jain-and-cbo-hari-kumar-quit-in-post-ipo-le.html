Swiggy Instamart COO Ankit Jain and CBO Hari Kumar Quit in Post-IPO Leadership Rejig

Key facts

  • Swiggy Instamart COO Ankit Jain resigned
  • Swiggy Instamart CBO Hari Kumar also resigned
  • Departures follow Swiggy's IPO
  • No replacements announced yet

Swiggy's quick-commerce unit Instamart has lost two of its most senior executives, with Chief Operating Officer Ankit Jain and Chief Business Officer Hari Kumar both stepping down as part of a top-level management rejig. The simultaneous departure of two C-suite leaders underscores the scale of the organisational change underway at the unit.

The exits follow Swiggy's IPO, after which several companies undergo leadership restructuring as they transition from startup-mode accountability to the expectations of public-market investors. Quick-commerce has become one of the most fiercely contested segments in Indian consumer tech, with Blinkit, Zepto, and Swiggy Instamart locked in a race for market share, delivery speed, and profitability.

Instamart has faced pressure to demonstrate a credible path to profitability while simultaneously defending and expanding its city footprint. Leadership continuity is seen as critical in this environment, and the back-to-back resignations of the COO and CBO will raise questions about strategic direction and execution bandwidth in the near term.

Swiggy has not yet announced replacements for either role. Observers will watch closely for the company's next moves on leadership appointments, as well as any changes to Instamart's operational or growth strategy that the rejig might signal.
