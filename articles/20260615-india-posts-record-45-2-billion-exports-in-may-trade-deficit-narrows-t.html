India Posts Record $45.2 Billion Exports in May, Trade Deficit Narrows to $28.21 Billion

What happened

Firstpost, Moneycontrol, Reuters, and The Economic Times all report that India's exports reached a record $45.2 billion in May, with the trade deficit coming in at $28.21 billion — nearly unchanged from the previous month. The Economic Times notes that petroleum exports helped cushion the impact of an elevated energy import bill. Moneycontrol describes the trade gap as 'nearly flat,' highlighting that strong export performance offset import pressures.

The context

India's trade deficit has been a persistent concern, often widening when global crude oil prices rise. A record export figure is significant because it reflects improved competitiveness and demand across sectors such as engineering goods, chemicals, and petroleum products. This data comes as India and the US are in active trade negotiations, making the export health of the economy particularly relevant to ongoing diplomatic discussions.

Why it matters

A narrower trade deficit reduces pressure on India's current account and helps stabilise the rupee, which in turn can moderate imported inflation affecting fuel and consumer goods prices. Record exports also signal stronger manufacturing and services output, with positive implications for jobs and industrial growth.

What's next

Expect the government to cite these record export figures as leverage in the upcoming India-US trade talks scheduled for June 23-24.
