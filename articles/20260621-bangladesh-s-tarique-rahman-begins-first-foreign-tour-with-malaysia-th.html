Bangladesh's Tarique Rahman Begins First Foreign Tour with Malaysia, Then China

Key facts

  • Tarique Rahman begins debut foreign tour with stops in Malaysia and China
  • He is the BNP chief and Bangladesh's dominant post-Hasina political figure
  • Visit signals potential Bangladesh tilt toward China at India's expense
  • India-Bangladesh relations have been strained since Sheikh Hasina's ouster

Tarique Rahman has set off on his first foreign tour since emerging as the dominant political figure in Bangladesh's post-Hasina landscape, visiting Malaysia before proceeding to China — a choice of destinations that carries unmistakable diplomatic signalling.

Rahman, son of former Prime Minister Khaleda Zia and the de facto leader of the Bangladesh Nationalist Party, has long been based abroad and is now asserting himself on the international stage. His decision to prioritise Malaysia, home to a significant Bangladeshi diaspora and a key source of remittances, makes economic sense. The inclusion of China, however, is the more geopolitically charged stop.

Beijing has been steadily expanding its presence in Bangladesh through infrastructure investment and defence ties, and a high-profile visit from Rahman would send a clear message about the direction Dhaka intends to take. It comes at a time when India-Bangladesh relations have grown notably tense following the ouster of Sheikh Hasina — long seen as a trusted partner of New Delhi — and the rise of a more sceptical political establishment in Dhaka.

For India, a neighbour that shares thousands of kilometres of border with Bangladesh and depends on stable ties for connectivity, trade, and security cooperation, the trajectory of Tarique Rahman's foreign policy priorities will be a matter of close and anxious attention.
