Shahid Kapoor Now Insists on Group Narrations After Deva and O Romeo Failures

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Shahid Kapoor has adopted a new approach to selecting scripts following the box-office failures of Deva and O Romeo, insisting on attending script narrations with his team rather than independently. The report suggests this collaborative approach is a deliberate course correction after two consecutive commercial disappointments. Deva and O Romeo both underperformed relative to expectations and their budgets. The change in process indicates that Kapoor and his team are taking a more cautious, consensus-driven approach to future projects. No specific upcoming films were named in the headline.

The context

Shahid Kapoor is one of Bollywood's established leading men, with major hits including Kabir Singh, Padmaavat, and Haider to his name. However, like many of his contemporaries, he has faced an increasingly unpredictable box office in recent years, with audience tastes fragmenting across OTT platforms and regional cinema. Deva, an action thriller, and O Romeo both failed to generate the kind of returns expected of a star of his profile. The broader Bollywood ecosystem has seen several A-list actors recalibrate their approaches following a wave of big-budget failures since 2022. Script selection has become one of the most debated topics within the Hindi film industry as production costs rise and theatrical audiences become more selective.

Why it matters

For Hindi film audiences and industry watchers, Shahid Kapoor's publicly reported strategy shift is a window into how Bollywood's star ecosystem is adapting to commercial uncertainty. It raises questions about the sustainability of the solo-star model and whether collective creative decision-making can improve hit rates. His next project will be closely scrutinised as a test of whether this recalibration works.

What's next

Expect Kapoor's next film announcement to be analysed intensely in light of this reported change in approach. Industry observers will likely watch whether the group narration model translates into stronger project choices and better box-office results.
