Indian IT stocks fall up to 7% after Accenture slashes revenue outlook, deepening sector growth fears

Key facts

  • Indian IT stocks fell up to 7% following Accenture's revenue outlook cut
  • Accenture is a key bellwether for Indian IT sector demand
  • TCS, Infosys, Wipro among firms with overlapping global client bases
  • Sell-off driven by fears of slowing discretionary tech spending in the West

CNBC reports that Indian IT stocks slumped by as much as 7% after Accenture, the global consulting and technology services giant, cut its revenue outlook — fuelling fresh concerns about the pace of growth across the sector. The move rattled markets because Accenture's earnings guidance is widely treated as a leading indicator for Indian IT bellwethers such as TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and HCL Technologies, all of which serve overlapping pools of global enterprise clients.

The Indian IT sector has already been navigating a cautious demand environment, with clients in the United States and Europe deferring or trimming discretionary technology spending amid macroeconomic uncertainty. Accenture's downward revision sharpened those worries, triggering a broad sell-off in IT counters on Indian exchanges.

For everyday Indian investors, the correction is consequential: technology stocks represent one of the largest sectoral weightings in the Nifty 50 and BSE Sensex, meaning index-linked mutual funds and SIP portfolios felt the impact immediately. Analysts will now watch upcoming quarterly results from Indian IT firms for signs of whether Accenture's caution reflects a wider structural slowdown or a company-specific issue.
