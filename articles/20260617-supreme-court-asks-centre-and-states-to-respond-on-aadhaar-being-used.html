Supreme Court asks Centre and states to respond on Aadhaar being used as citizenship proof

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has issued notices to the Centre and states seeking their response on a plea concerning the alleged misuse of Aadhaar cards as proof of citizenship. The petition argues that Aadhaar, which only establishes identity and residency, is being incorrectly used to determine citizenship status, which it is not legally designed to do.

The context

Aadhaar was created under the Aadhaar Act, 2016, as a biometric identity system establishing residency, not citizenship. The Supreme Court itself has previously clarified that Aadhaar cannot be used as citizenship proof. Yet field-level application — in welfare delivery, NRC proceedings, and police verification — has blurred this distinction, raising alarm among civil liberties groups.

Why it matters

Treating Aadhaar as citizenship proof could expose long-settled residents, including the elderly and migrant workers, to detention or disenfranchisement despite holding valid Aadhaar cards. A Supreme Court directive to the government could force a nationwide correction of this practice, affecting hundreds of millions of Aadhaar holders.

What's next

Expect the Centre and state governments to file their affidavits within the court's stipulated timeframe, after which the matter is likely to be listed for a fuller hearing.
