Modi and Carney Meet at G7, India and Canada Set 2026 Deadline for Trade Deal

What happened

NDTV reports that PM Narendra Modi and Canadian PM Mark Carney met at the G7 Summit and set a 2026 deadline to finalise a bilateral trade deal. The meeting signals a formal restart of high-level engagement between India and Canada after a prolonged and bitter diplomatic rupture.

The context

India-Canada relations hit their lowest point in decades in 2023 when PM Justin Trudeau publicly alleged Indian government involvement in the killing of Khalistani separatist Hardeep Singh Nijjar on Canadian soil, a charge India firmly denied. The two countries expelled diplomats and trade and immigration ties were severely strained. Mark Carney's election as Canada's new PM and his engagement with Modi at the G7 marks an attempted reset.

Why it matters

A renewed India-Canada trade relationship could unlock significant opportunities for Indian IT firms, pharmaceutical exporters, and the large Indian-origin population in Canada, while also helping India diversify its trade partnerships beyond China and the US. The 2026 deadline gives both governments a concrete, politically accountable target to work toward.

What's next

Expect working-level trade talks to resume formally between New Delhi and Ottawa in the coming months as both sides begin drafting a framework ahead of the 2026 deadline.
