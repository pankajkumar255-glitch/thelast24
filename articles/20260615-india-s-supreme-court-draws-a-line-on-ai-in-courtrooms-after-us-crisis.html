India's Supreme Court Draws A Line On AI In Courtrooms After US Crisis

What happened

NDTV Profit reports that India's Supreme Court is developing a framework to regulate artificial intelligence use in courtrooms, drawing lessons from controversies in the United States where AI-generated fake legal citations misled courts. The court is working to establish clear lines on permissible and impermissible AI applications in Indian legal proceedings. This is among the first formal steps by India's apex court to institutionally address AI's growing role in the justice system.

The context

In the US, several lawyers faced sanctions after submitting briefs containing fabricated case citations generated by AI tools such as ChatGPT, exposing serious risks of AI-assisted legal work without human verification. India's judiciary has been progressively adopting technology — including AI-based tools for case management and translation — but lacks formal guardrails for AI use in litigation. The Supreme Court's intervention comes as AI tools become widely accessible to the legal profession.

Why it matters

Clear guidelines will protect the integrity of court proceedings and prevent AI-generated errors or fabrications from influencing judicial outcomes in a country with over four crore pending cases. It also sets a precedent that will shape how the entire Indian legal ecosystem — from district courts to high courts — approaches AI adoption.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to release draft guidelines on AI use in legal proceedings, likely followed by consultation with Bar Councils and technology experts before formal adoption.
