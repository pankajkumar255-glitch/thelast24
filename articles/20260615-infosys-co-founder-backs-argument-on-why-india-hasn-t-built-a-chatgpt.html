Infosys Co-Founder Backs Argument on Why India Hasn't Built a ChatGPT

What happened

India Today reports that Infosys co-founder — the headline references an Infosys co-founder, contextually Sridhar Vembu whose views are cited — has backed arguments explaining why India has not yet built a large foundational AI model comparable to ChatGPT, amid broader global debate about AI development. The discussion was triggered in the context of Anthropic's Claude and a reported pause on Fable 5.

The context

India has a large base of AI engineers and several AI-focused startups, but has not yet produced a globally competitive foundational large language model. Sridhar Vembu of Zoho has been a prominent voice arguing that India must invest in deep, indigenous technology rather than building applications on top of foreign infrastructure. The global AI race is intensifying, with the US and China well ahead.

Why it matters

Without homegrown foundational AI, India risks technological dependency on foreign platforms for critical infrastructure, healthcare, and governance applications. The debate directly affects how Indian government and private capital is directed toward AI investment.

What's next

Expect the debate to intensify as India's AI mission spending is reviewed and pressure grows on domestic players to demonstrate foundational model capabilities.
