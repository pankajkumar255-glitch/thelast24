Trump Warns Syria to Handle Hezbollah if Israel Cannot

What happened

NDTV reports that President Donald Trump publicly stated that if Israel cannot handle Hezbollah, Syria will have to do the job, placing an explicit security expectation on the new Syrian government. The statement was made in the context of ongoing regional tensions following the US-Iran nuclear agreement and Israel's continued military operations.

The context

Syria's political landscape transformed in late 2024 when the Assad government fell and a new administration took power. The new Syrian leadership is navigating complex relationships with neighbouring states and global powers. Hezbollah, the Lebanese militant group backed by Iran, has historically operated with significant freedom across the Syria-Lebanon border, and Israel has conducted hundreds of strikes against it.

Why it matters

A more unstable or militarised Syria directly threatens the broader Middle East, which is home to over nine million Indians and supplies the bulk of India's crude oil imports. Any escalation could send oil prices sharply higher.

What's next

Expect the new Syrian government to respond cautiously to Trump's remarks while quietly signalling its stance to both the US and regional players.
