Prediction Markets Bet Anthropic Will Quickly Restore AI Model Access Despite Trump Curbs

What happened

CNBC reports that traders on prediction markets are speculating that Anthropic will restore access to its AI models quickly after the Trump administration directed the company to limit their reach. The market bets reflect a view that commercial and legal pressures will push back against the government's intervention. No timeline for any actual restriction or restoration has been confirmed.

The context

Prediction markets have increasingly become a barometer of informed sentiment on fast-moving events, including tech and policy disputes. Anthropic's Claude models are used by enterprises and developers globally, and any sustained restriction would have significant commercial consequences. The Trump administration's move is seen as part of a broader effort to assert government control over powerful AI systems.

Why it matters

If prediction markets are right and access is restored quickly, disruption to Indian users of Claude-based tools would be minimal; but if restrictions persist, businesses that have built products on Anthropic's API could face costly pivots to alternative models.

What's next

Expect Anthropic to negotiate with the Trump administration in the coming days, with clarity on the actual scope and duration of any access limits likely to emerge shortly.
