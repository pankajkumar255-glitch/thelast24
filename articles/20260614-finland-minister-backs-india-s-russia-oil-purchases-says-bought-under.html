Finland Minister Backs India's Russia Oil Purchases, Says Bought Under Price Cap

What happened

NDTV reports that a Finnish minister defended India's purchases of Russian oil, explicitly stating that the oil was acquired under the price cap set by Western nations. The remarks amount to a formal European endorsement of India's energy procurement strategy.

The context

Since Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022, India has dramatically increased imports of discounted Russian crude, becoming one of Russia's largest oil customers. The G7 and EU imposed a price cap of $60 per barrel on Russian oil to limit Kremlin revenues while keeping supply flowing. India has maintained that its purchases are consistent with this framework and serve its national energy security interests.

Why it matters

European backing reduces diplomatic pressure on India over Russian oil imports, giving New Delhi greater confidence to continue a policy that helps moderate domestic fuel prices and manages its current account deficit. It also signals a pragmatic shift in how some Western allies view India's energy balancing act.

What's next

Expect India to cite this statement in future diplomatic exchanges with Western partners as it continues to navigate its independent energy and foreign policy stance.
