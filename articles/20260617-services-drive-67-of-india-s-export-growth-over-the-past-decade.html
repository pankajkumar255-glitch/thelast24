Services Drive 67% of India's Export Growth Over the Past Decade

Key facts

  • Services drove 67% of India's export growth over the past decade
  • Shift away from goods exports like shirts and textiles
  • Software and IT services are the primary growth engine

Moneycontrol reports that services have contributed 67% of India's total export growth over the past decade, representing a fundamental shift in the structure of the country's external economy. Where India was once primarily known as an exporter of textiles, garments, and commodities, software, IT services, and business process management now dominate the export earnings mix.

This shift has been driven largely by the rapid expansion of India's technology sector, including IT services giants, global capability centres, and a growing software products industry. The rise of digital delivery has allowed Indian firms to scale globally without the logistical constraints that limit goods exports.

For everyday Indians, this structural change has profound implications: the health of the IT and services sector directly influences foreign exchange reserves, the strength of the rupee, and the government's fiscal capacity. It also means that policy choices around education, digital infrastructure, and trade in services carry outsized economic consequences for the nation.
