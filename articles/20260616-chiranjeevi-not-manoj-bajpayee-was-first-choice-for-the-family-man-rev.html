Chiranjeevi, not Manoj Bajpayee, was first choice for The Family Man, reveals director Raj Nidimoru

What happened

The Indian Express reports that director Raj Nidimoru disclosed that Chiranjeevi was the original choice to play the lead in The Family Man before Manoj Bajpayee was ultimately cast in the role. Nidimoru made the revelation in a recent interview, offering a rare glimpse into the pre-production casting process of the acclaimed Amazon Prime Video series.

The context

The Family Man, created by Raj Nidimoru and Krishna D.K. for Amazon Prime Video, became a landmark Indian OTT production when it launched in 2019, with Manoj Bajpayee's performance as a middle-class intelligence officer widely praised. Chiranjeevi is one of Telugu cinema's biggest stars with a massive pan-India following. The show's success spawned a second season and cemented Amazon Prime Video's position in the Indian streaming market.

Why it matters

The anecdote underscores how pivotal casting choices are in shaping a show's cultural impact — Bajpayee's everyman quality was central to The Family Man's enormous popularity across language groups. It also spotlights the increasing ambition of OTT creators to attract top-tier pan-India talent from the very beginning.

What's next

Expect renewed fan speculation about The Family Man Season 3, with Nidimoru's candid interviews suggesting the creators are in a reflective, possibly pre-announcement mode.
