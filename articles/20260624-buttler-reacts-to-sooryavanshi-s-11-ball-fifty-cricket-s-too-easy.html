Buttler reacts to Sooryavanshi's 11-ball fifty: 'Cricket's too easy'

Key facts

  • Sooryavanshi scored a fifty off just 11 balls for India A
  • Buttler reacted: 'Cricket's too easy'
  • Sooryavanshi is part of India's squad for the England tour

Jos Buttler, England's white-ball captain, could barely contain his astonishment after Vaibhav Sooryavanshi smashed an 11-ball fifty for India A — a knock so explosive that Buttler's tongue-in-cheek verdict, 'Cricket's too easy', quickly became the most-shared reaction across Indian sports media.

The remark, at once wry and genuinely admiring, captures the mood around the 14-year-old Rajasthan opener whose cricketing maturity has left opponents and observers scrambling for superlatives. An 11-ball half-century is the kind of innings that belongs in highlight reels for decades; that it came in a high-profile India A fixture only magnified its impact.

Sooryavanshi has already been included in India's squad for the upcoming England tour, making him one of the youngest players ever to earn a senior national call-up. His form in the lead-up games has silenced any doubters who felt the selection was premature, and Buttler's comment suggests even England's dressing room is taking notice.

For Indian fans, the buzz around Sooryavanshi is a rare, joyful phenomenon — a teenager from Rajasthan carrying the weight of enormous expectation and answering it with a smile and a swing of the bat. The England series now carries an extra subplot: whether the boy wonder can reproduce this pyrotechnics on the biggest stage of all.
