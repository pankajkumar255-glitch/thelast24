US Export Controls on AI: Why Washington Suddenly Blocked Foreigners from Anthropic's Most Powerful Models

What happened

Moneycontrol and The Indian Express report that the US government issued a directive under export control regulations requiring Anthropic to suspend access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models for users outside the United States. The directive was framed around security concerns, though specific details of the threat assessment have not been made public. The reports explain that this represents a new frontier in US technology controls, applying export restriction logic to AI software for the first time at this scale.

The context

The US has aggressively expanded export controls on advanced semiconductors and chip-making equipment targeting China since 2022, and the extension of such controls to AI models reflects a growing consensus in Washington that frontier AI is a national security asset. Anthropic, founded by former OpenAI researchers, develops some of the world's most capable AI systems and receives backing from US strategic investors. India is not the target of these controls, but its users are collateral casualties.

Why it matters

Indian AI startups, researchers, and enterprises that relied on Anthropic's top-tier models for competitive applications now face an uncertain future for those integrations, with potential knock-on effects for product timelines and investment decisions. More broadly, this episode illustrates that access to the world's best AI can be revoked at any time by geopolitical decree, a risk that affects every sector of India's digital economy.

What's next

Expect the US to clarify the scope of these controls and potentially extend similar restrictions to other frontier AI providers, prompting India's government to fast-track its national AI policy response.
