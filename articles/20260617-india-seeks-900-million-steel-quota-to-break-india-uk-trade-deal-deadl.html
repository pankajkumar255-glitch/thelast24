India Seeks $900-Million Steel Quota to Break India-UK Trade Deal Deadlock

Key facts

  • India demands $900-million duty-free steel export quota to UK
  • Quota demand is a key condition to unblock India-UK FTA talks
  • India-UK FTA negotiations began in January 2022
  • UK has post-Brexit safeguard tariffs on steel imports

What happened

The Indian Express reports that India is seeking a $900-million steel export quota to the UK as a condition to resolve the logjam in ongoing India-UK free trade agreement negotiations. The steel demand represents a concrete Indian ask in exchange for concessions on other sectors the UK wants access to.

The context

India and the UK launched FTA negotiations in January 2022, but talks have repeatedly stalled over disagreements on tariffs, mobility of Indian professionals, and market access. Steel has become a flashpoint because the UK has post-Brexit safeguard tariffs on steel imports, and India is one of the world's largest steel producers.

Why it matters

A finalised India-UK FTA could boost bilateral trade significantly beyond the current roughly $36 billion annually, benefiting Indian steel, textiles, and services sectors. For Indian steelmakers, a guaranteed $900-million quota would provide a stable, high-value export channel.

What's next

Expect both sides to intensify negotiations ahead of a potential deal deadline, with steel quota terms likely to be a make-or-break issue in the next round of talks.
