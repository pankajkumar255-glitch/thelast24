Apple MacBooks and iPads get up to 70% costlier in India after latest price hike

Key facts

  • MacBooks and iPads up to 70% costlier in India
  • India described as the hardest-hit market from Apple's latest price hike
  • Affects both MacBook and iPad product lines

Apple's latest round of price revisions has hit Indian consumers the hardest globally, with MacBooks and iPads becoming up to 70% more expensive in the country. The scale of the increase is striking — a jump of that magnitude on premium devices pushes them further beyond the reach of mainstream buyers and even budget-conscious professionals who rely on Apple's ecosystem for work.

India has been a key growth market for Apple in recent years, with the company ramping up local manufacturing and expanding its retail presence. The price hike runs counter to those efforts, potentially slowing the momentum Apple has built in the country. Premium laptops and tablets are already discretionary purchases; a 70% price increase could shift significant demand towards rivals or push buyers toward older-generation models.

The reasons behind such steep increases in India specifically could include a combination of import duty structures, currency dynamics, and the pricing tiers Apple applies across different regions. India's high tariffs on electronics imports have long inflated Apple device prices relative to Western markets, and any global cost adjustment tends to get amplified locally.

For consumers, the timing is difficult. Whether the hikes apply uniformly across the product range or are concentrated on specific models is unclear from the available details, but the headline figure of up to 70% signals a material shift in Apple's India pricing strategy — one that buyers and resellers will need to reckon with immediately.
