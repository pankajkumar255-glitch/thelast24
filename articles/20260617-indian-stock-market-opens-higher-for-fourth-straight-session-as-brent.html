Indian stock market opens higher for fourth straight session as Brent crude slips below $79

What happened

India Today reports that the Indian stock market opened higher for the fourth consecutive day on June 17, supported by Brent crude oil prices slipping below $79 per barrel. The easing in global oil prices is boosting investor sentiment by improving India's import bill outlook and reducing inflationary pressures.

The context

Indian equity markets had been under pressure earlier in 2025 amid global trade war fears and elevated crude prices. A sustained dip in Brent crude is a meaningful tailwind for India given its heavy dependence on oil imports, and tends to trigger broad-based buying across oil-sensitive sectors including airlines, paints, tyre companies, and FMCG.

Why it matters

A prolonged equity market rally improves the returns of over 10 crore retail mutual fund investors in India, while lower crude prices simultaneously reduce the risk of petrol and diesel price hikes, protecting household budgets across the country.

What's next

Expect markets to closely track further movements in Brent crude and any signals from the US-Iran diplomatic process, which could determine whether this rally has further legs.
