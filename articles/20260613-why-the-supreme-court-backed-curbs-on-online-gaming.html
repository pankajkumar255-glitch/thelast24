Why the Supreme Court backed curbs on online gaming

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has explained its reasoning for backing curbs on online gaming in India. The court's position addresses the regulatory boundaries around the sector, particularly in the context of real-money gaming and its social impact. The ruling engages with the longstanding legal distinction between games of skill — which have traditionally enjoyed more protection — and games of chance, which states have wider powers to restrict. The judgment is significant for an industry that has grown rapidly and attracted billions in investment over the past several years.

The context

India's online gaming sector has been one of the fastest-growing digital industries, with platforms like Dream11, MPL, and several real-money rummy and poker sites attracting tens of millions of users. The legal landscape has been contested, with different High Courts taking varying positions on whether specific games constitute skill or chance. The government introduced a regulatory framework through amendments to the IT Rules in 2023, creating a system of self-regulatory organisations for online gaming platforms. Several states have attempted blanket bans on online gaming, leading to constitutional challenges about the extent of state powers versus fundamental rights to trade and personal freedom.

Why it matters

The ruling provides judicial backing for stricter regulation of online gaming, which could result in more platforms being restricted or required to comply with tighter rules. For millions of users who play real-money games as a hobby or income source, access to certain platforms may be curtailed. The gaming industry, which has attracted significant domestic and foreign investment, faces greater regulatory uncertainty. The decision also signals that courts are willing to prioritise social harm concerns over industry growth arguments in the gaming space.

What's next

Expect gaming companies to study the judgment carefully and potentially challenge specific aspects in lower courts or through regulatory submissions. The government's self-regulatory framework for online gaming will likely come under renewed scrutiny as regulators decide how to implement the court's reasoning.
