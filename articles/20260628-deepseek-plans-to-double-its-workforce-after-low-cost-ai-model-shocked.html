DeepSeek Plans to Double Its Workforce After Low-Cost AI Model Shocked the Tech Industry

Key facts

  • DeepSeek plans to double its workforce
  • Company's low-cost AI model shocked global tech industry earlier in 2025
  • Expansion comes as Chinese AI firms gain ground amid US export restrictions

DeepSeek, the Chinese AI company whose low-cost model sent shockwaves through Silicon Valley and rattled stock markets at the start of 2025, is now planning to double its workforce. The Times of India reported the expansion, which reflects the company's confidence that it can sustain and build on the disruption it caused with its debut model — a system that matched or approached the performance of leading American AI at a dramatically lower cost.

The scale of that initial shock was significant: DeepSeek's model challenged the prevailing assumption that frontier AI required the kind of massive compute investment that only well-capitalised US labs could afford. Its release prompted a sharp reassessment of valuations across the AI hardware and software sectors globally, and triggered renewed debate about the true cost and resource requirements of cutting-edge AI development.

Doubling its workforce would give DeepSeek significantly more capacity for research, engineering and deployment — areas where it will need to compete as US companies and governments tighten access to American AI models. The expansion also aligns with broader signals from Chinese AI firms, including Zhipu, that they intend to press their advantage as export controls and access restrictions increasingly constrain what US labs can offer internationally.

For India, DeepSeek's growth trajectory is of direct relevance. Indian startups and developers have already begun experimenting with the company's open-weight models as cost-effective alternatives to commercial US offerings. A larger, better-resourced DeepSeek could accelerate that trend, deepening the presence of Chinese AI tools in one of the world's most important emerging technology markets.
