Rain Ruins India's Promising Start as First T20I vs England Abandoned at Chester-le-Street

Key facts

  • Match at Chester-le-Street abandoned due to rain
  • Shreyas Iyer and Abhishek Sharma both scored fifties
  • No result; five-match T20I series remains level
  • Vaibhav Sooryavanshi was not included in India's playing XI

India's first T20 international of their England tour ended in frustration on Wednesday night as persistent rain forced the abandonment of the opening match at Chester-le-Street, with no result possible.

Before the weather intervened, India had given their fans plenty to cheer. Shreyas Iyer and Abhishek Sharma both struck half-centuries, laying the foundation for what appeared to be a competitive total. The partnership and the quality of strokeplay suggested India had arrived in England in sharp batting form, despite the unsettled English summer conditions.

The abandonment meant neither side picked up points from the first fixture, leaving the five-match series finely poised from the very first evening. For India, it was a case of what might have been — a strong batting performance yielding nothing in the standings. For England, the rain provided a lucky reprieve from a chase that could have tested their top order.

Noticeable by his absence from the playing eleven was teenage sensation Vaibhav Sooryavanshi, whose omission had been widely discussed in the build-up. India's management opted to retain their settled top-three order, a decision that appeared justified by the fifties from Iyer and Abhishek. The series now moves forward with the second T20I offering both sides the first real chance to gain an advantage.
