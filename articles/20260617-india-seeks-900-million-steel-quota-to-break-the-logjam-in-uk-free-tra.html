India seeks $900-million steel quota to break the logjam in UK free trade deal talks

What happened

The Indian Express reports that India is seeking a $900-million steel quota from the UK as a condition to resolve the current logjam in free trade agreement negotiations between the two countries. The steel market access demand has emerged as one of the central sticking points preventing a final deal from being signed.

The context

India and the UK formally launched FTA negotiations in January 2022 and have completed over a dozen rounds of talks, but several contentious issues — including steel quotas, mobility for Indian professionals, and tariffs on whisky and automobiles — have prevented a final agreement. The deal is considered strategically important for both countries post-Brexit.

Why it matters

A breakthrough on the steel quota would accelerate the broader India-UK FTA, which could unlock market access for Indian IT professionals, garments, and pharmaceuticals in the UK, while bringing tariff reductions on imported goods that affect Indian businesses and consumers.

What's next

Expect the next round of India-UK trade talks to test whether the UK is willing to meet India's steel quota demand, which will determine whether a deal can be announced before the end of 2025.
