Grammy winner Ricky Kej says he'd rather stay lesser known than make music that doesn't define him

Key facts

  • Ricky Kej is a three-time Grammy Award winner
  • Kej says artistic identity matters more to him than mass popularity
  • Interview given at the Expresso event, reported by The Indian Express

The Indian Express reports that Ricky Kej, one of India's most celebrated musicians with three Grammy Awards to his name, has spoken openly about his approach to his craft at the Expresso event. In the interview, Kej states plainly that he would rather be lesser known for music that genuinely defines him than compromise his artistic identity in pursuit of wider popularity.

Kej has built his career largely outside mainstream Bollywood, working in ambient, world music, and environmental themes — a path that has earned him global critical recognition but keeps him less familiar to casual Indian listeners compared to film composers. His Grammy wins have nonetheless made him one of the highest-decorated Indian musicians in international awards history.

The comment resonates as Indian music increasingly grapples with the tension between algorithm-driven streaming hits and artistically driven work. For everyday listeners, Kej's perspective is a reminder that India's most internationally acclaimed musicians are often those who have deliberately chosen a road less travelled.
