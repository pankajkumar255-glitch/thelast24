Curaçao Make World Cup 2026 History in June 21 Group-Stage Action

Key facts

  • Curaçao made history at FIFA World Cup 2026 on June 21
  • 2026 World Cup co-hosted by USA, Canada, and Mexico
  • First World Cup with expanded 48-team format
  • Curaçao is a Caribbean island nation of ~150,000 people

Curaçao etched their name into FIFA World Cup 2026 history on June 21, producing a landmark result that marked a significant milestone for the small Caribbean nation on football's biggest stage. The moment added another chapter to what has been a tournament full of surprise storylines, with several lesser-fancied nations putting up strong performances in the group stages of the expanded 48-team competition.

The 2026 World Cup, co-hosted across venues in the United States, Canada, and Mexico, is the first to feature 48 teams — a format change that was always expected to open the door for smaller footballing nations that would previously have been eliminated in qualifying. Curaçao, a constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands with a population of around 150,000, has been one of the more prominent beneficiaries of the expanded tournament landscape in CONCACAF.

ESPN India's live coverage tracked the June 21 action across multiple venues, with Curaçao's historic outing drawing particular attention from fans logging in for real-time updates. The full details of their result and opposition will be confirmed in the day's final standings, but the achievement itself represents a high point for Caribbean football.

For Indian viewers, the World Cup has become must-watch television, with fans largely backing South American and European giants but increasingly drawn to the underdog narratives that every expanded tournament generates. Curaçao's story will resonate particularly with those who dream of India one day qualifying for the tournament itself.
