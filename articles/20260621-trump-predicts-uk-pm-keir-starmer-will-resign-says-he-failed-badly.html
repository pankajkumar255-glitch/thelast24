Trump Predicts UK PM Keir Starmer Will Resign, Says He "Failed Badly"

Key facts

  • Trump says UK PM Keir Starmer will resign
  • Trump claims Starmer "failed badly" in office
  • Starmer became PM after Labour's July 2024 election landslide
  • Comments add strain to US-UK diplomatic relations

Donald Trump has declared that British Prime Minister Keir Starmer will resign, publicly labelling him a leader who has "failed badly" — an extraordinary broadside against the head of government of the United States' most historically intimate ally. The remarks, made in Trump's direct and unfiltered style, mark one of the sharpest public criticisms any sitting US president has levelled at a serving UK prime minister.

Starmer, who led Labour to a sweeping general election victory in July 2024 after more than a decade of Conservative rule, has faced considerable domestic turbulence in his first year in office. Economic pressures, internal party debates over welfare reforms, and a difficult political environment have weighed on his approval ratings — providing a backdrop that Trump appears to be reading as terminal weakness.

The comments land at a sensitive moment for the transatlantic relationship. Trump's administration has already strained ties with several European allies over trade tariffs, defence spending demands, and differing positions on the Ukraine conflict. A US president openly predicting — and arguably encouraging — the fall of a sitting British leader adds a new and destabilising dimension to those tensions.

For India, which maintains strong bilateral ties with both Washington and London and is in active trade negotiations with the UK, political instability in Britain or a further rupture in the US-UK axis carries real consequences. A weakened or transitional UK government would slow the progress of the India-UK Free Trade Agreement, a deal long in the making and still awaiting finalisation.
