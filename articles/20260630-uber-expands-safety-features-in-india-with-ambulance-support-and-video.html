Uber expands safety features in India with ambulance support and video recordings

Key facts

  • Uber India adds ambulance support integration to its app
  • Video recording during trips is now available on Uber India
  • Features target passenger and driver safety in Indian cities

Uber has rolled out a set of new safety features for its Indian platform, including ambulance support integration and video recording during trips — upgrades that address two of the most frequently cited safety gaps for ride-hailing passengers in the country.

The ambulance support feature is designed to streamline emergency response during rides, potentially connecting users or bystanders to medical services more quickly in the event of an accident or health emergency. The video recording capability, meanwhile, creates an on-trip record that can serve as evidence in the event of disputes, harassment complaints, or safety incidents — a feature that has been demanded by women's safety advocates and urban commuters for years.

India is one of Uber's most strategically important markets, and safety has been a recurring pressure point — both for the company and for regulators. Incidents involving ride-hailing drivers have prompted calls for stronger in-app safety mechanisms, and Uber's latest rollout appears to be a direct response to that sustained pressure. The Indian Express, which reported the development, noted both features as significant additions to Uber's existing safety toolkit in India.

The expansion comes as competition in India's cab aggregator market remains fierce, with Ola and newer players vying for market share. Safety features have increasingly become a differentiator in user retention, particularly among women commuters and late-night travellers.

Whether these features will be enough to satisfy regulators and safety advocates remains to be seen, but their introduction marks a concrete step toward making ride-hailing safer for the everyday Indian commuter.
