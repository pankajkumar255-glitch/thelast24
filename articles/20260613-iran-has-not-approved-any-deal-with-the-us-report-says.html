Iran Has Not Approved Any Deal With the US, Report Says

What happened

NDTV reports that Iran has not yet approved any deal with the United States, citing a report that indicates nuclear negotiations between the two countries remain unresolved. The disclosure came around the same time that President Trump was issuing military threats against Iran on social media, suggesting that diplomatic and military pressures are running simultaneously. No specific details of the sticking points in the negotiations were provided in the headline, but the report signals that a breakthrough is not imminent.

The context

The United States and Iran have been engaged in indirect and, more recently, direct nuclear talks since Trump returned to office and reimposed maximum pressure sanctions. Iran has steadily advanced its uranium enrichment to near-weapons-grade levels, raising alarm among Western governments and Israel. Previous negotiations under the Biden administration also failed to produce a revived nuclear deal. India has historically maintained diplomatic and economic ties with Iran, including the Chabahar port agreement, making the trajectory of US-Iran relations directly relevant to Indian foreign policy.

Why it matters

A prolonged stalemate — or worse, a military escalation — between the US and Iran would keep upward pressure on global oil prices, raising India's import bill and potentially stoking domestic fuel price inflation. India's strategic investment in the Chabahar port in Iran would also be jeopardised if sanctions tighten further or conflict breaks out. The situation underscores how geopolitical risks thousands of kilometres away translate directly into economic costs for ordinary Indians.

What's next

Expect further rounds of US-Iran talks, though the pace and format remain uncertain given the hostile rhetoric from Washington. Watch for any Israeli response to the developments, as Israel has previously threatened unilateral military action against Iranian nuclear facilities.
