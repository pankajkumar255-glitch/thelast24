Trump to Visit India 'Early Next Year', Says US Secretary of State Marco Rubio

Key facts

  • Marco Rubio says Trump expected to visit India 'early next year'
  • Rubio describes himself as an 'enormous fan of PM Modi'
  • US and India hoping to finalise a bilateral trade deal soon
  • Announcement reflects strong India-US diplomatic momentum

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio has confirmed that President Donald Trump is expected to visit India early next year, with the two countries hoping to finalise a bilateral trade deal in the near future. Rubio made the announcement publicly, describing himself as an 'enormous fan of Prime Minister Narendra Modi' and signalling that ties between Washington and New Delhi are in strong shape.

The prospect of a Trump visit is the clearest indication yet that India-US relations remain a diplomatic priority for the current US administration. While the exact timing has not been locked down — Rubio used the phrase 'early next year' and noted they were 'hoping' to make it happen — the public commitment from America's top diplomat carries considerable weight.

Central to the diplomatic push is the ongoing effort to finalise a trade agreement between the two countries. India and the US have been in negotiations aimed at reducing tariffs and expanding market access across multiple sectors. A deal, if concluded, could significantly reshape trade flows, with major implications for Indian exporters in pharmaceuticals, textiles, agriculture, and technology services.

For India, the symbolism of a Trump visit would also be substantial. It would follow Prime Minister Modi's own engagement with the Trump administration and reinforce the trajectory of the relationship at a time when India is navigating complex geopolitical pressures on multiple fronts.
