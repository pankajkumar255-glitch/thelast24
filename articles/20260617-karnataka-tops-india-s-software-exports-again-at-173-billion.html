Karnataka tops India's software exports again at $173 billion

What happened

Deccan Herald reports that Karnataka has topped India's software and services exports chart for another consecutive year, recording $173 billion in exports. The figure makes Karnataka by far the largest contributor to India's overall IT export earnings. No other state comes close to matching this scale of technology-sector output.

The context

Bengaluru-based IT hubs housing giants like Infosys, Wipro, and hundreds of global captive centres have long made Karnataka the engine of India's software export economy. India as a whole targets over $300 billion in IT-BPM exports in coming years, and Karnataka alone accounts for a dominant share of that ambition.

Why it matters

For millions of IT professionals in Karnataka, sustained export leadership signals continued hiring momentum and wage growth. For India's forex earnings and current-account balance, Karnataka's performance is a critical stabiliser.

What's next

Expect other state governments to announce competing tech-park and incentive policies as they try to narrow the gap with Karnataka's software export dominance.
