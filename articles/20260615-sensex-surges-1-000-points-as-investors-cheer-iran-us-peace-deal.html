Sensex surges 1,000 points as investors cheer Iran-US peace deal

What happened

India Today reports that the Sensex surged 1,000 points after investors welcomed a peace deal between the US and Iran. The development sharply reduced fears of a prolonged West Asian conflict that had been weighing on global markets. The rally signals broad-based buying across Indian equities.

The context

West Asian tensions had been rattling global markets and pushing up oil prices, both of which hurt India disproportionately as one of the world's largest crude oil importers. Any de-escalation in the region historically triggers a relief rally in Indian equities. The Sensex is India's benchmark 30-stock index and a key barometer of investor sentiment.

Why it matters

A sustained rally could boost household wealth for millions of equity and mutual fund investors across India. It also signals improving macro conditions — lower oil import costs and reduced inflation pressure — that benefit the broader economy.

What's next

Expect markets to watch whether the Iran-US deal holds and translates into a sustained decline in crude oil prices over the coming week.
