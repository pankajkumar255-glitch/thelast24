India's Proposed AI Court Rules 2026 Would Ban Algorithmic Risk Scoring in Justice

What happened

Frontline Magazine reports that India's AI Court Rules 2026 are being framed to include a ban on the use of AI-based risk scoring in court proceedings. The rules signal a proactive regulatory stance by India's judiciary or government to prevent algorithmic tools from influencing judicial outcomes.

The context

AI risk-scoring tools, which assign probabilities to outcomes like reoffending or flight risk, have been widely criticised in the United States and Europe for encoding racial and socioeconomic biases. India, with its vast and diverse judiciary handling millions of cases annually, faces particular risks if such tools were adopted without safeguards. The proposed rules come as India's broader Digital India and AI governance frameworks are still evolving.

Why it matters

Keeping AI risk scores out of Indian courts ensures that bail, sentencing, and parole decisions remain grounded in human judgment and legal principles rather than data models that may reflect historical inequalities. It sets a precedent for how India will govern AI in other high-stakes public-sector domains.

What's next

Expect the AI Court Rules 2026 to be circulated for public and stakeholder consultation before formal notification, with legal scholars and civil society likely to weigh in on their scope.
