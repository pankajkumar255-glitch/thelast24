Ali Fazal's crime series Raakh brings the real Ranga-Billa case to OTT — and it hit close to home for Bobby Deol

What happened

The Indian Express reports on Raakh, an Ali Fazal-led crime series now available on OTT, which is based on the notorious Ranga-Billa criminal case from the 1970s. A separate Indian Express piece notes that the case had a direct and disturbing impact on Bobby Deol's childhood, adding a personal dimension to the story's cultural resonance. The Indian Express also highlights Jason Statham's survival thriller Shelter as another notable new OTT arrival this week. The two titles are positioned as the headline streaming offerings for Indian audiences this weekend.

The context

The Ranga-Billa case — involving Kuljeet Singh (Ranga) and Jasbir Singh (Billa) — was one of the most sensational criminal cases in post-independence India, culminating in a high-profile trial and the hanging of the two convicts in 1982. The case involved the kidnapping and murder of siblings Geeta and Sanjay Chopra, children of a naval officer, and gripped the nation. It has been referenced in popular culture multiple times over the decades. Ali Fazal has established himself as a credible lead in both Bollywood and international productions, making him a strong draw for an OTT crime series.

Why it matters

A prestige Indian crime series rooted in a real and widely remembered case gives OTT platforms compelling original content that resonates across age groups. For subscribers, it is a strong reason to engage with the platform this weekend. The Bobby Deol personal connection adds a layer of human interest that extends the story beyond pure entertainment.

What's next

Expect audience reactions and critical reviews to drive social media conversation around Raakh in the coming days. If the series performs well, it could prompt further investment in true-crime adaptations of landmark Indian criminal cases for OTT platforms.
