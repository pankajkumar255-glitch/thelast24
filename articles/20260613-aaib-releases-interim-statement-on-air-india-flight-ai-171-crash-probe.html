AAIB Releases Interim Statement on Air India Flight AI-171 Crash Probe

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau has released an interim statement regarding its investigation into the crash of Air India Flight AI-171. The AAIB is the statutory body responsible for investigating civil aviation accidents in India and operates independently of the Directorate General of Civil Aviation. An interim statement at this stage typically outlines the factual information gathered so far — including flight data recorder and cockpit voice recorder readouts — without assigning cause or blame. The full investigation report is expected to take considerably longer to complete.

The context

Air India Flight AI-171 was involved in a crash that drew immediate national and international attention, given Air India's status as India's flagship carrier now owned by the Tata Group following its privatisation in 2022. Aviation accident investigations in India are conducted by the AAIB under the Aircraft Act and in accordance with ICAO Annex 13 standards, which require interim reports to be published within 30 days of an accident when the final report will take more than a year. The crash prompted immediate scrutiny of maintenance practices, pilot training standards, and Air India's operational safety record under its new ownership. Global aviation safety bodies, including the NTSB and BEA, may be involved as technical advisers depending on the aircraft's country of manufacture.

Why it matters

The AAIB's findings, even in interim form, could trigger immediate safety directives affecting Air India's fleet and potentially other Indian carriers operating similar aircraft. For millions of Indians who fly Air India domestically and internationally, the investigation's conclusions will influence confidence in the airline and may result in visible operational changes. The crash and its investigation also have financial implications for Tata Group's flagship airline as it undertakes a multi-billion dollar modernisation and rebranding effort. Transparency in the probe process will be watched closely by international aviation regulators who certify Indian carriers for overseas routes.

What's next

Expect the AAIB to release more detailed factual data in subsequent interim reports, with a probable cause determination likely 12-18 months from the date of the accident. Watch for any emergency airworthiness directives from the DGCA that may follow specific findings in the interim statement.
