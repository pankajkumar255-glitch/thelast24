AI firm's stock jumps 33% after US government restricts access to Anthropic's Claude model

What happened

NDTV reports that an unnamed AI firm's stock jumped 33% after the US government restricted access to Anthropic's Claude model, referred to as the Mythos model. The restriction appears to have driven investors to back a competing platform that stands to gain the redirected business. The move signals the US government's growing role as a kingmaker in the commercial AI industry.

The context

Anthropic, backed by Google and Amazon, has positioned Claude as a leading large language model for enterprise and government use. US government contracts are among the most lucrative in the AI industry, and any restriction on a vendor's access can rapidly shift billions of dollars in potential revenue. This episode follows broader US scrutiny of AI safety and national security considerations around frontier AI models.

Why it matters

The 33% single-day stock surge shows how directly government AI policy translates into market wealth creation or destruction, affecting investors and pension funds globally. For Indian AI startups and investors watching US regulatory moves, it is a sharp reminder that government access decisions can override product quality overnight.

What's next

Expect Anthropic's White House meeting to clarify whether the restriction is temporary or permanent, with rival AI firms' valuations likely to remain volatile until the outcome is known.
