England Name Harry Brook-Led 17-Member Squad for India T20Is; James Coles Gets Maiden Call-Up

Key facts

  • Harry Brook named England T20I captain for India series
  • James Coles gets maiden England call-up after record-breaking hundred
  • England name 17-member squad for the T20I series
  • India vs England T20Is are among cricket's most watched contests

England have named a 17-member squad for the T20I series against India, with Harry Brook leading the side in what promises to be one of the most eagerly anticipated short-format series of the year. The squad was confirmed by selectors and reported by The Times of India and Wisden, with Brook's captaincy appointment signalling England's continued faith in aggressive, youth-forward leadership.

The headline selection is James Coles, who earned a maiden England call-up after scoring a record-breaking hundred that turned heads in domestic cricket. Wisden reported that Coles's century was the catalyst for his inclusion, with selectors rewarding his explosive form with an immediate international opportunity against one of the world's strongest T20 sides.

The series represents a marquee fixture in the international calendar. India, perennial contenders in the T20 format, will provide a stern examination of England's new-look lineup. Brook, who has established himself as one of the most exciting batters in world cricket across all formats, will be under pressure to impose himself in the captaincy role.

For Indian fans, the squad announcement signals the beginning of a build-up that will generate enormous viewership. The BCCI is yet to confirm India's own squad for the series, but the England announcement sets the stage for a contest that invariably draws some of the largest television and streaming audiences in world cricket.
