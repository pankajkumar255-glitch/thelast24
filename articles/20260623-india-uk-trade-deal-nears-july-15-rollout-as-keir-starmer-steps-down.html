India-UK trade deal nears July 15 rollout as Keir Starmer steps down

Key facts

  • India-UK FTA targeted for July 15 rollout
  • UK PM Keir Starmer resigned amid deal finalisation
  • Nigel Farage backed the deal, citing Brexit
  • Deal covers goods, services, and Indian professionals' access to UK

The India-United Kingdom Free Trade Agreement is heading toward a formal rollout around July 15, according to India Today, even as British political life was shaken by the resignation of Prime Minister Keir Starmer — the leader who shepherded the deal to its conclusion on the UK side.

Starmer's departure creates short-term political uncertainty about whether the agreement's domestic ratification process in the UK Parliament will proceed smoothly, or whether a new Labour leader might seek to revisit any of its terms. However, senior officials on both sides have indicated the deal is substantially locked in, covering goods tariffs, services market access, and provisions for Indian professionals working in Britain.

Adding a cross-party flavour to the debate, Nigel Farage — leader of the Reform UK party and a long-standing Brexit champion — endorsed the pact, arguing that leaving the European Union had given Britain the freedom to strike exactly this kind of bespoke bilateral agreement. His backing may help ease passage through Parliament regardless of who leads the Labour government.

For India, the agreement is seen as one of its most significant trade deals in decades, promising reduced tariffs on textiles, pharmaceuticals, and engineering goods, while opening doors for Indian IT and professional services firms in the British market. With both sides eager to demonstrate the deal's benefits, the July 15 target date is expected to hold despite the political turbulence in London.
