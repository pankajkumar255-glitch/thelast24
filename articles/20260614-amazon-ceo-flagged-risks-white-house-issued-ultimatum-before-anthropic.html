Amazon CEO Flagged Risks, White House Issued Ultimatum Before Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 Was Restricted

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that Amazon's intervention and a 90-minute White House ultimatum preceded Anthropic's decision to restrict access to its Claude Fable 5 and Mythos models for foreign users. Indiatimes separately reports that Amazon's CEO flagged risks associated with the Anthropic model before the US government imposed the restrictions. Business Standard and The Indian Express also confirm that the US has restricted foreign access to Claude Fable 5 and Mythos, citing national security considerations.

The context

Anthropic, one of the world's leading AI safety companies, is backed heavily by Amazon and counts among the developers of the most capable large language models available. The US government has grown increasingly protective of frontier AI models, viewing them as strategic assets, and has been tightening export controls on advanced technology. This episode marks one of the most direct interventions by the White House into an AI company's product availability.

Why it matters

Indian businesses, developers, and institutions using Anthropic's Claude API or products could lose access to these models if US restrictions are broadened, forcing a scramble for alternatives. The episode sets a precedent that even well-funded, safety-focused AI firms can be compelled to shut off access at short notice under government pressure.

What's next

Expect Indian AI startups and enterprises dependent on Anthropic's models to review their contingency plans, and watch for any official clarification from Anthropic on which geographies remain affected.
