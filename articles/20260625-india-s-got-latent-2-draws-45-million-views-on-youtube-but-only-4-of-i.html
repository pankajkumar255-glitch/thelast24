India's Got Latent 2 draws 45 million views on YouTube but only 4% of its audience on paid OTT Netflix

Key facts

  • India's Got Latent 2 earned 45 million views on YouTube
  • Only 2.2 million views on Netflix
  • Netflix accounted for just 4% of total viewership
  • Highlights India's resistance to paying for OTT content

India's Got Latent Season 2 has become a vivid case study in India's streaming divide: the show racked up 45 million views on YouTube while drawing just 2.2 million views on Netflix, with the paid platform accounting for a mere 4 percent of total viewership.

The figures underline a problem that has haunted the Indian OTT industry since its inception. Despite Netflix's global prestige and significant investment in Indian content, the platform struggles to convert audiences who can access similar or identical programming for free. For India's Got Latent, a show with obvious popular appeal, the overwhelming majority of viewers simply chose not to pay.

The data also raises questions about the content strategy for platforms like Netflix in India. If a show with tens of millions of fans generates only a fraction of its views on the paid tier, the economics of acquiring or producing such content become difficult to justify. It suggests that the sweet spot for paid Indian OTT may lie in exclusive content that has no free equivalent — prestige dramas, films, or sports — rather than user-generated or semi-viral formats.

For creators and producers, the numbers are simultaneously flattering and sobering. A 45 million view count signals genuine cultural reach; a 4 percent paid conversion rate signals that monetisation through subscription platforms remains an uphill battle in a market where YouTube has trained hundreds of millions of Indians to expect premium entertainment at no cost.
