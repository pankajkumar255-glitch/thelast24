Israeli Army Labels Killed Gaza Journalist A 'Hamas Terrorist' After Confirming His Death

Key facts

  • Israeli military confirmed killing a journalist in Gaza
  • IDF labelled the journalist a 'Hamas terrorist' after his death
  • Dozens of journalists have been killed in Gaza since October 2023
  • Israel has faced sustained international criticism over press casualties

The Israeli Defence Forces confirmed on Wednesday that a journalist killed in Gaza was their target, simultaneously labelling him a Hamas operative — a claim that immediately drew fierce pushback from press freedom organisations and international media bodies.

The killing adds to what Committee to Protect Journalists and Reporters Without Borders have described as an unprecedented toll on media workers in a single conflict. Dozens of journalists have been killed in Gaza since the Israel-Hamas war began in October 2023, making it one of the deadliest conflicts for the press in recorded history. Israel has consistently maintained that journalists embedded with or acting for Hamas are legitimate military targets under international law.

Critics, however, argue that the post-facto labelling of killed journalists as militants has become a pattern that effectively removes accountability for strikes on media workers. International humanitarian law requires that such designations be accompanied by verifiable evidence, a standard that rights groups say Israel has repeatedly failed to meet transparently.

The incident is being closely watched by global journalism bodies and governments that have called for the protection of press freedom in conflict zones. For India, which has positioned itself as a defender of a rules-based international order while maintaining strong ties with Israel, the episode is another data point in a complex diplomatic balancing act.
