Mercedes-Benz Eyes Bigger Role for India in Software Development and Sourcing

Key facts

  • Mercedes-Benz expanding India's role in software development
  • India also targeted for greater sourcing contributions
  • Part of broader automotive industry shift to software-defined vehicles

Bloomberg reports that Mercedes-Benz is planning to give India a significantly larger role in its global software development and sourcing operations. The automaker is looking beyond India's status as a premium car market and wants to leverage the country's engineering capabilities and cost competitiveness for core technology work.

India has steadily grown as a global capability centre destination for multinational corporations, with Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune hosting major R&D and tech operations. The automotive sector, undergoing a rapid shift toward software-defined vehicles, is increasingly reliant on large pools of software engineers — a resource India supplies in abundance.

For Indian professionals in the automotive technology space, this development points to expanding opportunities in embedded software, connected vehicle systems, and supply chain roles tied to a globally recognised luxury brand. It also reinforces India's positioning as a technology partner — not just a consumer market — for major global corporations.
