Telegram challenges India's temporary ban in Delhi High Court over NEET paper leak fears

Key facts

  • Telegram filed a petition in Delhi High Court against India's temporary ban
  • Ban linked to NEET-UG exam paper leak allegations on the platform
  • Hearing was scheduled for the same day as the filing
  • India is one of Telegram's largest global markets

Reuters and BBC report that Telegram has challenged an Indian government order imposing a temporary block on the platform, filing a petition before the Delhi High Court with a hearing scheduled for the same day. The ban stems from government concerns that Telegram was used to leak question papers for the NEET-UG medical entrance examination, one of India's most high-stakes tests with over 2 million candidates.

The NEET paper leak controversy has already triggered widespread protests, a Supreme Court review, and calls for a retest. Authorities have been scrutinising digital platforms used to distribute leaked papers, and Telegram — with its large anonymous channels — came under particular focus. A temporary block order was issued against the platform as part of the investigation response.

Telegram's legal challenge argues against the ban order, setting up a direct confrontation between the messaging app and the Indian government in court. India is one of Telegram's largest markets globally, making the outcome commercially significant for the platform.

For Indian users, the stakes are immediate: Telegram is widely used for educational content, news channels, and community groups. A sustained ban would disrupt millions of daily users while the broader NEET controversy continues to unfold.
