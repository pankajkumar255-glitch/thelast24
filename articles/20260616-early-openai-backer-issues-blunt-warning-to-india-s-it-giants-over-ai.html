Early OpenAI backer issues blunt warning to India's IT giants over AI disruption

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that the man who made an early bet on OpenAI has issued a blunt warning to India's IT giants about the disruptive threat posed by artificial intelligence. The investor's comments suggest that large Indian IT firms may be underestimating how rapidly AI will reshape the global technology services industry.

The context

India's IT sector — led by TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, and others — is the backbone of the country's services export economy, earning over $200 billion annually and employing over 5 million people. The rise of generative AI tools like ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot is already automating coding, testing, and support tasks that Indian IT firms have long monetised through large offshore teams.

Why it matters

A structural decline in demand for traditional IT outsourcing would directly affect employment and India's balance of payments. Indian IT companies are already reporting slower deal growth and increased automation-related workforce concerns.

What's next

Expect Indian IT majors to accelerate AI upskilling programmes and pivot towards higher-value AI consulting and platform services to counter these structural headwinds.
