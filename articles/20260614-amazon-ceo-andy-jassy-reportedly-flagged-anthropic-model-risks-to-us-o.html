Amazon CEO Andy Jassy Reportedly Flagged Anthropic Model Risks to US Officials Before Restrictions Were Imposed

What happened

Indiatimes reports that Amazon CEO Andy Jassy reportedly flagged risks related to Anthropic's AI models — specifically Fable 5 and Mythos — to US government officials ahead of the Trump administration's decision to restrict foreign access. Moneycontrol similarly reported that Jassy discussed Anthropic AI risks with US officials before the suspension. The reports suggest a direct line of communication between Amazon's leadership and US authorities in the lead-up to the unprecedented model suspension.

The context

Amazon has invested billions of dollars in Anthropic and is its primary cloud and infrastructure partner, making it one of the most consequential stakeholders in Anthropic's development trajectory. Jassy's reported involvement illustrates the blurred lines between Big Tech corporate interests, AI safety concerns, and US government national security policy. It also reflects the broader pattern of tech executives engaging directly with the Trump administration on AI governance.

Why it matters

If major AI investors are actively shaping government restrictions on AI models, it signals a new and opaque layer of influence over which technologies reach global markets — including India — with limited public accountability. Indian businesses and policymakers may need to factor in these corporate-government dynamics when planning long-term AI strategies.

What's next

Expect more scrutiny of Amazon's dual role as both Anthropic's biggest backer and an apparent participant in the policy process that led to restricting the same models it helped fund.
