AI models capable of taking down governments and businesses are months away, Five Eyes alliance warns

Key facts

  • Five Eyes (US, UK, Canada, Australia, NZ) issued joint AI warning
  • Governments and businesses could be targeted by AI within months
  • Statement is one of the most explicit public security alerts on AI
  • Comes amid global race to deploy frontier AI models

The Five Eyes alliance — the intelligence partnership spanning the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia and New Zealand — has issued a rare public warning that AI models capable of taking down governments and causing serious damage to major businesses could be ready for deployment within months. The statement, reported by The Guardian, is one of the most explicit security-framed alerts ever issued collectively by the bloc on an emerging technology.

The warning centres on so-called frontier AI models — large, highly capable systems that are approaching or may soon reach levels of autonomous reasoning and action that make them difficult to contain or predict. The Five Eyes' concern is not merely theoretical: the statement implies that adversarial actors, including state-sponsored groups, could harness such models for cyberattacks, disinformation campaigns, and infrastructure disruption at a scale and speed that current defences are not prepared for.

The alert arrives as AI labs in the US, China and Europe race to release increasingly powerful models, and as governments struggle to enact binding regulation. Recent months have seen dramatic claims about AI capabilities — including a US Senate hearing at which Anthropic's AI model was alleged to have breached nearly all NSA systems during a test scenario — fuelling debate about how close the technology truly is to crossing dangerous thresholds.

For India, which is developing its own AI policy framework and has significant exposure to both AI adoption and cyber threats, the Five Eyes statement carries direct relevance. It adds urgent weight to calls — including from analysts at Bernstein — that India must develop indigenous AI capabilities rather than remain dependent on foreign models whose safety and alignment cannot be guaranteed.
