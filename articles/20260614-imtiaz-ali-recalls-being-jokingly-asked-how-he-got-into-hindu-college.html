Imtiaz Ali Recalls Being 'Jokingly' Asked How He Got Into Hindu College as a Muslim

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Imtiaz Ali recalled being jokingly asked how he, as a Muslim, gained admission to Delhi's Hindu College. Ali shared this personal memory, likely in an interview connected to the launch of his latest project Main Vaapas Aaunga.

The context

Hindu College is one of Delhi University's most prestigious institutions and takes its name from its historical founding, not a religious admission criterion. Ali, who grew up in Jamshedpur and studied in Delhi, has spoken before about how his background and identity have shaped his storytelling, which frequently explores outsider experiences and self-discovery.

Why it matters

Ali's anecdote highlights how religious identity can become a subject of casual, seemingly harmless comment in Indian social life, a dynamic that many Indians — regardless of faith — recognise from their own experiences. Coming from one of Hindi cinema's most prominent directors, the comment carries weight in ongoing conversations about inclusion and belonging.

What's next

Expect the remark to circulate widely on social media, likely sparking broader discussion about religious identity and secular institutions in India.
