Soni Razdan Defends Alia Bhatt: 'Not Her Fault She's Daughter of Industry Insiders'

Key facts

  • Soni Razdan says it's not Alia Bhatt's fault she was born to industry insiders
  • Alia Bhatt has been a frequent target of Bollywood nepotism criticism
  • Razdan is herself a veteran Bollywood actress

Soni Razdan has addressed one of Bollywood's most persistent controversies head-on, arguing that her daughter Alia Bhatt cannot fairly be blamed for being born into a family of film industry insiders.

Razdan's remarks push back against the nepotism discourse that has trailed Alia Bhatt since the actress's debut. The argument is straightforward: no one chooses their parents or the advantages those parents confer, and penalising someone for a circumstance of birth is itself a form of unfairness. Razdan is a respected actress in her own right, and her willingness to speak publicly on the subject adds a maternal as well as an industry-insider perspective to a debate that often lacks nuance.

Alia Bhatt has, in recent years, built a body of work — including critically lauded performances in films like Gangubai Kathiawadi and Raazi — that many argue stands entirely on its own merits. Supporters contend that whatever door her surname opened, she has delivered the goods once inside. Critics, however, maintain that the structural advantages of a famous family background mean that equally talented actors from outside the industry never get the same door to open.

The nepotism debate in Bollywood shows little sign of fading. Soni Razdan's comments are unlikely to settle the argument, but they do add a dimension often missing from public discussion: the perspective of those inside the equation who believe the framing of inherited privilege misses the complexity of individual achievement.
