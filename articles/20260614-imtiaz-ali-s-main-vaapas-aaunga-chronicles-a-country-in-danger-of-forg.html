Imtiaz Ali's 'Main Vaapas Aaunga' Chronicles a Country in Danger of Forgetting Itself

What happened

The Indian Express reviews Imtiaz Ali's Main Vaapas Aaunga, describing it as a work that examines a country in danger of forgetting itself. The piece positions the project as a culturally significant statement from a filmmaker known for emotionally layered storytelling.

The context

Imtiaz Ali is among India's most respected mainstream directors, with films like Jab We Met, Rockstar, and Highway establishing him as a chronicler of inner lives set against the Indian landscape. A project addressing collective cultural forgetting carries weight given ongoing conversations about identity, heritage, and change in contemporary India.

Why it matters

For audiences, it signals the arrival of serious, ideas-driven Indian cinema that goes beyond entertainment. Critical attention from publications like The Indian Express can significantly shape how widely a project is seen and discussed.

What's next

Expect wider critical reviews and audience response to clarify whether Main Vaapas Aaunga becomes a significant cultural talking point in the coming weeks.
