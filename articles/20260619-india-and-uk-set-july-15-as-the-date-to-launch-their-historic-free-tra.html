India and UK set July 15 as the date to launch their historic free trade agreement

Key facts

  • July 15 set as implementation date for India-UK Free Trade Agreement
  • Last-minute steel tariff dispute resolved through UK reassurances
  • Deal covers goods, services; key Indian exports include textiles and pharma
  • Negotiations had been ongoing since 2022, delayed multiple times

The Hindu and Reuters report that India and the United Kingdom have announced July 15 as the rollout date for their free trade agreement, resolving a last-minute stumbling block over steel tariffs. The steel dispute — centred on import duty commitments — was settled after the UK provided reassurances to India, allowing both sides to finalise the implementation timeline.

The India-UK FTA has been years in the making, with negotiations that began in 2022 repeatedly delayed over sensitive issues including immigration, professional services, and tariffs on key industrial products like steel and automobiles. The deal announced earlier in 2025 is widely considered a milestone in post-Brexit British trade policy and a significant win for India's export ambitions.

For everyday Indian consumers and businesses, the agreement is expected to reduce duties on Indian exports such as textiles, garments, leather goods, gems, jewellery, and pharmaceuticals entering the UK market. On the import side, British goods including Scotch whisky and automobiles may become more accessible in India over time. The July 15 start date now gives industries on both sides a concrete deadline to prepare for the new trade regime.
