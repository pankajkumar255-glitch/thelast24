India's 'Hidden' AI Winners Add $48 Billion in Value on Data-Centre Boom

What happened

The Economic Times reports that a set of Indian companies it describes as 'hidden AI winners' have added a combined $48 billion in market value, driven by the global data-centre construction boom fuelled by AI demand. These companies are not AI developers themselves but supply the physical and electrical infrastructure that data centres require. The report frames the rally as a downstream consequence of massive hyperscaler investment decisions, including deals like the Meta-Reliance partnership announced the same day. The Economic Times presents this as an emerging investment theme that has so far received less attention than direct AI plays.

The context

India has a well-established capital goods and engineering sector with companies supplying transformers, cables, switchgear, cooling equipment, and specialised real estate — all critical inputs for data centres. Global AI investment has triggered a surge in data-centre construction worldwide, creating acute demand for these components. Indian manufacturers have benefited because domestic demand is rising simultaneously with export opportunities. The BSE capital goods and power indices have been among the stronger performers on Indian markets over the past two years, partly for this reason. Analysts have increasingly flagged this 'picks and shovels' theme as a way to gain AI exposure without betting on specific technology outcomes.

Why it matters

The $48 billion addition represents real wealth created for shareholders of these companies, many of whom are retail investors through mutual funds tracking capital goods or infrastructure indices. It also signals that India's AI opportunity is broader than software exports, encompassing manufacturing and industrial supply chains that employ large numbers of workers. For policymakers, the data strengthens the case for supporting domestic data-centre infrastructure development. The trend could sustain if global hyperscaler capital expenditure continues to rise, as most major forecasts suggest it will through at least 2027.

What's next

Expect analysts and fund managers to publish updated screeners identifying further companies in this supply chain that may not yet have re-rated. Watch whether order books at transformer and cable manufacturers confirm the revenue materialisation that stock prices are already pricing in.
