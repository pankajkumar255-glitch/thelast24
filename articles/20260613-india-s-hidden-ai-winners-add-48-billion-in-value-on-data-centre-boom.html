India's 'Hidden AI Winners' Add $48 Billion in Value on Data-Centre Boom

What happened

The Economic Times reports that a set of Indian companies described as 'hidden AI winners' have added $48 billion in combined market value, riding the surge in data-centre construction activity across India. These companies operate in supporting sectors — including power generation, land and real estate, cable and connectivity infrastructure, and cooling technology — that are indispensable to building large-scale AI data centres. The report frames their rise as a consequence of major hyperscaler deals, such as the Meta-Reliance agreement announced the same day, as well as broader government-backed AI infrastructure investment.

The context

India has seen a wave of data-centre investment announcements over the past two years, with commitments from Microsoft, Google, Amazon Web Services, and now Meta totalling tens of billions of dollars. Data centres are extraordinarily power-hungry: a single large facility can consume as much electricity as a small city, creating enormous demand for power infrastructure, land, and specialised engineering. Indian conglomerates including Adani, Tata, and CtrlS have been expanding data-centre capacity rapidly. The broader AI infrastructure supercycle has similarly lifted ancillary stocks in the US, Taiwan, and South Korea, and India is now seeing a domestic version of this trend.

Why it matters

The $48 billion in added market value signals that AI is reshaping capital allocation in India far beyond the software sector, pulling in hard infrastructure industries. For retail investors, this highlights opportunities — and frothy valuations — in sectors not traditionally associated with technology. For the broader economy, the data-centre boom is expected to generate significant employment in construction, power, and engineering services. It also underscores why securing reliable electricity supply and land clearances will be critical policy priorities for the government.

What's next

Expect continued re-rating of power and infrastructure stocks as more hyperscaler deals are formalised in India. Analysts will watch whether capacity announcements translate into actual construction starts, which will determine whether the market gains are sustained or speculative.
