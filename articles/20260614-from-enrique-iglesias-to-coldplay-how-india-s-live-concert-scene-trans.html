From Enrique Iglesias to Coldplay: How India's live concert scene transformed in five years

What happened

Firstpost reports on the evolution of India's live international concert industry over the last five years, using Enrique Iglesias and Coldplay as bookend examples of how dramatically the market has grown in scale and ambition. The piece analyses shifts in audience demand, ticket prices, and the kinds of global artists now choosing to tour India.

The context

India's live entertainment market has boomed post-pandemic, with Coldplay's 2024 India tour selling out within minutes and sparking widespread debate about ticket scalping and pricing. The country's young, aspirational middle class and improving venue infrastructure have made it increasingly attractive for top-tier global artists to include Indian cities on their world tours.

Why it matters

Rising ticket prices and demand mean ordinary fans are increasingly priced out of major concerts, making the economics of live entertainment a direct pocketbook issue for millions of young Indians. The sector's growth also signals new jobs and revenue in event management, hospitality, and media.

What's next

Expect more global artists to announce India tour dates in 2025 and 2026, likely accompanied by continued debate over ticket pricing regulation and scalping.
