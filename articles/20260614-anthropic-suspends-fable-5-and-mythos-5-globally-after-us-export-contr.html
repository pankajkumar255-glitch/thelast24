Anthropic Suspends Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Globally After US Export Control Directive

What happened

BBC, NDTV, Telegraph India, Indiatimes, and Moneycontrol report that Anthropic suspended access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models for users outside the United States following a directive from US export control authorities. The suspension came just days after the models were launched. Indian Express notes that the move bars foreigners from accessing what were Anthropic's most capable publicly available models.

The context

Anthropic is one of the world's leading AI safety companies and a major competitor to OpenAI, backed by billions in investment including from Google and Amazon. US export control laws, traditionally applied to hardware and defence technology, are increasingly being extended to advanced AI software, reflecting Washington's concern about frontier AI capabilities reaching adversarial states. This is one of the first high-profile instances of a frontier AI model being pulled from global access under such directives.

Why it matters

Indian developers, startups, and enterprises that had integrated or planned to integrate Fable 5 and Mythos 5 into their products now face an abrupt disruption, with no clear timeline for restored access. The episode underlines the risk of building critical digital infrastructure on US-controlled AI platforms, amplifying the case for India to accelerate its own AI development programmes.

What's next

Expect the Indian government and the tech industry to intensify discussions around domestic AI model development and data sovereignty policy in the wake of this sudden access cutoff.
