Shankar Mahadevan reveals the entire music for Dil Chahta Hai was composed in just 3.5 days

Key facts

  • Dil Chahta Hai music composed in just 3.5 days
  • Revealed by composer Shankar Mahadevan
  • Soundtrack released in 2001 and remains a cultural landmark

The music that defined a generation of young Indians was made in three and a half days. Shankar Mahadevan, one third of the composer trio Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy, has revealed that the entire soundtrack of Dil Chahta Hai — released in 2001 and still a fixture on playlists more than two decades later — was created in that remarkably compressed window.

The disclosure is a fascinating footnote to one of Bollywood's landmark films. Farhan Akhtar's directorial debut was a coming-of-age story about three friends navigating adulthood, and its music — breezy, melodic, and emotionally precise — became as defining as the film itself. Tracks from the album are routinely cited by millennials as the soundtrack of their youth, which makes Mahadevan's revelation all the more striking.

The anecdote speaks to how creative inspiration and deadline pressure can produce unexpectedly durable work. Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy, who were already an established force in Bollywood composing by the time Dil Chahta Hai arrived, went on to create several other celebrated soundtracks — but none has quite matched the cultural staying power of this one.

For the film and music industry, the story also serves as a counter-narrative to the idea that great art requires prolonged gestation. Three and a half days of intense creative focus produced something that has outlasted countless more laboured productions — a point that is unlikely to be lost on a new generation of musicians and filmmakers.
