India Now Has Over 2.3 Lakh Startups Generating 25 Lakh Jobs, Says Minister Jitendra Singh

What happened

The New Indian Express reports that Minister Jitendra Singh announced that India has crossed the milestone of 2.3 lakh startups, which are collectively responsible for generating 25 lakh jobs across the country. The minister made these remarks highlighting the success of India's startup policy framework. The figures reflect the cumulative growth of the ecosystem since the launch of the Startup India initiative.

The context

India's Startup India programme, launched in 2016, has been the cornerstone of government policy to foster entrepreneurship. The country has seen an explosion in startups across sectors including fintech, edtech, healthtech, and agritech. India is home to over 100 unicorns, and the ecosystem continues to attract significant domestic and foreign venture capital.

Why it matters

For millions of young Indians, the startup sector represents a growing source of employment and economic opportunity beyond government jobs or traditional industries. The sheer scale of 25 lakh jobs signals that startups have moved from a niche phenomenon to a mainstream pillar of the Indian economy.

What's next

Expect the government to use these figures to push for greater incentives and policy support for startups in the upcoming Union Budget discussions.
