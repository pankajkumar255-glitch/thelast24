India Now Has Over 2.3 Lakh Startups Generating 25 Lakh Jobs, Says Minister Jitendra Singh

What happened

The New Indian Express reports that Minister Jitendra Singh stated India now has over 2.3 lakh startups generating 25 lakh jobs. The minister made these remarks highlighting the scale and employment impact of India's startup ecosystem, attributing growth to government-backed initiatives.

The context

India's startup ecosystem has grown dramatically since the launch of the Startup India initiative in 2016, with the country now hosting over 100 unicorns. Jitendra Singh oversees the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), which registers and supports startups. The 25 lakh jobs figure underscores startups as a meaningful contributor to India's employment landscape alongside traditional sectors.

Why it matters

The scale of startup-generated employment means that policy decisions on taxation, funding regulation, and ease of doing business directly affect millions of workers and entrepreneurs across India. Continued growth in this sector is critical as India seeks to absorb its large young workforce.

What's next

Expect the government to use these figures to push further startup-friendly policies, particularly ahead of budget discussions and in the context of India's global investor outreach.
