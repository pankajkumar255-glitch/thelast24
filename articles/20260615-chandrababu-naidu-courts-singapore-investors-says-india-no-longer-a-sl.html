Chandrababu Naidu Courts Singapore Investors, Says 'India No Longer a Sleeping Economy'

What happened

The Hindu reports that Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister Chandrababu Naidu is on a visit to Singapore where he is courting investors with the pitch that India is now a dynamic, high-growth economy ready for large-scale investment. Naidu's outreach is focused on technology, infrastructure, and industrial sectors relevant to Andhra Pradesh's development plans, including the rebuilding of Amaravati. Singapore hosts significant Indian diaspora wealth and serves as a hub for institutional investors targeting South and Southeast Asia.

The context

Chandrababu Naidu has a long track record of aggressive investment promotion, having previously attracted major tech companies to Hyderabad during his earlier tenure as Chief Minister of undivided Andhra Pradesh. Andhra Pradesh is in the midst of rebuilding its capital Amaravati and needs large inflows of private investment to fund its infrastructure ambitions. Singapore-based funds and family offices are among the most active investors in Indian real estate, technology, and manufacturing.

Why it matters

Successful investment pitches from Singapore could translate into new factories, data centres, and technology parks in Andhra Pradesh — directly creating jobs and boosting the state's infrastructure development. Broader investor confidence in India also supports the rupee and keeps the cost of capital lower for Indian businesses.

What's next

Expect formal investment commitment announcements from Andhra Pradesh in the weeks following Naidu's Singapore visit, likely timed to coincide with an upcoming state investor summit.
