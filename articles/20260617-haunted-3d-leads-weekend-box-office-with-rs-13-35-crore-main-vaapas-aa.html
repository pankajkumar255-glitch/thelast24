Haunted 3D Leads Weekend Box Office With Rs 13.35 Crore; Main Vaapas Aaunga and Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata Struggle

Key facts

  • Haunted 3D collected Rs 13.35 crore at the box office
  • Main Vaapas Aaunga and Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata both struggled
  • Haunted 3D is a re-release of the 2011 Vikram Bhatt horror film
  • Result continues trend of re-releases outperforming new Bollywood films

The Indian Express reports that the re-release of the 2011 Bollywood horror film Haunted 3D has claimed the top spot at the domestic box office, collecting Rs 13.35 crore and comfortably surpassing two new releases: Main Vaapas Aaunga and Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata, both of which failed to gain meaningful traction with audiences.

Haunted 3D, originally directed by Vikram Bhatt and starring Mahaakshay Chakraborty, was one of Bollywood's early forays into 3D horror. Its re-release performance continues a broader trend of Hindi film industry re-releases — including older Shah Rukh Khan, Aamir Khan, and Hrithik Roshan films — drawing strong crowds in recent months, suggesting audience nostalgia is a commercially viable force.

The underperformance of the new releases reflects ongoing struggles for mid-budget Bollywood films to compete for theatrical footfall. Multiplex chains and distributors have increasingly turned to re-releases and Hollywood imports to fill screens when new Hindi films fail to excite advance bookings.

For everyday moviegoers and the film trade alike, the box office outcome raises pointed questions about the viability of new Bollywood productions and why audiences are choosing older content over fresh offerings at the multiplex.
