Supreme Court to hear plea challenging Aadhaar as proof of citizenship

What happened

India Today reports that the Supreme Court is set to hear a plea arguing that Aadhaar should not be treated as valid proof of citizenship. The petition challenges a practice that, if allowed to continue, could blur the legal distinction between identity and nationality.

The context

Aadhaar is India's biometric identity system issued by the UIDAI to residents, not exclusively citizens. The Supreme Court itself, in its landmark 2018 Aadhaar judgment, restricted its use to specific welfare purposes. Concerns around citizenship documentation have intensified following the passage of the Citizenship Amendment Act and discussions around a potential NRC.

Why it matters

A ruling against Aadhaar as citizenship proof would force authorities to rely on a narrower set of documents, potentially leaving vulnerable populations — including the poor and elderly — at risk of statelessness. It also sets a critical precedent on the boundaries of what Aadhaar can legally certify.

What's next

Expect the court to issue notices to the Centre and set a hearing schedule, with the government likely defending Aadhaar's administrative utility across verification processes.
