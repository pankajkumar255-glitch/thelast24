Supreme Court to Hear Plea Challenging Aadhaar as Proof of Citizenship

What happened

India Today reports that the Supreme Court has agreed to hear a plea challenging the use of Aadhaar as proof of citizenship. The specifics of who filed the plea and the precise context — whether it concerns the NRC, immigration proceedings, or another process — have not been detailed in the headline, but the court's willingness to hear the matter signals its constitutional significance.

The context

Aadhaar was established under the Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of Financial and Other Subsidies, Benefits and Services) Act, 2016, and explicitly does not confer citizenship or domicile rights. However, in various administrative and legal contexts, including NRC proceedings in Assam, Aadhaar has been cited as an identity marker, creating confusion about its scope. The Supreme Court had previously in 2018 upheld Aadhaar's constitutionality while restricting its mandatory use.

Why it matters

A ruling limiting or clarifying Aadhaar's role in citizenship determination could have direct consequences for NRC processes, deportation proceedings, and the legal standing of millions of long-term residents who hold Aadhaar cards but face citizenship challenges.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to issue notices to the Union Government and UIDAI, and to set a date for substantive arguments on the scope of Aadhaar as an identity versus citizenship document.
