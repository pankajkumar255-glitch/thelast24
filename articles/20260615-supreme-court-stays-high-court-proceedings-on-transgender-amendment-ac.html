Supreme Court stays high court proceedings on Transgender Amendment Act challenges

What happened

Hindustan Times reports that the Supreme Court stayed ongoing high court proceedings challenging the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Amendment Act. The order means that challenges to the amendment will now be heard and decided by the Supreme Court alone, rather than by multiple high courts simultaneously.

The context

The Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act was passed in 2019 and has faced sustained legal and civil society opposition, with critics arguing it dilutes rights and imposes intrusive screening processes. Subsequent amendments have continued to draw scrutiny. With petitions filed in multiple high courts, there was a risk of contradictory orders, prompting the Supreme Court to consolidate the matter.

Why it matters

The outcome will determine the legal framework governing identity recognition, employment, healthcare and protection from discrimination for India's estimated 4-5 lakh transgender persons. A single Supreme Court verdict will set a uniform national standard rather than a patchwork of state-level rulings.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to issue a schedule for hearing the consolidated petitions, with transgender rights groups and the government likely to present opposing arguments on the amendment's constitutionality.
