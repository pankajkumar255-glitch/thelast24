India Orders Temporary Ban on Telegram Over Exam Fraud Concerns

What happened

TechCrunch reports that India has ordered a temporary ban on Telegram over concerns about the platform being used to facilitate exam fraud. The order targets Telegram specifically amid a broader crackdown on the misuse of messaging platforms for leaking examination papers and coordinating cheating.

The context

Telegram has over 100 million users in India and is widely used for everything from news channels to student study groups. India has faced repeated, high-profile exam paper leak scandals in recent years, including controversies around NEET and other competitive exams, prompting authorities to act against digital platforms allegedly enabling malpractice.

Why it matters

A ban on Telegram would disrupt daily communication, news consumption, and study networks for tens of millions of Indians overnight. It also sets a precedent for the government's willingness to block major global platforms over domestic law-enforcement concerns.

What's next

Expect Telegram to legally challenge the order and intense public debate over the proportionality of a full platform ban versus targeted action against bad actors.
