BCCI weighing feasibility of bilateral series with Pakistan, IPL 2026 likely from early March

What happened

India Today reports that the BCCI is actively evaluating the feasibility of hosting bilateral cricket series involving Pakistan, a move that would be the first of its kind in well over a decade. The same report indicates that IPL 2026 is likely to be scheduled from early March, which would represent a shift from the traditional April start. The BCCI's deliberations on Pakistan bilaterals come in the context of improving diplomatic signals between the two countries, though no formal agreement has been announced.

The context

India and Pakistan have not played a bilateral cricket series since 2012-13, with their encounters limited to ICC tournaments and multi-nation events like the Asia Cup. Political tensions between the two countries have historically dictated the cricketing calendar, making any resumption of bilateral ties a landmark event. The IPL, typically held from late March or April, is the world's richest T20 league and a scheduling anchor for global cricket. An earlier IPL window could affect India's participation in international tours and bilateral commitments early in the year.

Why it matters

A resumption of India-Pakistan bilaterals would be one of the biggest events in world cricket, generating enormous viewership and revenue and satisfying a vast fanbase on both sides of the border. An early-March IPL start would give fans a longer season and could affect the availability of overseas players and India's own international schedule. Both developments underscore the BCCI's growing ambitions to shape global cricket's commercial and diplomatic landscape.

What's next

Expect official announcements from the BCCI on the IPL 2026 schedule in the coming weeks, with franchise preparations beginning shortly after. The Pakistan bilateral question will likely hinge on government-to-government clearances, so diplomatic signals from both New Delhi and Islamabad will be the key indicator to watch.
