India Now Has 2.3 Lakh Startups, Generated 25 Lakh Jobs: Minister Jitendra Singh

What happened

Deccan Herald reports that Minister Jitendra Singh announced India now has 2.3 lakh startups that have collectively generated 25 lakh jobs. The minister made these remarks highlighting the scale and employment impact of India's startup ecosystem.

The context

India has been building its startup ecosystem aggressively since the launch of the Startup India initiative in 2016. The country is now home to over 100 unicorns and ranks among the top three startup ecosystems globally. Government recognition and funding support have been central to this growth.

Why it matters

25 lakh jobs generated by startups represents a significant share of formal private-sector employment, particularly for young, educated Indians. As traditional sectors face automation pressures, the startup ecosystem's growth offers an important alternative employment pathway.

What's next

Expect further policy announcements aimed at deepening startup support, particularly in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities where the next wave of entrepreneurship is likely to emerge.
