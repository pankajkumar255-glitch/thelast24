A year on, Meta's Zuckerberg must now sell the AI model Alexandr Wang was tapped to build

What happened

CNBC reports that a year after Meta enlisted Alexandr Wang, founder of Scale AI, to help build a new AI model, CEO Mark Zuckerberg is now in the position of having to actively sell the model to enterprise customers and the broader market. The story points to the commercial challenges Meta faces in translating AI research investments into revenue.

The context

Meta has been investing heavily in AI, positioning it as central to the future of its platforms including WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook, all of which have massive user bases in India. Alexandr Wang and Scale AI are known for providing high-quality data labelling and AI infrastructure. The competitive AI market, dominated by OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic, makes it difficult for any new model to gain quick traction.

Why it matters

Meta's AI strategy has direct implications for the hundreds of millions of Indian users on its platforms, as AI features drive everything from content recommendations to business tools on WhatsApp. A stumble in Meta's AI commercialisation could delay or dilute AI-powered features rolling out to Indian users.

What's next

Expect Meta to ramp up enterprise sales efforts and announcements around its AI model's capabilities over the coming months as it looks to justify its massive AI investment.
