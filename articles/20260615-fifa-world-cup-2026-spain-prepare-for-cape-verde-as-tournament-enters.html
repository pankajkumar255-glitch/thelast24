FIFA World Cup 2026: Spain prepare for Cape Verde as tournament enters knockout phase

What happened

ESPN reports live updates and latest news from the FIFA World Cup 2026 on June 15, with Spain preparing for their match against Cape Verde. The updates cover ongoing group-stage action as the tournament progresses across venues in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

The context

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the first to feature 48 nations, up from 32, making it the largest edition in the tournament's history. Spain are among the top contenders, having won the 2024 UEFA European Championship. Cape Verde represent the African Football Confederation and are appearing in the World Cup for only the second time, making them notable underdogs.

Why it matters

The World Cup is the world's most watched sporting event, and its outcomes influence global sports broadcasting rights, advertising spends, and the growth of football viewership in India, where the sport is rapidly gaining a following alongside cricket.

What's next

Expect Spain to be tested by an energetic Cape Verde side, with the result likely to shape Group F standings and knockout round seedings.
