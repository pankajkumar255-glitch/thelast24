Supreme Court seeks Centre and states' response on plea over Aadhaar 'misuse'

Key facts

  • SC issues notice to Centre and states on Aadhaar misuse plea
  • Aadhaar linked to over a billion Indians' banking, telecom, and welfare accounts
  • SC's 2018 judgment restricted Aadhaar's use to specific government purposes
  • UIDAI is the statutory authority administering the Aadhaar system

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has sought responses from both the Central government and state governments on a plea alleging misuse of Aadhaar, India's biometric identification system. The court's issuance of notice means it has found the petition prima facie worthy of a formal government reply.

Aadhaar, administered by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI), is linked to over a billion Indians' bank accounts, mobile SIM cards, subsidies, and government welfare schemes. Concerns about its misuse have been raised periodically — including issues around unauthorised authentication, data sharing without consent, and use of Aadhaar for purposes beyond those permitted by the Supreme Court's landmark 2018 Aadhaar judgment.

The plea, details of which were not fully specified in the available headline, appears to allege that Aadhaar is being used in ways that go beyond its legally sanctioned purposes or that private or government entities are misusing access to Aadhaar-linked data.

For ordinary Indians, Aadhaar is the gateway to everything from LPG subsidies and ration cards to Jan Dhan bank accounts. Any judicial scrutiny of systemic misuse has direct implications for the security of personal data and the integrity of benefit delivery for hundreds of millions of citizens.
