India's Trade Surplus Shifts Towards Asia and Africa in Early FY27

What happened

Business Standard reports that India's trade surplus has been shifting towards Asian and African markets in the early part of FY27. The data suggests that India is running stronger positive trade balances with these regions compared to Western markets in the current financial year.

The context

India's trade relationships have been under pressure due to US tariff actions and a cautious global environment. Meanwhile, India has been actively pursuing trade agreements and partnerships across Asia, the Middle East, and Africa through frameworks like the India-UAE CEPA and various bilateral engagements. Africa is also a growing market for Indian pharmaceuticals, IT, and manufactured goods.

Why it matters

A more diversified export base reduces India's vulnerability to any single trading partner's policy shifts, including US tariffs, making the economy more resilient. For Indian businesses, growing surplus with Asian and African partners signals expanding market opportunities in fast-growing regions.

What's next

Expect the government to accelerate bilateral trade agreement negotiations with key Asian and African economies to consolidate and expand these emerging surplus relationships.
