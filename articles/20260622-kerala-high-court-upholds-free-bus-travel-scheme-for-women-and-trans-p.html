Kerala High Court upholds free bus travel scheme for women and trans persons

Key facts

  • Kerala HC upholds free bus travel for women and transgender persons
  • Scheme covers rides on KSRTC state buses
  • Court ruled the initiative constitutionally valid
  • Transgender persons explicitly included as beneficiaries alongside women

The Kerala High Court has upheld the state government's scheme providing free bus travel for women and transgender persons, delivering a significant legal endorsement to one of the most visible public welfare programmes in the state. The court dismissed challenges to the initiative, affirming that the government's decision to extend complimentary bus rides to these groups is constitutionally valid.

The scheme, which covers travel on Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) buses, was introduced as a measure to ease the financial burden of commuting on women and trans persons, who statistically earn less and are more reliant on public transport. Critics had challenged the programme on grounds including financial sustainability and questions of equal treatment, but the High Court found the scheme to be a permissible exercise of the state's power to make affirmative provisions.

The ruling is a boost for the Kerala government, which has positioned the programme as part of a broader social equity agenda. Transgender rights groups, who have long lobbied for inclusion in mainstream welfare schemes, welcomed the court's recognition of trans persons as explicit beneficiaries alongside women.

The judgment is likely to be studied by other state governments that are weighing similar transport subsidy programmes, and could face its own further legal scrutiny at the Supreme Court level if appeals are filed. For now, the scheme continues uninterrupted for its beneficiaries.
