Technology emerges as the new pillar of India-France strategic convergence

What happened

The Hindu reports that technology is driving a new phase of strategic convergence between India and France. The coverage points to growing alignment between the two countries across key technology domains as both seek to shape a multipolar digital order.

The context

India and France have maintained a robust strategic partnership since 1998, covering defence, space, and nuclear cooperation. In recent years, both governments have emphasised the need for technology sovereignty and trusted supply chains, leading to closer engagement on semiconductors, AI regulation, and space exploration through agencies like ISRO and CNES.

Why it matters

Deeper India-France tech ties could open doors for Indian companies in European regulated markets and attract French investment in India's growing semiconductor and deep-tech sectors.

What's next

Expect formal technology partnership agreements or joint working groups to be announced at upcoming bilateral summits between the two countries.
