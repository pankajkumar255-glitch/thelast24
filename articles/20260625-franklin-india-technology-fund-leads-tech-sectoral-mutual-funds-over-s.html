Franklin India Technology Fund leads tech sectoral mutual funds over six months — with a loss of 20.7%

Key facts

  • Franklin India Technology Fund returned -20.7% over six months
  • Despite the loss, it leads all technology sectoral mutual funds in the period
  • Highlights broad-based pressure on tech valuations in India

Franklin India Technology Fund has returned -20.7% over the past six months, topping its peer group among technology sectoral mutual funds in India — a category where the best outcome has still been a substantial loss, Moneycontrol reports.

The figure captures the severity of the downturn that has swept through technology stocks in India and globally. Sectoral mutual funds that concentrate their portfolios in a single industry are inherently more volatile than diversified equity funds, and the technology category has borne the brunt of a broad re-rating of growth stocks amid higher interest rates and shifting investor sentiment.

For retail investors who moved money into technology funds during the sector's peak run — attracted by strong returns in the preceding years — the past six months have been a difficult period. The fact that the category leader is still in negative territory signals that the pressure on tech valuations has been broad-based rather than confined to specific stocks.

The data serves as a timely caution about the risks of sectoral concentration in investment portfolios, particularly in sectors sensitive to global macro conditions. Fund managers and financial advisers are likely to face renewed questions from investors about whether the technology sector's longer-term growth story justifies the short-term pain being experienced right now.
