AI leaders at G7 Summit push for US-led coalition to set global rules and standards

Key facts

  • AI leaders at G7 called for a US-led coalition on AI rules
  • Push aims to set unified global AI standards and governance
  • G7's Hiroshima AI Process is an existing related framework
  • India is developing its own AI policy under the IndiaAI initiative

The Indian Express reports that prominent AI industry leaders attending the G7 Summit have issued a collective call for a United States-led coalition to establish common rules and technical standards for artificial intelligence. The appeal reflects mounting concern among top AI companies that a fragmented global regulatory landscape — with the EU, US, China, and others developing divergent frameworks — will create costly compliance burdens and slow innovation.

The G7, comprising the world's largest advanced economies, has increasingly become a forum for shaping AI governance, with previous summits producing the Hiroshima AI Process framework. The latest push goes further, explicitly advocating American leadership in convening a broader coalition that would set baseline standards for AI safety, interoperability, and deployment across borders.

For India, which is simultaneously developing its own national AI policy and positioning itself as a global AI hub under initiatives like IndiaAI, the outcome of these discussions carries real weight. If global standards are locked in by a US-led G7 coalition without adequate representation from major developing economies, Indian regulators and companies could face a situation where the rules of the AI road are written by others. India has previously advocated for inclusive AI governance at multilateral forums, making this G7 push a significant moment in the geopolitics of artificial intelligence.
