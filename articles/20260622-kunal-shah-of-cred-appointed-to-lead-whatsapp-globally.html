Kunal Shah of CRED appointed to lead WhatsApp globally

Key facts

  • Kunal Shah, founder of CRED, appointed global head of WhatsApp
  • WhatsApp has over 2 billion users worldwide; India is its largest market
  • Shah is known for building CRED into a leading Indian fintech platform
  • Meta is expanding WhatsApp into payments and business commerce

Kunal Shah, the founder of Indian fintech startup CRED, has been appointed to lead WhatsApp, Meta's messaging giant that serves over two billion users worldwide. The appointment places an Indian entrepreneur at the head of arguably the most consequential consumer app in the world, in a development that has drawn immediate attention across the global technology industry.

Shah is best known for building CRED, the Bengaluru-based rewards and credit-card payments platform that became one of the defining consumer fintech brands of India's startup boom. He is widely regarded as one of India's sharpest product thinkers, known particularly for his frameworks on consumer psychology and retention — qualities that Meta will be looking to harness as WhatsApp deepens its ambitions beyond messaging into payments, commerce and business tools.

The appointment comes at a pivotal moment for WhatsApp. Meta has been aggressively expanding WhatsApp's monetisation through WhatsApp Business, the WhatsApp Pay product, and integrations with AI-driven customer service tools. India — WhatsApp's single largest market — sits at the centre of this strategy, and Shah's familiarity with Indian digital consumers could prove a direct advantage.

The news was confirmed by Reuters and quickly circulated widely across the Indian startup ecosystem, where Shah is a prominent and respected voice. For India's technology community, his elevation to lead a global platform of this scale represents one of the most significant appointments an Indian founder has received in recent years, underscoring the growing influence of India-trained entrepreneurs at the very top of global big tech.
