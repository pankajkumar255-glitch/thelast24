Can AI crack UPSC? How technology is disrupting India's coaching industry

Key facts

  • AI tools offering personalised UPSC prep at lower cost than human coaching
  • India's UPSC coaching industry worth thousands of crores, centred in Delhi
  • AI provides mock tests, essay evaluation, and adaptive study plans
  • Debate ongoing on whether AI can replace human mentors for UPSC

The Hindu explores whether artificial intelligence can take on one of India's most formidable intellectual challenges — the UPSC civil services examination — and how AI is already disrupting the country's enormous test-preparation coaching industry. AI tools are increasingly being deployed to offer aspirants personalised study schedules, automated essay and answer evaluation, and adaptive mock testing at a fraction of the cost of traditional coaching.

The UPSC examination is considered among the toughest competitive tests in the world, demanding deep analytical thinking, essay writing, and a broad grasp of current affairs and governance. Coaching centres in Delhi's Karol Bagh and Mukherjee Nagar, and similar hubs across India, have historically been the dominant pathway for serious aspirants, commanding fees that can run into several lakhs of rupees.

The rise of AI-driven platforms threatens to democratise access to quality preparation, potentially benefiting students from smaller towns and lower-income backgrounds who cannot afford or access top coaching institutes. However, sceptics argue that nuanced subjects like ethics, essay writing, and interview preparation still require human insight that AI cannot replicate. The disruption is already under way, and its full impact on both aspirants and the coaching industry's business model will unfold over the coming years.
