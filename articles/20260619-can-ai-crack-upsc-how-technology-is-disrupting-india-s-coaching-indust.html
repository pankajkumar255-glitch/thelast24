Can AI crack UPSC? How technology is disrupting India's coaching industry

Key facts

  • AI tools offering personalised UPSC prep, mock-test analytics, doubt-solving
  • Challenges established coaching institutes in cities like Delhi
  • UPSC coaching industry worth thousands of crores annually
  • Could reduce costs for aspirants from smaller towns

The Hindu reports on how artificial intelligence is beginning to challenge the entrenched UPSC coaching industry in India, an ecosystem worth thousands of crores and deeply woven into middle-class aspirations. AI tools are now offering personalised study schedules, performance analytics from mock tests, and instant doubt resolution — functions that traditionally required expensive classroom enrolment at institutes in cities like Delhi's Mukherjee Nagar or Rajendra Nagar.

The piece raises the provocative question of whether AI can actually 'crack' UPSC — that is, whether technology-assisted self-study can match the structured mentorship and peer environment of top coaching centres. Early adopters among aspirants report meaningful improvements in efficiency, though sceptics argue that the unpredictable, essay-heavy nature of the UPSC mains and interview stage still demands human guidance.

For the average Indian family, this shift is consequential: UPSC coaching can cost anywhere from tens of thousands to several lakhs of rupees per year, and any credible low-cost AI alternative could democratise access for aspirants from smaller towns who cannot afford to relocate to metro coaching hubs.
