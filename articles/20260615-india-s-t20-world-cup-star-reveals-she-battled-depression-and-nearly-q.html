India's T20 World Cup star reveals she battled depression and nearly quit cricket

What happened

NDTV Sports reports that an Indian women's cricketer who was part of the T20 World Cup squad has publicly revealed that she experienced depression and wanted to give up cricket before overcoming those challenges. The disclosure was described as a 'bombshell' revelation about her personal journey.

The context

Mental health awareness in Indian sport has grown in recent years, with athletes like Deepika Kumari and others speaking about psychological pressures. Women cricketers in India face intense public scrutiny alongside the demands of high-performance sport. Such personal disclosures are still rare in Indian cricket culture.

Why it matters

Public acknowledgment of mental health struggles by elite Indian sportswomen helps normalise seeking help and can have a significant impact on young athletes who face similar pressures. It also highlights the need for better mental health support systems within Indian cricket's institutional structure.

What's next

Expect increased public and media attention on mental health support within the BCCI's women's cricket programme following this revelation.
