England held to goalless draw by Ghana, raising World Cup qualification concerns

Key facts

  • England drew 0-0 with Ghana at the FIFA World Cup
  • England's 13th World Cup goalless draw
  • Result raises concerns about England's scoring form

England were frustrated by Ghana in a goalless draw at the FIFA World Cup, a result that has reignited concerns about the side's ability to find goals and build momentum in the competition. The 0-0 scoreline was England's 13th World Cup goalless draw, a recurring feature of their tournament history that supporters and pundits find hard to ignore.

England came into the match as heavy favourites against the Ghanaian side, but found themselves unable to convert possession and territorial advantage into goals. Ghana defended resolutely and denied England the breakthrough that would have settled nerves and put the Three Lions in control of their group destiny.

The result has exposed what ESPN India described as England's "World Cup run worries" — a familiar anxiety about a team blessed with individual talent but prone to collective underperformance on the biggest stage. The draw means England must sharpen their attacking intent quickly if they are to advance deep into the tournament.

For Ghana, the point represents a creditable result against one of Europe's most fancied sides and keeps their own campaign alive. England's coaching staff now face difficult questions about selection and tactics as the stakes in the World Cup rise with every passing game.
