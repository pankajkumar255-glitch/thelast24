Ali Khamenei Is Dead: Who Really Runs Iran Now?

Key facts

  • Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei has died
  • Assembly of Experts must now select a new Supreme Leader
  • Death comes amid active US-Iran nuclear talks in Doha
  • Iran had simultaneously issued threats against Israel
  • Succession seen as a moment of acute vulnerability for Iran

Ali Khamenei, the Supreme Leader of Iran who held power for over three decades, has died, triggering an immediate and potentially destabilising succession crisis in one of the world's most strategically significant states. His death comes at a moment of extraordinary geopolitical tension, with US-Iran nuclear talks under way in Doha and Iranian forces on heightened alert following escalating confrontations with Israel.

Under Iran's constitution, the Assembly of Experts — a body of senior clerics — is responsible for selecting a new Supreme Leader. However, no single figure commands the religious authority, political legitimacy, and institutional loyalty that Khamenei wielded. Potential candidates include figures close to the Revolutionary Guards and hardline clerical factions, but each faces significant internal opposition. The transition period itself is seen as a moment of acute vulnerability for the Iranian state.

The timing is especially fraught. US and Iranian negotiators had just described talks in Doha as productive, with Trump calling them "very good meetings." Iran's foreign ministry had simultaneously issued sharp warnings about Israel, with officials threatening that Iran would "school" what they called Trump's Tel Aviv "pets" if not controlled. The death of Khamenei injects deep uncertainty into whether those diplomatic contacts can survive or whether hardliners will now consolidate power and abandon negotiations.

For India, the stakes are considerable. Iran remains an important energy and connectivity partner — including the Chabahar port — and Indian diplomats will be watching the succession struggle closely. A more hardline or fractured Iran could disrupt both energy flows and regional stability in ways that directly affect Indian interests.
