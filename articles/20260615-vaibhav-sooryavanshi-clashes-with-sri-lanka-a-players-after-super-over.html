Vaibhav Sooryavanshi clashes with Sri Lanka A players after super-over defeat in Dambulla

What happened

NDTV Sports and The Times of India report that Vaibhav Sooryavanshi clashed with Sri Lanka A players following India A's super-over defeat in Dambulla. Video evidence, as reported by NDTV Sports, shows the sequence of events that triggered Sooryavanshi's outburst. Hindustan Times separately reports that India A were handed a 10-run batting penalty in the same match due to a violation attributed to Vipraj Nigam.

The context

Vaibhav Sooryavanshi burst onto the scene as a teenage prodigy and has been touted as a future star of Indian cricket. India A tours serve as proving grounds for players on the cusp of senior selection, making on-field conduct especially scrutinised. On-field penalties for slow over-rates or player conduct are a standard ICC mechanism but are rare and embarrassing at this level.

Why it matters

Incidents of this kind can affect a young player's reputation and path to senior selection, putting Sooryavanshi's temperament under the microscope at a critical stage of his career. The dual controversy — a conduct clash and a penalty — raises questions about India A's discipline management during the Sri Lanka tour.

What's next

Expect the Board of Control for Cricket in India to review the incidents and potentially issue a statement on both Sooryavanshi's conduct and the circumstances surrounding the 10-run penalty.
