Trump Announces Plans to Visit Turkey and China This Year

Key facts

  • Trump plans visits to both Turkey and China in 2025
  • Any Trump–Xi summit would be the first amid current US–China trade war
  • Turkey is a NATO ally and potential Ukraine-conflict mediator

NDTV reports that US President Donald Trump has announced his intention to visit both Turkey and China in 2025. The declaration comes at a particularly charged moment in global diplomacy — the US and China are in the midst of a steep tariff conflict that has rattled global markets, and a direct presidential visit would signal a potential shift toward negotiation.

Turkey, a NATO member that has maintained relations with Russia throughout the Ukraine war, has also positioned itself as a potential mediator. A Trump visit to Ankara would carry its own geopolitical significance for the broader resolution of the Russia–Ukraine conflict, in which India has a direct stake given its energy and defence ties with Moscow.

For Indian readers, the prospect of a Trump–Xi meeting is especially consequential: any US–China trade détente or new economic framework could reshape the global supply chains and export opportunities that Indian manufacturers and businesses depend on, while a continued escalation would keep pushing inflation and uncertainty into Asian markets.
