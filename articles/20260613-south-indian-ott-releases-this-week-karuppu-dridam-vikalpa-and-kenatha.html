South Indian OTT releases this week: Karuppu, Dridam, Vikalpa and Kenatha Kanom arrive on streaming platforms

What happened

The Times of India and The Economic Times both report on the week's new South Indian OTT releases, covering Malayalam, Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada titles. Films highlighted include Karuppu, Dridam, Kenatha Kanom, and Vikalpa, with Netflix named among the platforms carrying the new content. NDTV Profit also covered the same slate of releases. The simultaneous arrival of multiple regional titles reflects the growing pipeline of South Indian content moving directly to streaming after theatrical runs or as direct OTT premieres.

The context

South Indian cinema — particularly Malayalam and Tamil films — has seen explosive growth in OTT viewership following the success of breakout hits that drew pan-India and global audiences on streaming platforms. Platforms including Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+ Hotstar, and SonyLIV have all invested heavily in acquiring South Indian content. The weekly cadence of new regional OTT releases has become a fixture for Indian streaming subscribers, with each Friday typically bringing multiple titles across languages.

Why it matters

For the tens of millions of Indians who subscribe to streaming platforms, a strong weekly OTT slate directly determines the value they get from their subscriptions. The consistent availability of regional language content across platforms also reflects how South Indian cinema has become mainstream national entertainment rather than niche regional viewing.

What's next

Audience reception and viewership numbers for titles like Karuppu and Dridam will likely determine whether they trend on their respective platforms over the coming week. Strong performance could influence future acquisition deals between streaming platforms and South Indian production houses.
