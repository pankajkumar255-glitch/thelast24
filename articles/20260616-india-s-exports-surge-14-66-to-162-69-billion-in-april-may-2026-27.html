India's exports surge 14.66% to $162.69 billion in April–May 2026-27

What happened

PIB reported that cumulative exports covering both merchandise and services during April–May 2026-27 are estimated at $162.69 billion, compared to $141.89 billion in April–May 2025-26, marking an estimated growth of 14.66%. The data covers the first two months of the current financial year and reflects performance across both goods and services trade.

The context

India has been pushing to diversify its export markets and boost outbound trade as part of its broader economic ambitions. The April–May period is an early indicator of the full-year trade trajectory, and strong numbers here are closely watched by policymakers, investors, and the RBI for their impact on the current account deficit.

Why it matters

A sharp rise in exports helps narrow India's trade deficit, supports the rupee, and signals that Indian manufacturers and service providers are winning more global business. It also underpins government revenue and can sustain job creation in export-oriented sectors.

What's next

Expect analysts to watch whether this pace holds through the monsoon quarter, particularly given ongoing volatility in West Asian shipping routes and the evolving US-Iran situation.
