Supreme Court Values Homemakers' Labour at Rs 30,000 a Month, Calls Them Nation Builders

What happened

The Hindu and India Today both report that the Supreme Court has ruled that homemakers' domestic labour should be valued at Rs 30,000 per month for the purpose of calculating compensation. The court described homemakers as 'nation builders', underlining the social and economic significance of unpaid care work. The ruling sets a quantified benchmark that lower courts and tribunals will now be expected to apply.

The context

Indian courts have long grappled with how to compensate families when a homemaker dies or is injured, since her work generates no formal income. The Motor Vehicles Act tribunals in particular have used inconsistent and often very low notional income figures. This Supreme Court directive brings uniformity and a significant upward revision to that benchmark.

Why it matters

Families filing compensation claims after accidents or deaths involving homemakers stand to receive substantially higher payouts under this benchmark. It also sends a powerful social signal about the economic worth of domestic care, which disproportionately falls on women in India.

What's next

Expect lower courts and motor accident claim tribunals across India to begin applying the Rs 30,000 monthly figure in compensation calculations, and watch for potential legislative follow-up on formalising this valuation.
