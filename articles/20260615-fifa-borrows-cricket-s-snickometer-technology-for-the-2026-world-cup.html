FIFA Borrows Cricket's Snickometer Technology for the 2026 World Cup

What happened

India Today reports that FIFA used Snickometer technology, borrowed from cricket, during the 2026 FIFA World Cup, with the tool deployed during the Sweden vs Tunisia fixture. The technology, known as Snicko in cricket broadcasting, was used to assist match officials in making accurate decisions. This marks a notable crossover of cricket-developed officiating technology into world football's biggest stage.

The context

Snickometer technology was originally developed for cricket to detect thin edges by combining audio spikes with slow-motion video. FIFA has been progressively adopting advanced officiating tools — including VAR (Video Assistant Referee) and goal-line technology — to reduce controversy in matches. The 2026 FIFA World Cup, hosted across the United States, Canada, and Mexico, is the most-watched sporting event in the world, making any officiating innovation highly visible.

Why it matters

For Indian fans who follow both cricket and football, this represents a proud moment of cricket-born innovation shaping global sport. Better officiating technology also reduces match-deciding controversies, improving the spectacle for billions of viewers.

What's next

Expect other sports governing bodies to evaluate adopting Snicko-style audio-visual edge-detection tools for officiating as FIFA's use case receives global scrutiny.
