Manipur Civil Groups Seek Action After Kuki Body Apologises for Killing 6 Naga Hostages

Key facts

  • Kuki body apologised for killing 6 Naga hostages in Manipur
  • Civil groups demand criminal action, not just an apology
  • Incident part of ongoing ethnic conflict in Manipur

Civil society groups in Manipur have called for concrete legal action after a Kuki organisation issued a public apology for the killing of 6 Naga hostages, a development that has deepened tensions in the already volatile state. The groups argue that an apology is insufficient and that those responsible for the killings must face criminal prosecution.

The killing of the Naga hostages represents a grim episode within the larger ethnic conflict that has consumed Manipur for over two years, claiming hundreds of lives and displacing tens of thousands. The fact that a formal apology has been issued at all is notable, but Naga civil groups have made clear that they view it as an inadequate response to the loss of six lives.

The Manipur conflict, broadly framed as a confrontation between the Meitei majority and Kuki-Zo communities in the hills, has drawn in numerous other ethnic and tribal groups in complex and sometimes violent ways. Incidents involving hostages and targeted killings point to the extreme deterioration of inter-community trust.

With the state administration and central security forces struggling to restore normalcy, calls for accountability from civil society add pressure on authorities to act. The episode is a reminder that lasting peace in Manipur requires not just ceasefires but genuine justice for victims.
