From Enrique Iglesias to Coldplay: How India's live concert scene transformed in five years

What happened

Firstpost reports on the significant evolution of India's concert and live entertainment scene over the past five years, tracing the journey from early international acts like Enrique Iglesias to marquee global tours by Coldplay. The piece highlights structural and cultural changes that have driven this growth.

The context

India's live entertainment market has expanded rapidly, fuelled by rising disposable incomes, a young population hungry for experiential spending, and improved large-venue infrastructure in cities like Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru. Coldplay's India tour in 2025 became a landmark moment, with tickets selling out almost instantly and sparking national conversations about ticketing and fan access.

Why it matters

The growth of the live concert industry is creating thousands of jobs in event management, production, and hospitality, while also signalling that India is now on the radar of global artists who previously bypassed the country. For fans, it means more choice — but also higher ticket prices and fiercer competition for seats.

What's next

Expect more global artists to announce India tour dates in the coming months as promoters and venues race to capitalise on the proven demand.
