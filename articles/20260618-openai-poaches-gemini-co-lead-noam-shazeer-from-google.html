OpenAI Poaches Gemini Co-Lead Noam Shazeer From Google

Key facts

  • Noam Shazeer, Gemini co-lead, hired by OpenAI from Google
  • Shazeer co-authored 'Attention Is All You Need', the transformer paper
  • Previously co-founded Character.AI before returning to Google
  • Regarded as one of the most influential AI researchers alive

NDTV reports that OpenAI has pulled off a landmark hire by recruiting Noam Shazeer, the co-lead of Google's Gemini AI model, away from the search giant. Shazeer is among the most consequential figures in modern AI — he is a co-author of the 2017 paper 'Attention Is All You Need', which introduced the transformer architecture that now forms the backbone of virtually every major large language model, including ChatGPT and Gemini itself.

Shazeer had previously left Google to co-found the AI startup Character.AI, which Google subsequently acquired — along with a licence to his work — in a deal that brought him back into the Google fold. His decision to now move to OpenAI signals a dramatic shift in the talent landscape of the global AI race.

For Indian users and businesses rapidly adopting AI tools built on these foundational models, this hire matters because it could accelerate OpenAI's technical edge. India is one of the fastest-growing markets for both OpenAI's ChatGPT and Google's Gemini, and any shift in the underlying capability gap between these platforms will directly influence which tools Indian developers, students, and enterprises rely on.
