Zabaan Sambhal Ke actor Dinyar Tirandaz passes away

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Dinyar Tirandaz, the actor widely recognised for his role in the popular Hindi sitcom Zabaan Sambhal Ke, has passed away. The publication broke the news without specifying the cause or exact circumstances of his death. Tirandaz was a prominent figure in Indian television, particularly associated with the Doordarshan era of the 1990s. His death has prompted an outpouring of condolences from the entertainment community and fans who grew up watching the show.

The context

Zabaan Sambhal Ke was a hugely popular Hindi sitcom that aired on Doordarshan in the 1990s, at a time when the state broadcaster was the dominant entertainment platform for Indian families. The show was known for its sharp, clean comedy and memorable ensemble cast, and it ran for multiple seasons, earning a devoted national following. Dinyar Tirandaz was a theatre-trained actor who brought considerable comic timing and character depth to his role. The 1990s Doordarshan era remains a touchstone of collective Indian cultural memory, with its shows continuing to be fondly referenced decades later.

Why it matters

For millions of Indians who grew up in the 1990s, Zabaan Sambhal Ke and its cast were a weekly ritual, making Tirandaz's death a genuinely felt cultural loss. The passing of veterans from this era serves as a reminder of the thinning ranks of Indian television's foundational generation of performers. His death will prompt renewed appreciation for the Doordarshan comedy legacy among older audiences.

What's next

Expect tributes from Bollywood and television fraternity members to continue emerging over the coming days. It is likely that retrospective coverage of Zabaan Sambhal Ke and Tirandaz's body of work will appear across entertainment platforms as fans revisit the show.
