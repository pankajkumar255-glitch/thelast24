Harry Brook leads 17-man England squad for India T20I series; James Coles earns maiden call-up

Key facts

  • Harry Brook captains England's 17-member T20I squad vs India
  • James Coles earns maiden England call-up after record-breaking hundred
  • Squad confirmed by multiple outlets including Hindustan Times and Times of India

England have announced a 17-member squad under the captaincy of Harry Brook for the T20I series against India, with James Coles earning a maiden call-up after a record-breaking hundred that caught the selectors' attention. The uncapped Coles is the headline selection, his inclusion a reward for exceptional domestic form that made his omission increasingly difficult to justify.

The squad announcement, confirmed by multiple outlets including Hindustan Times and The Times of India, signals England's intent to field a strong and evolving side for what is expected to be a competitive T20I contest against India. Brook, who has steadily grown into a leader across formats, will captain the side with the full backing of the ECB selectors.

Coles's call-up comes after his record-setting century in domestic cricket, a performance that Wisden described as earning him his maiden England recognition. His inclusion is seen as part of England's broader strategy of backing in-form players regardless of experience — a philosophy that has defined the Bazball era across red and white ball formats alike.

For India, the series represents another high-stakes home assignment and a chance to test their T20 depth ahead of future ICC events. The full England squad, with Brook at the helm and fresh faces like Coles injecting excitement, ensures that fans on both sides have plenty to look forward to when the sides take the field.
