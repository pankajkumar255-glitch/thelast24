ChatGPT's market share slips below 50% as Gemini and Claude gain ground

Key facts

  • ChatGPT market share has fallen below 50% for the first time
  • Google Gemini and Anthropic Claude are the primary gainers
  • ChatGPT launched in late 2022 and previously dominated the market
  • Signals AI assistant market shifting from monopoly to multi-player competition

Business Standard reports that ChatGPT's market share in the AI assistant segment has slipped below 50%, a symbolic threshold that signals the end of OpenAI's near-total dominance in consumer AI. The gains have primarily gone to Google's Gemini and Anthropic's Claude, both of which have substantially improved in capability and availability over the past year.

ChatGPT was virtually synonymous with AI assistants when it launched in late 2022, and maintained commanding market share through 2023. The decline below 50% reflects a combination of factors: Google's deep integration of Gemini into Android and Google Workspace, Anthropic's aggressive push into enterprise and developer markets with Claude, and a general maturing of user expectations that makes switching easier.

In India, where AI assistant usage has grown rapidly and Google's Android ecosystem gives Gemini a natural distribution advantage, the competitive shift is particularly relevant. Indian enterprises evaluating AI platforms, and individual users choosing a daily AI tool, now operate in a market where no single provider can be taken for granted — which generally favours buyers but raises questions about which ecosystems will consolidate around.
