India-US interim trade deal in final stages after Modi-Trump push, says Foreign Secretary

Key facts

  • India-US interim trade deal described as being in 'final stages'
  • PM Modi and President Trump personally pushed for the deal's conclusion
  • Foreign Secretary acknowledged trade talks had created uncertainty between the two nations
  • Deal is a stepping stone toward a broader bilateral trade agreement

India Today and Reuters report that India's Foreign Secretary has stated the interim trade deal between India and the United States is now in its final stages, with both Prime Minister Narendra Modi and President Donald Trump having personally pushed for its conclusion. The Foreign Secretary also acknowledged candidly that the prolonged negotiations had created uncertainty between the two nations — a rare admission of diplomatic friction in what is otherwise a strategically close partnership.

The push for an interim agreement comes after the United States announced sweeping reciprocal tariffs earlier in 2025 that rattled Indian exporters in sectors ranging from pharmaceuticals and textiles to IT hardware. An interim deal is seen as a stepping stone to a broader bilateral trade agreement that both governments have been working toward for several years, aiming to more than double trade to $500 billion by 2030.

For everyday Indians, the significance is concrete: a deal would shape the tariff environment for key export industries that employ millions, influence the cost of American goods in India, and determine how competitive Indian products remain in the US market. With both leaders now visibly invested in a resolution, the probability of a near-term agreement appears higher than at any previous point in negotiations.
