Rohit Returns, Jaiswal Dropped: BCCI Names India's ODI Squad for England Tour with Kohli and Bumrah Back

Key facts

  • Virat Kohli and Jasprit Bumrah return to India's ODI squad
  • Rohit Sharma named captain for England ODI tour
  • Yashasvi Jaiswal dropped from the squad
  • Squad announced by BCCI for upcoming England tour

India's ODI squad for the England tour has been announced by the BCCI, and it carries two headline comebacks and one significant omission: Virat Kohli and Jasprit Bumrah are back in the fifty-over setup, while Yashasvi Jaiswal — who had been a prominent white-ball prospect — has been dropped. Rohit Sharma retains the captaincy, confirming his central role in India's ODI plans.

Kohli's return is the story the cricketing world had been watching for. The former captain had stepped away from certain white-ball assignments in recent months, and his inclusion signals that India are treating the England series as a priority engagement. Bumrah's return, similarly, bolsters an attack that looked thinner without him — his ability to take wickets at the top and bottom of innings in English conditions makes him arguably India's most valuable bowler for this tour.

Jaiswal's exclusion is the sharpest talking point among the selectors' decisions. The young opener had cemented his Test credentials emphatically, but his ODI numbers and the competition for top-order spots appear to have cost him his place in the fifty-over squad for now. The selectors have clearly opted for experience and proven performers for what is a challenging overseas assignment.

The England tour will be a critical yardstick for India's ODI team as the next ICC cycle begins to take shape. With the squad now confirmed, the focus turns to combination and conditions — English pitches and overcast skies will test both batting depth and seam-bowling resources, making the returns of Kohli and Bumrah all the more consequential.
