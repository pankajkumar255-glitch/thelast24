Indian AI startups face fresh uncertainty after Anthropic pulls Fable 5 under US export curbs

What happened

Mint reports that Indian AI startups are now confronting new risks after Anthropic pulled its Fable 5 model, with the withdrawal driven by US export-control restrictions. The report highlights that startups which had built products or pipelines on Anthropic's API face immediate disruption. The development follows the US government's intervention that forced Anthropic to disable the model shortly after its launch.

The context

India's AI startup ecosystem has grown rapidly, with many companies choosing to build on top of foundation models from US firms like Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google rather than developing their own. This gives them speed to market but creates dependency on platforms that can be restricted by US export policy. The Fable 5 episode is the first high-profile instance of such a restriction directly affecting Indian builders.

Why it matters

Startups that had shipped products relying on Fable 5 now face the cost and delay of migrating to alternative models, and investors may reprice the risk of India-based AI ventures built on foreign platforms. The disruption strengthens the case for Indian sovereign AI infrastructure.

What's next

Expect Indian AI startups to accelerate evaluation of alternatives including domestic models, and for industry bodies to lobby the government for faster development of India-hosted foundation model options.
