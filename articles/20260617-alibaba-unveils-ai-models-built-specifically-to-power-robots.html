Alibaba unveils AI models built specifically to power robots

What happened

YourStory.com reports that Alibaba has unveiled new AI models specifically engineered for robotics, designed to enable machines to better perceive and operate in physical environments. The announcement is part of Alibaba's broader push to compete in the global AI race, particularly in applied and embodied AI.

The context

Embodied AI — giving physical robots the ability to reason and act intelligently — is considered the next frontier in artificial intelligence. Alibaba has been aggressively expanding its AI research and product portfolio after China's DeepSeek shook the global AI industry earlier this year. Chinese tech companies are under pressure to develop cutting-edge AI to counter US restrictions on advanced chip exports.

Why it matters

Alibaba's robotics AI models could find adoption in Indian manufacturing and logistics operations, where automation is growing rapidly. It also intensifies the US-China competition in AI that shapes the technology choices available to Indian businesses.

What's next

Expect Alibaba to demonstrate these robotics models at industry events and seek partnerships with hardware manufacturers, with potential availability to enterprise customers likely within months.
