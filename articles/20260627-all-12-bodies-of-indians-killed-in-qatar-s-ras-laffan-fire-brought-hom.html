All 12 Bodies of Indians Killed in Qatar's Ras Laffan Fire Brought Home

Key facts

  • All 12 Indian victims of the Ras Laffan fire repatriated
  • Eight bodies returned in the latest repatriation batch
  • Fire occurred at Qatar's Ras Laffan gas facility
  • Indian Embassy in Doha coordinated the repatriation

All 12 Indian workers killed in the deadly fire at Qatar's Ras Laffan gas facility have now been repatriated, with eight more bodies flown back to India in the latest consignment, completing the full handover of remains to grieving families.

Ras Laffan Industrial City, located on Qatar's northeast coast, is one of the world's largest concentrations of liquefied natural gas infrastructure, and home to a substantial workforce of Indian migrants. The fire, which killed at least 12 Indians, triggered an immediate consular response from the Indian Embassy in Doha and drew attention to the safety conditions faced by migrant workers in Gulf industrial facilities.

The repatriation was carried out in phases, with the final eight bodies returned in the latest operation. Indian diplomatic officials coordinated with Qatari authorities to ensure the process was completed in full, allowing families — many of them in Kerala, Tamil Nadu, and other states with large Gulf migration communities — to observe last rites.

The deaths add to a long and painful record of Indian worker fatalities in Gulf infrastructure projects. With over nine million Indians living and working in the Gulf Cooperation Council countries, incidents like this renew calls for stronger bilateral safety frameworks and faster compensation mechanisms for bereaved families.
