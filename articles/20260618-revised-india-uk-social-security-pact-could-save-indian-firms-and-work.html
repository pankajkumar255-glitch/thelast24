Revised India-UK social security pact could save Indian firms and workers $500 million

Key facts

  • Revised pact could save Indian firms and workers ~$500 million
  • Eliminates double social security contributions in India and UK
  • Major benefit for Indian IT professionals posted to the UK
  • Part of the broader India-UK free trade agreement framework

The Hindu reports that a revised social security agreement between India and the United Kingdom, part of the broader India-UK trade deal framework, could generate savings of around $500 million for Indian businesses and workers. The agreement addresses the long-standing problem of double social security contributions, where Indian employees posted to the UK were required to pay into both the Indian and British social security systems simultaneously without being able to draw full benefits from both.

This issue has particularly affected India's large IT and professional services sector, which regularly depots engineers, consultants and other skilled workers to the UK on medium to long-term assignments. Indian IT majors have lobbied for relief on this front for years, and the revised pact is seen as a significant win for the industry.

The $500 million in estimated savings would be split between employing firms — which bear employer-side contributions — and individual workers who pay the employee share. For Indian technology companies competing on thin margins in global markets, the reduction in payroll costs for UK-posted staff adds up meaningfully across large workforces.
