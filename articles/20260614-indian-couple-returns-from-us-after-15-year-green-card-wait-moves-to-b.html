Indian Couple Returns from US After 15-Year Green Card Wait, Moves to Bengaluru to Build Startup

What happened

NDTV reports that an Indian couple decided to leave the US after a 15-year wait for a green card and have relocated to Bengaluru to launch a startup. The story illustrates a broader trend of highly skilled Indian-Americans reconsidering their futures in the US amid an immigration backlog that can stretch decades for Indian nationals.

The context

Indian nationals face an extraordinarily long wait for US employment-based green cards — in some categories, the queue stretches over 100 years due to per-country caps. This has led to growing frustration among skilled Indian professionals in the US, many of whom have spent their most productive years in limbo. India's improving startup ecosystem, particularly in Bengaluru, has made returning a more attractive option.

Why it matters

Returning professionals bring capital, global networks, and high-end technical skills to India's startup ecosystem, potentially accelerating innovation and creating jobs. If the trend grows, India stands to benefit significantly from the US immigration system's failure to retain global talent.

What's next

Expect more stories of high-skilled Indian returnees as the US green card backlog shows no signs of resolution, and as India's startup environment continues to mature.
