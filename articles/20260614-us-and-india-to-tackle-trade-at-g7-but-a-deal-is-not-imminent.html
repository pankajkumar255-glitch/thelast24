US and India to Tackle Trade at G7, But a Deal Is Not Imminent

What happened

Reuters reports that US officials have said the US and India will address trade issues at the G7 summit but that a final deal is not expected anytime soon. The talks are likely to centre on tariff reductions and market access, though significant gaps between the two sides remain. WION separately flags the upcoming Modi-Trump meeting as a potential turning point in negotiations.

The context

India and the US have been in active trade negotiations for several years, with disputes over agricultural tariffs, digital trade, and intellectual property rights repeatedly slowing progress. The G7 summit provides a high-profile diplomatic setting where leaders can signal political will, even if the technical details of a deal take much longer to finalise.

Why it matters

An India-US trade deal could significantly boost Indian exports and attract American investment, but delays leave key sectors such as pharmaceuticals, IT services, and agriculture exposed to existing tariff barriers. For everyday Indians, the outcome shapes job prospects in export-oriented industries.

What's next

Expect the Modi-Trump bilateral on the G7 sidelines to be closely watched for any announcements on a trade framework or timeline, with formal deal negotiations likely continuing well into the second half of 2025.
