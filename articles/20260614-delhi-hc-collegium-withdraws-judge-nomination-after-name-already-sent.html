Delhi HC Collegium Withdraws Judge Nomination After Name Already Sent to Supreme Court

What happened

Hindustan Times reports that the Delhi High Court collegium has removed a judge candidate from its recommendation list after the proposal had already been dispatched to the Supreme Court. The reason for the withdrawal has not been publicly stated. The development is being closely watched as an uncommon breach in the normally linear flow of the collegium appointment process.

The context

India's collegium system, where senior judges recommend and appoint fellow judges, has long been criticised for its lack of transparency. Proposals moving from a High Court collegium to the Supreme Court collegium typically proceed smoothly; a mid-process withdrawal is exceptional and often indicates that adverse information surfaced about the nominee after the initial recommendation.

Why it matters

The withdrawal highlights systemic gaps in due diligence within the collegium process and may intensify calls for greater transparency and accountability in how India's judges are selected. It also delays filling a vacancy on a court already burdened with a massive backlog of cases.

What's next

Expect scrutiny from legal experts and opposition politicians over the undisclosed reasons for the withdrawal, and watch for whether the Supreme Court collegium formally acknowledges the recall.
