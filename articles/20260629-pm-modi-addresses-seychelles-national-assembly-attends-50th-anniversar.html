PM Modi Addresses Seychelles National Assembly, Attends 50th Anniversary of Independence

Key facts

  • PM Modi addressed the Seychelles National Assembly
  • India described as Seychelles' 'trusted partner'
  • Modi attended Seychelles' 50th independence anniversary celebrations
  • Modi met Indian diaspora members in Seychelles

Prime Minister Narendra Modi addressed the National Assembly of Seychelles during an official state visit, describing India as the island nation's 'trusted partner' and reaffirming bilateral ties that span development cooperation, security and people-to-people links. The address to the legislature is a mark of the high level of diplomatic engagement India is seeking with the archipelago.

Modi also attended the celebrations marking the 50th anniversary of Seychelles' independence — a milestone occasion that provided a high-profile platform for India to demonstrate its commitment to the relationship. He separately met members of the Indian diaspora in Seychelles, a community that has historically played a bridge-building role between the two countries.

The visit fits into India's broader Indian Ocean strategy, which prioritises close ties with island nations in the region. Seychelles holds strategic importance given its location in the western Indian Ocean and has been a partner in India's maritime security and humanitarian assistance frameworks.

The engagement comes at a time when major powers — including China — are competing for influence across Indian Ocean island states through infrastructure investment and development finance. India's emphasis on being a 'trusted partner', as opposed to a transactional investor, is a deliberate diplomatic positioning that New Delhi has cultivated across the region.
