Indian startup builds balcony escape system for high-rise fires after elevator failures

Key facts

  • Balcony escape system designed for high-rise fire evacuations
  • Triggered by real incidents of elevator failure and blocked stairwells
  • Requires no specialist training to operate
  • Targets gap in India's high-rise fire safety infrastructure

A fire breaks out on the 18th floor. The elevator shuts down automatically. Smoke fills the stairwell. For too many residents of India's rapidly multiplying high-rise buildings, that scenario has no safe exit — and an Indian startup is now trying to change that with a balcony-mounted emergency escape system that could offer a viable last resort.

The startup, profiled by NDTV, developed the system after studying real fire incidents where occupants on upper floors were left with no functional evacuation route once elevators failed and stairwells became impassable. The device is designed to be deployed from a standard balcony, allowing residents — including those without specialist training — to descend in a controlled manner rather than face the impossible choice between smoke and a fatal fall.

The innovation speaks to a structural gap in Indian urban fire safety. Hundreds of new high-rise residential and commercial towers are completed every year across cities like Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Pune, but fire-safety compliance has long been patchy. Building codes require fire escapes and sprinkler systems, but enforcement is inconsistent, and audits conducted after major fire incidents have repeatedly found that emergency infrastructure was non-functional or absent.

For the startup, the commercial opportunity is significant if it can navigate regulatory approvals and establish trust with builders, housing societies, and fire departments. More immediately, the balcony escape system represents a category of safety innovation that India's urban boom urgently needs — simple, deployable technology that works when official systems fail.
