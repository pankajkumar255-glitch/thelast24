China May Launch Mythos-Class AI Models Before Q1 2027, Says Zai Chief After GLM 5.2 Release

Key facts

  • Zai chief says China may deploy Mythos-class AI before Q1 2027
  • Claim follows release of Zai's GLM 5.2 model
  • Mythos-class denotes a next-generation frontier AI capability tier
  • China's AI labs have been rapidly closing gap with US counterparts

India Today reports that the chief of Chinese AI lab Zai has claimed China could release Mythos-class AI models — representing a new tier of frontier AI capability — before the first quarter of 2027, following the company's release of its GLM 5.2 model. The statement signals growing confidence in China's AI development pipeline.

GLM 5.2 is the latest iteration of Zai's General Language Model series, and the Zai chief's comments suggest the lab views it as a stepping stone toward significantly more powerful systems. The term "Mythos-class" has emerged in AI circles to describe a generation of models with substantially enhanced reasoning and general-purpose capabilities beyond current large language models.

The announcement comes as China's AI sector has been advancing at a striking pace, with models like DeepSeek R1 earlier this year demonstrating that Chinese labs can compete with and sometimes match leading American systems at lower cost.

For India, which is building out its own AI infrastructure under the IndiaAI Mission, the rapid advance of both American and Chinese frontier AI underscores the urgency of domestic capability-building and raises questions about which AI ecosystems Indian industry and government will align with.
