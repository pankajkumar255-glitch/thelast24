India A hit with rare 10-run penalty as Vipraj Nigam runs on pitch against Sri Lanka A

What happened

Hindustan Times and Cricbuzz both report that India A were penalised 10 runs during their match against Sri Lanka A in Dambulla after Vipraj Nigam, an IPL all-rounder, ran on the pitch in violation of cricket's Laws. The penalty was applied during India A's batting innings, directly reducing their total.

The context

Running on the pitch is penalised under Law 42 of cricket to protect the playing surface, which can deteriorate and assist spin bowlers unfairly. Such a penalty is extremely rare at any level of professional cricket. Vipraj Nigam has been a notable name from the IPL circuit, and his inclusion in India A reflects the selectors' interest in his development.

Why it matters

The 10-run penalty directly affected India A's match total, potentially influencing the result of the contest. The incident serves as a reminder to young Indian cricketers of the technical and disciplinary standards required as they push for senior national selection.

What's next

Expect the Board of Control for Cricket in India to review the incident internally, and selectors will likely keep a close eye on Vipraj Nigam's overall discipline and form going forward.
