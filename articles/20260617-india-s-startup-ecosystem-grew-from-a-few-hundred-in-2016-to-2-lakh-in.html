India's Startup Ecosystem Grew From a Few Hundred in 2016 to 2 Lakh in 2025

What happened

NDTV reports that India's registered startup count has grown from a few hundred in 2016 to 2 lakh by 2025, tracking the country's extraordinary startup journey over nearly a decade. The report marks a milestone in India's emergence as a global startup powerhouse.

The context

The Startup India initiative launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in January 2016 provided regulatory recognition, tax benefits, and funding support to new ventures. Combined with the JAM trinity (Jan Dhan, Aadhaar, Mobile), cheap data from Jio, and a growing pool of engineering talent, India created fertile ground for entrepreneurship across sectors and cities beyond traditional tech hubs.

Why it matters

A 2-lakh strong startup ecosystem has become a critical engine for employment and innovation in India, particularly for young graduates entering the workforce. It also signals India's growing capacity to solve domestic problems with homegrown technology and entrepreneurship.

What's next

Expect government policy focus to shift towards improving startup survival rates, access to late-stage funding, and facilitating more unicorn-level exits.
