Europe frets about US AI dominance as tech world flocks to France for G7 and VivaTech

Key facts

  • European leaders at G7 and VivaTech in France expressing AI dependency fears
  • Concern centres on US controlling access to frontier AI models
  • EU has regulation (AI Act) but few homegrown frontier model companies
  • Paris hosting both G7 summit and VivaTech conference simultaneously

Reuters reports that European leaders arriving in France for the G7 summit and the VivaTech technology conference are voicing serious concern about the continent's dependence on US-built AI. The anxiety has been sharpened by Washington's moves to treat access to frontier AI models as a geopolitical privilege, with discussions at the G7 centring on which countries qualify as 'trusted partners' worthy of access to top-tier American AI.

Europe, which has invested heavily in AI regulation through the EU AI Act but has fewer homegrown frontier model companies than the US or China, fears being caught in a dependent position. French President Emmanuel Macron has been among the most vocal advocates for European AI sovereignty, and Paris — hosting both the G7 and VivaTech — has positioned itself as the continent's AI capital.

The European debate has direct resonance for India. Like Europe, India is a large AI consumer that does not yet have domestic foundation models capable of matching GPT or Claude in scale. The question of whether to build sovereign AI capacity or deepen dependence on US platforms is one that Indian policymakers are actively wrestling with.
