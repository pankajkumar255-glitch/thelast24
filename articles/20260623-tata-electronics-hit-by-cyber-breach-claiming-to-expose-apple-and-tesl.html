Tata Electronics hit by cyber breach claiming to expose Apple and Tesla trade secrets

Key facts

  • Tata Electronics suffered a cyber breach exposing claimed Apple and Tesla trade secrets
  • Tata Electronics is a major iPhone component supplier for Apple
  • Breach raises supply-chain cybersecurity concerns for India's electronics sector
  • Full extent of data accessed remains under investigation

Tata Electronics, one of India's largest electronics manufacturers and a key supplier of iPhone components to Apple, has been hit by a cyber breach in which attackers claim to have obtained sensitive trade secrets belonging to Apple and Tesla. The incident, reported by Reuters, marks one of the most serious cybersecurity incidents to hit India's electronics sector in recent years.

Tata Electronics is a critical node in Apple's supply chain, producing iPhone casings at its facilities in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. The company also counts Tesla among its clients. The nature of the claimed data — trade secrets rather than just personal data — makes this breach particularly alarming, as it could expose proprietary designs, manufacturing processes, or contractual specifications.

The attack comes at a sensitive moment for India's electronics ambitions. The government has invested heavily in production-linked incentive schemes to attract global technology manufacturers, positioning India as an alternative to China. A high-profile breach involving two of the world's most valuable companies could prompt scrutiny of cybersecurity standards across India's vendor ecosystem.

Neither Tata Electronics nor Apple had issued official public statements at the time of reporting. Cybersecurity experts note that contract manufacturers holding the data of multiple global clients represent high-value targets for state-sponsored and criminal hacking groups alike. The full extent of the breach and what data was actually accessed remains under investigation.
