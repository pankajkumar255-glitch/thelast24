ICC makes landmark ruling: Motherhood and cricket can coexist, maternity protections formalised

Key facts

  • ICC formalises maternity protections for women cricketers
  • Policy affirms players need not choose between motherhood and career
  • Move affects all ICC member boards including BCCI

The International Cricket Council has taken a landmark step for women's cricket, formally acknowledging that motherhood and playing the sport at the highest level are not mutually exclusive. India Today reported the ICC's policy move, which introduces structured protections for women cricketers around maternity — a long-overdue recognition of the realities faced by female athletes.

The policy shift reflects a broader global reckoning in sport about gender equality and the conditions under which women compete professionally. For years, female cricketers have faced an unspoken ultimatum: prioritise their career or start a family. The ICC's intervention signals a formal rejection of that binary.

The move has been welcomed across the cricketing world as a meaningful step rather than a symbolic gesture. By embedding maternity protections into ICC frameworks, the governing body is pushing member boards — including the BCCI — to align their own contracts and policies accordingly. Several cricketers from various nations have in the past either retired early or taken extended breaks without adequate support structures.

For Indian women's cricket, which has grown dramatically in profile and commercial value over the past decade, this ruling could have immediate practical implications. As the BCCI expands investment in the women's game — including the Women's IPL — ensuring that top players are not forced to choose between family and career becomes not just an ethical imperative but a strategic one for the long-term health of the sport.
