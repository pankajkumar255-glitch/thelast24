Modi government launches push to reverse India's brain drain

Key facts

  • Government aims to attract back scientists, researchers, and tech professionals
  • Initiative includes improved research funding and industry linkages
  • India is a major net exporter of skilled talent, especially to US and UK

NDTV reports that the Modi government is pursuing a concerted strategy to reverse India's longstanding brain drain, targeting skilled professionals — especially in science, technology, and research — who have relocated to countries like the United States and the United Kingdom.

The initiative comes amid growing recognition that India's startup and technology ambitions are constrained by the departure of high-calibre talent. The government's approach reportedly includes better funding for research institutions, stronger academia-industry partnerships, and creating conditions that make India professionally and financially competitive for returnees.

India has for decades been a net exporter of skilled human capital, with large diaspora communities occupying senior roles at global technology companies, universities, and research labs. Policymakers have increasingly framed this as a structural weakness — one that limits domestic innovation even as India builds out its startup infrastructure.

For everyday Indians, the success of such a policy could have long-term consequences: more homegrown pharmaceutical research, stronger domestic technology development, and better-funded academic institutions. The challenge lies in execution — past efforts to attract diaspora talent have often stalled on bureaucratic hurdles, limited research infrastructure, and salary gaps.
