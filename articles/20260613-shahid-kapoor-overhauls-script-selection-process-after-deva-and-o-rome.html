Shahid Kapoor overhauls script selection process after Deva and O Romeo fail at box office

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Shahid Kapoor is now insisting on attending script narrations with his team rather than evaluating projects alone, a shift driven by the poor box-office performance of his recent films Deva and O Romeo. The report suggests the actor is taking a more guarded and collective approach to future project choices. Both films underperformed commercially, making this course correction a direct response to professional setbacks. The publication presents this as a significant change in how Kapoor manages his career decision-making.

The context

Shahid Kapoor is one of Hindi cinema's established A-list actors, having delivered major hits including Kabir Singh and Jab We Met. However, even top stars have faced severe box-office pressure in the post-pandemic era as Hindi films increasingly struggle against South Indian dubbed releases and OTT platforms. Deva, an action thriller, and O Romeo both failed to find audiences, adding financial and critical disappointments to Kapoor's recent record. The practice of stars involving managers, producers, and trusted collaborators in script decisions is becoming more common in an industry where a single big-budget failure can cost tens to hundreds of crores.

Why it matters

Repeated failures by a bankable star like Shahid Kapoor directly affect the livelihoods of hundreds of crew members and the finances of producers and distributors who back his films. His recalibration signals the acute uncertainty gripping mainstream Bollywood, where even established names are struggling to guarantee returns. For audiences, it raises the possibility that Kapoor's future projects will be more carefully chosen and potentially more watchable.

What's next

Expect Shahid Kapoor's next project announcement to be watched closely for signs of this more cautious approach. It is likely that his team will publicly emphasise script quality and audience research before his next major release is greenlit.
