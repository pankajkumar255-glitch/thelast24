'More Heinous Than Murder': Madras High Court Upholds Death Penalty in Child Abuse Case

Key facts

  • Madras High Court upholds death penalty in child abuse case
  • Court describes the crime as 'more heinous than murder'
  • Ruling affirms lower court's capital punishment award
  • Convict can still appeal to the Supreme Court

The Madras High Court has upheld the death sentence of a convict in a child abuse case, declaring in stark terms that the offence was 'more heinous than murder'. The court's ruling sustains capital punishment awarded at the trial stage, reflecting the bench's view that the crime fell squarely within the 'rarest of rare' category that warrants the ultimate penalty under Indian law.

The judgment is notable for its language as much as its outcome. By explicitly framing child abuse as exceeding murder in moral gravity, the court has articulated a position that goes beyond routine sentencing jurisprudence. Such observations carry weight in future sentencing decisions at lower courts across the Madras High Court's jurisdiction, which covers Tamil Nadu and Puducherry.

India's courts have in recent years grappled with the appropriate legal response to sexual and physical violence against children, with the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act providing a stringent framework. High court affirmations of death sentences in such cases remain rare but have been occurring with somewhat greater frequency as public and judicial intolerance for child abuse rises.

The convict retains the right to appeal the High Court's order before the Supreme Court. Child-rights groups have welcomed the ruling as a deterrent message, while legal scholars are likely to debate whether the blanket equation of child abuse with capital-worthy crimes reflects the nuanced standard the Supreme Court has historically applied in death penalty matters.
