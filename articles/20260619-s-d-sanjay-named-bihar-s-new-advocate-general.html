S.D. Sanjay named Bihar's new Advocate General

Key facts

  • S.D. Sanjay appointed Advocate General of Bihar
  • Advocate General is the state's chief legal officer under Article 165
  • Represents Bihar government before the Patna High Court

The Hindu reports that S.D. Sanjay has been appointed as the new Advocate General of Bihar, stepping into the state's most senior legal office. The Advocate General is a constitutional post under Article 165, responsible for advising the state government on legal matters and representing it before the Patna High Court in cases of public importance.

The appointment comes in the normal course of administrative change, with the Advocate General functioning as the government's chief counsel in all significant litigation involving the state. Bihar, India's third most populous state, regularly faces high-stakes legal battles in areas ranging from land rights and development projects to law-and-order matters.

S.D. Sanjay's appointment will determine the legal strategy the Nitish Kumar-led Bihar government pursues in its ongoing and future cases before the Patna High Court and, by extension, the Supreme Court of India.

For Bihar residents, the Advocate General's role is consequential because the office defends — or challenges — policies that affect public welfare, property, and rights across the state.
