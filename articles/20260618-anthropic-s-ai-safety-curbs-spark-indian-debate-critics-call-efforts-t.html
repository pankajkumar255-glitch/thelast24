Anthropic's AI safety curbs spark Indian debate — critics call efforts 'too slow, way too small'

Key facts

  • Anthropic imposed new curbs on its Claude AI models
  • Indian critics described the efforts as 'too slow, way too small'
  • Debate centres on pace of AI safety versus deployment speed
  • India is a major emerging market for AI application development

CNBC's Inside India newsletter reports that Anthropic's recent restrictions on its Claude AI models have set off a significant debate in India about the pace and scale of AI safety measures globally. Critics, including voices from India's fast-growing AI ecosystem, have described Anthropic's curbs as 'too slow, way too small' — pointing to a widening gap between the speed of AI deployment and the safeguards being put in place.

Anthropic, the US-based AI safety company and maker of the Claude family of models, has positioned itself as one of the more cautious players in the AI industry, building safety research into its core mission. However, its recent measures have drawn fire from both ends of the spectrum — those who believe even these steps are insufficient given AI's potential risks, and those who feel restrictions hinder innovation and access.

The debate is particularly relevant for India, which is emerging as a major market and development hub for AI applications. Indian startups, enterprises, and government initiatives are increasingly evaluating or integrating large language models, making questions of governance, restriction, and safety acutely practical. How global AI companies like Anthropic calibrate their guardrails will shape what tools are available to Indian developers and what liability frameworks eventually emerge in one of the world's largest AI growth markets.
