BCCI evaluating bilateral series feasibility; IPL 2026 likely to begin from early March

What happened

India Today reports that the BCCI is currently evaluating whether certain bilateral international series are feasible given scheduling and player workload concerns. The same report indicates that IPL 2026 is likely to be held from early March, suggesting a possible shift or extension of the traditional April-May window.

The context

The IPL is the BCCI's flagship property and the richest T20 league in the world, typically held between March and May. In recent years, bilateral series have increasingly clashed with IPL scheduling, forcing the board to make difficult trade-offs. An early March start could mean a longer tournament window or earlier conclusion before the Indian summer heat peaks.

Why it matters

An early IPL start affects franchise preparations, overseas player availability from other T20 leagues, and the national team's scheduling around major ICC events. For fans and broadcasters, the IPL window is the most commercially significant period in Indian sport.

What's next

Expect the BCCI to formally announce the IPL 2026 schedule and bilateral calendar in the coming weeks, with franchise owners and broadcasters watching closely.
