BCCI evaluating bilateral series feasibility; IPL 2026 likely to begin from early March

What happened

India Today reports that the BCCI is evaluating the feasibility of scheduling bilateral international series amid a congested cricket calendar. The same report indicates that IPL 2026 is likely to begin from early March, which would represent an earlier start than recent editions. The board is balancing international commitments, player availability, and the commercial demands of franchise cricket. No official announcement on IPL dates has yet been made by the BCCI.

The context

The IPL is the world's most lucrative T20 cricket league, generating billions of rupees in broadcast, sponsorship, and franchise revenue annually. In recent years, the tournament has typically started in late March or early April. The BCCI juggles a complex calendar that must accommodate international series commitments from the ICC's Future Tours Programme as well as domestic tournaments. Player workload management has become an increasingly pressing issue, with top Indian players often navigating back-to-back international and IPL commitments.

Why it matters

An early March IPL window could affect the availability of overseas players who may have international commitments, potentially altering the competitive balance across franchises. For Indian fans, it means cricket season arrives even earlier in 2026, with potential overlap with domestic tournaments. Advertisers and broadcasters will also need to adjust their planning around an earlier start date.

What's next

Expect the BCCI to make a formal announcement on IPL 2026 dates in the coming weeks, with franchise owners and broadcasters likely already in discussions. The outcome of the bilateral series feasibility review could also shape India's home international schedule for the first half of 2026.
