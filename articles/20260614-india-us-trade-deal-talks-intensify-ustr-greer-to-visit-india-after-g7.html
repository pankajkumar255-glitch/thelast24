India-US Trade Deal Talks Intensify: USTR Greer to Visit India After G7

What happened

Reuters, Moneycontrol, Times of India, NDTV Profit and Deccan Herald all report that US and Indian officials have confirmed USTR Jamieson Greer will visit India after the G7 summit to advance trade talks. Modi and Trump are expected to discuss trade at the G7, but senior US officials have explicitly stated that a final deal is not imminent.

The context

India and the US have been in an extended bilateral trade negotiation, with tensions elevated after Trump's broad tariff announcements in 2025. India has sought a reduction in US tariffs on its goods, particularly in sectors like steel, pharmaceuticals, and textiles, while the US has pressed India on market access and digital trade barriers.

Why it matters

A successful trade deal could lower duties on key Indian exports, boosting sectors from IT services to manufacturing and potentially creating jobs. Conversely, delays extend uncertainty for Indian exporters already navigating a volatile global trade environment.

What's next

Expect USTR Greer's India visit to set the agenda for a formal negotiating round, with markets watching for any signals on tariff rollbacks or a partial interim agreement.
