Supreme Court of India draws a line on AI use in courtrooms after US crisis

What happened

NDTV Profit reports that India's Supreme Court is formulating guidelines to regulate the use of artificial intelligence in courtrooms, drawing lessons from a crisis in the United States where lawyers submitted AI-generated fictitious case citations. The court's approach is intended to set guardrails before AI use becomes widespread and potentially harmful in Indian legal practice.

The context

In the US, several lawyers faced sanctions after submitting briefs containing fake case citations generated by ChatGPT and similar AI tools. Indian courts have been increasingly exploring technology including AI-assisted translation and case management, making the question of regulation urgent. The Supreme Court of India has a technology committee that has been examining these issues as part of broader court digitisation efforts.

Why it matters

Clear AI guidelines will protect litigants from the risk of their cases being argued with fabricated legal precedents, safeguarding the integrity of a judicial system that handles hundreds of millions of cases. It will also set a precedent for how other Indian institutions govern AI use in high-stakes professional settings.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to release a formal practice direction or circular on permissible AI use in court filings and proceedings within the coming months.
