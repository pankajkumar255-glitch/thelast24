India's AI Court Rules 2026 to ban risk-scoring of individuals by AI systems

Key facts

  • India's AI Court Rules 2026 will ban AI risk-scoring of individuals
  • Rules govern use of artificial intelligence in Indian court proceedings
  • Risk-scoring AI has been controversial globally for embedding bias
  • Rules place AI regulation in courts ahead of 2026 implementation

Frontline Magazine reports that India's forthcoming AI Court Rules 2026 will include a specific ban on the use of risk-scoring of individuals by artificial intelligence systems in court proceedings. The rules are positioned as a framework to govern the use of AI tools within the Indian judicial system.

Risk-scoring by AI — where algorithmic systems assign a numerical risk level to individuals, often based on demographic or historical data — has been adopted in some jurisdictions abroad for decisions like bail assessment, parole, and sentencing recommendations. Critics have long argued that such systems can embed and amplify biases, particularly against marginalised communities, and that their opacity undermines due process.

India's move to proactively ban this specific application before it takes root in courts reflects a cautious regulatory stance, aligning more closely with the European Union's AI Act, which also restricts high-risk AI applications, than with jurisdictions that have allowed such tools to proliferate.

For ordinary Indians, particularly those who interact with the criminal justice system, the ban has concrete significance: it ensures that bail decisions, risk assessments, and judicial outcomes will remain in the hands of human judges rather than being pre-shaped by algorithms — preserving a core element of the right to a fair hearing.
