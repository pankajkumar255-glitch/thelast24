US orders Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos AI models; India Inc sounds alarm

What happened

The Economic Times reports that the US government has directed Anthropic to disable its Fable 5 and Mythos AI models for users outside the United States, effectively locking out Indians and other foreign nationals. India Today further explains that the ban covers all foreigners, not just specific nationalities, though Indian users and businesses are among those most affected. The Economic Times notes that India Inc has sounded the alarm over the restrictions, signalling significant concern in the domestic technology and enterprise sector. The order appears to be linked to national security and export-control considerations that the US government is increasingly applying to advanced AI systems.

The context

Anthropic is one of the leading AI safety and research companies in the world, backed by billions of dollars from investors including Google and Amazon, and is regarded as a close rival to OpenAI. Its Claude family of models has gained rapid adoption among developers, startups, and enterprises globally, including in India. The United States has been steadily tightening restrictions on the export of advanced technologies — including semiconductors and AI — citing national security concerns and competition with China. This latest move reflects a broader trend of Washington treating frontier AI models as strategic assets subject to export-control regimes, similar to how it has handled advanced chips.

Why it matters

Indian startups, IT firms, and individual developers who have built workflows or products around Anthropic's models now face sudden disruption, with no immediate domestic alternative of equivalent capability. The restriction highlights the vulnerability of Indian businesses that depend on foreign AI platforms, and is likely to accelerate calls for investment in homegrown AI infrastructure. For everyday professionals using AI tools for productivity, the ban is a concrete reminder that access to global technology can be revoked at short notice by geopolitical decisions made in Washington.

What's next

Expect the Indian government and industry bodies to formally engage with both Anthropic and US counterparts seeking clarity or exemptions. The episode is likely to intensify policy debates around digital sovereignty and public investment in indigenous AI models.
