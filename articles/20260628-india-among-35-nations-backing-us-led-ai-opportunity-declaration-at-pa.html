India Among 35 Nations Backing US-Led 'AI Opportunity' Declaration at Pax Silica Summit

Key facts

  • India among 35 countries backing the US-led 'AI Opportunity' declaration
  • Declaration made at the Pax Silica Summit
  • Declaration frames AI as a driver of economic opportunity
  • Aligns with India's domestic push for AI infrastructure development

India was among 35 countries that backed a US-led 'AI Opportunity' declaration at the Pax Silica Summit, The Times of India reported, marking a significant multilateral alignment around Washington's vision for how artificial intelligence should be governed and promoted globally. The declaration frames AI as a tool of economic opportunity and development rather than primarily as a risk to be regulated.

The Pax Silica Summit has emerged as a key venue for countries to signal their positioning in the rapidly evolving global AI governance debate. By joining the US-led declaration, India is explicitly associating itself with a pro-innovation, pro-access approach to AI — a stance consistent with New Delhi's own domestic push to develop AI infrastructure and position Indian talent and firms at the frontier of the technology.

The endorsement also has geopolitical dimensions. As major powers compete to set the global agenda on AI standards, regulatory frameworks, and access to computing infrastructure, India's alignment with the US-led bloc strengthens the bilateral technology partnership that has become a cornerstone of the two countries' broader strategic relationship.

For India's technology sector — which ranges from global IT service giants to a fast-growing cohort of AI startups — the country's active participation in global AI governance frameworks signals government support for the industry's international ambitions. It also puts India in a stronger position to influence the rules that will shape AI development for years to come.
