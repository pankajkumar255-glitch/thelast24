Anthropic suspends Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models after US government security order

What happened

NDTV reports that Anthropic has suspended its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models following a US security order. Anthropic is one of the leading AI safety-focused companies in the United States, backed by major investors and known for its Claude series of AI assistants. The suspension of these two named models under a government directive is an unusual and significant development in the AI industry. The specific security concerns cited in the order have not been detailed in the headline, but the action reflects growing US government engagement with AI model oversight.

The context

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers, including Dario Amodei, with an explicit focus on AI safety. The company has received billions of dollars in investment from Amazon and Google among others, and its Claude models compete with OpenAI's ChatGPT and Google's Gemini. The US government has been developing frameworks for AI oversight, including through executive orders on AI safety signed by both the Biden and Trump administrations. Concerns about advanced AI models being misused for cyberattacks, biological weapons design, or propaganda have driven calls for tighter controls on the most capable systems.

Why it matters

Government-mandated suspension of AI models sets a precedent that could affect how AI companies worldwide operate and what tools are available to developers and businesses in India that rely on US-based AI platforms. It also signals that even well-regarded safety-focused AI labs are not immune from regulatory intervention. For Indian startups and enterprises building on top of US AI infrastructure, the risk of sudden model unavailability due to security orders becomes a real operational consideration.

What's next

Expect Anthropic to engage with US authorities to clarify the scope and duration of the suspension, and watch whether other AI companies face similar orders as Washington tightens its oversight of frontier AI models. Indian AI policy observers will likely monitor this closely as India develops its own AI governance frameworks.
