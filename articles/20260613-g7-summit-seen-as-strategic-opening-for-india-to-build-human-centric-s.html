G7 Summit seen as strategic opening for India to build human-centric, sovereign AI ecosystem

What happened

Organiser.org reports that the G7 Summit represents a strategic opportunity for India to advocate for and help build a human-centric AI ecosystem centred on sovereign models. The commentary argues that India should use its G7 partner status and its standing as the world's most populous democracy to push for AI governance frameworks that give developing nations more control over critical AI infrastructure. This comes as India's vulnerability to foreign AI platform decisions — illustrated by the Anthropic ban — has become a live political and economic issue. The piece frames India's IndiaAI Mission and emerging domestic model capabilities as the foundation for such a sovereign push.

The context

India has been a G20 presidency holder and has increasingly asserted itself in global technology governance discussions, including at the Global Partnership on AI and in UN forums on digital governance. The G7, historically a club of wealthy Western nations, has in recent years included India as an invited partner. India's AI policy positions — emphasising democratisation of AI, multilingual models, and data sovereignty — have resonated with the Global South but have yet to translate into binding international frameworks. The timing of this commentary, immediately after the Anthropic access ban, gives the sovereignty argument fresh political salience.

Why it matters

If India successfully shapes G7-level AI governance norms, it could establish protections against arbitrary access restrictions of the kind that just affected Indian Anthropic users. For Indian businesses and developers, an international framework that treats AI access as a shared global good rather than a tool of US export control would reduce the risk of sudden disruptions. More broadly, India positioning itself as a third pole in global AI governance — between US commercial dominance and Chinese state control — could yield significant diplomatic and economic dividends.

What's next

Watch India's official positions at the G7 Summit for concrete proposals on AI access, sovereignty, and governance, and whether the Anthropic controversy is explicitly referenced in diplomatic communications. Expect Indian tech industry bodies to formally brief the government ahead of the summit on their concerns about foreign AI platform dependence.
