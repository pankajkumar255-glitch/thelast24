BHASHINI and GeM Sign MoU to Bring Multilingual Access to India's Public Procurement Platform

What happened

PIB reports that the Digital India BHASHINI Division and Government e-Marketplace signed an MoU aimed at strengthening multilingual access across India's public procurement ecosystem. The partnership will leverage BHASHINI's AI-driven language translation tools to make GeM's interface and services available in multiple Indian languages. No financial terms were disclosed.

The context

GeM was launched in 2016 to streamline government procurement and reduce corruption, and has grown into a major platform handling hundreds of thousands of crores in transactions annually. However, its predominantly English interface has long been a barrier for small vendors and buyers from non-English-speaking regions. BHASHINI is the government's flagship initiative to democratise technology access by building AI tools for India's 22 scheduled languages.

Why it matters

Multilingual access on GeM could enable small and medium businesses in vernacular-language regions to participate in government tenders and procurement for the first time, potentially boosting their revenues and livelihoods. It also strengthens Digital India's inclusion agenda by ensuring language is no longer a barrier to accessing government services.

What's next

Expect a phased rollout of multilingual features on GeM, with BHASHINI's translation tools likely to be integrated starting with the most widely spoken Indian languages beyond Hindi and English.
