India's 2026 Startup IPO Pipeline: A Tracker of the Year's Biggest Tech Listings

What happened

Inc42 reports on its Indian Startup IPO Tracker for 2026, providing a comprehensive rundown of the new-age tech companies planning to go public this year. The tracker aggregates information on startups across sectors that have filed or are preparing to file for IPOs on Indian exchanges.

The context

India witnessed a surge of startup IPOs from 2021 onwards, with companies like Zomato, Paytm, and Nykaa listing on Indian exchanges. While 2022 saw a correction in valuations, subsequent years saw a recovery in appetite for new listings. The 2026 pipeline reflects a maturing startup ecosystem where companies are reaching the scale and profitability metrics required for public market scrutiny.

Why it matters

A robust IPO pipeline means more opportunities for retail investors to participate in India's startup growth story directly through the stock market, while also unlocking wealth for thousands of startup employees holding stock options. It also tests the market's confidence in the profitability trajectories of India's new-age companies.

What's next

Expect market regulator SEBI to closely scrutinise the disclosure quality of startup IPO filings in 2026, particularly around path-to-profitability metrics following lessons from earlier volatile listings.
