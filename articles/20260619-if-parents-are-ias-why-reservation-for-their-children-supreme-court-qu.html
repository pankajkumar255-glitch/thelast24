'If Parents Are IAS, Why Reservation For Their Children?': Supreme Court Questions Creamy Layer in SC/ST Quotas

Key facts

  • Supreme Court questioned reservation benefits for children of IAS officers from SC/ST communities
  • Court asked why 'creamy layer' exclusion should not apply to SC/ST categories
  • Creamy layer currently applies only to OBC reservations, not SC/ST

NDTV reports that the Supreme Court raised pointed questions about the continued eligibility of children of IAS officers belonging to Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe communities for reservation benefits. The bench asked why, if a family has already achieved significant socioeconomic advancement through reservation, their children should continue to receive the same quota benefits.

The court's remarks draw attention to the concept of the 'creamy layer' — a ceiling already applied in Other Backward Classes reservations that excludes more affluent members of the community from quota benefits. The question before the court is whether a similar principle should be extended to SC/ST categories, which have so far been exempt from any creamy layer exclusion.

This is not the first time the issue has been raised judicially, but observations from the Supreme Court carry significant weight and could lead to a formal ruling or referral to a larger constitutional bench. Any change to creamy layer applicability in SC/ST reservations would be among the most consequential shifts in India's affirmative action framework in decades.

For everyday Indians, the outcome could affect access to government jobs and university seats for lakhs of applicants who currently qualify under SC/ST quotas.
