Modi Hails India as World's Third-Largest Startup Ecosystem, Vows Reforms Will Continue

What happened

ANI News reports that Prime Minister Narendra Modi publicly declared India the world's third-largest startup ecosystem and stated that the 'express train of reforms will not stop,' signalling continued government support for innovation and entrepreneurship. The remarks were made at a high-profile event focused on India's startup and technology sector.

The context

India has grown into one of the most active startup markets globally, with over 100 unicorns and a thriving venture capital ecosystem. Government initiatives like Startup India and DPIIT recognition schemes have been central to this growth. Modi's repeated emphasis on reform continuity is significant given upcoming regulatory and tax policy decisions that affect founders and investors.

Why it matters

For millions of startup employees, founders, and retail investors holding startup-linked stocks, sustained reforms mean a more predictable environment for funding, hiring, and IPOs. It also strengthens India's hand in attracting foreign deep-tech investment over China-wary global capital.

What's next

Expect the government to unveil further startup-friendly policy measures in the coming months, particularly around easing foreign direct investment norms and expanding ESOP tax relief.
