Israel carries out air strikes on Lebanon even as Iran claims US nuclear deal is near

What happened

The BBC reports that Israel carried out air strikes on Lebanon, citing state media, at the same time that Iran claimed a deal with the United States is near. The dual developments — military escalation by Israel and diplomatic progress between Washington and Tehran — highlight the contradictory pressures currently shaping the Middle East. Israel has historically taken independent military action when it perceives diplomatic processes as threatening its security. The strikes on Lebanon add to the ongoing volatility in the region following months of conflict linked to the Israel-Gaza war.

The context

Israel has been conducting periodic strikes on Lebanon targeting Hezbollah infrastructure, which it regards as an Iranian proxy threatening its northern border. The Israel-Gaza conflict that began in October 2023 dramatically widened regional tensions, drawing in Hezbollah in Lebanon, Houthi forces in Yemen, and Iran-backed militias across the region. Israel has openly stated it will not allow Iran to develop a nuclear weapon and has previously struck Iranian targets directly. The United States has been Israel's primary security guarantor but has also pursued diplomatic engagement with Iran separately.

Why it matters

Continued Israeli military action in Lebanon risks further escalating a regional conflict that has already disrupted global shipping through the Red Sea and kept energy markets on edge. For India, which imports the bulk of its oil from the Middle East, any escalation raises the risk of supply disruptions and price spikes. India also has a significant diaspora in Gulf countries and Lebanon, making regional stability a direct human concern as well.

What's next

Watch whether Israel publicly responds to news of a US-Iran deal framework, and whether Washington applies any diplomatic pressure on Israel to halt strikes as negotiations reach a critical stage. A formal deal announcement could trigger a strong Israeli reaction that further destabilises the region.
