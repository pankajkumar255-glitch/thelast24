OpenAI poaches Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer from Google in major AI talent coup

Key facts

  • Noam Shazeer was co-lead of Google's Gemini AI model
  • Shazeer co-authored 'Attention Is All You Need', the transformer paper
  • He has joined OpenAI, Google's direct AI rival
  • Gemini underpins Google Search, Workspace, and Android features

NDTV reports that OpenAI has poached Noam Shazeer, the co-lead of Google's Gemini AI project, marking one of the most consequential talent moves in the AI industry in recent years. Shazeer is not merely a senior researcher — he is a foundational figure in modern AI, widely credited as a co-author of the 2017 'Attention Is All You Need' paper that introduced the transformer architecture, the technical backbone of virtually every large language model in use today, including ChatGPT and Gemini itself.

His departure from Google, where he was centrally involved in building and scaling Gemini, deals a significant blow to the company's most important AI product. Gemini is Google's answer to ChatGPT and powers features across Search, Workspace, and Android — products used by hundreds of millions of Indians. Losing its co-lead to a direct rival is both a reputational and operational setback for Google.

For OpenAI, the hire is a statement of ambition. The company, already the most prominent AI lab in the world, is aggressively expanding its research talent even as it faces intensifying competition. For everyday Indians who use AI-powered tools across education, work, and entertainment, the long-term significance is clear: the race to build more capable and affordable AI is accelerating, and this move shifts one of its most important players.
