Supreme Court declares right to walk on safe footpaths a fundamental right, seeks law

Key facts

  • Supreme Court declares safe footpaths a fundamental right
  • Court directs footpaths be provided on all roads
  • Court seeks legislation to enforce the right
  • Ruling targets encroachment and absence of pavements nationwide

The Indian Express reports that the Supreme Court of India has declared the right to walk on safe footpaths a fundamental right under the Constitution, in a landmark ruling with sweeping implications for urban infrastructure across the country. The court stated that footpaths must be provided on all roads and indicated it would seek legislation to give this right statutory backing.

The ruling comes amid decades of neglect of pedestrian infrastructure in Indian cities, where footpaths are routinely encroached upon by vendors, parked vehicles, and construction, leaving pedestrians — particularly the elderly, disabled, and children — forced onto busy roads. The court's intervention follows a series of cases highlighting fatalities and injuries caused by the absence of safe walking spaces.

By elevating the issue to a fundamental rights question, the Supreme Court has significantly raised the legal stakes for municipal bodies and state governments, who can now be held constitutionally accountable for failing to provide and maintain walkable footpaths. The directive to pursue legislation signals that the court views administrative action alone as insufficient to address the problem at scale.

For the vast majority of Indians who walk as their primary mode of daily movement — to markets, schools, bus stops, and workplaces — this ruling offers a powerful legal tool to demand safe public infrastructure from local authorities.
