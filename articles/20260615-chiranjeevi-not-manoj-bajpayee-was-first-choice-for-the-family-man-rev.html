Chiranjeevi, not Manoj Bajpayee, was first choice for The Family Man, reveals director Raj Nidimoru

What happened

The Indian Express reports that director Raj Nidimoru has disclosed that Telugu megastar Chiranjeevi was the original choice for the lead role in The Family Man, the acclaimed spy thriller series that eventually starred Manoj Bajpayee. Nidimoru made this casting revelation publicly, offering a rare glimpse into the early development decisions behind the hit Amazon Prime Video series.

The context

The Family Man, created by Raj Nidimoru and Krishna D.K. for Amazon Prime Video, premiered in 2019 and became one of the most-watched Indian original series on the platform. Manoj Bajpayee's portrayal of intelligence officer Srikant Tiwari won widespread acclaim and multiple awards, making it difficult to imagine anyone else in the role. Chiranjeevi, one of Telugu cinema's biggest stars, would have brought an entirely different cultural and stylistic identity to the character.

Why it matters

The revelation highlights how casting decisions can fundamentally shape a show's identity and reach — had Chiranjeevi been cast, The Family Man might have targeted a very different audience and may not have achieved the pan-India crossover it did with Bajpayee.

What's next

Expect renewed interest in the long-awaited third season of The Family Man, with fans likely to re-examine the show's origins following this disclosure.
