HCLTech to buy 10.5% stake in Sarvam AI at $1.5 billion valuation

What happened

Reuters reports that HCLTech has agreed to purchase a 10.5% stake in Sarvam AI, valuing the Bengaluru-based AI startup at $1.5 billion. The transaction represents a significant corporate investment in a homegrown Indian AI company building large language and foundational models.

The context

Sarvam AI was founded by IIT and IISc alumni and has been developing AI models specifically tailored for Indian languages and enterprise applications. HCLTech is one of India's largest IT services companies with a global client base, and this investment reflects the growing race among Indian tech majors to gain a foothold in generative AI infrastructure rather than simply delivering AI-enabled services.

Why it matters

For Indian businesses and consumers, a well-funded Sarvam AI could accelerate access to AI tools built for Indian languages and local contexts. For HCLTech, the stake gives it a competitive edge as global clients increasingly demand AI-native capabilities.

What's next

Expect further consolidation in India's AI startup space as other IT majors like Infosys and Wipro face pressure to announce similar strategic investments.
