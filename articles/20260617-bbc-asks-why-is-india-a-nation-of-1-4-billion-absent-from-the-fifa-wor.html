BBC asks: Why is India, a nation of 1.4 billion, absent from the FIFA World Cup?

Key facts

  • India has never qualified for a FIFA World Cup
  • India's population is 1.4 billion — largest in the world
  • India's FIFA ranking sits between 100-130
  • AIFF was suspended by FIFA in 2022 over governance issues

What happened

BBC published a feature asking why India, despite being home to 1.4 billion people, has not qualified for a FIFA World Cup. The piece interrogates structural, administrative, and cultural reasons for the nation's football underperformance at the global stage.

The context

India's FIFA ranking has hovered between 100 and 130 for years, well below the level needed to qualify through the Asian Football Confederation. Cricket dominates national sporting culture, funding, and sponsorship, leaving football starved of institutional support. The All India Football Federation has faced governance crises, including a 2022 FIFA suspension. Despite pockets of football culture in states like West Bengal, Goa, and Kerala, no national breakthrough has come.

Why it matters

India's World Cup absence represents a missed opportunity for soft power, youth inspiration, and sporting revenue at a moment when football is the world's most watched sport — the BBC feature signals growing international scrutiny of India's sporting priorities.

What's next

Expect renewed domestic debate on football funding and infrastructure investment as the World Cup captures global attention through the summer.
