Indian Startup Builds Balcony Escape System for High-Rise Fire Emergencies

Key facts

  • Startup designed a balcony descent system for high-rise fire evacuations
  • System works when elevators fail and stairwells are smoke-filled
  • Targets the gap in India's urban high-rise fire safety infrastructure
  • Device is designed for individual apartment installation

An Indian startup has designed and demonstrated an emergency balcony escape system aimed at saving lives in high-rise building fires, targeting the lethal combination of smoke-filled stairwells and non-functional elevators that has cost lives in urban fire incidents across the country. The device allows residents to evacuate from their own balconies in a controlled descent, bypassing the need for functional lifts or accessible staircases.

The product comes in response to a well-documented pattern: when fires break out in tall residential or commercial buildings, elevators are typically shut down or become inoperable, while staircases fill with smoke, trapping residents on upper floors. In several past incidents in Indian cities, victims have been found on balconies or near windows, unable to find a safe way down before fire or smoke overwhelmed them.

The startup's system is designed to be compact enough for individual apartment installation, deployable without technical expertise under panic conditions. While the full technical specifications were detailed in the NDTV report, the core proposition is a controlled-descent mechanism that individuals can activate from their own balcony without external assistance.

India's building fire safety framework has long been criticised for weak enforcement, with many high-rises lacking compliant fire escapes or working sprinkler systems. A consumer-level balcony escape product, if it clears regulatory approvals and reaches affordable price points, could serve as a practical stopgap in the vast stock of existing buildings that cannot easily be retrofitted with conventional fire-safety infrastructure.
