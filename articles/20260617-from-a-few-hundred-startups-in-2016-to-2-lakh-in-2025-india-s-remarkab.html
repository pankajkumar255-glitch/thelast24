From a Few Hundred Startups in 2016 to 2 Lakh in 2025: India's Remarkable Startup Journey

What happened

NDTV reports that India's registered startup count has surged from a few hundred in 2016 to 2 lakh in 2025, marking one of the fastest expansions of any national startup ecosystem globally. The milestone tracks the trajectory of India's entrepreneurial growth over nearly a decade.

The context

The Startup India initiative, launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in January 2016, provided tax benefits, easier compliance, and funding access to encourage new ventures. India now has over 100 unicorns and a deep pool of angel investors and venture capital funds, with cities like Bengaluru, Delhi-NCR, and Mumbai emerging as major global startup hubs.

Why it matters

A 2-lakh-strong startup ecosystem directly translates into millions of jobs for young Indians and drives innovation in sectors from fintech and healthtech to agritech and defence. It also increasingly attracts foreign investment, boosting India's broader economic growth.

What's next

Expect further policy announcements around startup funding and deep-tech support as the government looks to accelerate the next phase of growth beyond the 2 lakh milestone.
