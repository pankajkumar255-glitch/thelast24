India crush Afghanistan by an innings and 300 runs in biggest-ever Test victory

What happened

The Times of India reports that India crushed Afghanistan by an innings and 300 runs, registering the biggest victory in their Test history. The result was a comprehensive one-sided contest that ended well inside the scheduled time. No Indian team had previously won a Test match by such a large margin. The achievement sets a new benchmark for India's dominance in home Test cricket.

The context

India have historically been one of the most dominant Test sides at home, regularly defeating lower-ranked opposition by large margins. Afghanistan were granted Test status by the ICC in 2017 and have played limited Tests since, with their squad still developing at the highest level. India's pace and spin attack has been particularly lethal in home conditions. This match was played as Afghanistan's Test opportunities remain rare, making India heavy favourites from the outset.

Why it matters

A win of this magnitude boosts India's net run rate and points tally in the World Test Championship standings, where every margin counts. It also provides valuable match practice and form for Indian players ahead of bigger challenges. For Afghanistan cricket, the scale of the defeat highlights the work still needed to compete at the top level of the game.

What's next

Expect India's selectors and coaching staff to use this result to fine-tune squad combinations ahead of upcoming bilateral series. The World Test Championship standings will be updated and India are likely to consolidate their position near the top of the table. Afghanistan will need to regroup and look at structural improvements to close the gap with elite Test nations.
