24-Year-Old Indian Techie Among 11 Skydivers Killed in Missouri Plane Crash

Key facts

  • 24-year-old Indian tech professional killed in Missouri plane crash
  • Total of 11 skydivers died — entire group on board
  • One of the deadliest skydiving-related crashes in recent US history
  • US aviation authorities expected to investigate the cause

NDTV reports that a 24-year-old Indian technology professional was among 11 skydivers who died when their aircraft crashed in Missouri. All 11 people on board — a full skydiving group — were killed, making it one of the most lethal single skydiving-related plane crashes in recent American history. The victim's young age and profession reflect the profile of thousands of Indians who travel to the United States each year on work and student visas to pursue careers in technology.

The crash occurred in Missouri and US aviation and safety authorities are expected to investigate the cause. Skydiving aircraft are typically small, lightly-regulated planes, and crashes, while rare, can be catastrophic when they occur at altitude before a jump.

The incident will reverberate strongly in India, where families of young tech professionals working abroad follow such news with acute anxiety. It also raises questions about safety standards at skydiving operations in the US, which many young Indian-Americans and Indian visa holders participate in as a recreational activity.
