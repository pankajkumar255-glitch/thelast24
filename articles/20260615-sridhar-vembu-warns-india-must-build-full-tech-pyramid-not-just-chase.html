Sridhar Vembu Warns India Must Build Full Tech Pyramid, Not Just Chase AI

What happened

India Today reports that Zoho founder Sridhar Vembu has called on India to move beyond an exclusive focus on AI and instead invest in building the full technology stack, which he describes as a 'tech pyramid'. Vembu argued that foundational layers — including semiconductors, operating systems, and core software — must be developed domestically alongside AI. His comments come at a time when India is rapidly expanding its AI policy and investment agenda.

The context

Sridhar Vembu is one of India's most respected technology entrepreneurs, having built Zoho into a global software company largely from small-town Tamil Nadu. His critique echoes long-standing concerns among technologists that India's IT sector remains heavily reliant on foreign hardware and cloud infrastructure. The government's semiconductor mission and the push for homegrown AI models have renewed this debate.

Why it matters

Without investment in foundational technology layers, Indian startups and enterprises remain vulnerable to supply-chain disruptions and foreign pricing power — costs that ultimately filter through to consumers and businesses. Building a full tech stack would also create a far broader and more resilient base of skilled jobs across India.

What's next

Expect Vembu's remarks to intensify calls within policy circles for expanded support to semiconductor design, open-source software, and deep-tech research beyond the current AI-first agenda.
