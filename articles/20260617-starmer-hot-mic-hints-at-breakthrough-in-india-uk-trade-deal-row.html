Starmer hot mic hints at breakthrough in India-UK trade deal row

Key facts

  • Starmer caught on hot mic hinting at India-UK trade row resolution
  • India-UK FTA negotiations ongoing since January 2022
  • Key disputes involve worker mobility, whisky tariffs, and auto duties

Bloomberg reports that British Prime Minister Keir Starmer was caught on a hot microphone making remarks that suggest a breakthrough may be imminent in the long-running trade dispute between India and the United Kingdom. The inadvertent comment has raised fresh hopes that the two sides are close to resolving key differences that have stalled the free trade agreement.

India and the UK have been in formal FTA negotiations since January 2022, making it one of the longest-running such processes for either country. Major sticking points have included India's demand for easier mobility for its skilled professionals and students, and the UK's push for lower Indian tariffs on goods like whisky and automobiles. Talks have gone through multiple rounds without a final deal.

A concluded FTA would carry significant economic weight for Indian exporters in sectors like textiles, pharmaceuticals, gems and jewellery, and IT services. For Indian professionals, improved visa and mobility provisions could open new opportunities in the UK job market, making this negotiation one that resonates well beyond corporate boardrooms.
