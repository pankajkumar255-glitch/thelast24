Lagaan was shot in extreme heat, amid injuries and jet interruptions, reveals actor Yashpal Sharma

What happened

The Indian Express reports that actor Yashpal Sharma has recalled the extreme hardships during the filming of Lagaan, including intense heat, on-set injuries, and jet flyovers that disrupted shooting schedules. Sharma played Lakha, one of Hindi cinema's most memorable villains, in the film.

The context

Lagaan (2001), directed by Ashutosh Gowariker and starring Aamir Khan, was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film and remains a milestone of Indian cinema. The film was famously shot over several months in the scorching heat of Kutch, Gujarat, with an ensemble cast and elaborate cricket sequences.

Why it matters

Such behind-the-scenes revelations keep iconic Indian films alive in public memory and are particularly resonant as the film approaches its 25th anniversary. They also underscore the physical demands placed on actors in pre-CGI Bollywood productions.

What's next

Expect renewed interest in Lagaan's legacy as the film nears its silver jubilee, with more cast members likely to share memories in interviews and retrospectives.
