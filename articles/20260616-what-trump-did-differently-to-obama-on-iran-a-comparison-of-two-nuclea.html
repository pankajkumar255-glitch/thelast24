What Trump Did Differently To Obama On Iran: A Comparison Of Two Nuclear Deals

What happened

BBC published an explainer comparing how Trump and Obama each handled diplomatic negotiations with Iran over its nuclear programme. The piece contextualises Trump's new deal announcement against Obama's 2015 JCPOA framework.

The context

Obama's JCPOA involved multilateral negotiations with the P5+1 group and lifted sanctions in exchange for verified nuclear limits. Trump withdrew the US from that deal in 2018 and imposed sweeping sanctions before now announcing a separate agreement in his second term.

Why it matters

The durability and verification standards of Trump's deal compared with Obama's will determine whether Iran's nuclear threat is genuinely contained — a question with profound consequences for global energy markets and regional security that directly touches India's interests.

What's next

Expect detailed analysis of the deal's text and international reactions, particularly from European powers who were parties to the original JCPOA.
