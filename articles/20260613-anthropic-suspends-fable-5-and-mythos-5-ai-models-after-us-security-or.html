Anthropic Suspends Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI Models After US Security Order

What happened

NDTV reports that Anthropic has suspended its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models following a US security order. The nature of the specific security concerns that prompted the order has not been detailed in the headline. Anthropic, one of the leading AI safety-focused companies in the US, appears to have complied with the directive. The suspension underscores the US government's increasing willingness to intervene directly in AI development and deployment on security grounds.

The context

Anthropic was founded by former OpenAI researchers and is known for its Claude series of AI models, positioning itself as a safety-first AI company. The US government has been developing frameworks to regulate advanced AI, including export controls on AI chips and models. The Biden and Trump administrations have both treated cutting-edge AI as a matter of national security, particularly regarding capabilities that could be exploited by adversaries. This is among the first reported instances of a US security order leading to the direct suspension of named AI models.

Why it matters

Indian businesses and developers increasingly rely on US-built AI platforms and models for everything from coding to customer service. Security-driven suspensions of AI models could disrupt access to tools that Indian firms depend on, and signals that the AI landscape is becoming subject to geopolitical and regulatory unpredictability.

What's next

Expect Anthropic to work with US authorities to address security concerns before potentially relaunching the models. Watch for whether other AI companies face similar orders, which could accelerate regulatory frameworks around AI model safety.
