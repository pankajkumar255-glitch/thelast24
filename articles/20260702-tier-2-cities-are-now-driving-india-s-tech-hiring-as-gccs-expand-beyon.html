Tier-2 Cities Are Now Driving India's Tech Hiring as GCCs Expand Beyond Metros

Key facts

  • Tier-2 cities now driving growth in India's tech hiring
  • GCCs expanding beyond Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune metros
  • Trend driven by cost pressures and untapped talent pools

India's tech hiring market is witnessing a decisive pivot, with tier-2 cities increasingly driving employment growth as Global Capability Centres — the India-based back-offices of multinational corporations — push their footprints well beyond the traditional big-city clusters.

For years, cities like Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai dominated GCC hiring, creating intense competition for talent and driving up salaries and real-estate costs. Companies are now actively exploring locations such as Coimbatore, Jaipur, Indore, Nagpur, and Vizag, drawn by lower operational costs, improving infrastructure, and a generation of engineering graduates who prefer not to migrate.

The shift has structural implications for India's technology workforce. Tier-2 cities have long produced engineering talent that then flowed to metros; the GCC expansion is beginning to reverse that dynamic, allowing professionals to build meaningful careers closer to home. This also eases the chronic congestion and housing pressures facing India's top tech cities.

Industry observers note that the trend is self-reinforcing: as anchor employers establish operations in smaller cities, supporting services, co-working spaces, and a broader tech community tend to follow. For state governments competing to attract investment, this creates a new battleground in which improving connectivity, skilling infrastructure, and ease of doing business are the key differentiators.
