Trump says Anthropic is no longer a US national security threat

Key facts

  • Trump says he no longer views Anthropic as a national security threat
  • Anthropic makes the Claude series of AI models
  • The shift could enable Anthropic to pursue US government contracts
  • Follows Trump administration's broader re-engagement with Silicon Valley

US President Donald Trump has reportedly stated that he no longer views Anthropic as a national security threat, a notable reversal that could significantly alter the AI company's relationship with the US federal government. The development was reported by The Indian Express, citing sources familiar with the matter.

Anthropic, founded by former OpenAI researchers including Dario and Daniela Amodei, had faced scrutiny under the broader lens of US government concerns about AI safety, data security, and the potential for frontier models to be exploited. Being formally or informally flagged as a national security risk would have severely limited Anthropic's ability to win federal contracts or participate in US government AI initiatives.

Trump's latest position marks a softening consistent with his administration's recent overtures toward major tech and AI companies. Since returning to the White House, Trump has moved to cultivate ties with Silicon Valley leaders, hosting several high-profile meetings with tech executives. Anthropic's Claude models, which compete directly with OpenAI's ChatGPT, have been gaining ground in enterprise and government-adjacent deployments.

For India, the development matters in a downstream sense: Anthropic's standing with the US government shapes its global expansion appetite, the regulatory frameworks it will operate under, and ultimately the reliability and accessibility of its AI services for Indian businesses and developers who increasingly rely on Claude-based tools.
