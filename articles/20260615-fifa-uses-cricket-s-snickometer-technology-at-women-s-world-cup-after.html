FIFA uses cricket's Snickometer technology at Women's World Cup after Sweden vs Tunisia controversy

What happened

India Today reports that FIFA has adopted the Snickometer, a technology originally developed for cricket, during the Women's World Cup. The technology proved decisive during the Sweden vs Tunisia fixture, where its use settled a contentious on-field incident.

The context

The Snickometer, or 'Snicko', was developed for cricket by British company and has been a staple of TV broadcasting and umpire reviews in the sport for decades. FIFA has been progressively adopting new technologies such as VAR and goal-line technology to improve decision-making accuracy in football. The use of a cricket-origin tool at a FIFA tournament is an unprecedented crossover.

Why it matters

For Indian sports fans, the adoption of cricket technology at the FIFA World Cup is a point of national pride and demonstrates the global influence of innovations nurtured in cricket. It also signals a broader trend of technology-driven fairness in elite sport.

What's next

Expect FIFA to expand its use of multi-sport technologies if the Snickometer proves effective throughout the Women's World Cup.
