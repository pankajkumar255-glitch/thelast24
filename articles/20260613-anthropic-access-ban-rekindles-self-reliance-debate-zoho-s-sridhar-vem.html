Anthropic access ban rekindles self-reliance debate; Zoho's Sridhar Vembu declares 'Globalisation Is Dead'

What happened

Deccan Herald reports that Sridhar Vembu, founder of Zoho, made the declaration that 'Globalisation Is Dead' in direct response to the controversy surrounding the US restricting Indian access to Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and Mythos models. Vembu's statement is part of a broader chorus from Indian tech leaders calling for homegrown AI development. The episode has rekindled debates in Indian policy and startup circles about over-reliance on US platforms for critical digital infrastructure. This comes at a time when India is already investing in domestic AI initiatives under the IndiaAI Mission.

The context

Sridhar Vembu built Zoho into a global software company headquartered in rural Tamil Nadu, and has long been an advocate for India building its own technology stack rather than depending on Silicon Valley. The self-reliance debate in Indian tech is not new — it intensified after US chip export restrictions and has parallels in sectors from semiconductors to defence equipment. India's IndiaAI Mission, announced in 2024 with over Rs 10,000 crore in funding, is aimed at building sovereign AI compute and model capacity. The Anthropic ban is being seen as a live demonstration of why such missions are necessary.

Why it matters

Vembu's intervention carries weight because Zoho is one of the few Indian software companies that has consistently competed globally without depending on US venture capital or American cloud infrastructure. His framing of the issue as a geopolitical rupture rather than a temporary policy hiccup could shift how Indian policymakers, investors, and entrepreneurs prioritise domestic AI development. For Indian developers and businesses, the message is that tools and platforms built on foreign AI can be switched off by a foreign government at any time. This could accelerate investment into Indian AI startups and government-backed sovereign model initiatives.

What's next

Expect the Anthropic controversy to be cited in upcoming parliamentary and policy discussions on India's AI strategy, and for the IndiaAI Mission to receive renewed political attention. Watch whether Zoho and other large Indian tech firms publicly commit resources to domestic AI model development in the coming weeks.
