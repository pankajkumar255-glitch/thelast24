At 25, ex-Google intern Michael Truell built Cursor into a $60 billion SpaceX-scale deal

What happened

India Today reports on Michael Truell, a 25-year-old who interned at Google and went on to co-found Anysphere, the company behind the AI coding assistant Cursor. The company is reportedly being valued at around $60 billion in its latest funding discussions — a figure that puts it in the same league as storied private companies like SpaceX. Truell's rapid ascent is being cited as emblematic of the AI-native startup generation.

The context

Cursor is an AI-powered code editor that sits on top of VS Code and uses large language models to help developers write, debug, and refactor code faster. It has gained enormous traction among professional developers globally, including a large user base in India's IT sector. The $60 billion figure reflects the broader investor frenzy around AI developer tools as companies race to embed AI into every stage of software production.

Why it matters

For India's 5-million-plus software developer community, tools like Cursor are already changing productivity benchmarks — meaning Indian IT firms and developers must rapidly adapt or risk falling behind global peers who are leveraging AI-assisted coding at scale.

What's next

Expect Anysphere to close its funding round at or near the reported $60 billion valuation, which would make Cursor one of the most valuable private AI companies in the world within just a few years of launch.
