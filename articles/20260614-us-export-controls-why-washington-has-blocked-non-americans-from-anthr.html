US Export Controls: Why Washington Has Blocked Non-Americans From Anthropic's Most Powerful AI Models

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that the US has suddenly blocked foreigners from using Anthropic's most powerful AI models, with the government issuing a directive that Anthropic has complied with by suspending access to Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all non-US users. The Indian Express explains that the move is rooted in US export control law, which the government is now applying to advanced AI models on national security grounds. Indiatimes confirms that access to both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 has been suspended globally for non-US users following this directive. Telegraph India notes that the suspension is worldwide in scope and was implemented swiftly after the US order.

The context

US export control law, administered by agencies including the Commerce Department's Bureau of Industry and Security, has historically governed the export of physical technologies with military or strategic applications. The application of these controls to AI software — specifically to API access for large language models — is a relatively new and significant policy development, reflecting US concerns about rivals accessing frontier AI capabilities. Anthropic's Claude models are among the most capable in the world, competing directly with OpenAI's GPT series and Google's Gemini. India is one of the largest markets for AI API usage globally, with thousands of startups and enterprises building products on top of US AI platforms.

Why it matters

The extension of export controls to AI models sets a precedent that could see other US AI companies — including OpenAI and Google — similarly restrict access to their most powerful tools for Indian and other non-US users. Indian businesses that have built products, workflows, and services on these APIs face immediate disruption and the longer-term risk of repeated access uncertainty. It signals that access to frontier AI is no longer a purely commercial question but a geopolitical one, with serious implications for India's digital economy.

What's next

Expect close scrutiny of whether other US AI companies receive similar directives covering their frontier models. Indian policymakers and trade negotiators may raise the issue in bilateral tech discussions with Washington. Watch for Indian AI companies and research institutions to accelerate work on domestic alternatives.
