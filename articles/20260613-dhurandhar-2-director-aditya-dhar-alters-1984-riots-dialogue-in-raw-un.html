Dhurandhar 2 Director Aditya Dhar Alters 1984 Riots Dialogue in Raw, Uncut Version

What happened

The Indian Express reports that director Aditya Dhar has altered a dialogue related to the 1984 anti-Sikh riots in the Hamza-Pinda scene of Dhurandhar 2's raw, uncut version. The report highlights that the change was made to the scene in what is described as the 'raw, uncut' cut of the film, suggesting the alteration may differ from the theatrically released version. The specific nature of the dialogue change and the reasons stated by the director or producers are not fully detailed beyond the headline. The move signals the sensitivity surrounding cinematic depictions of the 1984 violence.

The context

The 1984 anti-Sikh riots, which followed the assassination of Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, remain one of the most sensitive and contested episodes in modern Indian history. Bollywood films that reference these events have historically faced pressure from political groups, community organisations, and certification bodies. Aditya Dhar is best known for directing Uri: The Surgical Strike, one of Bollywood's biggest hits. Dhurandhar 2 is a high-profile action sequel that has attracted significant public and critical attention since its release.

Why it matters

The alteration of historical dialogue in a mainstream Bollywood film underscores the ongoing tension between creative freedom and political sensitivity in Indian cinema. It raises questions about self-censorship and the pressures directors face when depicting real historical events. For audiences, such changes affect the authenticity and completeness of the stories they see on screen.

What's next

Expect continued debate among film critics, historians, and community groups about the responsibilities of filmmakers when portraying the 1984 riots. The uncut version's reception will likely be closely watched to see whether the altered dialogue becomes a wider controversy.
