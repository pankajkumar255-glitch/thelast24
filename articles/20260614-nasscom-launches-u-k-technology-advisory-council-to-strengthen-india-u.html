Nasscom Launches U.K. Technology Advisory Council to Strengthen India–U.K. Digital Partnership

What happened

The Hindu reports that Nasscom has formally launched a U.K. Technology Advisory Council designed to strengthen the India–U.K. digital partnership. The council represents a structured, high-level forum for technology cooperation between the two nations. Nasscom, which represents India's $250-billion IT and business process management industry, framed the launch as a strategic step to deepen bilateral digital engagement. The move aligns with broader diplomatic and trade discussions between New Delhi and London.

The context

India and the U.K. share one of the world's most significant technology partnerships, with Indian IT firms like Infosys, TCS, Wipro, and HCL among the largest employers in the U.K. tech sector. Nasscom has historically played a key role in shaping trade and policy frameworks for India's IT industry globally. The two countries have been negotiating a Free Trade Agreement for several years, with digital services and data flows among the key sticking points. The U.K.'s post-Brexit strategy has emphasised deepening ties with major technology and services economies like India.

Why it matters

A stronger India–U.K. digital partnership could unlock new business for Indian IT and services companies, directly benefiting millions of employees in the sector. It could also ease regulatory and data-sharing barriers that currently complicate cross-border technology projects. For Indian startups and tech entrepreneurs, a formal advisory council provides a new channel to access the U.K. market and investment. The initiative also adds momentum to the stalled India–U.K. FTA negotiations.

What's next

Expect the advisory council to hold its first formal meetings and publish an agenda around digital trade, talent mobility, and AI cooperation in the coming months. Progress here could give a fresh impetus to the broader India–U.K. Free Trade Agreement talks.
