Siya Goyal and Chetan Chaudhary Turn on Each Other as Pune Fort Murder Case Tightens

Key facts

  • Accused Siya Goyal and Chetan Chaudhary blame each other in custody
  • Police say Chetan persuaded Siya to murder fiancé Ketan Agarwal
  • Victim's father meets CM Fadnavis, seeks fast-track trial
  • 26/11 prosecutor Ujjwal Nikam engaged to fight victim's case
  • Blackmail angle formally under investigation

Siya Goyal and Chetan Chaudhary, the two accused in the murder of Ketan Agarwal at a Pune fort, have turned on each other in custody, with each seeking to shift the blame as police tighten the noose around them. Investigators say Chetan Chaudhary persuaded Siya to kill her fiancé Ketan, and a blackmail angle — whether Chetan coerced Siya into the act — is now formally under probe. A Ram Mandir donation scam accused was also sent to judicial custody in a related development, underscoring the tangled web of individuals involved.

The victim's father has revealed new details about Ketan, saying his son wore a wig patch and that the accused Siya was aware of it — a detail he raised amid swirling theories about the motive. He told reporters that Ketan had told Siya he would not be able to escape the marriage, a statement the accused herself reportedly relayed to police. Siya's own parents, in a dramatic statement, declared she should be pushed from the same fort if found guilty.

Ketan's father met Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis and sought a fast-track trial; Fadnavis assured justice to the victim's family and agreed to the demand. The family has also engaged Ujjwal Nikam — the famed public prosecutor who handled the 26/11 Mumbai attacks case — to fight Ketan's case, signalling the family's resolve to seek the strongest possible legal representation.

As Siya and Chetan continue to implicate each other, investigators are working to establish who masterminded the killing. The case has gripped Maharashtra and drawn national attention, with MP Kangana Ranaut weighing in to say neither family should be judged collectively. The fast-track trial, if granted, would mark one of the swifter judicial responses to a high-profile murder in recent memory.
