Jayshree Ullal, Indian-origin CEO of Arista Networks, named among US's richest self-made women

What happened

NDTV reports that Jayshree Ullal, an Indian-origin CEO, has been featured on the United States' richest self-made women list. Ullal is the chief executive of Arista Networks, a California-based company that makes cloud networking hardware and software used by some of the world's largest data centres. Her inclusion on the list recognises both her personal wealth and her role in building Arista into a company that competes with networking giants. The recognition adds to a growing list of Indian-origin executives who have reached the pinnacle of American business.

The context

Jayshree Ullal was born in London to Indian parents and grew up partly in India before emigrating to the United States. She joined Arista Networks as CEO in 2008 and has been credited with transforming it from a startup into a publicly listed company with a market capitalisation of over $100 billion at its peak. Arista's networking technology powers the infrastructure of major cloud providers including Microsoft and Meta. Ullal joins a distinguished group of Indian-origin leaders in US tech, including Sundar Pichai of Google and Satya Nadella of Microsoft, though she stands out as one of the very few women in that cohort.

Why it matters

Ullal's achievement is a source of national pride and also a data point in the broader story of how Indian-origin professionals have come to dominate leadership roles in American technology companies. For young Indians aspiring to careers in technology and business, her story is a tangible example of what is possible. It also underscores India's significant soft power in the global economy through its diaspora.

What's next

Watch for Arista Networks' continued growth as demand for cloud networking infrastructure surges with the AI boom, which is likely to further increase Ullal's profile and wealth. Expect her to remain a prominent voice on technology leadership and enterprise networking.
