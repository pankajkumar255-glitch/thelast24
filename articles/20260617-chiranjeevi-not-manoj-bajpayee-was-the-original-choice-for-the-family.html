Chiranjeevi, not Manoj Bajpayee, was the original choice for The Family Man, reveals director Raj Nidimoru

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Raj Nidimoru, co-director of The Family Man, disclosed that Chiranjeevi was the original choice for the lead role before Manoj Bajpayee was brought on board. The statement offers a striking alternate history for one of India's most celebrated OTT productions.

The context

The Family Man, created by Raj and DK (Raj Nidimoru and Krishna DK) for Amazon Prime Video, debuted in 2019 and ran for two critically acclaimed seasons. Manoj Bajpayee's performance as middle-class intelligence officer Srikant Tiwari won him multiple awards and became a benchmark for Indian streaming content.

Why it matters

The revelation underscores how casting choices can define the identity of a show — Bajpayee's everyman appeal was central to The Family Man's massive success. A Chiranjeevi-led version would have had a fundamentally different tone and audience reach.

What's next

Expect renewed fan speculation about a third season of The Family Man, with the casting revelation likely reigniting interest in the show's production history.
