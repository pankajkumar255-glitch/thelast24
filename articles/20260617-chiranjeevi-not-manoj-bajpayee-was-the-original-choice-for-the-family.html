Chiranjeevi, not Manoj Bajpayee, was the original choice for The Family Man, reveals director Raj Nidimoru

Key facts

  • Chiranjeevi was original choice for The Family Man lead
  • Role went to Manoj Bajpayee instead
  • Revealed by director Raj Nidimoru
  • The Family Man streams on Amazon Prime Video

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Raj Nidimoru, one half of the directing duo Raj & DK, revealed in a statement that Chiranjeevi was the original choice for the lead role in The Family Man. The role eventually went to Manoj Bajpayee, whose performance became the defining element of the critically acclaimed Amazon Prime Video series.

The context

The Family Man, created by Raj & DK (Raj Nidimoru and Krishna D.K.), premiered on Amazon Prime Video in 2019 and ran for two seasons, with a third season long anticipated. Manoj Bajpayee's portrayal of an overworked intelligence officer balancing domestic life and covert missions became a benchmark for Indian OTT drama. Chiranjeevi is one of Telugu cinema's biggest icons, and his casting would have likely positioned the show very differently.

Why it matters

The revelation underscores how the Hindi-language OTT boom of the late 2010s might have looked entirely different, and it adds context to ongoing conversations about cross-language casting in Indian streaming originals.

What's next

Expect the disclosure to fuel renewed fan speculation about The Family Man Season 3's casting and direction, which Raj & DK are expected to announce.
