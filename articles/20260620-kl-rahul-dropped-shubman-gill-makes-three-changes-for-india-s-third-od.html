KL Rahul dropped, Shubman Gill makes three changes for India's third ODI vs Afghanistan

Key facts

  • Shubman Gill makes three changes to India's XI for 3rd ODI vs Afghanistan
  • KL Rahul is not playing in the third ODI
  • Third ODI is a dead rubber with series outcome already decided
  • Gill captaining India in the absence of Rohit Sharma

The Times of India reports that India captain Shubman Gill has named a changed playing XI for the third ODI against Afghanistan, making three alterations from the previous game. Most notably, KL Rahul has been left out of the lineup, raising questions about the senior wicketkeeper-batter's role in India's white-ball setup going forward.

The third ODI is a dead rubber with the series outcome already settled, giving team management room to try different combinations. Gill, leading the side in the absence of regular captain Rohit Sharma, appears to be using the opportunity to look at fringe players and assess options for the future.

For Indian fans, Rahul's exclusion is the headline moment — the Karnataka batter has had a complicated relationship with consistent selection across formats, and being rested or dropped from a low-stakes game could either be rotation management or a broader signal about his ODI standing. The identity of the three changes will be closely watched by selectors ahead of upcoming series.
