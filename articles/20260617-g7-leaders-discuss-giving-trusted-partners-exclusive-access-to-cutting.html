G7 leaders discuss giving 'trusted partners' exclusive access to cutting-edge US AI models

Key facts

  • G7 leaders discussing restricting top US AI models to 'trusted partners'
  • Talks held at G7 summit, reported by Reuters citing sources
  • Framework would create a tiered global AI access system
  • India, not a G7 member, could be affected depending on its 'trusted' status

Reuters reports that G7 leaders are actively discussing a framework to limit access to cutting-edge US AI models to a circle of 'trusted partners', based on sources familiar with the talks at the G7 summit. The proposal would give allied nations preferential access to the most powerful frontier AI systems developed by American companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google.

The discussions reflect a broader US strategy of treating advanced AI as a strategic asset — similar to semiconductor export controls — rather than a freely available commercial technology. The G7 forum, which includes the US, UK, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, and Japan, is seen as a key arena for shaping the rules of the global AI order.

For India, which is not a G7 member but has deepening technology ties with the US, the framework raises significant questions. Whether India qualifies as a 'trusted partner' and on what terms it gains access to top-tier AI models could have direct implications for Indian enterprises, startups, and government AI programmes that increasingly depend on US-built foundation models.
