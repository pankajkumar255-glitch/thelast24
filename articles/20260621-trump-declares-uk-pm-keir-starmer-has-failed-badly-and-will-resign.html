Trump Declares UK PM Keir Starmer Has "Failed Badly" and Will Resign

Key facts

  • Trump said Starmer "failed badly" and will resign
  • Starmer became UK PM after Labour's July 2024 landslide
  • UK-US trade deal talks are currently ongoing
  • Trump's attack is rare public assault on a close ally's leader

Donald Trump has declared that British Prime Minister Keir Starmer has "failed badly" and will resign, delivering a blunt public verdict on one of America's closest allies in terms that are virtually unprecedented between friendly heads of state. The comments, made by the US President, add a jarring new dimension to the already complicated relationship between Washington and London under both Trump's and Starmer's watch.

Starmer, who led Labour to a commanding parliamentary majority in July 2024, has faced significant domestic pressure over his government's handling of the economy, welfare reform, and public services. Trump's assessment, however unsolicited, will hand Starmer's Conservative opposition fresh ammunition and risks further destabilising a government already navigating a difficult first year in office.

The timing is particularly pointed: Britain and the United States are in active negotiations over a bilateral trade deal, with London hoping to secure relief from Trump's sweeping tariff regime. A public broadside from Trump against the sitting Prime Minister complicates those talks considerably, raising questions about whether the two governments can maintain the working relationship such negotiations require.

Trump has previously shown a pattern of sharply criticising foreign leaders he views as ideologically misaligned — Starmer, a centre-left politician, falls well outside the orbit of the nationalist right that Trump champions. Whether the remarks translate into concrete diplomatic friction, or are simply treated as characteristic Trumpian noise by the British government, will become clear in the days ahead.
