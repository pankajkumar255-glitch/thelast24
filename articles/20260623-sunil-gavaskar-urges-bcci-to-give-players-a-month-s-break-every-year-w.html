Sunil Gavaskar urges BCCI to give players a month's break every year, warns India cap is being 'cheapened'

Key facts

  • Gavaskar calls for a mandatory one-month break for India players every year
  • Says India-Afghanistan series 'failed to grab eyeballs'
  • Warns BCCI that heavy scheduling is 'cheapening the India cap'

Sunil Gavaskar, India's batting legend and one of the most respected voices in world cricket, has called on the Board of Control for Cricket in India to build a mandatory one-month break into every player's annual schedule. Gavaskar argued that the non-stop fixture calendar is actively 'cheapening the India cap' — diminishing the honour and excitement that comes with wearing the national colours.

His comments, carried by both News18 and Hindustan Times, were prompted in part by the recently concluded India-Afghanistan series, which he said failed to generate meaningful public interest. For Gavaskar, that audience apathy is a direct consequence of oversaturation: when India play too often, the novelty and gravitas of each contest inevitably diminishes.

The criticism is pointed at a time when Indian cricket's schedule is among the most packed in world cricket, driven by the BCCI's commercial commitments and the IPL ecosystem. Player workload has been a persistent talking point, with several senior cricketers managing injuries or rotating out of series — but Gavaskar's framing of the issue as one of prestige, not just fitness, adds a new dimension to the debate.

The BCCI has not yet responded publicly to Gavaskar's remarks. But with the India-England T20 series and the upcoming Ireland assignment on the horizon, the question of how much cricket is too much is unlikely to fade from the conversation anytime soon.
