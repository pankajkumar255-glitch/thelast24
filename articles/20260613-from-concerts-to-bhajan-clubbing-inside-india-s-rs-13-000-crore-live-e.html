From Concerts to Bhajan Clubbing: Inside India's Rs 13,000-Crore Live Entertainment Boom

What happened

NDTV reports that India's live entertainment industry is in the midst of a major boom, valued at Rs 13,000 crore, driven by a surge in concerts, festivals, and new hybrid formats such as bhajan clubbing that blend devotional music with clubbing-style production. The report highlights that demand spans a wide range of consumer preferences and age groups, reflecting broader economic prosperity and changing lifestyle patterns. The sector has recovered strongly from the near-total shutdown during the COVID-19 pandemic and is now growing rapidly. Event management companies, ticketing platforms, and hospitality businesses are key beneficiaries of this expansion.

The context

India's live entertainment sector was virtually wiped out during the COVID-19 pandemic of 2020-21, when all public gatherings were banned for extended periods. The recovery since 2022 has been dramatic, fuelled by what industry analysts call 'experience economy' spending — a global trend where consumers prioritise experiences over physical goods. International artists including Cold Play, Ed Sheeran, and Dua Lipa have performed in India in recent years to sold-out crowds, signalling India's arrival as a viable destination for premium live events. Domestically, the rise of Bollywood concert tours, classical music festivals, and now spiritually-themed entertainment formats has broadened the market considerably.

Why it matters

For everyday Indians, the live entertainment boom translates into higher ticket prices as demand outstrips venue capacity, making quality events increasingly expensive and exclusionary for middle-income audiences. For investors, the sector presents significant opportunities in event infrastructure, ticketing technology, and artist management. The emergence of formats like bhajan clubbing also reflects how Indian entrepreneurs are innovating at the intersection of culture and commerce, creating new consumption categories that are uniquely Indian in character.

What's next

Expect continued investment in large-format venues and ticketing infrastructure to meet growing demand. Watch for whether the government introduces formal policy support — such as entertainment zones or licensing reforms — to help the sector scale. The bhajan clubbing trend may attract both mainstream and devotional music artists seeking new revenue streams.
