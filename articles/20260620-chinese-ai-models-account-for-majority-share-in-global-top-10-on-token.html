Chinese AI Models Account For Majority Share In Global Top 10 On Token Usage

Key facts

  • Chinese AI models hold majority share in global top-10 by token usage
  • Token usage measures real-world adoption, not just benchmark scores
  • Key Chinese models include DeepSeek, Alibaba Qwen, and Baidu ERNIE
  • DeepSeek drew global attention by matching frontier AI at lower cost

NDTV Profit reports that Chinese AI models now command a majority share among the world's top-10 large language models when ranked by token usage — a metric that measures the actual volume of text processed, making it one of the most direct indicators of real-world adoption rather than laboratory performance. The finding underscores how rapidly China's AI ecosystem has scaled beyond benchmark competition into mainstream deployment.

While American models from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have dominated AI headlines and investment cycles, Chinese developers — including companies such as DeepSeek, Alibaba's Qwen, and Baidu's ERNIE — have rapidly expanded their user bases globally, partly driven by competitive pricing and open-source releases. DeepSeek in particular attracted global attention earlier this year when its models matched frontier performance at a fraction of the training cost.

For India, which is building domestic AI infrastructure while also relying heavily on foreign foundation models for government and enterprise applications, the rise of Chinese models in global usage charts adds a geopolitical dimension to technology procurement decisions. Indian policymakers and developers will need to weigh capability and cost against data-security concerns as they decide which AI platforms to integrate into critical services.
