Chinese AI Models Account For Majority Share In Global Top 10 On Token Usage

Key facts

  • Chinese AI models hold majority share of global top 10 by token usage
  • DeepSeek, Baidu, and Alibaba among key Chinese AI model developers
  • Rise occurred despite US export restrictions on advanced chips to China
  • Token usage measures real-world adoption of AI models globally

NDTV Profit reports that Chinese AI models now hold a majority share of the global top 10 rankings when measured by token usage, the standard metric for how extensively an AI model is actually being used worldwide. The finding represents a sharp shift from even a year ago, when American models from OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic dominated every major usage benchmark.

The rise of Chinese models — developed by companies such as DeepSeek, Baidu, and Alibaba — has been notable partly because it occurred despite sustained US export controls on high-end semiconductors to China. DeepSeek's open-weight models in particular attracted global attention earlier this year for achieving competitive performance at significantly lower computational cost, challenging the assumption that frontier AI requires the most advanced Nvidia chips.

For India, the implications are strategic as well as practical. Indian developers, researchers, and companies choosing which AI models to build on are now operating in a genuinely multipolar AI landscape. The question of which country's AI infrastructure India integrates into its economy carries long-term consequences for data sovereignty, technology dependency, and geopolitical alignment — making this global usage shift directly relevant to Indian policymakers and the tech industry alike.
