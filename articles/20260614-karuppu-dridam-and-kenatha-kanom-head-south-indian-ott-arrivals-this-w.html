Karuppu, Dridam and Kenatha Kanom Head South Indian OTT Arrivals This Week

What happened

The Times of India and NDTV Profit report that multiple South Indian films including Karuppu, Dridam, Kenatha Kanom, and Vikalpa are releasing on OTT platforms this weekend across Malayalam, Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada languages.

The context

South Indian cinema has seen an extraordinary surge in OTT viewership since the post-pandemic streaming boom, with films in Malayalam and Tamil in particular drawing pan-India audiences who watch with subtitles. Weekly regional OTT drops have become a significant driver of engagement for major streaming platforms operating in India.

Why it matters

The steady stream of South Indian content on OTT keeps Indian regional cinema visible and commercially viable beyond home states, boosting both the platforms and the film industries. It also deepens cross-cultural appreciation of regional storytelling among Indian audiences.

What's next

Viewership data from this weekend's South Indian releases will likely inform how aggressively platforms commission and acquire regional content in the months ahead.
