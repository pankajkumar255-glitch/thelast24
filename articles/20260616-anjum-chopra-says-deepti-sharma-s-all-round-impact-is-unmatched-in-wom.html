Anjum Chopra says Deepti Sharma's all-round impact is 'unmatched' in women's cricket

What happened

Rediff reports that former India women's captain Anjum Chopra has praised all-rounder Deepti Sharma, stating her impact on the Indian women's team is unmatched. Chopra's comments highlight Deepti's dual value as both a frontline spinner and a capable batter in the middle order.

The context

Deepti Sharma has been a regular fixture in India's white-ball women's cricket setup for several years, contributing crucial runs and wickets in high-pressure matches. Anjum Chopra, as a former captain and respected analyst, is one of the most credible voices in Indian women's cricket commentary. Deepti's profile has risen sharply since the Women's Premier League brought greater visibility to Indian women cricketers.

Why it matters

Recognition from a former captain reinforces Deepti Sharma's value in the squad and could influence how selectors and the public view her role going into major tournaments. It also reflects the broader rise of Indian women's cricket as a sport that now generates serious analytical attention.

What's next

Expect Deepti Sharma to remain a key selection pick and a closely watched performer in India's upcoming women's cricket fixtures.
