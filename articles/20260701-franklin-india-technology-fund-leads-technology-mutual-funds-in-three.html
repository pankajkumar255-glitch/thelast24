Franklin India Technology Fund leads technology mutual funds in three-year CAGR returns

Key facts

  • Franklin India Technology Fund leads technology sectoral mutual funds in 3-year CAGR
  • Rankings based on compounded annual growth rate over three years
  • Data reported by Moneycontrol

Franklin India Technology Fund has topped the rankings among technology-focused sectoral mutual funds in India on the basis of three-year CAGR returns, according to data reported by Moneycontrol. The fund's performance stands out in a category that has attracted growing interest from retail investors seeking to ride India's digital economy boom.

Technology sectoral funds have been among the more volatile categories in the mutual fund universe, given their concentration in IT and technology stocks that are sensitive to global interest rate cycles, currency movements, and shifts in discretionary spending by multinational clients. Franklin India Technology Fund's leadership in three-year CAGR returns suggests it has navigated that volatility more effectively than peers over the measured period.

The fund's outperformance is likely to attract fresh inflows, as rankings and past-performance data remain powerful drivers of retail investor behaviour in India despite regulator warnings that past returns do not guarantee future performance. For Franklin Templeton India, which faced significant challenges following its debt fund wind-up episode in 2020, strong equity fund performance is strategically important for rebuilding its brand.

The broader context is positive for the category: India's IT sector continues to generate strong free cash flows, and domestic technology companies — from software exporters to new-age internet platforms — have seen renewed investor interest. Sectoral fund rankings like these serve as a key point of reference for distributors and financial advisors guiding clients toward thematic allocations.
