BCCI evaluating bilateral series feasibility; IPL 2026 likely from early March

What happened

India Today reports that the BCCI is evaluating the feasibility of fitting bilateral international series into the cricket calendar. The board is also planning to start IPL 2026 from early March, an earlier window than the April start seen in recent editions. The dual planning exercise reflects the growing complexity of managing India's international and franchise cricket obligations simultaneously. No final decisions have been announced by the BCCI as of the report.

The context

The IPL has grown into the world's most valuable cricket league, and its scheduling has increasingly influenced the global cricket calendar. The BCCI's dominant position in world cricket gives it significant leverage in arranging bilateral series and negotiating broadcast windows. Bilateral series between India and top nations are highly lucrative for host boards and broadcasters alike. Moving IPL earlier risks reducing recovery time for players coming off international assignments and could affect the quality of competition.

Why it matters

An earlier IPL window affects fans' viewing schedules and potentially the availability of international stars, who may still be on national duty in March. Broadcasters and advertisers will need to recalibrate campaigns around the new window. For Indian cricket as a whole, how the BCCI balances bilateral commitments with franchise cricket will shape the team's preparation for the 2027 World Cup.

What's next

Expect the BCCI to make formal announcements about the IPL 2026 schedule in the coming weeks as franchise planning requires early confirmation. The feasibility review of bilateral series will likely result in either curtailed tours or a renegotiated Future Tours Programme with other boards. Player unions and franchise owners will be watching closely for the final calendar.
