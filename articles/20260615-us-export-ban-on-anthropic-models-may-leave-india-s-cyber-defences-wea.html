US export ban on Anthropic models may leave India's cyber defences weaker, experts warn

What happened

Indiatimes reports that experts have raised alarms that the US export restrictions on Anthropic's AI models may weaken India's cyber defence, as these models are embedded in security platforms used to detect and respond to threats. The report cites expert opinion linking access to advanced AI to the effectiveness of modern cybersecurity operations. The concern comes alongside broader industry anxiety about India's dependence on US AI platforms.

The context

Modern cybersecurity increasingly relies on large language models for tasks such as log analysis, malware detection, phishing identification, and incident response. Anthropic's Claude models are integrated into several commercial security products used by Indian enterprises and government-adjacent bodies. India faces a growing volume of sophisticated cyber attacks, including on critical infrastructure, financial institutions, and government networks.

Why it matters

Restricted access to cutting-edge AI tools used in cyber defence could slow India's ability to detect and respond to sophisticated attacks, potentially increasing vulnerability in sectors like banking, power, and telecoms. It strengthens the argument for investing in domestically developed AI security tools rather than relying entirely on foreign platforms.

What's next

Expect India's CERT-In and cybersecurity agencies to review their AI tool dependencies and accelerate procurement of alternative platforms or domestic AI-driven security solutions.
