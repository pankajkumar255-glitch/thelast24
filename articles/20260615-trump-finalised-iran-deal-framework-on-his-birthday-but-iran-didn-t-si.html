Trump finalised Iran deal framework on his birthday, but Iran didn't sign — 7.5 hours apart

What happened

NDTV reports that President Trump moved to finalise an Iran nuclear deal on his birthday but Iran did not complete the agreement on the same timeline, with the two sides reportedly 7.5 hours apart. The account suggests the deal remains incomplete despite apparent momentum from the US side.

The context

The US and Iran have been engaged in indirect negotiations mediated through intermediaries, seeking a new framework to replace the collapsed 2015 JCPOA. Trump's first-term withdrawal from that deal and re-imposition of sanctions severely damaged trust, making current talks fragile. Iran's domestic politics, including hardliner opposition to any deal perceived as capitulation, adds another layer of complexity to negotiations.

Why it matters

An unresolved US-Iran nuclear standoff keeps sanctions on Iranian oil exports in place, which affects global crude supply and India's oil import strategy. India has historically been a significant buyer of Iranian oil and would benefit from sanctions relief, but uncertainty delays any such planning.

What's next

Expect further rounds of mediated talks as both sides attempt to close the remaining gaps, with any breakthrough or breakdown likely to move global oil markets immediately.
