Trump threatens 100% tariff on French wine over digital tax

What happened

Business Standard reports that Trump has threatened to slap a 100% tariff on French wine in direct response to France's digital tax, which the US views as discriminatory against American tech firms. The move signals that Trump's aggressive tariff posture — already applied to multiple trading partners — is now being directed squarely at Europe.

The context

France introduced a digital services tax targeting large technology platforms, most of which are American companies such as Google, Apple, Facebook, and Amazon. The US has long objected to such levies, arguing they amount to discrimination. Trump has repeatedly used tariff threats as leverage in trade negotiations.

Why it matters

A full-blown US-EU trade war would disrupt global supply chains and dampen economic growth, potentially hurting Indian export sectors that supply components or finished goods to both markets. It also signals a more volatile global trade environment that Indian businesses must navigate.

What's next

Expect France and the EU to consider retaliatory measures, and watch whether this becomes part of broader US-EU trade negotiations in the coming weeks.
