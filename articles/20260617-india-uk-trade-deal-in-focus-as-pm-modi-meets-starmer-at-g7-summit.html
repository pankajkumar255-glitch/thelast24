India-UK Trade Deal in Focus as PM Modi Meets Starmer at G7 Summit

What happened

NDTV reports that PM Narendra Modi met UK PM Keir Starmer at the G7 Summit, with the India-UK Free Trade Agreement dominating their bilateral agenda. The report highlights that the deal has taken centre stage, suggesting the two leaders may be close to a political agreement that has been years in the making.

The context

India and the UK launched FTA negotiations in January 2022 under Boris Johnson, but talks stalled repeatedly over issues including tariffs on Scotch whisky, Indian visas for professionals, and intellectual property rules. Starmer's Labour government restarted negotiations with fresh urgency, and both sides have described 2025 as a critical window for concluding the agreement.

Why it matters

A concluded India-UK FTA would open a major western market to Indian goods and IT services, potentially boosting exports and creating jobs, while also easing pathways for Indian students and professionals to work in the UK. For domestic consumers, it could eventually lower prices on British goods from whisky to luxury cars.

What's next

Expect a formal announcement on the FTA timeline or a framework agreement in the coming weeks, given the high-level political signal sent by the Modi-Starmer meeting.
