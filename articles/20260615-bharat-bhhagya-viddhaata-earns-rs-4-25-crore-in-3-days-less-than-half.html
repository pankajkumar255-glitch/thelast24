Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata earns Rs 4.25 crore in 3 days — less than half of Kangana's Emergency

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata, starring Kangana Ranaut, earned Rs 4.25 crore in its first three days of release — a figure described as less than half of what her previous film Emergency had collected over the same period. The report highlights the stark underperformance relative to expectations.

The context

Kangana Ranaut has been one of Bollywood's most talked-about figures, but her recent films have struggled commercially. Emergency, which she also directed and starred in, faced a delayed release due to certification issues and had a weak box-office run. Bharat Bhhagya Viddhaata was positioned as a patriotic drama, a genre that has had mixed results at the box office in recent years.

Why it matters

Consecutive flops from a major star affect not just her career but the confidence of producers and distributors who fund big-budget Bollywood films. It also reflects the increasingly discerning tastes of Indian multiplex audiences.

What's next

Expect the film to see a sharp drop in screen count in the coming days, with its theatrical run likely to be brief before an OTT release.
