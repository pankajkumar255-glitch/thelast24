Alibaba Unveils AI Models Built Specifically for Robots

What happened

YourStory reports that Alibaba has unveiled AI models specifically developed for robotic applications, expanding its AI portfolio beyond conventional language and image models. The new models are designed to enable robots to understand and act on real-world tasks with greater intelligence. The announcement was made by Alibaba, one of China's largest technology conglomerates.

The context

Alibaba has been aggressively expanding its AI research and product portfolio, competing with Chinese rivals like Baidu and DeepSeek as well as US companies. The global robotics AI market is seeing intense activity, with companies like Figure AI, Boston Dynamics, and Tesla also investing heavily in AI-powered robots. Robot-specific AI models are seen as the next frontier after large language models.

Why it matters

For India, where manufacturing and logistics automation is accelerating, the availability of powerful robot-specific AI models from global players like Alibaba could influence which foreign technology platforms Indian factories and warehouses adopt — and at what pace jobs in those sectors are automated.

What's next

Expect other major AI players, including Google DeepMind and OpenAI, to announce competing robotics-focused AI models as the sector heats up through 2025.
