Amazon AI chief sees path to catch up with OpenAI and Anthropic in 'coming year'

Key facts

  • Amazon AI chief admits company has lagged OpenAI and Anthropic
  • Sees path to catch up 'in the coming year'
  • Amazon has invested heavily in Anthropic and runs AWS cloud platform
  • No Amazon-built frontier AI model currently rivals GPT-4 or Claude

CNBC reports that Amazon's AI chief has openly acknowledged the company has lagged behind OpenAI and Anthropic in the generative AI race, but sees a concrete path to catching up in the coming year. The admission is striking because Amazon has invested heavily in Anthropic and operates AWS, the world's dominant cloud platform, yet has been widely seen as a follower on frontier AI model development.

The statement signals that Amazon is accelerating its own internal AI capabilities rather than depending entirely on its Anthropic partnership. Amazon has been investing in its Bedrock platform and custom AI chips, but has not produced a frontier large language model that rivals GPT-4 or Claude in public benchmarks. The AI chief's confidence in a one-year timeline suggests significant internal development is underway.

For Indian businesses and developers, this matters because AWS underpins a huge share of India's cloud infrastructure. If Amazon successfully closes the AI gap, Indian startups and enterprises that already run on AWS could gain direct access to competitive frontier AI models without switching platforms — potentially reshaping the AI tools market in India over the next twelve months.
