Rohit Sharma becomes oldest Indian man to play ODIs, leads side against Afghanistan in Dharamsala

What happened

India Today reports that Rohit Sharma has set a historic record by becoming the oldest man ever to represent India in ODI cricket, achieving the milestone in the first ODI against Afghanistan in Dharamsala. The Times of India notes that rain threatened proceedings at the Dharamsala venue, raising questions about the cut-off time for the match to produce a valid result. The Indian Express also covered the playing XI, specifically asking who would replace Virat Kohli — who is absent from the squad — in the lineup. Business Standard highlighted the match's wide viewership interest, detailing live streaming options for fans across India.

The context

Rohit Sharma has been one of India's most decorated ODI cricketers, holding the record for the most centuries in ODI World Cup history and serving as captain across formats. The India vs Afghanistan ODI series marks a return to bilateral ODI cricket for India after a period dominated by Test and T20 commitments. Virat Kohli's absence from the squad has been a significant talking point, with selectors under scrutiny over his omission or rested status. Dharamsala's picturesque but weather-prone venue has historically seen matches disrupted by rain and cloudy conditions.

Why it matters

Rohit Sharma's record underscores the remarkable physical and mental demands of sustaining an international cricket career deep into one's late thirties. For fans, the India-Afghanistan ODI series is a chance to see the next wave of Indian cricketers stake their claim alongside established names. The absence of Virat Kohli makes the batting lineup composition a subject of intense debate and speculation among cricket followers nationwide.

What's next

Expect the remaining ODIs in the India-Afghanistan series to be closely watched for emerging players who could cement their places ahead of future ICC tournaments. Rohit Sharma's fitness and form will continue to be scrutinised as selectors balance experience with youth in the ODI setup.
