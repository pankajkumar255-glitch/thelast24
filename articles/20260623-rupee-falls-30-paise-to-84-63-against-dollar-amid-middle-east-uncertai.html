Rupee falls 30 paise to 84.63 against dollar amid Middle East uncertainty

Key facts

  • Rupee fell 30 paise to 84.63 against the US dollar
  • Decline driven by Middle East geopolitical tensions
  • India imports over 85% of its crude oil needs
  • Weaker rupee raises risk of higher fuel and import costs

The Indian rupee fell 30 paise to 84.63 against the US dollar on Monday, its sharpest single-day drop in recent weeks, as escalating tensions in the Middle East rattled global markets and drove investors toward safe-haven assets like the dollar.

The sell-off in the rupee came alongside a rise in crude oil prices, compounding concerns for India's import-heavy economy. India imports the vast majority of its crude oil requirements, meaning any sustained spike in oil prices translates directly into a wider current account deficit and further downward pressure on the currency. Currency traders noted that uncertainty about the trajectory of the Middle East conflict was the primary driver of the day's volatility.

The Reserve Bank of India has in recent months intervened periodically in forex markets to prevent excessive volatility, though the central bank generally allows the rupee to find its level within a managed range. Monday's move will keep the RBI's forex desk on alert, particularly if geopolitical tensions persist into the coming week.

For ordinary Indians, a weaker rupee raises the spectre of higher fuel prices and costlier imports ranging from electronics to edible oils. It also increases the burden on companies with dollar-denominated debt. Analysts will be watching whether the Middle East situation stabilises or deteriorates further, as that will largely determine the rupee's near-term trajectory.
