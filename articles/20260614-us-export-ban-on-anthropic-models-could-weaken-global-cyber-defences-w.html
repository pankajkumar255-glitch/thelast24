US export ban on Anthropic models could weaken global cyber defences, warn experts

What happened

Indiatimes reports that experts have raised alarms that a US export ban on Anthropic's AI models could weaken cyber defence capabilities for countries outside the US. The warning follows reports of the White House intervening in Anthropic's model releases and tightening controls on who can access frontier AI. Security professionals argue that threat actors will not face the same restrictions, creating an asymmetry that favours attackers.

The context

Anthropic's Claude models are widely used in enterprise security tools, threat detection, and vulnerability analysis. The US has been progressively tightening AI export controls as part of its competition with China, but critics argue these measures can hurt allies as much as adversaries. India is a major consumer of US AI platforms for both commercial and government security applications.

Why it matters

Indian government agencies, banks, and critical infrastructure operators that use Anthropic-powered security tools could face gaps in protection if access is restricted. The situation also strengthens the case for India to accelerate development of domestic AI capabilities for sensitive applications.

What's next

Expect Indian cybersecurity agencies and IT industry bodies to assess exposure to US AI export restrictions and potentially push for bilateral carve-outs in ongoing tech diplomacy talks.
