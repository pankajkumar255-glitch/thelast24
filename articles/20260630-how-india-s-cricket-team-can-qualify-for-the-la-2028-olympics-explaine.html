How India's cricket team can qualify for the LA 2028 Olympics — explained

Key facts

  • Cricket returning to Olympics at LA 2028 for first time since 1900
  • India's qualification depends on ICC rankings and regional routes
  • Olympics.com published a detailed explainer on the qualification pathway

Cricket's return to the Olympic stage at Los Angeles 2028 has opened up a new frontier for the sport, and the Indian men's team's path to qualification is now being mapped out in detail. The Games will mark cricket's first Olympic appearance in over a century, making the qualification race one of the most consequential processes in the sport's modern history.

The qualification pathway, as explained by olympics.com, involves ICC rankings and structured competitive routes that will determine which nations earn the coveted spots in the LA 2028 tournament. India, given their standing as the world's most powerful cricketing nation by both playing strength and commercial weight, are expected to be frontrunners — but the process is not automatic and will require consistent performances in the right tournaments.

The inclusion of cricket at the Olympics is a transformative moment for the sport globally, opening doors to audiences and athletes in nations where cricket has traditionally had limited reach. For Indian players, an Olympic medal would add a dimension to their legacy that no ICC title — however prestigious — can replicate.

For the BCCI and Indian fans, the LA 2028 timeline also injects fresh urgency into bilateral and ICC competitions between now and the Games, as each result could have a bearing on India's ultimate Olympic qualification. The explainer makes clear that the journey starts now.
