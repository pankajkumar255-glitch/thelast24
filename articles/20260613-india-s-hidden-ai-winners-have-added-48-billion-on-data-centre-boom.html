India's 'Hidden' AI Winners Have Added $48 Billion on Data-Centre Boom

What happened

The Economic Times reports that a group of Indian companies described as 'hidden AI winners' have together added $48 billion in value on the back of the data-centre construction and AI infrastructure boom. The story highlights that these are not the usual high-profile technology names but rather companies in adjacent sectors benefiting from surging demand for compute infrastructure. The gains reflect both domestic AI investment and India's role as a destination for global hyperscaler spending.

The context

The global AI boom, accelerated by the rise of large language models and generative AI tools, has driven unprecedented demand for data centres, power infrastructure, cooling systems, and semiconductor supply chains. In India, government initiatives like the India AI Mission and private investments from Reliance, Adani, and global tech giants are fuelling data-centre construction. Indian markets have seen a rotation into infrastructure and capital goods stocks as investors seek to play the AI theme beyond pure software companies.

Why it matters

The $48 billion wealth creation signals that AI's economic benefits in India are broader than just software startups or IT services firms. Companies in power, real estate, cable, and construction sectors are emerging as significant beneficiaries. For retail investors, this underscores the importance of looking beyond obvious tech stocks to capture the full AI investment wave.

What's next

Expect analysts and fund managers to increasingly identify and profile these second-order AI beneficiaries as the data-centre pipeline grows. Further large infrastructure deals — like the Meta-Reliance announcement — are likely to add more names to this list.
