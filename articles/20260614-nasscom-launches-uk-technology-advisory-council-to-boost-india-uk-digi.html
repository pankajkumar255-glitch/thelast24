Nasscom Launches UK Technology Advisory Council to Boost India-UK Digital Ties

What happened

The Hindu reports that Nasscom has formally launched the UK Technology Advisory Council, a new body intended to deepen the India-UK digital partnership. The council will bring together technology leaders from both countries to advise on policy, collaboration frameworks, and joint innovation. The announcement follows growing momentum in bilateral ties between India and the UK, with trade and technology identified as priority areas. Nasscom stated that the council would focus on areas including AI, digital public infrastructure, and cybersecurity.

The context

Nasscom represents India's $245 billion IT-BPM industry, which employs over five million professionals and serves clients globally, with the UK being one of its largest markets. India and the UK have been negotiating a free trade agreement, with technology services a major component of India's negotiating interest, particularly around the mobility of skilled workers. The UK has also been seeking to reposition itself as a global technology hub post-Brexit, making deeper engagement with India's tech ecosystem strategically important for both sides.

Why it matters

A formal advisory council can accelerate collaboration on high-growth areas like AI and cybersecurity, potentially generating significant new business for Indian IT companies and creating jobs domestically. It also strengthens India's position in the ongoing India-UK FTA talks by institutionalising tech ties. For Indian professionals, closer UK-India digital cooperation could ease pathways for skilled migration and cross-border project work.

What's next

Expect the council to issue early recommendations on regulatory harmonisation and talent mobility as the India-UK FTA negotiations progress. The body is likely to become a formal consultative mechanism for both governments as they finalise technology-related chapters of the trade deal.
