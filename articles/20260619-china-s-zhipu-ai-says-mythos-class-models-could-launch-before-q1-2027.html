China's Zhipu AI Says 'Mythos-Class' Models Could Launch Before Q1 2027 After GLM 5.2 Release

Key facts

  • Zhipu AI chief says Mythos-class models possible before Q1 2027
  • Statement follows release of Zhipu's GLM 5.2 model
  • Mythos-class suggests a new frontier AI capability tier

India Today reports that the chief of Zhipu AI has stated that China could launch so-called 'Mythos-class' AI models before Q1 2027, following the company's release of its GLM 5.2 model. The term 'Mythos-class' suggests a new tier of frontier AI capability that would represent a significant step beyond current-generation large language models.

Zhipu AI is one of China's leading AI research organisations, and GLM 5.2's release has been positioned as a demonstration of continued rapid progress. The chief's public timeline claim is notable because it signals confidence that Chinese labs can reach a new frontier AI threshold within roughly 18 months — a timeline that, if accurate, would intensify the global AI capability race considerably.

For India, which has its own stated AI mission and is in the early stages of building domestic AI infrastructure, the pace of advancement from both American and Chinese labs sets the competitive backdrop. Indian policymakers and technology companies will be watching whether Chinese frontier models become accessible, affordable, or restricted — each scenario carries different implications for India's AI strategy.
