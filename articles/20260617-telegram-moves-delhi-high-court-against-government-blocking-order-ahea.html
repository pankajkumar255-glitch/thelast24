Telegram Moves Delhi High Court Against Government Blocking Order Ahead of NEET UG Re-Exam

Key facts

  • Telegram challenged India's temporary blocking order in Delhi High Court
  • Hearing was scheduled for the same day as the news broke
  • Block linked to NEET UG paper leak — exam re-scheduled
  • NEET UG 2024 controversy led to CBI investigation and re-exams

What happened

India Today and NDTV Profit report that Telegram has approached the Delhi High Court to challenge a temporary blocking order issued by the Indian government. The hearing was scheduled for the day the news broke. The block was reportedly linked to government concerns that Telegram channels were being used to spread NEET UG exam leaks ahead of a scheduled re-examination.

The context

The NEET UG 2024 paper leak controversy triggered a nationwide uproar, leading to the cancellation of results for some centres and the ordering of re-examinations. The Central Bureau of Investigation has been probing the leak. Telegram, with its large private channels and minimal moderation, has repeatedly been flagged by Indian authorities as a conduit for sensitive leaked material.

Why it matters

A court-backed block on Telegram would affect tens of millions of Indian users — students, professionals and journalists — who rely on the platform daily. The outcome will also set a precedent for how quickly governments can compel courts to block major platforms on national-interest grounds.

What's next

Expect the Delhi High Court to either stay the blocking order pending fuller arguments or uphold it temporarily, with the outcome likely influencing Telegram's operational status in India through the NEET re-exam period.
