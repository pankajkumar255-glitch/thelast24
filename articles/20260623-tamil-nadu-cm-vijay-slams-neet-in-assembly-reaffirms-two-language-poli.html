Tamil Nadu CM Vijay slams NEET in Assembly, reaffirms two-language policy and opposition to BJP

Key facts

  • CM Vijay says NEET creates inequality; state to stick to two-language policy
  • Vijay declares himself ideologically opposed to BJP
  • TVK and DMK engaged in heated Assembly debate over Vijay's remarks on party funds
  • Vijay acknowledged need to work with Union government on governance

Tamil Nadu Chief Minister Vijay took a sharp political stance in the state Assembly, asserting that NEET — the national medical entrance examination — creates inequality and reaffirming that Tamil Nadu will not deviate from its two-language policy. The statements mark one of Vijay's most direct public articulations of his government's position on two of the most sensitive issues in the state's political discourse.

Vijay also declared himself ideologically opposed to the BJP, even as he acknowledged the practical necessity of engaging with the Union government on governance matters. The remarks are significant given that Vijay, who entered politics after a celebrated film career, leads the Tamil Vettri Kazhagam and came to power with a mandate partly built on anti-NEET sentiment and the preservation of Tamil linguistic identity.

His comments triggered a sharp debate within the Assembly, with the DMK engaging in a heated exchange with TVK over Vijay's remarks about party funds. The nature of that dispute — centred on what Vijay said about the financial sources or use of party money — added an unexpected internal dimension to what was otherwise a policy statement.

The NEET debate has deep roots in Tamil Nadu, where a series of student suicides linked to examination stress fuelled years of protest. The two-language policy — resisting the imposition of Hindi as a third language — is similarly a defining issue for the state. Vijay's Assembly remarks signal that on both fronts, his government intends to hold its ground.
