US trade body forms task force on India-US critical minerals cooperation

What happened

The Indian Express reports that a US trade advocacy body has established a task force specifically to advance cooperation between India and the United States on critical minerals, identifying five priority areas for engagement. The development comes as both countries are working to deepen economic and strategic ties across sectors including energy transition and technology.

The context

Critical minerals are at the heart of the global race to build supply chains for electric vehicles, clean energy, and advanced electronics, sectors where China currently dominates both mining and processing. The US has been actively courting allies including India to diversify these supply chains, and India has significant deposits of minerals such as lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements.

Why it matters

If this cooperation gains momentum, India could attract significant US investment in mineral exploration and processing, generating skilled jobs and boosting exports in a high-value sector. It also positions India strategically in the global competition over clean energy supply chains.

What's next

Expect formal engagement between Indian government bodies and the US task force to begin soon, likely feeding into the broader bilateral trade deal framework being finalised next week.
