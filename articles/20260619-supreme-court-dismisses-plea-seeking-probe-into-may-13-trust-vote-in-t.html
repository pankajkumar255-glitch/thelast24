Supreme Court dismisses plea seeking probe into May 13 trust vote in Tamil Nadu Assembly

Key facts

  • Supreme Court dismisses plea seeking probe into Tamil Nadu trust vote
  • Trust vote in question was held on May 13 in the Tamil Nadu Assembly
  • Court found no grounds to order a judicial investigation
  • Decision closes legal challenge to the floor test's conduct

Deccan Herald reports that the Supreme Court has dismissed a petition seeking a court-ordered probe into the trust vote conducted in the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly on May 13. The petitioner had sought judicial scrutiny into the conduct of the floor test, but the apex court found insufficient grounds to entertain the plea and dismissed it.

A trust vote, or floor test, is a constitutional mechanism by which a government in power demonstrates that it commands a majority in the legislature. The May 13 trust vote in Tamil Nadu had been a subject of political controversy, prompting the legal challenge that has now been turned down by the country's highest court.

The Supreme Court's dismissal brings finality to the legal dispute around the Tamil Nadu Assembly floor test. By declining to order an investigation, the court has effectively upheld the outcome of the trust vote, reinforcing the standing of the government that survived it and settling questions about the legitimacy of the legislative proceedings on that date.
