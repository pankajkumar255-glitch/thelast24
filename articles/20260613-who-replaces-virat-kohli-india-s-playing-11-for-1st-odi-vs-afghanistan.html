Who replaces Virat Kohli? India's Playing 11 for 1st ODI vs Afghanistan in Dharamsala

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Virat Kohli will not feature in the India Playing 11 for the first ODI against Afghanistan in Dharamsala, raising the question of who steps in to replace him. The publication discusses potential candidates who could fill the spot left by one of India's most prolific ODI batters. The first match of the series against Afghanistan also faces a rain threat in Dharamsala, adding uncertainty around whether a full game will be possible. The series represents an opportunity for India to test squad depth ahead of future ICC commitments.

The context

Virat Kohli is India's all-time leading ODI run-scorer and one of the greatest batters in the format's history, making his absence from any lineup a major talking point. India have been using the Afghanistan series and similar bilateral fixtures to experiment with their batting order and give fringe players opportunities. Dharamsala's HPCA Stadium is known for its scenic setting and unpredictable weather, with rain interruptions being a recurring factor. Afghanistan have grown into a competitive ODI side, making them a reasonable but manageable challenge for a second-string or rotated Indian squad.

Why it matters

Kohli's replacement will get a rare opportunity to cement an ODI spot in a relatively lower-pressure fixture — a strong performance could set up a longer run in the squad. For fans and selectors alike, the series is a live audition that could shape India's ODI plans for upcoming tournaments. The outcome also signals how India manage workload and succession for their senior stars.

What's next

Expect the chosen replacement's performance to be closely watched by selectors ahead of future ODI series. Rain's impact on the Dharamsala fixture could shorten playing time and limit how much can be assessed in the first match.
