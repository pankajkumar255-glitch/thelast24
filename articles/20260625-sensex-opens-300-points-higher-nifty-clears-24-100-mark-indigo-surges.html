Sensex opens 300 points higher, Nifty clears 24,100 mark; IndiGo surges 3% at open

Key facts

  • Sensex opened 300 points higher on June 25
  • Nifty 50 crossed above 24,100 at open
  • IndiGo shares rose 3% in early morning trade
  • Sensex eventually closed around 800 points higher

Indian stock markets opened on the front foot on Wednesday, with the BSE Sensex adding 300 points and the Nifty 50 climbing above the 24,100 mark in early trade. The positive start reflected overnight cues from global markets and a fall in crude oil prices that encouraged buying across rate-sensitive and commodity-linked sectors.

Among the standout early movers was IndiGo, which jumped 3 percent in morning trade. The aviation sector is among the most directly sensitive to crude oil prices, given that jet fuel typically accounts for a large share of airline operating costs. A sustained drop in crude prices can translate into improved margins for carriers — and potentially lower airfares for passengers down the line.

The early gains built progressively through the session, with broader market participation driving the Sensex to close around 800 points higher by the end of the day. The trajectory underscored how quickly sentiment can shift when several positive factors align — in this case, cheaper oil, supportive Asian equity trends, and cautious optimism around the India-US trade negotiations.

For retail investors who have entered the market in large numbers in recent years, sessions like these reinforce the appeal of equity investment even amid global uncertainty. The morning's early signal proved an accurate preview of the day's direction.
