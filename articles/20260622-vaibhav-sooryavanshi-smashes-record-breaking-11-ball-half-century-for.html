Vaibhav Sooryavanshi smashes record-breaking 11-ball half-century for India A against Sri Lanka A

Key facts

  • Sooryavanshi scored a half-century off just 11 balls
  • Match: India A vs Sri Lanka A
  • Innings described as record-breaking in pace of fifty
  • Sooryavanshi is a Rajasthan Royals IPL prodigy

Vaibhav Sooryavanshi announced himself on the India A stage in the most emphatic way possible, blazing to a half-century off just 11 balls against Sri Lanka A — a record-breaking knock that left spectators and cricket analysts scrambling for comparisons. The teenager, who became a household name during IPL 2025 with Rajasthan Royals, showed that his fearless approach to batting translates beyond franchise cricket and onto the international representative stage.

Sooryavanshi's assault was characterised by clean striking, natural timing and a remarkable ability to pick the right ball to attack from the very first delivery he faced. Reaching fifty in 11 balls places his innings among the fastest half-centuries ever recorded in any format of the game, and it came against a Sri Lanka A attack that is far from ordinary.

For India's cricket ecosystem, the innings carries significant weight. The selectors and the national team management have been keeping a close watch on Sooryavanshi's progress, and a performance of this magnitude in an India A setting — one tier below full international cricket — makes it increasingly difficult to ignore his case for a senior call-up. His ability to accelerate from ball one is a skill set that T20 teams around the world pay a premium for.

Sooryavanshi is still in his mid-teens, making the record all the more staggering in its context. BBC Sport's coverage of the knock drew global attention, underlining that Indian cricket's next big thing may already be here — and in a hurry.
