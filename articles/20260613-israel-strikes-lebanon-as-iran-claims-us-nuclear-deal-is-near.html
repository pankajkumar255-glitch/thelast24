Israel Strikes Lebanon as Iran Claims US Nuclear Deal Is Near

What happened

BBC reports that Israel carried out air strikes on Lebanon, continuing its military operations in the region. Simultaneously, Iranian state media claimed that a deal with the United States on Iran's nuclear programme is near. NDTV had earlier reported that a US official expressed 80–85% confidence in reaching a peace deal but noted that Iran has set certain conditions. The convergence of military action and diplomatic signalling reflects the deeply contradictory dynamics playing out across the Middle East.

The context

Israel has been conducting military operations against Hezbollah in Lebanon since the October 2023 escalation that followed the Hamas attacks. Iran backs both Hamas and Hezbollah and has been engaged in indirect and direct nuclear negotiations with the US, with several rounds of talks held in Oman and elsewhere in 2025. A US–Iran nuclear deal, if concluded, would theoretically ease sanctions on Iranian oil exports, with major implications for global crude supply. India is one of the world's largest oil importers and historically sourced significant volumes from Iran before US sanctions tightened.

Why it matters

Any escalation in Lebanon risks a broader regional war that could spike global crude oil prices, squeezing Indian consumers at the fuel pump and widening India's import bill. Conversely, a US–Iran nuclear deal could ease oil supply pressures. The safety of millions of Indian workers in the Gulf Cooperation Council states is also a direct concern whenever regional tensions rise sharply.

What's next

Expect close monitoring of whether Iran's stated conditions become a dealbreaker in nuclear talks with the US. Watch for Israeli escalation or a ceasefire in Lebanon as US diplomatic pressure intensifies.
