Meta and Reliance Strike AI Data Centre Deal, Deepening India Bet

What happened

Reuters and CNBC report that Meta has agreed to an AI data centre deal with Reliance Industries, the conglomerate controlled by Mukesh Ambani. The partnership deepens an existing relationship between the two companies and forms part of Meta's broader strategy to bolster its hyperscaler infrastructure in India. CNBC describes the move as Meta reinforcing its regional infrastructure base, while Reuters characterises it as a deepening of the Meta-Reliance partnership specifically around AI computing capacity. The deal signals that India-based infrastructure will play a direct role in supporting Meta's AI products and services globally.

The context

Meta and Reliance have a prior working relationship: Reliance Jio and Meta struck a high-profile investment deal in 2020 when Meta invested approximately $5.7 billion for a roughly 10% stake in Jio Platforms. Since then, the two companies have collaborated on WhatsApp-based commerce and digital connectivity initiatives. Data centres are now the critical battleground for AI competitiveness, as models like Meta's Llama require enormous computing power. India has emerged as a priority destination for hyperscaler investment, with the government actively courting global tech firms to build AI infrastructure domestically. Reliance, with its vast land holdings, energy assets, and telecom reach, is uniquely positioned to host such facilities at scale.

Why it matters

For everyday Indians, the deal could accelerate improvements in AI-powered services on platforms like WhatsApp and Instagram, which hundreds of millions of Indians use daily. Domestic data centre expansion typically generates construction, engineering, and technology jobs, and can attract further foreign investment into India's digital economy. It also strengthens India's strategic position as an AI infrastructure hub, potentially giving the country more leverage in global technology governance. Investors in Reliance and adjacent infrastructure sectors are likely to view the partnership as a positive signal for future earnings.

What's next

Expect formal announcements detailing investment figures and timelines as both companies seek regulatory clearances and land acquisition approvals. Analysts will watch whether other global hyperscalers — Microsoft, Google, Amazon — respond with competing India infrastructure deals, intensifying the race for AI capacity on Indian soil.
