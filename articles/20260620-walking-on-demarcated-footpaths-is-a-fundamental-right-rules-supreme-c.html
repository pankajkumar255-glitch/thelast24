Walking on Demarcated Footpaths Is a Fundamental Right, Rules Supreme Court

Key facts

  • Supreme Court rules walking on demarcated footpaths is a fundamental right
  • Ruling addresses chronic encroachment of footpaths by vendors and vehicles
  • Citizens can now invoke constitutional protection for pedestrian access

NDTV reports that the Supreme Court has declared walking on demarcated footpaths a fundamental right of Indian citizens. The ruling directly addresses the widespread and chronic problem of footpath encroachments across Indian cities, where pavements are routinely occupied by street vendors, illegally parked vehicles, and construction material, forcing pedestrians onto roads.

The court's observation elevates pedestrian access from a matter of civic administration to one of constitutional protection. This means citizens can now invoke fundamental rights to challenge encroachments or the failure of municipal authorities to maintain and clear footpaths.

For the vast majority of Indians — especially in urban areas — who walk as their primary or supplementary mode of transport, this ruling has direct practical significance. It empowers residents to approach courts demanding safe walking infrastructure and puts the onus on local bodies to enforce encroachment-free footpaths as a matter of rights, not mere convenience.
