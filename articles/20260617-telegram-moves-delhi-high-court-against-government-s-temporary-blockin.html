Telegram moves Delhi High Court against government's temporary blocking order ahead of NEET UG re-exam

What happened

India Today and NDTV Profit report that Telegram has filed a petition in the Delhi High Court challenging the Indian government's temporary order blocking the platform. The hearing was scheduled for the same day. The block is connected to government action in the context of NEET UG paper leak allegations, with authorities claiming the platform was used to disseminate leaked content.

The context

The NEET UG 2024 controversy erupted after allegations of widespread paper leaks and malpractice, triggering protests, court cases, and a government-ordered re-examination for affected candidates. Telegram, with its large group and channel features, was identified as a conduit for circulating leaked papers. India has previously blocked apps and websites under the IT Act, though such orders are rarely challenged so publicly and swiftly.

Why it matters

A successful challenge by Telegram could limit the government's ability to swiftly block communication platforms without due process, while a court upholding the ban would validate broader executive powers over digital infrastructure. For millions of everyday users, the outcome directly affects access to one of India's most widely used messaging services.

What's next

Expect the Delhi High Court to either stay or uphold the blocking order in its initial hearing, with a fuller ruling likely in subsequent proceedings.
