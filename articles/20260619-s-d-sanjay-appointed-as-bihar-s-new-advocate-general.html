S.D. Sanjay Appointed as Bihar's New Advocate General

Key facts

  • S.D. Sanjay appointed Advocate General of Bihar
  • Advocate General is Bihar's chief legal officer under Article 165
  • Represents the state in Patna High Court and Supreme Court

The Hindu reports that S.D. Sanjay has been named as the new Advocate General of Bihar, replacing the previous incumbent in the state's most senior legal post. The Advocate General is a constitutional appointment under Article 165, made by the Governor on the advice of the state government.

As Bihar's top law officer, Sanjay will represent the state government in the Patna High Court and the Supreme Court, advising the government on legal matters and leading its litigation. The role is one of considerable influence, as the Advocate General's office handles cases touching on state policy, administration, and citizens' rights.

Changes to the Advocate General post typically reflect shifts in the state government's legal strategy or a desire to bring in fresh counsel aligned with the administration's priorities. Bihar is one of India's most populous states, and its legal affairs span a wide range of public-interest matters.

For Bihar residents, the appointment matters because the Advocate General's effectiveness directly shapes how robustly the state defends — or challenges — policies that affect everyday life, from land rights to welfare schemes.
