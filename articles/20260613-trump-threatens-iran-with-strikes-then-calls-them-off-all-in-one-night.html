Trump Threatens Iran With Strikes, Then Calls Them Off — All In One Night

What happened

NDTV reports that President Donald Trump publicly threatened to strike Iran 'very hard tonight' and invoked the possibility of taking Venezuela-like control of Iran's oil fields, dramatically escalating rhetoric against Tehran. Within hours, NDTV reported again that Trump had called off those same strikes, leaving the situation unresolved. Separately, NDTV had earlier reported that Iran had not approved any deal with the United States, suggesting nuclear or sanctions negotiations remain stalled. The entire sequence — threat, oil ultimatum, and stand-down — played out publicly over a matter of hours on the same evening.

The context

The United States and Iran have been locked in a prolonged standoff over Iran's nuclear programme, with successive rounds of sanctions and failed diplomatic talks. Trump previously withdrew the US from the 2015 JCPOA nuclear deal during his first term and reimposed sweeping sanctions on Iran. The 'Venezuela model' reference alludes to US attempts to pressure Caracas by sanctioning its oil sector and recognising rival leadership — a template Trump appears to be floating for Iran. Iran has significantly advanced its uranium enrichment since the JCPOA collapse, raising alarm among Western nations and Israel.

Why it matters

Any military strike on Iran could trigger a regional war involving Israel, Gulf states, and proxy forces across the Middle East, with immediate consequences for global oil supply. India imports a significant share of its crude oil from the Gulf region, meaning a conflict could push fuel prices sharply higher and widen India's trade deficit. The erratic signalling from Washington also complicates India's own diplomatic balancing act between the US and Iran, with whom India has historically maintained energy and strategic ties.

What's next

Expect diplomatic back-channels between Washington and Tehran to intensify in the coming days as both sides process the night's events. Analysts will watch whether Iran responds with any military posturing or further nuclear escalation, and whether Trump issues fresh ultimatums.
