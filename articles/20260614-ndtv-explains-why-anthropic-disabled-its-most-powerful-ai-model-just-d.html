NDTV Explains Why Anthropic Disabled Its Most Powerful AI Model Just Days After Launch

What happened

NDTV reports that Anthropic disabled its most powerful AI model, Fable 5, within days of its launch following intervention by the Trump administration, which restricted foreign access on national security grounds. The report explains the sequence of events leading up to the suspension, including reported discussions between Amazon CEO Andy Jassy and US officials. The shutdown marks the first time a US government action has forced an AI company to retract a live, publicly available model so quickly after release.

The context

Anthropic launched Fable 5 and Mythos as its most capable models to date, with enterprise customers and developers globally having begun integrating them almost immediately. The swift disabling of a freshly launched frontier AI model is without precedent and exposes a new kind of risk for the AI industry: that geopolitical intervention can interrupt product availability with no warning. This has amplified calls for clearer AI governance frameworks internationally.

Why it matters

The incident demonstrates that even the most advanced AI products can be switched off overnight by government policy, forcing Indian and global businesses to rethink how they manage dependency on any single AI provider or model. It also accelerates pressure on India to build more resilient, domestically sourced AI capabilities.

What's next

Expect Anthropic to clarify whether and when international access to Fable 5 and Mythos might be restored, and whether a licensing or vetting framework for allied-nation access could be established.
