Trump vs Obama on Iran: BBC Breaks Down the Key Differences in Approach

What happened

BBC published an explainer comparing former President Obama's Iran policy — centred on the 2015 JCPOA multilateral nuclear deal — with Trump's approach, which involved withdrawing from that deal, imposing maximum pressure sanctions, and now pursuing a new agreement on stricter terms. The piece contextualises current Iran-US tensions within this longer policy arc.

The context

The 2015 JCPOA, negotiated under Obama, curtailed Iran's nuclear programme in exchange for sanctions relief and was signed by the US, UK, France, Germany, Russia, and China. Trump withdrew in 2018, reimposed sanctions, and Iran progressively broke its commitments. Trump's return to office has restarted high-stakes negotiations, but on very different terms than the Obama-era framework.

Why it matters

India imports significant volumes of crude oil from the Middle East and was previously one of Iran's largest oil customers before US sanctions forced it to halt purchases. Any new deal — or breakdown — will reshape India's energy sourcing options and crude prices.

What's next

Expect the Iran-US diplomatic track to intensify in the coming weeks, with a deal or breakdown likely to produce immediate oil market reactions.
