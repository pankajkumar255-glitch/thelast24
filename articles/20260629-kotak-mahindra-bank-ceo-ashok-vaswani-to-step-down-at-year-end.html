Kotak Mahindra Bank CEO Ashok Vaswani to step down at year-end

Key facts

  • Ashok Vaswani stepping down as MD & CEO at year-end
  • Departure cited as for personal reasons
  • Two internal candidates reportedly in contention for CEO role
  • Shortlist expected to be sent to RBI for approval

Ashok Vaswani will step down as Managing Director and CEO of Kotak Mahindra Bank by the end of the year, the bank confirmed, citing personal reasons for his departure. Vaswani, who took charge of India's fourth-largest private bank in January 2023, leaves at a time when the lender has been navigating regulatory challenges and expanding its retail banking footprint.

With the corner office set to be vacated, attention has quickly turned to who will succeed him. Two internal candidates are understood to be in contention for the top role, according to Moneycontrol, signalling the bank's preference for continuity and a smooth handover rather than an outside hire. The shortlist is expected to be submitted to the Reserve Bank of India for its mandatory approval — a process that typically takes several months and means the transition could extend into the first half of 2026.

The RBI's approval is central to any CEO succession at a scheduled commercial bank, giving the regulator significant influence over who ultimately leads Kotak Mahindra. The bank's board will need to move quickly to ensure there is no extended period of leadership uncertainty, something markets and regulators alike are sensitive to.

Kotak Mahindra Bank is one of India's most closely watched financial institutions, with a large retail deposit base and a strong presence in wealth management and lending. The identity of the successor will be a key signal of the bank's strategic direction under founder Uday Kotak's continued shadow as a major shareholder.
