NSE flags monsoon, El Niño and global trade as key risks to India's 2026 economy

Key facts

  • NSE identified monsoon, El Niño, and global market trends as top 2026 risks
  • Agriculture vulnerability means rain shortfall raises food inflation
  • Global trade tensions threaten IT, pharma, and textile exports
  • Warning relevant to RBI policy and budget planning cycles

India's National Stock Exchange has sounded a formal caution on the country's economic trajectory for 2026, identifying the monsoon, El Niño climate patterns, and turbulent global market trends as the three most significant risks on the horizon, according to a report covered by The Times of India Thursday.

The NSE's assessment points to India's enduring vulnerability to rainfall variability. Agriculture still underpins the livelihoods of a large share of the population, and a deficient or poorly distributed monsoon can simultaneously push food inflation higher, compress rural incomes, and dampen consumption-driven GDP growth. The added spectre of El Niño — a Pacific warming event associated with drier-than-normal conditions in South Asia — raises the probability of exactly such an outcome in 2026.

On the global side, the exchange flagged that ongoing trade realignments, including US tariff actions and shifting supply chains, continue to create earnings uncertainty for India's export-oriented sectors such as IT services, pharmaceuticals, and textiles. Equity markets, which have shown resilience through much of 2024-25, could face headwinds if these external shocks materialise simultaneously.

The NSE's risk register will inform how institutional investors and policymakers price in uncertainty over the coming months, particularly as the Union Budget cycle and the Reserve Bank of India's monetary policy decisions approach.
