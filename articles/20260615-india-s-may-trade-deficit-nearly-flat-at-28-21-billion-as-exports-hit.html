India's May Trade Deficit Nearly Flat at $28.21 Billion as Exports Hit Record High

What happened

Moneycontrol reports that India's May trade deficit was nearly flat at $28.21 billion even as exports hit a record high for the month. Reuters notes that the narrowing trade gap comes with US trade talks in sharp focus. PIB separately confirmed that cumulative merchandise and services exports for April-May 2026-27 are estimated at $162.69 billion, a 14.66% rise over the year-earlier period.

The context

India's export sector has been under pressure from global uncertainty and the threat of US tariffs, making the record May export figure particularly significant. Services exports — led by IT and business process outsourcing — have been a consistent driver of India's overall trade performance. A stable deficit helps contain currency volatility, which feeds into inflation and import costs for businesses and households.

Why it matters

Record exports signal healthy demand for Indian goods and services globally, supporting manufacturing employment and foreign exchange earnings that help keep the rupee stable. A contained trade deficit reduces the risk of currency depreciation that would otherwise push up prices for imported goods including fuel and electronics.

What's next

Expect the monthly trade data and the outcome of the June 23-24 India-US talks to together set the tone for India's export outlook for the rest of the financial year.
