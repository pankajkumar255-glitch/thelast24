Data governance, not AI, is Indian startups' biggest regulatory fear, survey finds

Key facts

  • Data governance ranked as top regulatory concern by Indian startups
  • AI regulation considered less pressing than data rules
  • Survey reported by Mint

Indian startups are more worried about data governance than artificial intelligence regulation, according to a survey reported by Mint — a finding that cuts against the assumption that AI rules are the defining regulatory concern of the moment.

The survey captures the mood across India's startup ecosystem at a pivotal time, as the government moves toward implementing its data protection legislation. For founders building products that depend on user data — from fintech and healthtech to edtech and e-commerce — the rules around data localisation, consent, and cross-border transfers carry immediate, practical consequences for their business models.

By contrast, while AI regulation is a live global conversation, Indian startups appear to regard it as a more distant or manageable concern. This could reflect the fact that most Indian startups are applying AI rather than building foundational models, making them less exposed to the kind of AI-specific liability questions exercising policymakers in Europe and the United States.

The survey results arrive alongside a separate report warning that restrictive digital rules broadly could put Rs 91,500 crore in value and 2.45 lakh jobs at risk — underscoring that regulatory design choices in Delhi have billion-dollar stakes for the ecosystem.

For policymakers, the message from the startup community is clear: get data governance right first. The rules around data will shape India's digital economy more profoundly in the near term than any specific AI legislation, and founders are watching the legislative process closely.
