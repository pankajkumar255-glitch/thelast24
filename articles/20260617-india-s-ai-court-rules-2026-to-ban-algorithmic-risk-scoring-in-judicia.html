India's AI Court Rules 2026 to Ban Algorithmic Risk Scoring in Judicial Settings

Key facts

  • India's AI Court Rules to come into effect in 2026
  • Rules will ban risk-scoring algorithms in judicial proceedings
  • Risk scores predict reoffending or flight risk using AI
  • India among first Asian jurisdictions to explicitly ban such tools in courts

What happened

Frontline Magazine reports that India's proposed AI Court Rules, expected to take effect in 2026, will prohibit the use of risk-scoring algorithms in court proceedings. The rules appear to be framed by the judiciary or a judicial committee tasked with governing the use of artificial intelligence tools in legal processes. The ban targets systems that assign numerical risk scores to individuals based on algorithmic predictions.

The context

Courts in the United States and parts of Europe have experimented with AI risk-assessment tools like COMPAS for decades, drawing sustained criticism from civil liberties groups for racial and socioeconomic bias. India's Supreme Court and high courts have been exploring AI for administrative tasks such as translation and case management, but the use of AI in substantive judicial decisions remains contested. The 2026 rules would be among the first in Asia to explicitly prohibit risk-scoring.

Why it matters

Banning risk-scoring algorithms prevents the entrenchment of data-driven bias in bail and sentencing decisions, protecting accused persons — disproportionately from poorer and marginalised communities — from being judged by opaque automated systems rather than evidence.

What's next

Expect stakeholder consultations and possible challenges from legal-tech firms before the 2026 rules are finalised, with the Bar Council and civil society groups likely to weigh in on the scope of permitted versus banned AI applications.
