India's AI Court Rules 2026 to ban algorithmic risk scoring in judicial proceedings

What happened

Frontline Magazine reports that India's proposed AI Court Rules 2026 will include a ban on risk-scoring algorithms being used in court proceedings. The rules are framed as part of a broader effort to regulate artificial intelligence applications within the Indian judicial system.

The context

AI-driven risk-scoring tools have been used in several countries, most notably the United States, to assist judges in bail, sentencing, and parole decisions. These systems have faced intense criticism for perpetuating racial and socioeconomic biases. India's judiciary has been increasingly engaging with questions of AI regulation as the technology becomes more prevalent in legal and governance contexts.

Why it matters

Prohibiting algorithmic risk scoring in Indian courts would safeguard the rights of undertrials and accused persons from being judged by opaque automated systems, upholding the principle of individual judicial discretion. It also sets a legal benchmark for how AI tools may — and may not — be used across India's vast court system.

What's next

Expect the draft AI Court Rules 2026 to be circulated for stakeholder consultation before formal adoption, with legal and civil society groups likely to weigh in on the scope of the ban.
