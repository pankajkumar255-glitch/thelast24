India's AI Court Rules 2026 to ban algorithmic risk scoring in judiciary

What happened

Frontline Magazine reports that India's proposed AI Court Rules, expected to come into effect in 2026, will include a ban on the use of AI-based risk scoring in court processes. The rules represent the judiciary's formal regulatory response to the growing use of artificial intelligence tools in legal systems globally.

The context

AI risk-scoring tools — which predict recidivism or assess bail risk — have been used in jurisdictions such as the United States but have faced sharp criticism for racial and socioeconomic bias. India's judiciary, handling hundreds of millions of cases, has explored technology for administrative efficiency, but concerns have grown about AI influencing substantive judicial outcomes. The proposed rules reflect a cautious, rights-protective approach.

Why it matters

For any Indian who might face court proceedings, a ban on AI risk scoring means that bail, sentencing, and other judicial decisions remain grounded in human judgment and legal principle rather than algorithmic profiling.

What's next

Expect the draft rules to be circulated for stakeholder consultation before 2026, with legal scholars, bar associations, and civil society groups likely to weigh in on their scope and implementation.
