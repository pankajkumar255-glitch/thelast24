Europe voices alarm over US AI dominance as G7 and VivaTech draw global tech world to France

What happened

Reuters reports that European officials and tech leaders gathering in France for the G7 and VivaTech conference are expressing concern about the dominance of US AI companies. The event is drawing significant global attention as Europe debates how to develop competitive, independent AI capabilities and set its own regulatory standards.

The context

Europe has long sought to regulate and compete with US Big Tech, most visibly through the EU AI Act. France has emerged as a leading voice for European AI independence, backed by the success of Mistral AI. VivaTech is one of Europe's largest tech conferences, and its co-location with G7 discussions this year amplifies the policy stakes around AI governance.

Why it matters

The outcome of G7 AI discussions could result in new international standards or trade frameworks around AI that affect how Indian companies access, deploy, or export AI tools and services. A US-Europe rift on AI regulation could also open space for India to assert its own position in global AI governance.

What's next

Expect the G7 communiqué to include language on AI governance that signals how Western nations plan to manage AI competition with the US, China, and emerging players like India.
