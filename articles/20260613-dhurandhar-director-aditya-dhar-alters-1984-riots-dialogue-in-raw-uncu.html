Dhurandhar Director Aditya Dhar Alters 1984 Riots Dialogue in Raw, Uncut Version

What happened

The Indian Express reports that director Aditya Dhar has altered a dialogue pertaining to the 1984 riots in a scene involving characters named Hamza and Pinda in Dhurandhar 2's raw, uncut version. The report specifically identifies the altered scene and the characters involved. The change indicates that the original dialogue was considered potentially contentious or sensitive. Dhurandhar 2 is the sequel to Dhar's directorial project following his debut with the Uri franchise.

The context

Aditya Dhar rose to national prominence with Uri: The Surgical Strike (2019), which became one of Bollywood's biggest hits and a cultural touchstone. The 1984 anti-Sikh riots remain an extremely sensitive historical and political subject in India, with ongoing legal and social debates about accountability. Films that depict or reference these events often face scrutiny from community groups, censor authorities, and the courts. Dialogue alterations at the raw-cut stage are not unusual in Indian cinema when handling such material.

Why it matters

For Sikh communities and all Indians who lived through or know the history of 1984, how cinema chooses to depict those events carries significant emotional and political weight. Alterations made even at the pre-release stage show the pressure on filmmakers to handle such subjects responsibly. The decision will be watched closely by community groups and critics as the film moves toward release.

What's next

Expect the Central Board of Film Certification to scrutinise the film closely, and community groups may weigh in once the film nears its release date. Audiences will be watching whether the final cut handles the 1984 references with the sensitivity the subject demands.
