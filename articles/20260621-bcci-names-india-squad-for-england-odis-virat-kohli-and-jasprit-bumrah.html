BCCI names India squad for England ODIs; Virat Kohli and Jasprit Bumrah return to the side

Key facts

  • Virat Kohli named in India's ODI squad for England series
  • Jasprit Bumrah also returns to the ODI setup
  • Squad announced by BCCI for the England ODI series

The Board of Control for Cricket in India announced the national squad for the ODI series against England, with Virat Kohli and Jasprit Bumrah both named in the XI-eligible group after periods away from the format. Their inclusion was the defining headline of the selection announcement, restoring two of India's most influential cricketers to a format where they remain irreplaceable assets.

Kohli, India's most prolific ODI batter, had been managed carefully through a packed international calendar, with selectors cautious about workload after a gruelling Test season. Bumrah, meanwhile, has been India's premier pace bowler across formats but has required careful handling given recurring back-related fitness concerns. The BCCI's confirmation that both are available and selected suggests they have been cleared to resume full duties.

The series against England represents a significant opportunity for India to fine-tune their ODI combination with an eye on future ICC white-ball tournaments. England, as defending ODI World Cup holders, remain a tough benchmark, and a full-strength India squad will be expected to compete hard across the series.

Beyond Kohli and Bumrah, the squad selection will also be watched for indications of where young contenders — including those starring at the India A level — stand in the national pecking order. The BCCI's announcement sets the stage for one of the summer's most anticipated bilateral series.
