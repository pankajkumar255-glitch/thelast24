Cash crunch, prison and lawsuits: The turbulent story behind Vikram Bhatt's Haunted 3D: Echoes of the Past

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Vikram Bhatt's Haunted 3D: Echoes of the Past faced a severe cash crunch during production, with Bhatt himself reportedly dealing with a prison episode and an ongoing lawsuit linked to the project. The article lays out the overlapping crises that have dogged the film's journey to completion.

The context

Vikram Bhatt is one of Bollywood's most prolific horror directors, known for franchises like Raaz and 1920. The original Haunted 3D was a commercial milestone and remains one of the highest-grossing Bollywood horror films. Sequels and follow-ups in the horror genre carry significant audience expectation, making production troubles all the more damaging to a film's prospects.

Why it matters

Financial and legal troubles in production can delay or scuttle a film's release, directly affecting cast, crew livelihoods, and audience anticipation. The story also highlights the broader risk environment for mid-budget Bollywood productions that lack major studio backing.

What's next

Expect more clarity on the film's release timeline as legal proceedings and financial disputes are likely to come under greater scrutiny following this report.
