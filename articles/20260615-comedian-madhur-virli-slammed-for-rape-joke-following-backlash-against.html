Comedian Madhur Virli slammed for rape joke, following backlash against Pranit More

What happened

The Indian Express reports that comedian Madhur Virli has been publicly criticised and slammed for making a rape joke during a performance. The report notes this follows a near-identical controversy involving fellow comedian Pranit More, indicating the issue has become a recurring flashpoint in India's stand-up comedy scene.

The context

Indian stand-up comedy has grown enormously as a profession and content genre over the last decade, with comedians gaining large social media followings. However, the industry has repeatedly faced scrutiny over misogynistic and sexually offensive material, with the #MeToo movement having earlier called out several prominent figures. Audiences — particularly women — and civil society groups have become increasingly vocal in demanding accountability.

Why it matters

Content that normalises sexual violence, even under the guise of humour, contributes to broader cultural attitudes toward women; public callouts and potential platform bans can shape what material comedians and streaming services are willing to promote.

What's next

Expect pressure on comedy clubs, YouTube platforms, and streaming services to review their content policies, and watch for whether Madhur Virli issues a public apology or faces professional consequences.
