Chiranjeevi, not Manoj Bajpayee, was first choice for The Family Man, reveals director Raj Nidimoru

What happened

The Indian Express reports that Raj Nidimoru, co-director of The Family Man, has disclosed that Chiranjeevi was the original choice to play the lead role later immortalised by Manoj Bajpayee. Nidimoru made the revelation in a recent interview, shedding light on the early development of the Amazon Prime Video series.

The context

The Family Man, created by Raj and DK (Raj Nidimoru and Krishna DK), became a landmark Indian OTT production when it launched on Amazon Prime Video in 2019. Manoj Bajpayee's portrayal of intelligence officer Srikant Tiwari won critical acclaim and multiple awards, making it hard to imagine the show without him. Chiranjeevi is one of Telugu cinema's biggest stars, and his casting would have given the show an entirely different cultural flavour.

Why it matters

The revelation highlights how differently India's OTT landscape might have developed had the show taken a South Indian star route from the start, and underscores how pivotal casting choices are in shaping content that defines a streaming platform's identity.

What's next

Expect renewed fan discussion and media attention around The Family Man Season 3, which is already in development, with audiences speculating on further casting surprises.
