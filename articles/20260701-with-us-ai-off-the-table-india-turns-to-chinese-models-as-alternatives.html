With US AI off the table, India turns to Chinese models as alternatives to OpenAI and Anthropic

Key facts

  • India exploring Chinese AI models as alternatives to OpenAI and Anthropic
  • Shift driven by uncertain access to US frontier AI platforms
  • Reported by The Economic Times

India is pivoting toward Chinese AI models as access to US frontier platforms such as OpenAI and Anthropic remains uncertain, a development that carries major strategic and commercial implications for the country's fast-growing technology sector. The Economic Times reports that Indian enterprises are actively evaluating Chinese AI alternatives to fill the gap left by the unpredictable availability of American models.

The uncertainty around US AI access stems from a combination of export control pressures, licensing concerns, and the broader geopolitical tension between Washington and the rest of the world over advanced technology. For Indian developers, startups, and large enterprises that have built workflows around platforms like ChatGPT or Claude, the inability to rely on those tools is a practical operational problem that demands immediate solutions.

The irony is not lost on analysts: India, which has maintained a careful distance from Chinese technology in sectors like telecom and consumer apps, may now be drawn toward Beijing-backed AI systems by the very restrictions its close partner the United States is imposing. Chinese models — several of which have demonstrated competitive performance benchmarks — are filling a vacuum that US policy is inadvertently creating.

The development also puts Nasscom and Indian policymakers in a difficult position, as they simultaneously advocate for domestic AI capability-building while managing short-term dependency on foreign systems. How India navigates this choice — between American platforms with uncertain access and Chinese alternatives with different risks — will define much of its AI strategy in the near term.
