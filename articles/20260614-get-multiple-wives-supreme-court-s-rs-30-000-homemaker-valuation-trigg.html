'Get Multiple Wives': Supreme Court's Rs 30,000 Homemaker Valuation Triggers Backlash Online

What happened

The Times of India reports that following the Supreme Court's valuation of homemakers' work at Rs 30,000 per month, a significant number of men responded on social media with comments trivialising and mocking the ruling, including suggestions about taking multiple wives. The report highlights the contrast between the court's intent to dignify domestic labour and the public reaction that reduced the ruling to a transactional joke.

The context

The Supreme Court's valuation was made in a compensation context to ensure homemakers' contributions are recognised in legal proceedings. However, social media reactions in India frequently reflect patriarchal attitudes that undervalue women's domestic roles. The backlash is part of a broader pattern in which progressive judicial decisions on gender issues face resistance in public discourse.

Why it matters

The reaction illustrates the gap between legal progress and social attitudes in India, signalling that awareness campaigns and implementation of the ruling will face cultural resistance. For women and advocacy groups, the episode reinforces the urgency of changing societal narratives around domestic work alongside legal reform.

What's next

Expect women's rights organisations and commentators to use this backlash to call for broader public education campaigns around gender equity and the value of care work.
