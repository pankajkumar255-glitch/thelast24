Abhishek Bachchan recalls battling insecurity in Bollywood: 'Kaam milega ya nahi'

Key facts

  • Abhishek Bachchan spoke about career insecurity in Bollywood
  • Described anxiety with phrase 'Kaam milega ya nahi' (will I get work or not)
  • Remarks reported by The Indian Express

Abhishek Bachchan has spoken candidly about the career insecurities that plagued him during his time in Bollywood, admitting that the constant fear of whether work would come defined a significant part of his professional journey.

In remarks reported by The Indian Express, Bachchan encapsulated that anxiety in a plain, relatable phrase — 'Kaam milega ya nahi' (will I get work or not) — a sentiment that resonates far beyond star corridors and into the everyday reality of thousands who chase dreams in India's film industry. Despite carrying one of Hindi cinema's most recognisable surnames, Bachchan acknowledged that lineage alone provides no insulation from professional doubt.

The confession is striking given that Bollywood's star-kid debate has intensified in public discourse in recent years, with audiences and industry insiders frequently discussing whether nepotism paves an easier path. Bachchan's account complicates that narrative, suggesting that even those born into privilege navigate their own versions of uncertainty and self-doubt.

His remarks are likely to resonate with audiences who have watched his career arc — from celebrated early roles to a period of diminished visibility, and a recent return to prominence. The actor's willingness to speak about vulnerability, rather than project invulnerability, marks a notable shift in how Bollywood's second generation is choosing to engage with the public conversation about their place in the industry.
