Sridhar Vembu Calls for India to Build Full Technology Pyramid, Not Just Chase AI

What happened

India Today reports that Sridhar Vembu, founder of Zoho, argued that India must build a full technology pyramid rather than focusing narrowly on AI, cautioning against a strategy that skips foundational layers such as semiconductors, operating systems, and core software infrastructure. Vembu's comments were made in the context of India's current AI push and its broader ambitions to become a global technology leader.

The context

India's technology sector has historically been dominated by IT services rather than product or deep tech creation. The government's current push — through initiatives like the IndiaAI Mission, the semiconductor incentive programme, and 'Bharat Innovates' — represents an attempt to shift that mix. However, critics like Vembu argue that without investing in the lower layers of the tech stack, India will simply build a services economy on top of foreign infrastructure, repeating the same dependency in a new form.

Why it matters

Vembu's argument has direct implications for where government policy and private investment should flow — toward chip design, systems software, and core research rather than exclusively AI startups and applications. For Indian engineers and students, it signals that the most critical and durable career opportunities may lie in foundational technologies rather than the AI application layer that currently attracts most attention and funding.

What's next

Expect Vembu's remarks to fuel ongoing policy debates around India's semiconductor mission and the allocation of the government's AI and deep tech funding between foundational research and application-layer startups.
