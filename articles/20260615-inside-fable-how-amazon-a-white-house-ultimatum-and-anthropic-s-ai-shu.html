Inside Fable: How Amazon, a White House ultimatum, and Anthropic's AI shutdown unfolded

What happened

Moneycontrol reports on the behind-the-scenes story of an AI project called Fable, detailing Amazon's intervention, a 90-minute ultimatum from the White House, and Anthropic's decision to shut down the AI system. The account sheds light on the increasingly political and regulatory environment surrounding advanced AI development in the US.

The context

Anthropic, backed by Amazon among others, is one of the leading AI safety-focused companies in the world. The episode illustrates the growing tension between rapid AI development and government oversight, with the White House taking an increasingly hands-on approach to AI governance. Amazon's deep financial stake in Anthropic makes any major decision involving the company significant for the broader AI investment landscape.

Why it matters

The Fable episode signals that even well-funded, safety-focused AI projects can be shut down at government discretion, raising questions for Indian enterprises and investors about the stability of partnerships with US AI firms. It also highlights how AI governance is rapidly becoming a geopolitical battleground.

What's next

Expect more details to emerge about the Fable shutdown and for the episode to intensify calls globally, including in India, for clearer AI governance frameworks that reduce dependence on opaque US regulatory decisions.
