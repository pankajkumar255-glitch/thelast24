Israel rejects US-Iran nuclear deal framework, complicating Trump's plans

What happened

NDTV reports that Israel's first official reaction to the US-Iran deal being negotiated by President Donald Trump is sharply negative, with Israeli leadership signalling strong opposition to the framework. The response is seen as a significant diplomatic setback for Trump's efforts to seal a landmark agreement with Tehran.

The context

The United States and Iran have been engaged in indirect nuclear negotiations, with Trump pushing for a deal that would curb Iran's nuclear programme in exchange for sanctions relief. Israel, under Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, has long opposed any deal that does not fully dismantle Iran's nuclear infrastructure, arguing that a partial agreement would leave Iran capable of eventually building a bomb. The US-Israel alliance is a cornerstone of American Middle East policy, making Israeli opposition politically costly for any US administration.

Why it matters

An Iran nuclear deal — or its collapse — directly affects global oil supply, as Iran holds vast reserves, and any shift in sanctions could alter crude prices that India pays. Regional instability triggered by Israeli-Iranian tensions also poses risks to Indian workers and investments in the Gulf.

What's next

Expect intense US-Israel diplomatic consultations in the coming days, with pressure on Trump to either modify deal terms or risk a public breach with a key ally.
