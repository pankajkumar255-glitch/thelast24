Rs 91,500 crore and 2.45 lakh jobs at risk as digital rules squeeze India's startup ecosystem

Key facts

  • Rs 91,500 crore in startup value potentially at risk
  • 2.45 lakh jobs could be lost due to digital regulations
  • Data governance — not AI — is startups' top regulatory concern (Mint survey)

India's startup ecosystem — one of the largest in the world — faces a potential blow of Rs 91,500 crore in economic value and as many as 2.45 lakh jobs, according to a report flagged by The Economic Times. The warning comes as a wave of digital regulations, spanning data governance, platform liability, and compliance mandates, creates mounting uncertainty for businesses of all sizes.

The report's findings are particularly striking because they put precise numbers to a concern that founders and venture capitalists have been voicing for months: that well-intentioned regulation, poorly timed or designed, can choke off the very innovation India needs to compete globally. Startups, especially early-stage and growth-phase companies, often lack the legal and compliance infrastructure to absorb rapidly shifting regulatory demands.

A separate Mint survey published the same day reinforced this anxiety, finding that data governance — not artificial intelligence — is the single biggest regulatory worry among Indian startups. Together, the two reports paint a picture of an ecosystem that feels squeezed from multiple directions at once, with founders prioritising regulatory navigation over product development.

The stakes are high for the broader Indian economy. The startup sector has been a key driver of tech employment and foreign investment over the past decade. If the report's projections materialise, the damage would ripple beyond founders and venture funds into salaries, vendor ecosystems, and consumer services that millions of Indians use daily. Policymakers now face the challenge of balancing legitimate concerns around data security and platform accountability with the need to keep India's entrepreneurial engine running.
