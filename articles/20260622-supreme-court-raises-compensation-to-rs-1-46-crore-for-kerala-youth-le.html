Supreme Court raises compensation to Rs 1.46 crore for Kerala youth left paraplegic in road accident

Key facts

  • SC raises compensation to Rs 1.46 crore for paraplegic accident victim
  • Victim is a Kerala youth rendered paraplegic in a road accident
  • Earlier compensation was found inadequate by the Supreme Court

The Supreme Court of India has enhanced compensation to Rs 1.46 crore for a Kerala youth who was left paraplegic after a road accident, holding that the amount previously awarded was grossly insufficient for the scale of suffering and permanent disability involved. The bench's decision reflects a firm judicial stance on ensuring that Motor Accident Claims Tribunal awards are commensurate with the lifelong consequences victims face.

Paraplegia — paralysis of the lower body — involves permanent loss of mobility and requires continuous medical support, assistive equipment, and care. The court's ruling takes into account not just immediate medical costs but the long-term financial burden on the victim and his family, including the effective end of his earning capacity.

Road accidents in India claim over 1.5 lakh lives annually and leave hundreds of thousands with permanent disabilities, yet compensation awarded by lower courts and tribunals has often been criticised as inadequate. Supreme Court interventions of this nature serve as corrective guidance to lower forums on how to calculate just and fair compensation in serious disability cases.

The enhanced award of Rs 1.46 crore is likely to be cited as precedent in similar cases across the country, potentially raising the floor for compensation in accidents leading to paralysis or other catastrophic injuries. For families devastated by road accidents, the judgment offers both financial relief and a clear signal from the highest court that inadequate awards will not stand.
