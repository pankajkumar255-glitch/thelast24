Can Indian Investors Get a Piece of the Biggest AI IPOs? Mint Weighs In

What happened

Mint examines the question of whether Indian investors — both retail and institutional — can access and should consider investing in major upcoming artificial intelligence IPOs. The report weighs the regulatory pathways available to Indian investors for participating in overseas listings as well as the potential risks and rewards involved. It reflects growing investor interest in AI as a category following the sector's rapid global expansion.

The context

Indian investors can access US-listed stocks through the Liberalised Remittance Scheme, which allows individuals to remit up to $250,000 per year for overseas investments. Several Indian brokerages and fintech platforms now offer access to US markets. Major AI companies — including OpenAI, Anthropic, and others — are widely anticipated to pursue IPOs in the coming years, making this a timely question for Indian wealth management.

Why it matters

Indian retail investors who can navigate the regulatory and platform requirements could gain exposure to some of the fastest-growing companies in the world — but foreign equity investments carry currency risk, information asymmetry, and valuation risk at a time when AI stocks are trading at steep premiums.

What's next

Expect Indian fintech platforms to ramp up marketing around international AI stock access as major AI IPOs approach, and watch for SEBI to potentially update guidelines on overseas investment to address growing retail demand.
