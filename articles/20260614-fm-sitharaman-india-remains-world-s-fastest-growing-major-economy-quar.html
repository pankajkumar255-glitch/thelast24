FM Sitharaman: India Remains World's Fastest-Growing Major Economy, Quarter After Quarter

What happened

The Economic Times reports that Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman stated that India remains the fastest-growing major economy in the world, describing the performance as consistent 'quarter after quarter, year after year.' The remarks were made at a public forum and underline the government's confidence in India's economic fundamentals. No specific GDP figure was cited in the headline.

The context

India has consistently ranked among the fastest-growing large economies globally, with the IMF and World Bank projecting growth above 6% for the current fiscal year. The statement comes at a time when China's growth is under pressure and Western economies face slowdowns, making India's relative performance a key talking point for policymakers and investors.

Why it matters

Strong growth rhetoric supports investor sentiment and can influence foreign direct investment inflows and capital market performance. However, sustained growth must translate into broader employment and income gains for the claim to resonate with ordinary Indians.

What's next

Expect the government to use this framing ahead of the Union Budget discussions and international investor forums to attract capital and consolidate India's position as a preferred emerging-market destination.
