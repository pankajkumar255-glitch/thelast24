ChatGPT's market share falls below 50% as Gemini and Claude gain ground

Key facts

  • ChatGPT market share has fallen below 50% for the first time
  • Google Gemini and Anthropic's Claude are the primary gainers
  • ChatGPT launched in November 2022 and quickly dominated AI chatbot market
  • Gemini benefits from Android and Google Workspace integration

Business Standard reports that ChatGPT's share of the global AI chatbot market has fallen below 50%, citing new market data that points to significant gains by Google's Gemini and Anthropic's Claude. It is the first time since OpenAI launched ChatGPT in November 2022 that the pioneering chatbot has held less than half the market, a symbolic milestone in the maturing AI industry.

Google's Gemini has benefited from deep integration with Android devices, Google Workspace, and Search — giving it a massive built-in distribution advantage, particularly in mobile-first markets like India. Anthropic's Claude, meanwhile, has carved out a strong position among enterprise users and developers who prize its longer context windows and safety-focused design.

The shift signals that the AI chatbot market is moving from a one-horse race to a genuine multi-player competition. For Indian users — among the largest and fastest-growing audiences for AI tools globally — the intensifying rivalry between ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude is likely to accelerate feature improvements, drive down subscription costs, and expand free-tier capabilities. OpenAI retains a dominant brand presence, but its era of unchallenged market leadership appears to be over.
