India Puts Up Strong Show at Eurosatory, World's Largest Defence Exhibition

What happened

The Times of India reports that India put up a strong show at Eurosatory, described as the world's largest defence exhibition. Indian defence firms and public sector undertakings participated in the event, demonstrating platforms and systems aimed at international buyers. The report highlights India's growing ambition to become a significant defence exporter.

The context

India has set an ambitious target of achieving Rs 50,000 crore in defence exports by 2029, a dramatic leap from its historically import-heavy defence posture. Eurosatory, held in Paris, is a flagship global platform where nations and companies showcase land warfare systems to military buyers worldwide. India's defence exports have been rising steadily, with countries in Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Middle East showing interest in Indian-made systems.

Why it matters

Success at events like Eurosatory translates into export orders that support domestic defence manufacturing jobs and reduce India's massive defence import bill. It also boosts India's geopolitical standing as a reliable defence supplier.

What's next

Expect announcements of follow-on export inquiries or preliminary agreements with interested nations in the weeks after the exhibition.
