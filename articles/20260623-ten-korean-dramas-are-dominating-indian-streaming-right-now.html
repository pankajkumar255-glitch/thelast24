Ten Korean Dramas Are Dominating Indian Streaming Right Now

Key facts

  • Ten Korean dramas currently among most-watched on Indian streaming platforms
  • Trend reflects deepening of Hallyu (Korean Wave) in India
  • Phenomenon accelerated post-pandemic with titles like Squid Game

Ten Korean dramas are currently capturing the most Indian streaming attention, according to India Today, in a sign that the Korean Wave is not a passing fad but a settled part of how urban — and increasingly small-town — India consumes entertainment.

The Hallyu phenomenon first found serious Indian footing during the pandemic years, when locked-down viewers discovered binge-friendly Korean series on Netflix and other platforms. What began as curiosity has hardened into habit: dedicated fan communities, dubbed and subtitled versions, and active social media fandoms have all taken root across India's metro and tier-2 cities.

For Indian streaming platforms, the numbers carry clear strategic weight. Investing in Korean content licensing and localised subtitling delivers a disproportionate return because Korean dramas tend to inspire intense loyalty and repeat viewing — audiences who finish one series almost invariably seek out another. This creates a predictable, self-sustaining audience segment that platforms can rely on.

The broader cultural implication is also notable: Indian viewers, traditionally assumed to prefer domestic content, are demonstrating a growing appetite for well-produced foreign drama regardless of language. That shift is forcing Indian producers and OTT executives to rethink assumptions about what Indian audiences will and won't watch — a conversation that will shape commissioning decisions for years to come.
