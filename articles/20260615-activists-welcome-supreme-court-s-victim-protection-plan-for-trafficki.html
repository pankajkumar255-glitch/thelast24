Activists welcome Supreme Court's victim protection plan for trafficking survivors

What happened

The Hindu reports that the Supreme Court has put forward a victim protection plan specifically for survivors of human trafficking. Activists and civil society organisations working with trafficking survivors have publicly welcomed the plan. The court's initiative comes amid longstanding concerns that survivors face inadequate legal protection and are often treated as suspects rather than victims within the system.

The context

Human trafficking remains a severe problem in India, with the country being both a source and destination for trafficked persons — particularly women and children. The absence of a comprehensive central rehabilitation and protection law has left survivors vulnerable to re-trafficking and secondary trauma during legal proceedings. The Supreme Court has previously directed governments to strengthen anti-trafficking infrastructure, and this plan appears to be a further step in that judicial oversight.

Why it matters

A structured protection plan from the Supreme Court can compel state governments and police forces to change how they handle trafficking survivors, improving their safety and the quality of evidence in prosecutions. Stronger victim protection also increases the likelihood of convictions, which serves as a deterrent.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to issue directions to state governments to implement the protection framework within a defined timeline, with compliance reports likely to be sought in subsequent hearings.
