Activists Welcome Supreme Court's Victim Protection Plan for Trafficking Survivors

What happened

The Hindu reports that activists have welcomed a victim protection plan formulated by the Supreme Court for human trafficking survivors. The report highlights positive reception from those working on the ground with affected communities, signalling that the plan addresses longstanding gaps in how the justice system treats survivors.

The context

Human trafficking remains a serious crime in India, with women and children disproportionately affected. The Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act and subsequent laws have been criticised for treating victims as offenders rather than providing them protection. Civil society organisations have for years pushed for a survivor-centred approach to trafficking cases, and the Supreme Court has periodically stepped in to fill legislative gaps through suo motu cases and guidelines.

Why it matters

A Supreme Court-backed protection framework could standardise how police, courts, and welfare bodies across all states treat trafficking survivors, reducing harassment and encouraging more victims to seek justice and rehabilitation.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to issue formal directions to state governments to implement the victim protection plan and to set up a monitoring mechanism to track compliance.
