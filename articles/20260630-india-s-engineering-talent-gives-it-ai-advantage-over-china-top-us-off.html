India's engineering talent gives it AI advantage over China, top US official tells NDTV

Key facts

  • US official said India's engineering talent gives it AI edge over China
  • Remarks made to NDTV by a senior US official
  • Positions India and China as rivals in global AI competition
  • Aligns with broader US push for India as a technology partner

India's vast pool of engineering talent gives it a meaningful artificial intelligence advantage over China, a top US official declared in an interview with NDTV, framing the two Asian giants as rivals in the defining technology competition of the coming decades.

The official's assertion zeroes in on human capital as the decisive variable — an argument that plays strongly to India's strengths. While China has poured enormous state resources into AI compute infrastructure and foundational model development, India's competitive edge, in the US official's framing, lies in the sheer scale and quality of its engineering workforce, honed through decades of building global technology services.

The remarks are part of a visible US diplomatic push to deepen technology ties with India, particularly as Washington seeks partners to shape global AI governance, standards, and supply chains in ways that reflect democratic values rather than authoritarian ones. Describing India's talent as superior to China's is a deliberate signal to New Delhi about the strategic importance Washington attaches to the relationship.

For India, the endorsement carries both opportunity and obligation. It invites greater collaboration with US technology firms and research institutions, but also raises questions about whether India can convert its talent advantage into genuine AI leadership — through investment in compute infrastructure, domestic model-building, and an enabling regulatory environment — rather than remaining primarily a services provider to the global AI industry.
