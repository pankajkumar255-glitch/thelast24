SpaceX Enters World's Top 10 Most Valuable Companies After Market Debut

What happened

The Economic Times reports that SpaceX has become the seventh most valuable company in the world following its market debut. The listing values the company — founded by Elon Musk and best known for its Falcon rockets, Starship programme, and Starlink satellite internet service — at a level that places it above many of the world's largest traditional corporations. The debut was described as blockbuster, suggesting strong investor demand and a high opening valuation. This marks a significant milestone for a company that had for years remained one of the most valuable privately held firms in the world.

The context

SpaceX was founded in 2002 by Elon Musk with the stated goal of making humanity multi-planetary. It has since become the dominant force in commercial launch services, regularly ferrying cargo and astronauts to the International Space Station for NASA. Its Starlink subsidiary operates one of the world's largest satellite internet constellations and has strategic relevance in India, where it is seeking regulatory approval to offer broadband services. SpaceX had been valued at over $150 billion in private funding rounds before its public debut, making it one of the most anticipated listings in years.

Why it matters

SpaceX's public market valuation sets a benchmark for the global space economy and could accelerate investment into India's own growing private space sector, including startups like Skyroot Aerospace and Agnikul Cosmos. Starlink's commercial ambitions in India mean its financial strength now directly affects the timeline and terms of its potential India launch. For Indian investors with access to US markets, SpaceX's debut creates a new major investment option. The listing also intensifies competition with ISRO's commercial arm, NewSpace India Limited.

What's next

Expect Indian space startups and the Department of Space to respond to SpaceX's elevated market profile with renewed urgency around domestic launches and policy frameworks. Starlink's India regulatory clearance process is likely to attract fresh attention in light of the company's new public status.
