US 'export ban' on Anthropic models may weaken India's cyber defence, warn experts

What happened

Indiatimes reports that cybersecurity experts have warned that the US export ban on Anthropic AI models may weaken India's cyber defence ecosystem. The concern is that Indian security agencies and private firms relying on these models for threat detection and response could face capability gaps.

The context

Anthropic's Claude models have been integrated into various cybersecurity workflows globally, including in India. The US has been tightening AI export controls as part of broader technology competition with China, but the restrictions are having unintended consequences for allied and partner nations like India. India faces a growing volume of sophisticated cyberattacks targeting financial, healthcare, and government systems.

Why it matters

Indian organisations using Anthropic-powered cybersecurity tools may need to urgently find alternatives, increasing costs and potentially leaving temporary gaps in protection for critical systems. This underscores the broader vulnerability of India's digital infrastructure to foreign policy decisions.

What's next

Expect Indian cybersecurity agencies and CERT-In to assess exposure to Anthropic-dependent tools and accelerate evaluation of alternative AI-powered security solutions.
