Anthropic Suspends Access to New AI Models as India Debates Its AI Future

What happened

TechCrunch reports that Anthropic has suspended access to its new AI models, coinciding with a broader debate in India about the country's AI policy and strategic direction. The report frames the suspension in the context of India's uncertainty about its dependence on foreign AI providers. Further details on the reason for Anthropic's suspension or the specific models affected were not provided in the headline.

The context

Anthropic, founded by former OpenAI researchers, is one of the most prominent AI safety-focused labs globally and competes with OpenAI and Google DeepMind. India has been formulating its National AI Mission and debating regulatory frameworks for AI, with a focus on sovereign AI capabilities. Indian startups and enterprises have increasingly integrated third-party AI APIs into their products, creating dependencies on foreign platforms.

Why it matters

Disruptions at major AI providers like Anthropic can immediately affect Indian startups, enterprises, and developers who rely on these APIs for their products and services. This reinforces calls for India to invest in domestic AI models and infrastructure to reduce strategic vulnerability.

What's next

Expect India's AI policy deliberations to gain urgency, with greater emphasis on developing sovereign AI infrastructure and diversifying away from single foreign providers.
