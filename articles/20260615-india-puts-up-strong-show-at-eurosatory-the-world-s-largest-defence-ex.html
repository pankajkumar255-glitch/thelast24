India puts up strong show at Eurosatory, the world's largest defence exhibition

What happened

The Times of India reports that India put up a strong show at Eurosatory, the world's largest defence exhibition, with Indian firms displaying indigenously developed defence equipment and systems. The participation signals India's intent to expand its footprint in the global defence market at one of the most prestigious international arms showcases.

The context

India has set an ambitious target of achieving Rs 50,000 crore in annual defence exports by 2025. Eurosatory, held in Paris, draws buyers, governments, and defence ministries from across the world and is considered a premier platform for showcasing military technology. Indian participation has grown substantially in recent years as domestic defence production ramps up.

Why it matters

A stronger Indian defence export profile brings in foreign exchange, creates skilled manufacturing jobs, and reduces India's own import bill for military equipment — savings that can be redirected to public spending. It also elevates India's geopolitical standing.

What's next

Expect announcements of potential export deals or government-to-government agreements in the weeks following the exhibition, as buyer nations evaluate Indian offerings.
