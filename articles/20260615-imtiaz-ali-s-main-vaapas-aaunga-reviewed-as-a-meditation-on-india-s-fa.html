Imtiaz Ali's 'Main Vaapas Aaunga' reviewed as a meditation on India's fading identity

What happened

The Indian Express has published a review of Imtiaz Ali's film Main Vaapas Aaunga, describing it as a work that explores a country at risk of forgetting its own identity. The review frames the film as a culturally significant meditation rather than straightforward commercial entertainment.

The context

Imtiaz Ali is known for emotionally layered, often road-trip-structured Hindi films including Jab We Met, Rockstar, and Highway, which earned him a reputation as one of Bollywood's most distinctive voices. His films frequently explore themes of self-discovery, loss, and belonging. A new directorial project from Ali carries considerable weight among both critics and audiences invested in meaningful Hindi cinema.

Why it matters

Films that engage seriously with questions of Indian identity and cultural memory contribute to the broader national conversation about who Indians are and where the country is headed — and can shape public discourse beyond the multiplex.

What's next

Expect audience response and further critical reviews to determine whether the film finds commercial traction alongside its critical recognition.
