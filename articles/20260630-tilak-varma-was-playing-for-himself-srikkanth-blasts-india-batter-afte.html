'Tilak Varma was playing for himself': Srikkanth blasts India batter after series defeat

Key facts

  • Srikkanth accused Tilak Varma of 'playing for himself'
  • Criticism follows a series defeat for India
  • Tilak Varma faces scrutiny ahead of the England T20I series

Krishnamachari Srikkanth, one of India's most celebrated former openers and a member of the 1983 World Cup-winning squad, has launched a pointed attack on Tilak Varma, accusing the young middle-order batter of playing for himself rather than for the team during India's recent series defeat.

Srikkanth's remarks, carried by The Times of India, reflect wider concerns within Indian cricket circles about Tilak Varma's approach at the crease. The allegation that he prioritised personal milestones or individual strokeplay over the team's run-chase or defensive requirements cuts to the heart of what Indian cricket expects from its middle-order batters.

The timing of the criticism is significant. With the five-match T20I series against England imminent, Tilak Varma will be desperate to silence his critics with performances that clearly demonstrate team-first intent. The backlash from a figure as prominent as Srikkanth adds pressure that the 22-year-old will need to handle maturely.

The episode also reignites broader questions about how India's emerging middle-order batters are being developed and whether the pressure of expectation is shaping their in-game decision-making in the wrong direction. Srikkanth's blast is a wake-up call that will not be easily ignored.
