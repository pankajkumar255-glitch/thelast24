Sarvam AI raises $234 million in first tranche of Series B round

What happened

The New Indian Express reports that AI startup Sarvam has raised $234 million in the first tranche of its Series B funding round. The company focuses on building AI models designed for Indian languages and use cases, distinguishing it from global generalist AI platforms.

The context

Sarvam was founded to address the gap in AI capabilities for India's diverse linguistic landscape, developing models that support Indian languages rather than relying on English-centric systems. The raise comes amid a global race to build sovereign and localised AI infrastructure, with India increasingly prioritising homegrown AI development.

Why it matters

The funding could fast-track the development of AI tools accessible to hundreds of millions of non-English-speaking Indians, with potential applications in healthcare, education, and government services. It also signals India's emergence as a serious destination for large-scale AI investment.

What's next

Expect Sarvam to announce further tranches of its Series B and unveil expanded model capabilities or enterprise partnerships in the coming months.
