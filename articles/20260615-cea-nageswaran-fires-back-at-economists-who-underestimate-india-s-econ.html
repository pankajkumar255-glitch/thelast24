CEA Nageswaran Fires Back at Economists Who Underestimate India's Economy

What happened

ANI News reports that Chief Economic Adviser V. Anantha Nageswaran delivered a pointed message to economists who underestimate India's economy, citing the country's adaptability, capacity to innovate, and resilience as key strengths. His remarks were made in the context of ongoing debates about India's economic performance and outlook among domestic and international economists.

The context

India has faced periodic scepticism from international institutions and economists over its growth data, the quality of job creation, and the sustainability of its consumption-led expansion. The CEA is India's top economic advisory official and his public statements carry significant weight in shaping investor and policy sentiment. India has consistently positioned itself as the world's fastest-growing major economy in recent years.

Why it matters

The CEA's public rebuttal signals that the government remains firmly confident in India's economic fundamentals, which can influence foreign investor sentiment and the country's ability to attract capital. For ordinary Indians, it reinforces the official narrative that the economy is on a strong footing despite global uncertainty.

What's next

Expect Nageswaran to elaborate on India's economic resilience in upcoming policy forums or the pre-budget Economic Survey, where the government typically lays out its assessment of growth prospects.
