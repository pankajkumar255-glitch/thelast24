India's Supreme Court Draws Up Rules for AI in Courtrooms After US Crisis

What happened

NDTV Profit reports that India's Supreme Court is actively planning to set boundaries around AI use in courtrooms, referencing the crisis triggered by AI in US courts as a cautionary backdrop. The report outlines the court's intent to draw clear lines on permissible and impermissible AI applications in legal proceedings.

The context

AI tools, including large language models, have already been used by lawyers in the US to draft briefs, some of which cited fabricated case references — leading to sanctions against attorneys. Indian courts have been cautiously exploring AI for translation, case management, and research tools. The Supreme Court's e-committee has been a key driver of judicial technology adoption in India.

Why it matters

Clear AI guidelines from the Supreme Court will set the standard for all courts in India, affecting how lawyers prepare cases and how judges evaluate AI-assisted submissions — ultimately influencing the quality and reliability of justice delivered to ordinary Indians. Without guardrails, AI errors in legal documents could lead to wrongful outcomes in cases touching on liberty, property, and rights.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to release a formal framework or practice direction on AI use in Indian courts in the coming months.
