China Launches World's First Commercial Brain Chip, Beating Elon Musk's Neuralink

What happened

The Times of India reports that China has launched what is described as the world's first commercial brain chip, beating Elon Musk's Neuralink to market in this space. Musk had previously described the level of technology required for such a device as akin to 'Jesus-level technology', making China's commercial launch a striking development in the global neurotechnology race.

The context

Neuralink, Elon Musk's brain-computer interface company, has been conducting human trials but has not yet launched a commercial product. China has been investing heavily in biotechnology and neurotechnology as part of its strategic push to lead in frontier tech. Brain-computer interfaces have potential applications ranging from treating paralysis and neurological disorders to eventually enabling direct human-computer interaction.

Why it matters

China pulling ahead in commercial neurotechnology intensifies the global tech rivalry and raises questions about regulation, ethics, and national security in countries like India that are still formulating their own AI and emerging-tech policies. For Indian researchers and policymakers, this is a signal that frontier biotech competition is accelerating rapidly.

What's next

Expect international scrutiny of China's safety and regulatory standards for the commercial brain chip, and likely pressure on the US, Europe, and others to fast-track their own neurotechnology approval processes.
