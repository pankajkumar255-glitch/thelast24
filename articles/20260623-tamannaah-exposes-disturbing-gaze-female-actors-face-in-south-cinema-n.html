Tamannaah exposes disturbing gaze female actors face in South cinema: 'Not complimentary'

Key facts

  • Tamannaah Bhatia calls male gaze in South cinema 'not complimentary'
  • Describes the experience as disturbing
  • Remarks reported by The Indian Express

Tamannaah Bhatia has broken her customary reticence on industry politics, speaking bluntly about the objectifying male gaze that female actors navigate in South Indian cinema. In an interview with The Indian Express, the actress was unequivocal: the attention women receive from certain quarters of the industry is, as she put it, 'not complimentary'. Her description of the experience as 'disturbing' signals that even commercially successful, well-established women in the industry do not feel insulated from it.

Tamannaah's intervention carries particular weight given her position — she is one of the most bankable actresses across Telugu, Tamil, and Hindi cinema, with decades of top-line billing behind her. For her to publicly call out an entrenched culture of objectification is a departure from the careful image management that most stars of her standing typically maintain.

Her remarks land in a charged moment for South Indian cinema. The Justice Hema Committee report released last year exposed systemic sexual harassment and exploitation in the Malayalam film industry, triggering arrests, public confessions, and industry-wide reckonings. While the Tamil and Telugu industries have faced similar accusations for years, institutional responses have been slower and more muted.

Tamannaah's words are likely to amplify calls for concrete structural reform — from stronger on-set codes of conduct to genuine representation of women in production decision-making roles — in an industry whose global ambitions are increasingly at odds with its internal culture.
