CEA Nageswaran Defends India's GDP Data, Denies Methodology Manipulation

What happened

The Times of India reports that Chief Economic Adviser V. Anantha Nageswaran publicly defended India's GDP data, stating that the country does not use methodology changes to inflate growth numbers. His remarks address recurring criticism from some economists and international observers who have questioned the reliability of India's official growth figures.

The context

India has frequently reported among the highest GDP growth rates globally, drawing both admiration and scepticism. Questions about whether base-year revisions and methodological shifts artificially elevate headline numbers have been raised by independent economists for several years, making official defences of the data periodically necessary.

Why it matters

Trust in GDP data influences how global investors, credit rating agencies, and multilateral institutions assess India's economic health, affecting the cost of borrowing and capital inflows. For ordinary Indians, the credibility of these numbers shapes government spending decisions on welfare, infrastructure, and employment schemes.

What's next

Expect the debate over India's statistical methodology to continue, with independent economists and international bodies likely scrutinising the next set of national accounts data closely.
