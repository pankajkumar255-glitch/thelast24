SEBI Moves Supreme Court Against Relief Granted to Managers of Sahara Group Entity in OFCD Case

What happened

Mint reports that SEBI has moved the Supreme Court to challenge relief that was granted to managers of a Sahara Group entity in the long-running OFCD case. The specifics of the relief being challenged and the tribunal or court that granted it were not detailed in the headline.

The context

The Sahara-SEBI dispute is one of India's most prolonged corporate legal battles, stemming from Sahara's fundraising of tens of thousands of crores from millions of retail investors through OFCDs that the Supreme Court ruled in 2012 were illegal. Sahara's founder Subrata Roy spent time in custody over non-compliance with court orders to refund investors. Despite years of litigation, full investor repayment remains unresolved.

Why it matters

SEBI's latest move keeps pressure on those who managed Sahara's financial operations, potentially widening personal accountability beyond the group's top leadership. For retail investors who are still owed money, the regulator's aggressive stance is the primary mechanism through which repayment obligations might eventually be enforced.

What's next

Expect the Supreme Court to schedule a hearing on SEBI's petition, with the outcome potentially affecting how individual managers of errant financial entities are held liable going forward.
