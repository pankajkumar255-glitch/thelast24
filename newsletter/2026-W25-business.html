India-U.S. Trade Deal Down to 'Final Touches' as Greer Set to Visit New Delhi on June 23-24

A bilateral trade agreement between India and the United States is in its closing stages, with only 'final touches' remaining before it can be signed. → A finalised India-U.S. trade deal could directly affect tariffs on Indian exports and the prices of American goods in India — hitting sectors from textiles and pharma to electronics that millions of workers and consumers depend on.

Meta taps CRED founder Kunal Shah as WhatsApp's new chief, invests $900M in his startup

Meta has appointed Kunal Shah, the Indian entrepreneur who founded CRED, as the new head of WhatsApp — one of the world's most widely used messaging platforms. Alongside the leadership appointment, Meta is also investing $900 million into Shah's startup, a significant vote of confidence in one of India's most prominent fintech founders. WhatsApp has over 500 million users in India alone, making this a consequential leadership change for the platform's largest market. The dual move signals Meta's intent to deepen its engagement with Indian tech talent and the Indian market.

→ With WhatsApp's 500 million-plus Indian users and a $900 million Meta investment flowing into an Indian-founded startup, this is as direct a validation of India's tech ecosystem as it gets — and the new boss will shape how Indians message, pay, and transact.

India becomes world's top ship recycling nation in 2025, hitting Vision 2030 target five years early

India has overtaken all rivals to become the world's number one ship recycling nation in 2025, achieving a target set under its Vision 2030 maritime policy a full five years ahead of schedule. The milestone places India — primarily through the Alang ship-breaking yards in Gujarat, the world's largest such facility — ahead of Bangladesh, Turkey, and Pakistan in global ship recycling volumes. The achievement reflects both growing global shipping fleet retirements and India's improved safety, environmental, and regulatory standards at its yards. It is a significant milestone for the country's blue economy ambitions.

→ Ship recycling is a multi-billion dollar industry and a major source of steel scrap for India's domestic market — being number one globally means more revenue, more recovered raw material, and stronger footing in the global maritime supply chain.
