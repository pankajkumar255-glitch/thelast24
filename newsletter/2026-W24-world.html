This week in World

The 6 stories that mattered, 14 Jun–21 Jun.

20:29 IST

Trump Slams Israel Over Beirut Bombing: 'Should Not Have Happened'

US President Donald Trump publicly criticised Israel for bombing Beirut, saying the strike "should not have happened." The statement is a rare moment of open disagreement between Trump and the Israeli government, which has been conducting military operations in Lebanon. Trump's rebuke signals potential friction in the US-Israel relationship at a sensitive moment in the Middle East conflict. The comment comes as international pressure mounts over civilian casualties in Lebanon.

→ Tensions between the US and Israel could reshape the direction of the Lebanon conflict and affect India's significant diaspora and diplomatic interests in the region.

03:20 IST

Trump Declares Iran Peace Deal 'Now Complete', Strait of Hormuz Open

US President Donald Trump announced that a peace deal with Iran is "now complete" and that the Strait of Hormuz — the critical waterway through which a significant share of the world's oil passes — is open. The declaration comes in the wake of Israeli strikes on Lebanon and rising tensions across the Middle East. The announcement signals a potential de-escalation after weeks of heightened conflict in the region, with major implications for global energy markets.

→ If the Strait of Hormuz stays open and tensions ease, crude oil prices that directly affect petrol and diesel costs for every Indian could stabilise.

15:09 IST

Russian Strikes Kill Nine, Damage Historic Kyiv Cathedral

Russian missile and drone strikes on Kyiv killed nine people and caused significant damage to a historic cathedral in the Ukrainian capital. The attack is among the deadliest on Kyiv in recent weeks, striking civilian areas alongside cultural heritage sites. The assault comes as international diplomatic efforts to broker a ceasefire remain stalled, highlighting the continued intensity of the war now in its fourth year.

→ With no ceasefire in sight, Russian strikes on Ukrainian cities are escalating — affecting global energy prices, refugee flows, and India's delicate balancing act between Moscow and the West.

22:35 IST

Trump Says Strait of Hormuz Will Be 'Completely Open' on Friday

US President Donald Trump has declared that the Strait of Hormuz — the narrow waterway through which roughly a fifth of the world's oil passes — will be completely open by Friday. The announcement follows the US-brokered ceasefire between Israel and Iran and signals a rapid de-escalation of the Gulf crisis. An open Strait is critical to global energy supply chains and oil price stability.

→ A blockaded or restricted Strait of Hormuz would send global oil prices soaring, directly hitting petrol and diesel costs for every Indian household.

07:30 IST

Trump Says Iran Has Agreed To Never Have Nuclear Weapons After Historic Deal

US President Donald Trump declared that Iran has agreed to never pursue nuclear weapons, following a landmark deal between Washington and Tehran. Trump also rejected claims of a $300 million fund connected to the agreement. The announcement marks a dramatic shift in US-Iran relations, coming after years of maximum-pressure sanctions and repeated military tensions over Iran's nuclear programme.

→ A nuclear deal involving Iran directly affects oil prices and regional stability — both of which have an outsized impact on India's economy and the large Indian diaspora living across the Middle East.
