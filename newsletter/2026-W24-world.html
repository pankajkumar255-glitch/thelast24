The 6 stories that mattered, 13 Jun–20 Jun.

16:45 IST

Israel Strikes Lebanon as Iran Claims US Nuclear Deal Is Near

Israel carried out fresh air strikes on Lebanon even as Iran's state media claimed a deal with the United States over Tehran's nuclear programme is close to finalisation. A senior US official had earlier expressed 80–85% confidence in a peace agreement, though Iran has attached conditions. The twin developments highlight how volatile the Middle East remains even as diplomacy accelerates.

→ A nuclear deal or a wider Middle East war directly affects India's oil import costs, the safety of over 8 million Indians in the Gulf, and global markets.

18:06 IST

Trump Threatens to Hit Iran 'Very Hard Tonight', Then Calls Off Strikes Hours Later

US President Donald Trump threatened on social media to strike Iran 'very hard tonight' and also declared he would take 'Venezuela-like control' of Iranian oil if Tehran did not comply with his demands. Hours later, NDTV reported that Trump called off the planned strikes, in what appears to be another episode of brinkmanship in the ongoing US-Iran nuclear standoff. The dramatic about-turn within a single evening has rattled global markets and raised fresh questions about the direction of US foreign policy in West Asia.

→ A US military strike on Iran would spike global oil prices overnight, directly hitting fuel costs and inflation for every Indian household.

18:06 IST

Trump Threatens to Hit Iran 'Very Hard Tonight' and Seize Control of Its Oil

US President Donald Trump escalated his rhetoric against Iran dramatically, threatening to strike the country 'very hard' and take Venezuela-style control of its oil if a nuclear deal is not reached. The statement, reported by NDTV, came hours after a separate report indicated Iran had not yet approved any deal with the United States. The threat marks one of the most severe public ultimatums Trump has issued in the ongoing US-Iran nuclear standoff, raising fears of a significant military escalation in the Middle East.

→ A military strike on Iran or a spike in global oil prices would immediately push up fuel and everyday commodity costs for Indian households and businesses.

22:31 IST

Trump Claims Iran Deal Set for Sunday, Strait of Hormuz to Stay Open

US President Donald Trump announced that a deal with Iran is expected to be finalised on Sunday, assuring that the Strait of Hormuz will remain open to all nations. The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most critical oil shipping chokepoints, through which nearly a fifth of global oil supply passes. A diplomatic agreement, if concluded, would mark a significant shift in US-Iran relations after years of maximum-pressure sanctions and nuclear standoffs.

→ A US-Iran deal could directly affect global oil prices and India's energy import bill, making it one of the most consequential diplomatic developments for Indian consumers right now.

20:29 IST

Trump Slams Israel Over Beirut Bombing: 'Should Not Have Happened'

US President Donald Trump publicly criticised Israel for bombing Beirut, saying the strike "should not have happened." The statement is a rare moment of open disagreement between Trump and the Israeli government, which has been conducting military operations in Lebanon. Trump's rebuke signals potential friction in the US-Israel relationship at a sensitive moment in the Middle East conflict. The comment comes as international pressure mounts over civilian casualties in Lebanon.

→ Tensions between the US and Israel could reshape the direction of the Lebanon conflict and affect India's significant diaspora and diplomatic interests in the region.

03:20 IST

Trump Declares Iran Peace Deal 'Now Complete', Strait of Hormuz Open

US President Donald Trump announced that a peace deal with Iran is "now complete" and that the Strait of Hormuz — the critical waterway through which a significant share of the world's oil passes — is open. The declaration comes in the wake of Israeli strikes on Lebanon and rising tensions across the Middle East. The announcement signals a potential de-escalation after weeks of heightened conflict in the region, with major implications for global energy markets.

→ If the Strait of Hormuz stays open and tensions ease, crude oil prices that directly affect petrol and diesel costs for every Indian could stabilise.
