Sensex surges 1,000 points as investors cheer Iran-US peace deal; RIL up 2%

The Sensex jumped 1,000 points in early trade after the United States and Iran announced a peace deal, sharply reducing geopolitical risk in West Asia and lifting global investor sentiment. Reliance Industries climbed 2%, reflecting optimism around easing energy supply chains and trade routes. The rally signals broad-based buying across rate-sensitive and oil-linked sectors. A calmer West Asia directly improves India's macro outlook given its heavy dependence on Gulf oil imports.

→ A 1,000-point Sensex jump means your mutual fund and equity portfolio likely got a meaningful boost overnight — and cheaper oil ahead could ease inflation at the pump.

Sensex surges 1,000 points as investors cheer Iran-US peace deal

Indian stock markets rallied sharply on Sunday morning, with the Sensex jumping 1,000 points after the United States and Iran reached a peace deal. The agreement has significantly reduced geopolitical tensions in West Asia, a region critical to global oil supply chains. The rally reflects broad investor relief that a prolonged conflict scenario has been averted, boosting risk appetite across emerging markets including India.

→ A 1,000-point Sensex jump means your mutual funds and equity investments are likely richer today — this is the market directly rewarding geopolitical calm.

17:33 IST

India-US Trade Talks Set for June 23-24 as First-Phase Deal Nears Finish Line

India and the United States are scheduled to hold fresh trade talks on June 23-24, with US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer travelling to India for the negotiations. Both sides are working to finalise the first tranche of a bilateral trade deal, with the June meetings seen as a critical milestone. The talks come amid India's effort to secure a trade agreement before a potential reimposition of higher US tariffs threatens export competitiveness.

→ A US trade deal could shield Indian exporters — from textiles to pharmaceuticals — from punishing American tariffs, directly affecting jobs and prices at home.

16:18 IST

SpaceX Overtakes Microsoft and Amazon in Market Cap After IPO

SpaceX has surpassed both Microsoft and Amazon in market capitalisation following its initial public offering, making it one of the most valuable companies in the world. The listing marks a landmark moment for the private space and satellite industry, elevating Elon Musk's rocket and satellite firm into the ranks of the world's biggest corporations. The development signals a seismic shift in how investors value deep-tech and space ventures globally.

→ For Indian investors and tech-sector watchers, SpaceX's valuation leap reshapes the global pecking order of Big Tech and could influence how Indian space startups are valued.

21:32 IST

India and UK set July 15 as the date to launch their historic free trade agreement

India and the United Kingdom have overcome a last-minute dispute over steel tariffs to formally announce July 15 as the implementation date for their long-negotiated free trade agreement. The steel hurdle — a key sticking point around import duties — was resolved through reassurances, clearing the path for what both sides are calling a landmark bilateral deal. The agreement is expected to significantly boost trade in goods and services between the two countries.

→ The India-UK FTA could lower duties on Indian goods like textiles, leather, and pharmaceuticals entering the UK, while also shaping the cost of imported British goods for Indian consumers and businesses.
