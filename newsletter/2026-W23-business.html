Sensex Jumps 1,700 Points in a Single Session

The BSE Sensex surged 1,700 points in a dramatic single-session rally, according to India Today. The sharp move was driven by three distinct catalysts, signalling a broad-based recovery in Indian equities. The gain marks one of the more significant single-day advances for the benchmark index in recent months, reflecting a sharp reversal in investor sentiment. Such moves often reset the mood for retail and institutional investors alike.

→ If you have money in mutual funds, a pension, or direct stocks, a 1,700-point Sensex jump directly lifts your portfolio value — even if just for the day.

Sensex Surges 1,700 Points, Investors Richer by Rs 10.2 Lakh Crore in Single Session

The BSE Sensex jumped nearly 1,700 points in a single trading session, marking one of its sharpest single-day rallies in recent months. The surge added approximately Rs 10.2 lakh crore to investor wealth in one day. India Today and The Times of India both reported on the dramatic move, pointing to three key drivers behind the rally. The gains were broad-based across sectors, reflecting a strong return of buying interest in Indian equities.

→ If you have money in mutual funds, a provident fund, or direct stocks, a Rs 10.2 lakh crore wealth addition in a single day means your portfolio likely got a significant boost today.

21:18 IST

India-US Trade Deal Talks Intensify: USTR Greer to Visit India After G7

US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer is set to travel to India for trade negotiations following the G7 summit, where Prime Minister Modi and President Trump are expected to discuss the bilateral trade relationship. Multiple US officials have confirmed that while a deal is possible, it is not imminent. The development signals that both sides are moving to a more structured phase of negotiations after months of tariff-related tensions.

→ A US trade deal could reshape tariffs on Indian exports — directly affecting prices and jobs across manufacturing, tech, and agriculture.

15:30 IST

Sensex falls 151 points to 73,833 as IT drags; second straight losing session

The Sensex ended 151 points lower at 73,833 and the Nifty 50 dropped 53 points to 23,162, falling for a second straight session. The Nifty IT index led the decline with a fall of about 1.6% as 11 of 15 major NSE sector gauges closed lower. Mahindra & Mahindra, ICICI Bank, Kotak Mahindra Bank and Bharti Airtel were among the few gainers, while market breadth stayed firmly negative.

→ IT — the biggest weight in many mutual fund portfolios — is bearing the brunt of global AI-spending jitters; if your SIPs are tech-heavy, expect choppiness while West Asia tensions and FII selling persist.

09:30 IST

May inflation print due today; foreign outflows hit a record $30.6 billion

India's May consumer inflation data is due later today, with the annual rate expected to accelerate to around 4.0% from 3.48% in April. Foreign portfolio investors sold another $207 million of Indian equities on Thursday, taking year-to-date outflows to a record $30.6 billion. The rupee's weakness near 95.5 to the dollar adds to imported-inflation pressure from costlier crude.

→ A 4% print would still be within the RBI's comfort band — but the combination of rising oil, a weak rupee and record foreign selling is exactly the mix that delays rate cuts on your home loan.

17:30 IST

Canara Bank raises MCLR by 5 bps across key tenures from June 12

Canara Bank hiked its marginal cost of funds-based lending rate by 5 basis points across the month, quarter and six-month tenures, effective June 12. UCO Bank separately raised its treasury-bill-linked rates by up to 15 basis points earlier in the week. The moves come as banks reprice funds amid tighter liquidity and a weak rupee.

→ MCLR hikes feed straight into older floating-rate loans — if your home or business loan is MCLR-linked, your EMI maths just shifted slightly against you.
