13:15 IST

Supreme Court declares right to walk on footpaths a fundamental right

The Supreme Court of India has ruled that the right to walk on footpaths is a fundamental right, a landmark judgment that extends constitutional protections to pedestrians across the country. The ruling signals that the state has an obligation to ensure footpaths are usable, safe, and free from encroachments. The decision is significant in a country where pavements are routinely blocked by illegal constructions, vendors, and parked vehicles, effectively forcing pedestrians onto roads. The judgment is expected to have wide consequences for urban planning and civic enforcement.

→ In India's chaotic cities, where millions walk to work daily on encroached or nonexistent footpaths, this ruling gives citizens a constitutional tool to demand safe, clear pavements from local governments.

19:01 IST

Lucknow coaching centre fire kills at least 14, high-level inquiry ordered

A devastating fire tore through a coaching centre in Lucknow, killing at least 14 people and injuring several others, with students reportedly jumping from the first floor to escape the blaze. Uttar Pradesh Deputy Chief Minister ordered a high-level inquiry into the tragedy. The fire broke out during what appeared to be active class hours, trapping students inside the building.

→ Coaching centre fires have claimed young lives repeatedly across India — this tragedy renews urgent questions about whether commercial educational buildings are being allowed to operate without basic fire safety compliance.

13:23 IST

Akasa Air Eyes 30% Capacity Growth in FY27, Adds 39th Aircraft

Akasa Air has set its sights on 30% capacity growth in the financial year 2027, as the young airline continues a rapid fleet expansion that recently saw it add its 39th aircraft. The carrier is also reportedly weighing participation in a government credit scheme to support its growth plans. The targets mark one of the most ambitious expansion trajectories among Indian carriers and come as Akasa seeks to cement its position in an increasingly competitive domestic aviation market.

→ For travellers, more Akasa seats on more routes could mean greater competition and better fares — making this expansion directly relevant to anyone who flies domestically.

23:36 IST

Man Stabbed to Death on Moving Mumbai Local Train After Row Over Closing Door; Accused Arrested

A passenger was stabbed to death aboard a moving Mumbai local train after a quarrel broke out over closing a door — a shocking act of violence on one of the city's most heavily used public transport arteries. The accused, identified as Roshan Suvarna, was subsequently tracked down and arrested by police. The victim's family has demanded the death penalty for the perpetrator. The incident has triggered outrage and prompted questions about security aboard Mumbai's suburban rail network.

→ A stabbing death on a Mumbai local train — used by millions of commuters daily — is a direct public safety concern for ordinary Mumbaikars and raises urgent questions about security on the suburban rail network.

21:54 IST

India's Financial Crime Agency Flags Forex Breaches and Missing Records at Rajesh Exports

India's financial crime agency has flagged foreign exchange violations and missing records at Rajesh Exports, one of the country's best-known gold and jewellery companies, in a significant regulatory development for the sector. The agency's findings point to potential breaches of foreign exchange rules alongside gaps in documentation that companies are required to maintain. The move places Rajesh Exports under intensified scrutiny at a time when regulators are taking a harder look at compliance in the gems and jewellery sector. The company has yet to publicly respond in detail to the allegations.

→ A financial crime agency flagging forex breaches at a major Indian jewellery exporter is significant for investors, industry stakeholders, and anyone watching how India enforces compliance in its large export-oriented sectors.

14:52 IST

Pune Fort Murder: Woman Met Lover at Café Hours Before Killing Fiancé; Victim's Father Demands Answers

New details have emerged in the Pune fort murder case, with investigators revealing that the accused woman met her lover at a café just hours before the killing of her fiancé, apparently to finalise their plan. The victim's father has reacted with anguish, asking why his son had to be killed when the couple could simply have called off the wedding. The case has drawn significant public attention for its cold premeditation and the betrayal it represents. Police have been gathering evidence including CCTV footage and witness accounts.

→ The Pune fort murder case — with its premeditated planning and a victim whose family is publicly seeking justice — has become a flashpoint for debates about personal safety and accountability in intimate relationships.
