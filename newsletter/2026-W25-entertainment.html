'Peek In, Peek Out': New Documentary Brings Delhi's Khirki Village Migrant Story to Screen

A new film titled Peek In, Peek Out, set in Delhi's Khirki village, brings the story of India's internal migrants to the fore, according to The Indian Express. Khirki, a dense urban village in South Delhi, has long been a transit and settlement point for migrants from across India and parts of Africa, making it a microcosm of the country's complex urban demographic shifts. The film's release marks a rare instance of Indian documentary or indie filmmaking training its lens on an often-overlooked neighbourhood and its residents.

→ India's internal migration story is one of the defining socioeconomic realities of our time, and films that humanise it beyond statistics fill a genuine gap in the national cultural conversation.

Sonam Khan Reacts to 40-Year-Old Rumour About Being Cast in Tridev's 'Oye Oye'

Actress Sonam Khan has responded publicly to a decades-old rumour that she was originally cast to perform the iconic 'Oye Oye' song in the 1989 blockbuster Tridev, a track that became one of Hindi cinema's most enduring dance numbers. The rumour, apparently circulating for forty years, has resurfaced and prompted Khan to address it directly. The song was ultimately performed by Sangeeta Bijlani and remains closely associated with the era's popular cinema.

→ For fans of 1980s Bollywood, a veteran actress finally addressing a forty-year casting rumour around one of that decade's most iconic songs is a rare piece of film history being set straight.

The exchange, reported by The Indian Express, underlines Akhtar's long-held secular and rationalist public stance. The 79-year-old has consistently been one of Bollywood's most outspoken voices on religion, nationalism, and social issues.
