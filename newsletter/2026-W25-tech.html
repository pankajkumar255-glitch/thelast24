The 6 stories that mattered, 21 Jun–28 Jun.

20:51 IST

CRED's Kunal Shah Named Head of WhatsApp as Meta Invests $900M in His Startup

Meta has appointed Kunal Shah, the Indian entrepreneur who founded fintech startup CRED, as the new chief of WhatsApp — the world's largest messaging platform with over two billion users. Alongside the appointment, Meta is investing $900 million in CRED, marking one of the largest bets on an Indian startup by a global tech giant. Shah becomes the first Indian founder to lead a flagship Meta product. The move signals Meta's deepening interest in India's digital economy.

→ An Indian founder now runs the world's biggest messaging app — used by hundreds of millions of Indians daily — while Meta's $900M bet supercharges one of India's most prominent fintechs.

19:53 IST

X Goes Down for Thousands Globally, India Among Countries Hit by Outage

X, the social media platform formerly known as Twitter, suffered a widespread outage that left thousands of users unable to access the service, with India among the countries significantly affected. Users reported being unable to load timelines, post content, or access the app entirely. The disruption was reported across both mobile and desktop platforms. Outages on X have become a recurring concern since Elon Musk's acquisition and subsequent restructuring of the platform's engineering teams.

→ With millions of Indians relying on X for news, political discourse, and business updates, even a brief outage disrupts a key information channel — and raises questions about platform reliability.

22:30 IST

Valve Prices the Steam Machine at $1,049

Valve has officially priced its Steam Machine gaming PC at $1,049, marking a significant development for the PC gaming hardware market. The Steam Machine, which runs on Valve's Linux-based SteamOS, aims to bring the Steam gaming library to a living-room console-style form factor. The pricing positions it above mainstream gaming consoles but below high-end custom PC builds. The announcement is expected to reignite interest in SteamOS as a Windows alternative for gamers.

→ For India's rapidly growing PC gaming community, the Steam Machine's pricing sets a benchmark for what a dedicated living-room Linux gaming device costs, influencing import and grey-market interest.

20:25 IST

Air India Appoints Former Civil Aviation Secretary as Advisor to Chairman

Air India has appointed a former civil aviation secretary of India as advisor to its chairman, the airline announced. The appointment brings high-level regulatory and policy expertise into the carrier's leadership structure at a critical period of its transformation under the Tata Group. Air India is in the midst of a sweeping overhaul — expanding its fleet, integrating acquired carriers, and rebuilding its service reputation after decades of government ownership. Adding a former top bureaucrat as a strategic advisor signals the airline's focus on navigating complex aviation policy and regulatory relationships.

→ As Air India undergoes its biggest transformation in decades under the Tatas, bringing in a former civil aviation secretary strengthens its hand in dealing with DGCA regulations, airport slot negotiations, and government policy — all of which affect ticket prices and route availability for Indian flyers.

20:48 IST

Kunal Shah, CRED founder, appointed WhatsApp CEO as Meta invests $900 million in his startup

Meta has appointed Kunal Shah, the Indian entrepreneur who founded fintech rewards platform CRED, as the new head of WhatsApp — the world's largest messaging app with over two billion users. Simultaneously, Meta is investing $900 million into CRED, marking one of the largest single investments into an Indian startup. Shah will step back from day-to-day operations at CRED to lead WhatsApp globally. The appointment marks the first time an Indian founder will run one of the world's most consequential consumer technology platforms.

→ An Indian founder taking charge of WhatsApp — used daily by nearly every smartphone user in India — signals how central India's tech ecosystem has become to global big tech strategy, and could shape the app's future direction for its largest market.

19:53 IST

X goes down globally as thousands of users report widespread outages

X, the social media platform formerly known as Twitter, experienced a significant outage with thousands of users worldwide reporting they were unable to access the platform. Downtime tracking services recorded a sharp spike in outage reports from users across multiple countries and regions. The cause of the disruption was not immediately confirmed by X or its parent company. The outage drew widespread attention given X's role as a real-time public information platform used by journalists, governments, businesses and ordinary users.

→ With X increasingly used in India for breaking news, political discourse and public announcements, even a short outage disrupts the information flow that millions of Indians — and institutions — rely on daily.
