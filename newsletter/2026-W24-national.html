Telegram Moves Delhi High Court Against Government Blocking Order Ahead of NEET UG Re-Exam

Telegram has challenged a temporary government blocking order in the Delhi High Court, with a hearing scheduled for the same day. The move comes directly ahead of the NEET UG re-examination, with the government reportedly ordering the block following concerns that the platform was being used to circulate leaked exam content. The case puts digital freedoms and exam integrity on a collision course in a high-stakes legal setting.

→ Millions of NEET UG aspirants are directly affected — if Telegram remains accessible with leaked content circulating, the integrity of the re-exam is undermined; if blocked, students lose a widely used study resource overnight.

Telegram challenges Indian government order temporarily blocking its use over NEET paper leak fears

Telegram is legally contesting an Indian government order that temporarily blocks its use in the country, reportedly issued over fears linked to the NEET examination paper leak controversy. The messaging app, which has hundreds of millions of users in India, is pushing back against the ban through official channels. The move puts the government's internet-blocking powers and the platform's accountability in the spotlight.

Delhi High Court to Deliver Judgment on Telegram Ban in India

The Delhi High Court is set to deliver its judgment in a case concerning a potential ban on Telegram in India, The Indian Express reports. The case has drawn significant attention given Telegram's widespread use across the country for personal messaging, news channels, and group communications. The ruling could have major implications for the app's availability and operation in India.

→ A Delhi High Court ban or restriction on Telegram could directly affect millions of Indians who use the app daily for news, group chats, and file sharing.

10:34 IST

Delhi High Court upholds temporary Telegram ban, says government had sufficient reasons

The Delhi High Court has upheld the central government's temporary ban on the messaging platform Telegram, ruling that the ban was not disproportionate and that authorities had sufficient reasons to impose it. The court denied Telegram any interim relief against the Centre's move. The ruling marks a significant judicial endorsement of the government's power to restrict a major communications platform in India, which has tens of millions of users.

→ If the government can temporarily block Telegram with court backing, millions of Indians who rely on it for communication, work channels, and communities face real disruption with limited legal recourse.

12:25 IST

Supreme Court declares right to walk on footpaths a fundamental right, calls for dedicated pedestrian law

The Supreme Court of India has ruled that the right to walk on demarcated footpaths is a fundamental right, marking a significant expansion of constitutional protections for pedestrians. The court directed that all roads must have proper footpaths and called for the creation of a dedicated law to protect pedestrian safety. The ruling comes against the backdrop of a sharp surge in pedestrian deaths on Indian roads, with millions of citizens daily denied safe walking space by encroachments and poor infrastructure. The bench's order puts the onus on governments to ensure compliant, usable footpaths across the country.

→ For millions of Indians who walk to work, school, or markets daily, this ruling means governments are now constitutionally bound to build and maintain safe footpaths — giving citizens legal standing to demand walkable streets.
