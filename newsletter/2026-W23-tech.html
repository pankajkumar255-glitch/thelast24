This week in Technology

The 6 stories that mattered, 07 Jun–14 Jun.

13:11 IST

Anthropic access row rekindles self-reliance debate; Sridhar Vembu says 'Globalisation Is Dead'

A US government ban restricting Indians and all foreign nationals from accessing Anthropic's advanced AI models — Claude Fable 5 and Mythos — has triggered a sharp debate in India's technology community about the risks of dependence on foreign AI platforms. Zoho founder Sridhar Vembu has publicly declared 'Globalisation Is Dead,' using the Anthropic access restrictions as evidence that India cannot rely on Western technology companies for critical AI tools. India Today explains the ban stems from US government directives controlling the export of frontier AI capabilities to non-US persons. The episode has given fresh urgency to India's push for indigenous AI models like the just-launched Varya.

→ If the US can cut off Indian access to advanced AI tools overnight, every Indian company and developer using foreign AI platforms faces a real business continuity risk — this is not a hypothetical threat anymore.

15:00 IST

US orders Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos AI models globally; Indian firms sound alarm

The United States government has directed AI company Anthropic to suspend its Fable 5 and Mythos AI models worldwide, citing export control and national security concerns. The move effectively bars Indians and all non-US users from accessing these models. Indian companies and professionals who had integrated these tools into their workflows are now scrambling to find alternatives, raising urgent questions about dependency on foreign AI infrastructure.

→ If your company or team uses Anthropic's AI models for work, those tools are now switched off — a sharp reminder of how exposed Indian businesses are to US tech policy decisions.

17:26 IST

Meta and Reliance Strike AI Data Centre Deal, Deepening India Bet

Meta has agreed to a significant AI data centre partnership with Mukesh Ambani's Reliance Industries, deepening an existing alliance between the two giants. The deal positions Reliance's infrastructure as a key pillar for Meta's AI expansion in India. CNBC and Reuters both confirm the arrangement, underscoring its scale and strategic importance for Meta's hyperscaler ambitions in the region. This marks a meaningful escalation in the role Indian infrastructure will play in global AI deployment.

→ A Meta-Reliance data centre tie-up means India's digital backbone is becoming central to the world's AI race — with potential knock-on effects for local jobs, cloud services, and internet costs.

11:00 IST

TCS partners with Anthropic to build a dedicated enterprise AI unit

Tata Consultancy Services has partnered with AI company Anthropic to set up a new business unit focused on scaling enterprise AI adoption. The move deepens the tie-up between India's largest IT services firm and one of the leading frontier AI labs. It comes as Indian IT majors race to convert AI demand into services revenue amid investor scrutiny of the sector.

→ India's IT giants are repositioning from coding shops to AI-deployment partners — that's where the next wave of tech hiring and reskilling demand will come from.

12:30 IST

Lenskart shares worth Rs 1,960 crore change hands in a single block deal

About 4 crore shares of Lenskart Solutions, worth roughly Rs 1,960 crore, reportedly changed hands in a block deal. Large block trades of this size typically signal an early investor paring its stake or institutional repositioning. The eyewear retailer has been among the most watched new-age listings on the exchanges.

→ Big block deals in new-age stocks are worth watching — early-investor exits can pressure prices short-term but also widen public float, often a precursor to index inclusion.

14:00 IST

Power Grid approves Rs 485 crore upgrade of national power monitoring systems

Power Grid Corporation's board approved a Rs 485 crore capital expenditure to upgrade the systems that monitor India's electricity network. The modernisation targets the technology layer that watches grid flows in real time. It follows a period of record peak demand and growing renewable inputs that make grid management harder.

→ Smarter grid monitoring is what stands between record summer demand and blackouts — and it's a growing order pipeline for Indian grid-tech and automation firms.
