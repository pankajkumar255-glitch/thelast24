Homemakers are 'nation builders', their work worth at least Rs 30,000 a month: Supreme Court

The Supreme Court has ruled that homemakers deserve to be recognised as 'nation builders' and that the value of their unpaid domestic labour should be pegged at a minimum of Rs 30,000 per month. The ruling is significant in the context of compensation claims — particularly in accident and insurance cases — where courts have historically undervalued or ignored the economic contribution of women who work at home. The judgment marks a shift in how Indian law formally reckons with unpaid domestic work.

→ If you or a family member is a homemaker, this ruling could directly raise the compensation you receive in insurance claims, accident cases, or legal disputes involving domestic labour.

The Supreme Court has issued a landmark ruling that fundamentally reframes how India understands sex work, placing individual consent and personal liberty at the heart of the legal framework. The judgment marks a significant shift from treating sex work purely through the lens of criminality or morality.
