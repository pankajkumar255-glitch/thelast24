The 90-minute White House ultimatum that killed Anthropic's most powerful AI model

Moneycontrol reveals the dramatic backstory behind Anthropic's sudden shutdown of its Fable 5 AI model: a 90-minute ultimatum from the White House and an intervention by Amazon, which is a major investor in Anthropic. The sequence of events — combining US export-control pressures and a high-level government demand — forced Anthropic to disable the model just days after launch. The episode has sent shockwaves through the global AI industry over how geopolitics can override technical ambition overnight.

→ If the US government can shut down a leading AI model within 90 minutes, every Indian startup or enterprise betting on American AI platforms needs to reckon with that fragility.

Anthropic Halts Access to New AI Models After US Order — Indian Tech Leaders Respond

Anthropic has blocked access to its newest AI models following an order from the US government, in a move that has sent shockwaves through the global tech industry. Indian tech leaders are now publicly weighing in on what the sudden restriction means for businesses that had built workflows and products around Anthropic's Claude models. The development has exposed the vulnerability of depending on foreign AI platforms subject to US export-control decisions, and is already forcing Indian companies to reassess their AI vendor strategies.

→ If your company or favourite app relies on Anthropic's AI, this US government order could mean disruptions to tools and services you use every day — and it shows how little control India currently has over its AI supply chain.

23:10 IST

Sarvam AI raises $234 million in Series B to build India's own AI stack

Indian AI startup Sarvam has raised $234 million in the first tranche of its Series B funding round, marking one of the largest raises by an Indian AI company. Sarvam, which focuses on building large language models in Indian languages, has been positioning itself as a homegrown alternative to Western AI platforms. The fresh capital is expected to accelerate its model development and expand its enterprise deployments across India.

→ A massive bet on India-first AI — Sarvam's raise signals that global investors see a real market in building AI tools tailored for Indian languages and users.
