HCLTech Buys 10.5% Stake in Sarvam AI as Indian Startup Turns Unicorn at $1.5 Billion Valuation

HCLTech has agreed to acquire a 10.5% stake in Sarvam AI, valuing the Bengaluru-based artificial intelligence startup at $1.5 billion. The deal comes alongside Sarvam raising $234 million in the first tranche of its Series B funding round, pushing it into unicorn territory. Sarvam AI, which builds large language models designed specifically for Indian languages, is now one of India's most prominent homegrown AI ventures. The HCLTech investment signals growing corporate India interest in backing sovereign, India-first AI infrastructure.

→ A major Indian IT giant betting big on a homegrown AI startup signals that the race to build India-specific artificial intelligence is now serious business — with real money and real stakes.

20:17 IST

HCLTech to buy 10.5% stake in Sarvam AI at $1.5 billion valuation

HCLTech has agreed to acquire a 10.5% stake in Sarvam AI, India's homegrown artificial intelligence startup, valuing it at $1.5 billion. The deal marks one of the largest corporate bets on Indian AI infrastructure to date. HCLTech, one of India's top IT services giants, is positioning itself at the forefront of the country's AI push by backing a startup focused on building foundational models for Indian languages and enterprise use cases.

→ This deal signals that Indian IT giants are moving beyond services into AI ownership — which could shape who controls India's AI future and on whose terms.

21:19 IST

India Orders Temporary Ban on Telegram Over Exam Fraud Concerns

India has ordered a temporary ban on Telegram, citing concerns over the messaging app's use in facilitating exam fraud. The move marks a significant escalation by Indian authorities against the platform, which has over 100 million users in the country. The ban raises immediate questions about enforcement, user access, and the government's broader approach to regulating large encrypted messaging platforms.

→ If the Telegram ban takes effect, tens of millions of Indians who use it for study groups, news channels, and work communication could lose access overnight.

10:37 IST

Accenture's Revenue Warning Triggers IT Bloodbath; Indian Stocks Tumble Up to 7%, Investors Lose Rs 2 Trillion

Indian IT stocks fell sharply by up to 7% after Accenture cut its revenue outlook, triggering a sector-wide sell-off. Investors in Indian software companies lost approximately Rs 2 trillion in market capitalisation in a single session. The warning from Accenture, one of the world's largest IT services firms, is seen as a bellwether for the broader global IT spending environment. The rout has reignited concerns about sluggish discretionary tech spending by clients in the US and Europe.

→ Indian IT giants like TCS, Infosys, and Wipro derive a large share of revenue from the same global clients whose spending Accenture just warned is shrinking — making this a direct threat to jobs, earnings, and dividends that millions of Indian shareholders depend on.

14:28 IST

Jio IPO approved: DRHP to be filed with SEBI on June 19, says Mukesh Ambani

Mukesh Ambani announced at Reliance's AGM 2026 that the board has approved the Jio IPO, with the Draft Red Herring Prospectus to be filed with SEBI on June 19. The listing of Jio — Reliance's telecom and digital services giant — would rank among the largest IPOs in Indian stock market history. Ambani also outlined Reliance's AI ambitions, pledging to build AI that is 'powerful, trusted and affordable'.

→ A Jio IPO would give ordinary Indian retail investors direct access to one of the country's most widely used telecom and digital platforms — and could reshape the size and character of India's stock market.

23:12 IST

Jio files for India's biggest-ever IPO, likely to raise ₹35,000–₹40,000 crore

Reliance Jio has filed for what is set to be India's largest-ever initial public offering, with the share sale expected to raise between ₹35,000 crore and ₹40,000 crore. The filing marks a landmark moment for India's telecom and startup ecosystem, as Jio — the country's largest telecom operator with hundreds of millions of subscribers — prepares to list on domestic exchanges. The IPO has been anticipated for several years and is expected to attract enormous retail and institutional investor interest.

→ If you hold a Jio SIM or are an equity investor, this IPO could be among the biggest wealth events in Indian market history — and will reshape how telecom giants are valued on Indian exchanges.
