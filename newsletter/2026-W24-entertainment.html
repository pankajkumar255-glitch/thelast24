Actress Sanchita Ugale dies at 22: Cine Workers body demands high-level probe, writes to CM Fadnavis

Twenty-two-year-old actress Sanchita Ugale has died in what is being described as an alleged suicide, sending shockwaves through the Marathi film and television industry. The All Indian Cine Workers Association has demanded a high-level inquiry into the case and written directly to Maharashtra Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis. The incident has intensified a broader conversation about the mental health and working conditions of junior artists and young talent in the entertainment industry.

→ A young actress's death has prompted industry bodies to seek government accountability, shining a light on the pressures faced by those trying to break into Bollywood and regional cinema.

Sanchita Ugale's death at 22 triggers demand for high-level probe; cine workers write to CM Fadnavis

Marathi actress Sanchita Ugale, just 22 years old, has died, prompting the Cine Workers body to demand a high-level investigation into the circumstances of her death. The organisation has written directly to Maharashtra Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis seeking intervention. Earlier in the day, her grandfather broke down speaking to the media, saying she had no godfather in the industry and succeeded purely on her own work. The case has cast a spotlight on the vulnerabilities of young artists navigating the film industry without powerful backing.

→ The death of a 22-year-old actress and the subsequent demand for a CM-level probe raises uncomfortable questions about the pressures and safety nets — or lack of them — for young people in the entertainment industry.

09:02 IST

Zabaan Sambhal Ke actor Dinyar Tirandaz passes away

Dinyar Tirandaz, best known for his role in the iconic Hindi sitcom Zabaan Sambhal Ke, has died. The show, which aired in the 1990s, was one of Doordarshan's most beloved comedies and made Tirandaz a familiar face in Indian households. His passing marks the loss of a veteran of Indian television's golden era. Details about the cause of death have not been specified in reports.

→ The death of a beloved face from Doordarshan's golden age of comedy is a moment of nostalgia and loss for an entire generation of Indian TV viewers.

11:39 IST

Shahid Kapoor overhauls script selection process after Deva and O Romeo fail at box office

Shahid Kapoor is reportedly now insisting on taking film narrations alongside his core team following the back-to-back box-office failures of Deva and O Romeo. The actor appears to be recalibrating his professional approach to script selection after a difficult run. The change signals a more collaborative, committee-style decision-making process for one of Bollywood's prominent leading men. It reflects a wider industry trend of stars becoming more cautious after costly flops.

→ When a top Bollywood star publicly changes how he picks films after repeated failures, it reveals the enormous financial stakes involved and the pressure big-budget Hindi cinema faces from OTT competition.

15:59 IST

Akshay Kumar dismisses Welcome To The Jungle salary rumours and addresses retirement

Akshay Kumar has publicly denied rumours about his salary for the upcoming ensemble comedy Welcome To The Jungle and also spoke about his future plans regarding retirement from films. The star's salary has been a subject of industry gossip, prompting him to address it directly. His comments on retirement are noteworthy given his unusually prolific output and his recent string of box-office disappointments. Welcome To The Jungle is one of the most high-profile Bollywood releases in the pipeline.

→ Akshay Kumar addressing both his pay and retirement in one breath underlines the intense scrutiny Bollywood's highest-paid stars face as the industry grapples with a credibility and box-office crisis.
