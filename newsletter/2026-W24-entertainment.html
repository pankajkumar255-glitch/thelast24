Actress Sanchita Ugale dies at 22: Cine Workers body demands high-level probe, writes to CM Fadnavis

Twenty-two-year-old actress Sanchita Ugale has died in what is being described as an alleged suicide, sending shockwaves through the Marathi film and television industry. The All Indian Cine Workers Association has demanded a high-level inquiry into the case and written directly to Maharashtra Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis. The incident has intensified a broader conversation about the mental health and working conditions of junior artists and young talent in the entertainment industry.

→ A young actress's death has prompted industry bodies to seek government accountability, shining a light on the pressures faced by those trying to break into Bollywood and regional cinema.

Sanchita Ugale's death at 22 triggers demand for high-level probe; cine workers write to CM Fadnavis

Marathi actress Sanchita Ugale, just 22 years old, has died, prompting the Cine Workers body to demand a high-level investigation into the circumstances of her death. The organisation has written directly to Maharashtra Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis seeking intervention. Earlier in the day, her grandfather broke down speaking to the media, saying she had no godfather in the industry and succeeded purely on her own work. The case has cast a spotlight on the vulnerabilities of young artists navigating the film industry without powerful backing.

→ The death of a 22-year-old actress and the subsequent demand for a CM-level probe raises uncomfortable questions about the pressures and safety nets — or lack of them — for young people in the entertainment industry.

Drishyam 3 crosses Rs 330 crore worldwide, OTT release date announced

Ajay Devgn's Drishyam 3 has surpassed Rs 330 crore in worldwide box office collections, cementing its place as one of the bigger Hindi hits of the year. The film, the third instalment in the beloved Drishyam franchise, has now also had its OTT release date announced, opening up the next phase of its commercial run. The franchise, known for its gripping suspense and strong repeat-viewing appeal, has consistently performed well across multiplex and single-screen audiences.

→ If you missed Drishyam 3 in theatres, the OTT release date means you can soon watch one of the year's biggest Hindi hits at home.

From Enrique Iglesias to Coldplay: How India's live concert scene transformed in five years

Firstpost examines how the Indian live music market has undergone a dramatic shift over the past five years, moving from occasional international pop acts like Enrique Iglesias to marquee global tours by artists such as Coldplay performing to sold-out stadium crowds. The piece traces the growth in ticketing scale, infrastructure, corporate sponsorship, and fan willingness to spend on live experiences. The transformation signals India's arrival as a serious destination on the global concert circuit.

→ If you've tried — and failed — to get Coldplay tickets, this story explains exactly why Indian concerts have become so competitive and expensive.

18:05 IST

How Lagaan's 'Captain Russell' helped channel relief funds after the 2001 Gujarat earthquake

The Indian Express has published a story revealing how Paul Blackthorne, the British actor who played the antagonist Captain Russell in Aamir Khan's Oscar-nominated 2001 film Lagaan, played a real-life humanitarian role by helping direct relief funds to Gujarat after the devastating earthquake that struck the state the same year. The piece draws a striking parallel between the fictional British villain and the actor's off-screen compassion. The 2001 Gujarat earthquake killed over 20,000 people and remains one of India's deadliest natural disasters.

→ It's a rare and heartwarming behind-the-scenes story that reframes one of Bollywood's most memorable screen villains in a completely different light.
